## 135 - The Remembering That Restores

> *You are not lost because you wandered*  
> *You are found when you remember*

I saw a great height—  
clear,  
open,  
held in quiet light.

From it,  
something drifted downward—  
not falling in violence,  
but carried  
as though it had forgotten  
where it belonged.

Below,  
the air felt unsettled—  
warm in one moment,  
cold in the next—  
never resting,  
never whole.

And yet,  
the height remained.

Unchanged.  
Unmoved.

Not withdrawn,  
not distant—  
simply steady,  
as it had always been.

Then,  
in the midst of the drifting,  
there came a pause.

A stillness  
small enough  
to be missed.

And in that stillness,  
a remembering—  
not forced,  
not explained—  
just a quiet knowing:

I belong there.

The movement slowed.

The pull downward loosened.

Not everything changed at once—  
but the direction shifted.

And I saw  
that the path back  
was not made by striving upward,  
but by turning again  
toward what had never left.

#### Living the Vision

You sense a distance within yourself.

Not a sharp break—  
but a quiet drifting  
from what once felt clear.

You may remember  
a time when something in you  
was more aligned—  
more rooted,  
more at rest.

And now,  
it feels further away.

You do not need  
to retrace every step.

Only to remember.

What once mattered deeply?  
What still carries truth  
when you pause long enough to feel it?

This remembering  
does not accuse.

It invites.

You may begin  
with something small—  
a return to stillness,  
a word of prayer,  
a simple act  
that reflects what you know is true.

You are not rebuilding  
from nothing.

You are turning back  
toward what has remained  
beneath the surface.

And even this small turning  
is already  
a movement home.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Help me remember

> **Exhale:** I return to You
