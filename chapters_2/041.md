## 41 — The Turning Toward Life

> *Even the ache of absence is a call*  
> *What feels empty can lead you home*

I saw a distant place—  
quiet,  
dimmed,  
set apart from the flowing light.

It was not filled with noise,  
but with a heaviness—  
like a space  
where something once offered  
had not been received.

There were many there.

Not lost beyond reach,  
but turned inward—  
as if holding tightly  
to what could not sustain them.

Their presence carried  
a deep longing—

not sharp,  
but steady,  
like a thirst  
that had gone unnoticed  
for too long.

And within that longing,  
there was a soft stirring—

a memory  
of something more.

Not fully seen,  
but faintly felt.

Like light  
remembered  
from a distance.

Nothing held them there  
but their own turning.

And even now,  
there remained  
a quiet openness—

a space  
where something new  
could begin.

Not forced.  
Not demanded.

Only waiting.

#### Living the Vision

At timrs, you feel a kind of emptiness—

not dramatic,  
but persistent.

A sense that something  
is missing,  
or not quite aligned.

You may try  
to fill it,  
to move past it,  
to quiet it.

But something in you  
knows  
this feeling is not your enemy.

It is a signal.

A gentle awareness  
that something  
is asking for your attention.

You may notice  
how you turn—  
toward distraction,  
toward what is familiar,  
even if it does not satisfy.

And yet,  
there is always  
another direction—

a small turning  
back toward what is real.

You do not need  
to judge where you have been.

You only need  
to notice  
where you are now.

And in that noticing,  
there is already  
a doorway.

A quiet beginning  
that remains open.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Turn me toward life  
>
> **Exhale:** I return to what is real
