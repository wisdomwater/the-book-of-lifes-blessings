## 8 — The Stillness Beyond the Turning

> *What spins within you can grow quiet*  
> *A deeper breath is always near*

A great wheel turned in the open air.

Not fast—  
but without rest.

Round and round,  
its rhythm steady,  
its motion unbroken.

At its center,  
a form clung tightly—  
hands gripping,  
feet braced,  
caught in the movement  
it could not escape.

Each turn carried a surge—  
a rising heat,  
a tightening force,  
like a spark seeking fuel.

And with every rotation,  
the motion fed itself.

Again.  
Again.  
Again.

Nothing outside  
caused it to turn.

It turned  
because it had not yet  
grown still.

Then,  
softly,  
almost beyond notice—

a breeze moved through.

Not strong enough to stop the wheel.  
Not forceful enough to break it.

But present.

Gentle.  
Steady.

It touched the edges of the turning,  
not resisting—  
but surrounding.

And where the breeze lingered,  
something began to change.

The grip loosened.

The breath deepened.

The heat softened  
into something more spacious.

The wheel did not stop all at once.

But its hold  
began to fade.

And in the quiet space  
between one turn and the next—

stillness appeared.

Not as an end,  
but as a possibility  
that had always been there.

#### Living the Vision

Something inside you begins to turn.

A memory.  
A word.  
A wound.

You may feel the cycle start—  
the replaying,  
the tightening,  
the quiet pull  
toward reaction.

It can feel automatic.

As if once it begins,  
it must complete itself.

But there is a space  
you may begin to notice.

Small at first—  
almost hidden  
between one thought  
and the next.

A pause.

Not forced.  
Not manufactured.

Simply there.

In that pause,  
something softer becomes available.

A breath that is not caught in the turning.  
A presence that is not defined by the moment.

You do not have to stop the wheel  
by effort alone.

You can begin  
by stepping gently  
into that space.

Letting the breath come.  
Letting the body soften.

And over time,  
what once felt endless  
begins to lose its hold.

Not because the world has changed—

but because something in you  
has found a deeper stillness  
that the turning cannot reach.
