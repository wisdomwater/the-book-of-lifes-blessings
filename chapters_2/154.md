## 154 - Where Your Feet Rest

> *You do not need to gather the whole world*  
> *You are already held where you stand*

I saw a wide plain  
bathed in a quiet morning light.

The air was gentle,  
carrying the scent of open earth  
after rain.

A figure moved across the field—  
not hurried,  
but searching.

Its hands reached here and there,  
gathering small things,  
holding them close,  
then reaching again.

Yet beneath it,  
the ground was hidden—  
soft shadows  
covering where its feet should rest.

It walked,  
but did not feel  
where it stood.

Then,  
not far away,  
there was a clearing.

The light rested there  
without movement—  
steady,  
warm,  
enough.

And in that place,  
the earth was visible—  
firm,  
open,  
waiting.

As the figure slowed,  
just slightly,  
something changed.

The hands lowered.  
The reaching paused.

And for the first time,  
its feet touched the ground—  
felt it—  
solid,  
present,  
real.

Nothing new was gathered.

Nothing was added.

But something deeper  
was received.

A quiet sufficiency—  
as if the ground itself  
whispered:  
you are already carried.

And the need to reach  
softened into rest.

#### Living the Vision

Your thoughts turn  
toward what you might need.

What to secure.  
What to prepare.  
What to hold onto  
just in case.

It can feel wise—  
even necessary.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a subtle urgency beneath it—  
a quiet fear  
that what is here  
may not be enough.

Something in you  
keeps reaching.

There is another way  
that sometimes appears—  
not loud,  
not demanding.

You may feel it  
when you pause  
before reaching again.

When you let your attention  
drop  
from what you might gain  
to what is already here.

A breath.  
A moment.  
A simple presence  
that does not ask you  
to secure anything.

In that noticing,  
your footing becomes clearer.

You begin to feel  
where you stand.

And from there,  
desire loosens—  
not because you force it,  
but because you no longer need  
to grasp so tightly.

You are not asked  
to have less.

Only to discover  
what is already enough.
