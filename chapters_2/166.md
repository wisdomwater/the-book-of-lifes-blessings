## 166 - The Settled Waters

> *What no longer reaches begins to receive*  
> *The heart at rest discovers it is already held*

I saw a wide body of water  
stretching beyond sight—  
once stirred by many winds,  
once pulled in every direction.

Now,  
it had grown still.

The surface lay quiet,  
like a mirror that did not strain  
to reflect.

Light rested upon it  
without distortion—  
clear,  
undivided.

There was no grasping in it,  
no reaching beyond its shore.

It did not search for more streams  
to fill it,  
nor did it resist what came.

It simply held  
what was given.

And beneath the surface,  
life moved—  
unhurried,  
untroubled.

Nothing pushed.  
Nothing chased.

All was carried  
within a deeper rhythm.

And I understood:

What is always seeking  
cannot see what is already here.

But what becomes still  
begins to know  
a quiet fullness  
that was never absent.

#### Living the Vision

You notice a subtle reaching within you.

A movement toward something else—  
something more,  
something different.

It can feel natural,  
almost invisible.

A thought that says:  
this is not quite enough.

And so you look,  
you consider,  
you lean forward again.

But there may also be  
another way  
gently appearing.

You may notice it  
in a pause—  
when you stop,  
even briefly,  
from following the next desire.

In that stillness,  
something shifts.

Not everything changes.  
But the urgency softens.

You begin to sense  
what is already present—  
what has been quietly holding you  
all along.

This is not the end of desire.

It is its quiet re-centering.

A turning from endless reaching  
into simple receiving.

And in that receiving,  
you may discover  
a kind of sufficiency  
that does not need to prove itself.

It simply is.
