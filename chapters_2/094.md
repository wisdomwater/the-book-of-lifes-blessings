## 94 - The Returning Joy

> *Restlessness softens when it is seen*  
> *Joy waits beneath the searching*

I saw a quiet figure  
moving through a wide and shifting space.

It did not settle.

It moved from place to place—  
pausing briefly,  
then turning again.

Around it were many coverings—  
light,  
layered,  
easily changed.

They brushed against the figure  
like leaves in a passing wind—  
offering a moment of shelter,  
then falling away.

Nothing remained.

The air felt restless—  
not heavy,  
but unsettled—  
as though something deeper  
had not yet been touched.

Then,  
at the edge of the space,  
a stillness appeared.

It did not call out.

It simply remained—  
quiet,  
open—  
like a place that did not shift.

For a time,  
the figure continued moving.

Then slowly,  
it paused.

Not completely—  
just enough.

And in that small pause,  
something within  
began to quiet.

The movement softened.

The searching eased.

And beneath it all,  
a gentle presence  
was felt—  
not new,  
but long forgotten.

Like a well  
waiting  
to be returned to.

#### Living the Vision

You feel a quiet restlessness—

a sense of searching  
without quite knowing  
what you are looking for.

You may move  
from one thing to another—  
hoping something will stay,  
hoping something will satisfy.

And yet,  
the feeling remains.

Not because something is wrong—  
but because something deeper  
is calling.

You may begin to notice  
that the movement itself  
keeps you from hearing it.

But even a small pause  
can open a space.

You do not need  
to stop everything.

Only to slow—  
just enough  
to listen.

In that listening,  
you may sense  
a different kind of presence.

Steady.  
Quiet.  
Unchanging.

It does not come and go.

It waits.

And as you return  
to that stillness—  
even briefly—  
something begins to settle.

The searching softens.

The heart becomes lighter.

And gently,  
you may find  
that what you were looking for  
was not far away.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** I return to stillness  
>
> **Exhale:** Restore my joy
