## 16 — The Sound Before the Word

> *Before clarity, there is a quiet calling*  
> *Before the word, there is a sound*

I saw a soft radiance  
resting like a cloud—

not obscuring,  
but holding  
what could not yet be seen.

It moved gently,  
like breath across still water.

Within it,  
something stirred—

not fully formed,  
yet alive.

There were tones  
woven into the light—

not words,  
but something like their beginning.

A resonance.  
A calling.

It did not press for understanding.  
It did not explain.

It simply sounded—  
again and again—  
like a distant echo  
drawing nearer.

And those who lingered there  
did not strain to interpret.

They listened.

And in their listening,  
something within them  
began to open—

not with effort,  
but with recognition.

As if the sound  
was shaping a space  
where meaning could arrive.

And then—  
without urgency—  
the light deepened.

What had been heard  
became known.

Not as something added,  
but as something revealed  
from within the sound itself.

The cloud did not disappear.

It became luminous.

And within it,  
what was once only sensed  
stood present—

whole,  
alive,  
and quietly understood.

#### Living the Vision

You sense something stirring  
just beneath your awareness.

A feeling.  
A pull.  
A quiet knowing  
that has not yet taken shape.

You may want to name it,  
to understand it fully.

But something in you hesitates—  
not from fear,  
but from a deeper patience.

You may notice  
how life speaks to you  
in small ways—

through a conversation,  
a pause,  
a feeling that lingers  
longer than expected.

These are not incomplete.

They are invitations.

You are learning  
to listen beneath the surface—  
to receive  
before you define.

And in this listening,  
something begins to gather.

Clarity does not arrive  
as a sudden answer.

It unfolds  
as recognition.

You may find  
that what once felt distant  
has quietly become near.

That what you were hearing  
all along  
was already carrying  
the meaning you sought.

And you—  
without striving—  
have learned  
to hear it.
