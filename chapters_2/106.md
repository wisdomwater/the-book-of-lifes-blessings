## 106 - The Still Waters

> *What stirs in unrest can be quieted*  
> *Peace deepens where you choose to remain*

I saw a deep place  
where movement never ceased.

Currents turned upon themselves—  
rising,  
falling,  
circling—  
without rest.

The air above it trembled—  
warm and unsettled—  
like a wind  
that could not be still.

And beneath it,  
a hollow space opened—  
drawing things downward,  
then releasing them again—  
only to repeat  
the same motion.

Nothing remained steady there.

Everything returned  
to the same restless turning.

Then,  
at the edge of this movement,  
I saw a quiet pool.

Its surface was still—  
not rigid,  
but calm—  
holding its shape  
without effort.

It did not resist the currents.

It simply remained  
unchanged by them.

And where its stillness touched the edge,  
the circling began to slow.

Not stopped—  
but softened.

Until the movement  
lost its urgency.

And I saw  
that the stillness  
was not fragile.

It was deeper  
than the motion around it.

And it held.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you get pulled into reaction—

a comment,  
a disagreement,  
a rising need  
to respond quickly.

It can feel immediate—  
as though something must be said  
right away.

But you may also notice  
another presence within you—  
quieter,  
less urgent.

It does not push.

It invites you  
to pause.

You may begin  
with a single breath—  
allowing the first wave  
to pass without speaking.

In that space,  
something shifts.

The intensity softens.  
The need to engage lessens.

You are no longer  
caught in the movement.

You are choosing  
where to stand.

This is not avoidance.

It is clarity.

You can still speak  
what is true—  
but from steadiness  
instead of heat.

And as you return  
to this stillness,  
again and again,  
you may find  
that what once pulled you in  
no longer has the same hold.

You remain.

And in remaining,  
you carry peace  
into places  
that once unsettled you.
