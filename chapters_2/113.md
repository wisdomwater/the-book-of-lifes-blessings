## 113 - The Gifted Light

> *What is beautiful need not be possessed*  
> *It becomes fuller when it is received*

I saw a quiet garden  
at the edge of morning.

Green branches stretched gently upward,  
and flowers opened  
without effort—  
each one held  
in its own simple place.

A figure moved among them,  
pausing often—  
not to gather,  
not to take—  
but to notice.

Its hands were open.

It touched the leaves lightly,  
as though honoring  
what it had not made.

The flowers remained where they were.

Nothing was removed.  
Nothing was claimed.

And yet,  
the space felt full—  
alive with quiet beauty  
that needed no display.

Above,  
the light rested softly  
on everything—  
not favoring one bloom over another,  
but giving itself  
freely.

And I saw  
that the beauty of the garden  
did not belong  
to the one who walked within it.

It was received.

And in that receiving,  
the figure itself  
became gentle—  
at ease,  
unburdened—  
part of the life  
it beheld.

#### Living the Vision

You notice something good within you—

a skill,  
a thought,  
a way of seeing  
that feels clear or beautiful.

And something in you  
may begin to hold it tightly—  
to define yourself by it,  
to measure your worth through it.

But there is another way  
to meet what has been given.

You may begin  
by simply noticing—  
without claiming.

Let the gift be what it is.

You do not need  
to build yourself around it.

You can receive it  
with gratitude—  
as something that flows through you,  
not from you alone.

In this,  
something softens.

The pressure to be seen  
begins to ease.

You are no longer  
carrying what was meant  
to be shared.

And as you release  
the need to possess,  
your life becomes lighter—  
more open,  
more free.

You do not lose the gift.

You discover  
how to live within it.
