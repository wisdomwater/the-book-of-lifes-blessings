## 153 - The Softening Ground

> *What has hardened can become tender again*  
> *Mercy meets you where you are held*

I saw a low place in the earth—  
quiet,  
heavy,  
holding the memory of rain.

The ground was thick,  
softened into clay,  
marked by the weight  
of what had pressed into it.

And there,  
a figure stood—  
not trapped,  
but unmoving,  
as though unsure  
how to rise.

The air was still.

No wind pushed.  
No voice called.

Only a gentle presence  
resting near—  
like warmth  
waiting at the surface.

Then,  
from beneath the ground,  
a subtle stirring began.

Not force,  
but yielding.

The earth itself  
seemed to loosen—  
releasing its hold  
without resistance.

And the figure,  
feeling this shift,  
did not struggle.

It simply allowed.

One foot lifted,  
then the other—  
slowly,  
as if remembering  
how to move again.

And where the ground had been heavy,  
a small stream  
began to form—  
clear,  
cool,  
washing the surface clean.

The weight did not vanish.

But it no longer held.

And in that gentle rising,  
something softened—  
not only the ground,  
but the heart  
that had forgotten  
how to yield.

#### Living the Vision

There are times  
when you feel set  
in a certain way.

Your thoughts feel firm.  
Your responses come quickly.  
Your stance feels certain.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a quiet tension beneath it—  
a sense of holding tight,  
of not wanting to bend.

Something in you  
may feel tired of this.

Not broken—  
just weary  
of carrying the weight  
of always being fixed.

There is a softer movement  
available to you.

Not forced.  
Not sudden.

You may feel it  
in a moment of pause—  
when you choose  
not to defend,  
not to react,  
not to hold your ground  
so tightly.

Just for a breath.

In that small yielding,  
something begins to shift.

Not everything—  
just enough.

You may sense  
a gentleness  
meeting you there—  
not to correct,  
but to support.

You are not asked  
to pull yourself free.

Only to allow  
what holds you  
to loosen  
in the presence of mercy.

And to rise  
when you are ready.
