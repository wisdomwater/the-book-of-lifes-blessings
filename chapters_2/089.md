## 89 - The Remembering Hunger

> *What dulls the heart can be gently released*  
> *What is true within you still calls*

I saw a quiet place  
covered in soft shadow.

Nothing harsh—  
only a dimness  
that seemed to settle  
over everything.

At its center,  
there was a figure  
resting low to the ground.

Not in pain.  
Not in struggle.

But still.

Around it,  
there were many small offerings—  
close at hand,  
easily taken.

The figure reached for them  
without thought,  
again and again—  
not out of need,  
but habit.

And with each taking,  
the air grew heavier—  
not dark,  
but muted—  
as though something brighter  
had been gently forgotten.

Then,  
from beyond the shadow,  
a soft light appeared.

It did not push forward.

It waited.

Its presence was simple—  
clear,  
steady—  
like something familiar  
returning.

For a moment,  
nothing changed.

Then slowly,  
the figure paused.

Its hand stilled.

And in that small pause,  
something opened—  
a space  
where the light  
could be seen again.

Not distant.

Not lost.

Only waiting  
to be remembered.

#### Living the Vision

You move through life  
almost without noticing—

reaching for what is easy,  
what comforts,  
what fills the moment  
without truly satisfying.

It may not feel harmful.

It may even feel normal.

And yet,  
you may begin to notice  
a subtle dullness—  
a quiet distance  
from something deeper.

There is a different kind of hunger  
within you.

Quieter.  
More patient.

It does not demand attention.

But it waits.

You may sense it  
in small pauses—  
when you step back,  
when you choose not to reach  
right away,  
when you allow space  
instead of filling it.

In that space,  
something begins to return.

Clarity.  
Lightness.  
A sense of being  
more fully present.

You are not asked  
to remove everything.

Only to notice.

To choose,  
gently,  
what brings you closer  
to what is alive.

And slowly,  
your hunger remembers  
what truly satisfies.
