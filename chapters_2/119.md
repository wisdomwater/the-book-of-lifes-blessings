## 119 - The Breath That Restores

> *Nothing is separate from the Whole*  
> *Even the unseen is gently held*

I saw the world  
breathing.

Not as something distant,  
but as a living presence—  
rising and falling  
in a quiet rhythm.

The wind moved outward,  
then returned again.

The air softened what it touched,  
then settled back into stillness.

Moisture gathered,  
nourishing the earth,  
then lifted again—  
as though nothing was ever lost,  
only carried.

And I sensed  
that all of it  
was held  
within a deeper life—  
a hidden Source  
from which everything flowed  
and to which everything returned.

But there were places  
where the rhythm felt strained—  
not broken,  
but burdened.

Like breath  
held too long.

Like a current  
meeting resistance.

And still,  
beneath it all,  
there remained  
a steady movement—  
patient,  
restoring,  
drawing everything  
back toward harmony.

Not by force,  
but by a quiet persistence  
that did not cease.

And I saw  
that nothing was beyond this movement—  
that even what had grown tense  
was being gently invited  
to release  
and return.

#### Living the Vision

Something in you feels out of rhythm.

A tightness.  
A holding.  
A sense that life is not flowing  
as it once did.

You may try to correct it quickly—  
to fix,  
to adjust,  
to bring yourself back into balance.

But often,  
the deeper invitation  
is not urgency,  
but awareness.

To notice  
where you are holding your breath—  
within your thoughts,  
your body,  
your heart.

To gently allow  
what has been clenched  
to soften.

You are not separate  
from the life that sustains you.

Even now,  
something within you  
is moving toward restoration—  
quietly,  
steadily,  
without strain.

You can join that movement  
by yielding  
just a little.

A breath released.  
A truth acknowledged.  
A moment of openness  
where resistance loosens.

And in that small returning,  
you may begin to feel again  
the rhythm  
that has never truly left you.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Restore my rhythm  
>
> **Exhale:** I return to You
