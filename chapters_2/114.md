## 114 - The Rooted Way

> *What moves in restlessness can be gathered*  
> *Life deepens when it aligns with what is true*

I saw a wide expanse  
where movement rushed in every direction.

Shapes passed quickly—  
turning,  
shifting,  
never settling—  
as though carried by unseen winds.

Nothing remained in one place.

The air felt stirred—  
not alive,  
but unsettled—  
as if something  
kept reaching outward  
without finding ground.

Then,  
at the center of this movement,  
I saw a single tree.

Its roots went deep—  
hidden,  
steady—  
holding firmly beneath the surface.

Its branches did not rush.

They opened slowly,  
receiving light  
as it came.

The wind moved around it,  
but did not carry it away.

And where the tree stood,  
the ground was different—  
stable,  
nourished,  
at rest.

The movement around it  
began to soften—  
not stopped,  
but gathered.

And I saw  
that the strength of the tree  
was not in reaching everywhere,  
but in remaining  
where life was given.

#### Living the Vision

You feel pulled  
by many directions—

thoughts,  
impulses,  
decisions  
that all seem urgent.

You may feel  
that you must rely only on yourself—  
to figure everything out,  
to choose quickly,  
to stay in control.

But you may also notice  
a quieter invitation.

A place where you do not rush.

Where you pause  
before acting—  
not out of hesitation,  
but to listen.

You may begin  
by asking gently:

What is true here?  
What leads to life?

You do not need  
to abandon your understanding.

Only to allow it  
to be shaped  
by something deeper.

In this,  
you begin to feel  
more grounded.

Less driven.  
More steady.

Your choices  
no longer scatter you.

They root you.

And as you remain  
in this way—  
open,  
listening,  
aligned—  
your life begins  
to carry a quiet strength.

Not from forcing your own way,  
but from walking  
within what is already true.
