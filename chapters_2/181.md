## 181 - The Unburdened Heart

> *What is released becomes light*  
> *What trusts is free to receive*

I saw a traveler  
walking along a quiet path.

At first,  
his arms were full—  
holding many things,  
gathered tightly against himself.

His steps were heavy.  
Each movement  
required effort.

And though the path was open,  
he could not see far ahead.

Then,  
he paused.

Not from force,  
but from a quiet knowing  
that had begun to rise within him.

One by one,  
he loosened his grip.

What he carried  
was set gently upon the ground.

Nothing was taken from him.  
Nothing was lost.

But as his hands opened,  
his body grew lighter.

His breath deepened.

The path seemed to widen.

Light touched his face  
in a way it had not before.

And with empty hands,  
he began to walk again.

Not hurried.  
Not afraid.

Free to receive  
what the journey would bring.

And I understood:

There is a freedom  
that cannot be found  
while holding everything.

It begins  
when the hands learn  
to open.

#### Living the Vision

You feel the need to hold on.

To secure what you have,  
to gather a little more,  
to make sure nothing is missing.

It can feel responsible—  
like care,  
like wisdom.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
how it weighs on you.

A quiet heaviness,  
a sense that your hands  
are never fully at rest.

But there may be  
a softer movement  
available to you.

You may notice it  
in a small release—  
a willingness  
to let something go,  
even just inwardly.

Not everything.  
Not all at once.

Just enough  
to feel the space again.

In that space,  
something shifts.

Your breath opens.  
Your vision clears.

You begin to sense  
that what you need  
is not created  
by holding tighter.

It is received  
in trust.

And as you allow  
this gentle unburdening,  
you may discover  
a quiet sufficiency—  
one that does not depend  
on having everything,  
but on being free  
to receive what is given.
