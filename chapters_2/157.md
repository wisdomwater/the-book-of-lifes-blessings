## 157 - The Turning Toward Home

> *Even your wandering holds a memory of home*  
> *Each turning can become a return*

I saw a wide path  
curving gently through the land—  
not straight,  
but winding,  
as if shaped by many footsteps.

Along the path,  
a wheel turned.

Slowly,  
steadily,  
touching the ground  
at one point,  
then another.

It did not rush.  
It did not scatter.

It simply moved—  
round and round,  
carrying the marks  
of where it had been.

At times,  
it leaned toward the edge—  
drawn by softer ground,  
by familiar impressions  
left behind.

But always,  
it returned  
to the path beneath it.

And in the turning,  
something became clear:

The movement was not aimless.

Each rotation  
brought it forward—  
not away,  
but deeper  
into the way.

Above,  
a quiet light followed—  
not pushing,  
not correcting—  
only present,  
as if remembering  
what the wheel  
was made for.

And as the wheel continued,  
its motion softened—  
less pulled  
by what lay to the side,  
more shaped  
by the path itself.

Not perfect.  
Not finished.

But no longer lost  
in its turning.

#### Living the Vision

At times, you feel  
pulled in different directions.

A desire rises,  
then fades.  
A resolve forms,  
then loosens.

You may notice  
how easily you turn—  
toward what you know,  
away from what you long for,  
and back again.

It can feel like failure.

But something in you  
is also learning.

You may begin to sense  
a quiet awareness  
within the turning—  
a recognition  
of what leads to life  
and what leaves you empty.

Even if you cannot  
hold to it perfectly,  
you see it.

And seeing  
is already a kind of return.

You do not need  
to stop the turning  
to begin again.

You may simply  
choose—  
in this moment—  
to lean  
slightly  
toward what is true.

A small turning.

A quiet yes.

And then,  
when you turn again,  
you can return again.

You are not asked  
to be finished.

Only to keep  
coming home.
