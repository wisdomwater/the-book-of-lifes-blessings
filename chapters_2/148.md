## 148 - The Ground That Holds You

> *You do not need to force your way forward*  
> *You can stand where truth is steady*

I saw a figure  
moving quickly—  
leaping from place to place,  
never settling,  
as though speed itself  
could carry it into safety.

Its path was uneven.  
Its footing uncertain.

At times it pushed forward  
with great force—  
resisting anything  
that stood in its way.

And yet,  
the ground beneath it  
was soft—  
giving way  
with each step.

It moved,  
but did not stand.

Then,  
beyond the restless motion,  
I saw something simple.

A place of firm earth—  
clear,  
steady,  
unmoved.

Nothing rushed there.  
Nothing forced its way.

It simply remained—  
holding what stood upon it  
without strain.

The figure slowed.

For a moment,  
it felt the difference.

Where there had been slipping,  
there was now support.

Where there had been effort,  
there was rest.

And I saw  
that this ground  
had always been present—  
not far away,  
not hidden—  
only waiting  
to be chosen.

#### Living the Vision

You feel the urge  
to push forward—  
to make things happen,  
to shape outcomes  
according to your own will.

It can feel necessary—  
even justified.

But you may also notice  
how this effort  
can leave you unsteady—  
as though something beneath you  
is not quite holding.

There is another way  
that begins with a pause.

A quiet question:  
What is true here?

Not what is most convenient.  
Not what feels strongest in the moment.  
But what is real,  
what is fair,  
what aligns with something deeper.

You may not see it all at once.

But you may sense  
a small place  
where your footing becomes clearer.

When you choose that place—  
even in a small way—  
something steadies.

You do not need  
to force your way forward.

You can stand.

And from that place,  
your next step  
will carry a different weight—  
less driven,  
more grounded,  
more whole.

> **Inhale:** Ground me in what is true
