## 95 - The Restored Balance

> *What reaches without rest can be gathered again*  
> *The soul finds peace in gentle measure*

I saw a low movement  
across an open field—

restless,  
searching,  
never still.

It moved from place to place,  
drawn by whatever lay near—  
pausing only briefly  
before turning again.

The ground beneath it  
showed signs of taking—  
disturbed,  
uneven—  
as though much had been gathered,  
yet nothing held.

The air carried a quiet tension,  
a sense of reaching  
without arrival.

Then,  
from a distance,  
a calm presence appeared.

It did not pursue.

It stood—  
steady,  
open—  
like a place  
that needed nothing.

The movement continued for a time.

Then slowly,  
it began to slow.

Not stopped—  
just softened.

Its turning grew less urgent.

Its searching less wide.

Until, at last,  
it came to rest  
within the stillness.

There,  
nothing was taken.

Nothing was chased.

Only a quiet sufficiency—  
like the earth itself  
holding what was needed.

And I saw  
that what had been restless  
did not lose anything.

It found  
what had been missing all along—  
a place to remain.

#### Living the Vision

There is a constant pull to keep moving—

to reach,  
to gather,  
to respond quickly  
to whatever arises.

It can feel like freedom—  
to follow every desire  
without pause.

And yet,  
you may notice  
a quiet fatigue beneath it—  
a sense that nothing quite settles.

You may begin to sense  
another way of being—  
one that does not rush  
to fulfill every impulse.

A pause.  
A breath.  
A moment of awareness  
before choosing.

This is not loss.

It is space.

And within that space,  
something begins to steady.

You are no longer driven.

You are present.

You begin to choose  
what truly nourishes—  
what brings clarity,  
what sustains.

Balance does not come  
all at once.

It grows  
through small, steady returns  
to what is enough.

And gently,  
you may find  
that the need to take  
begins to loosen—  
replaced by a quiet trust  
that you already have  
what you need.
