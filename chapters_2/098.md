## 98 - The Deepening Roots

> *What is given to grow can be tended again*  
> *Life returns where it is gently rooted*

I saw a wide garden  
whose soil had grown thin.

The surface was disturbed—  
touched too often,  
taken from too quickly—  
as though what had been planted  
had not been allowed to rest.

Some places were bare.  
Others held small shoots—  
fragile,  
waiting.

Then,  
beneath the surface,  
something began to shift.

A warmth moved slowly—  
not burning above,  
but working below—  
loosening what had hardened,  
opening space for roots.

The soil softened.

What had been scattered  
began to settle.

And where the ground  
was gently held,  
roots began to reach—  
downward,  
quietly—  
finding depth again.

Nothing was forced.

Nothing rushed upward.

Growth returned  
from below—  
hidden,  
steady.

And I saw  
that what had once been strained  
could be restored—  
not by taking more,  
but by allowing  
what is planted  
to deepen.

The garden breathed again—  
not in excess,  
but in quiet sufficiency.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes life feels depleted—

as though what was once alive  
has been stretched too thin.

You may notice it  
in how you give,  
how you receive,  
how you move through your days.

It can feel like  
there is not enough left.

But there is a gentle return  
available to you.

You may begin  
not by doing more,  
but by tending differently.

Giving space  
where there has been pressure.

Allowing rest  
where there has been constant taking.

You may sense  
what needs to be protected—  
what needs to be nourished  
instead of used.

This is how roots deepen again.

Not quickly.

But truly.

You are not asked  
to produce immediately.

Only to become grounded—  
to let what is within you  
find stability  
in what is real.

And as you do,  
life begins to return—  
not in excess,  
but in a quiet strength  
that endures.
