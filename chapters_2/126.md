## 126 - The Clear and Gentle Mirror

> *You are not asked to be perfect*  
> *Only willing to come near*

I saw a still pool of water  
resting in quiet light.

Its surface was clear—  
not because nothing had touched it,  
but because it had become calm again.

Above it,  
the sky opened softly.  
Light fell without force,  
and the water received it.

Nothing strained to reflect.  
Nothing tried to become more.

It simply held  
what was given.

And in that stillness,  
something appeared—  
not the water itself,  
but what it carried.

A reflection,  
gentle and true.

Then I saw another place—  
the same water,  
but stirred.

Ripples crossing ripples,  
never settling.

The light was still there,  
but it could not be seen clearly.

Not because it had gone,  
but because the surface  
could not rest long enough  
to receive it.

And I understood  
that the clarity was not forced.

It came  
when the water  
was allowed  
to be still.

#### Living the Vision

You feel divided within.

Part of you reaches toward what is true,  
and another part  
holds back—  
uncertain,  
guarded,  
unsettled.

You may think  
you must resolve everything first—  
become clearer,  
more certain,  
more steady.

But something in you  
already knows how to begin.

Not by striving,  
but by becoming simple again.

You may notice  
a small opening—  
a willingness to be honest,  
to let yourself be seen  
without arranging your thoughts  
into perfection.

That is enough.

You do not need  
to force clarity.

Only to allow  
what is restless  
to settle,  
even briefly.

And in that soft settling,  
you may begin to sense  
that something is already reaching you—  
a quiet presence  
that does not demand certainty,  
only openness.

You are not far.

You are already near.
