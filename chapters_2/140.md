## 140 - The Ladder of Quiet Choice

> *Not every path leads to life*  
> *But you can choose the one that does*

I saw the turning wheel again—  
moving without rest,  
carrying the figure  
through the same motion  
again and again.

But now,  
something else appeared.

Before the wheel,  
two ways opened.

One descended—  
not sharply,  
but gradually,  
like a slope  
that invited easy steps.

It led toward a still, heavy place—  
a quiet surface  
that gave nothing back.

The other rose—  
not in steep demand,  
but in gentle ascent.

A ladder—  
simple,  
steady,  
each step placed  
within reach.

It did not call out.  
It did not force itself.

It simply stood—  
waiting.

The figure paused.

For a moment,  
the turning slowed.

Its hands loosened  
from what they grasped.

And in that space,  
it saw—  
not everything,  
but enough.

The next step.

The ladder did not ask  
for the whole journey.

Only one movement upward—  
a small choosing  
toward what is true.

And I saw  
that with each step,  
the turning lost its hold—  
not by struggle,  
but by direction.

#### Living the Vision

You feel pulled  
by what is immediate.

A thought.  
An impulse.  
A desire that asks  
to be followed now.

It can feel like freedom—  
to act without pause,  
to move with whatever arises.

But you may also notice  
how it leaves you unsettled—  
as though something in you  
is being carried  
rather than choosing.

There is a quiet space  
available to you  
before each step.

It may be brief—  
just enough  
to ask:

Where does this lead?

You do not need  
to see the whole path.

Only to notice  
the direction.

You may sense  
a small invitation—  
toward what is clearer,  
truer,  
more whole.

It may not feel easier.

But it will feel steady.

And when you follow it—  
even once—  
something begins to change.

The movement within you  
becomes less reactive,  
more intentional.

You are no longer  
just turning.

You are walking.
