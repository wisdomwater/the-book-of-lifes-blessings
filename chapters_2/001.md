## 1 - The Light That Speaks

> *The voice of God does not come to the strong, but to the ones who are willing to listen.*

I became aware of a quiet, living light.

It did not arrive suddenly, nor with force. It had always been there—like a presence that had been gently waiting, patient and steady.

It did not overwhelm.

It invited.

I sensed that this light did not depend on my strength. It did not require certainty or readiness. It simply sought a place to rest—a listening heart, open and unguarded.

There was a softness in it, like nourishment given in its proper time. First gentle, like milk to a child. Then deeper, asking for something more—not effort, but trust.

The light did not speak loudly.

But it was unmistakable.

“Do not speak from yourself,” it seemed to say. “Let what is true move through you.”

For a moment, I wanted to turn away. To remain hidden. To stay in what was familiar and contained.

But the light did not press.

It remained.

And in its presence, something in me grew still enough to hear.

So I did not reach for words.

I listened.

And in that listening, something began.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when something quiet rises within you.

Not a demand. Not a command.

A nudge.

A sense that something wants to be expressed, or spoken, or lived.

Often, the first response is hesitation.

You may feel unprepared. Unsure. Not quite ready to step into what is being asked.

But this vision suggests something different.

The invitation does not come because you are ready.

It comes because you are listening.

You are not being asked to create something from yourself.

You are being asked to make space.

To notice what is already present.

To trust that what is true does not need to be forced—it only needs to be received and allowed to move.

So begin simply.

Pause long enough to hear.

Let the pressure to perform fall away.

And take one small step in response to what feels quietly alive.

You do not need to have the whole path.

You only need to listen.
