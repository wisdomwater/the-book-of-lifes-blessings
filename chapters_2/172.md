## 172 - The Open Receiving

> *What is released can be filled*  
> *What trusts begins to rest*

I saw a pair of hands  
held gently open.

They did not reach outward.  
They did not close around anything.

They simply remained—  
soft,  
available,  
at ease.

Light rested within them  
like a quiet gift.

Not grasped,  
not secured,  
only received.

And as the hands stayed open,  
something subtle began to change.

What they held  
did not diminish.

It deepened.

The light seemed to grow  
without effort,  
as if fullness came  
not from holding tighter,  
but from not holding at all.

Around them,  
a tree stood—  
rooted in clear water,  
its branches steady,  
its fruit whole and unstrained.

Nothing was taken from it.  
Nothing was forced.

Yet what was needed  
was always present.

A gentle rhythm moved  
between the hands and the tree—  
receiving,  
releasing,  
without fear.

And I understood:

There is a way of living  
that does not depend on grasping.

It trusts  
what is given,  
and rests  
in that trust.

#### Living the Vision

Something in you  
wants to hold on.

To secure what you have,  
to gather a little more,  
to make sure nothing is lost.

It can feel wise—  
like care,  
like preparation.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a quiet tension within it.

A sense of tightening,  
as if everything depends  
on your holding.

But there may be  
another movement  
gently available.

You may notice it  
in a small release—  
a softening of your grip,  
even for a moment.

In that space,  
something shifts.

Not everything disappears.  
Not everything becomes certain.

But the fear eases.

You begin to sense  
that what you need  
is not created  
by your grasping.

It is received.

This does not ask you  
to abandon care.

Only to notice  
where trust  
can begin to open  
what fear has closed.

And in that opening,  
something quiet grows—  
a sufficiency  
that does not depend  
on holding everything together.
