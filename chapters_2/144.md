## 144 - The Quiet That Holds All

> *Truth gathers what fear scatters*  
> *What is real remains undivided*

I saw a figure  
turned out of order—  
its movement restless,  
its voice unsettled,  
as though it could not rest  
in one place.

It spoke in fragments—  
this against that,  
one against another—  
as though everything  
stood in opposition.

Its words moved quickly,  
spinning outward,  
touching many things  
without holding any of them  
in peace.

And yet,  
beneath the motion,  
I sensed a lack—  
not of activity,  
but of center.

Then,  
beyond the unrest,  
a stillness appeared.

Not opposing,  
not arguing—  
simply present.

It held everything  
without dividing it.

Light rested there.  
Space opened.

Nothing needed to prove itself.  
Nothing needed to push  
against anything else.

And slowly,  
the spinning lost its force—  
not because it was silenced,  
but because it was no longer believed.

The stillness remained.

Whole.  
Unmoved.  
Sufficient.

And I saw  
that what is true  
does not need to divide  
in order to stand.

It simply is.

#### Living the Vision

Your thoughts begin to scatter.

One thing against another.  
One person against another.  
A sense that everything  
is pulling apart.

It can feel convincing—  
as though you must sort it out,  
take sides,  
find where you stand.

But you may notice  
how it leaves you unsettled—  
moving without rest,  
thinking without clarity.

There is another place  
available to you.

A quiet return  
to what is whole.

You do not need  
to resolve every tension.

Only to step out  
of the inner spinning.

You may do this simply—  
by pausing,  
by breathing,  
by letting your thoughts  
settle without chasing them.

In that stillness,  
something becomes clear.

Not everything—  
but enough.

You are not held together  
by dividing the world.

You are held  
within something  
that does not break.
