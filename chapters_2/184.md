## 184 - The Gentle Word

> *What is spoken with care gives life*  
> *What honors truth allows it to grow*

I saw a quiet garden  
resting in soft afternoon light.

The air was clear,  
open,  
untouched by harshness.

In the center,  
a small stream moved slowly—  
steady,  
refreshing,  
giving life to everything it reached.

Around it,  
plants rose in quiet strength.

Their leaves were full,  
their fruit forming without strain.

Nothing pushed.  
Nothing withered.

And near the garden,  
there was a voice.

It did not rise loudly.  
It did not press itself forward.

It moved like the stream—  
measured,  
gentle,  
clear.

Where it passed,  
the air remained light.

Nothing was disturbed.  
Nothing was diminished.

The garden received it  
as it received the water—  
without resistance,  
without fear.

And I understood:

There is a way of speaking  
that nourishes what is living—  
that leaves behind  
a quiet growth.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes words come quickly.

A thought forms,  
and with it,  
a sharp edge—  
something that could wound,  
or diminish,  
or rise above another.

It can feel easy.  
Even satisfying.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
what follows.

A heaviness in the air.  
A quiet closing within.

But there is another way  
already present.

You may notice it  
in the space before you speak—  
a moment where nothing  
has yet been said.

In that space,  
something in you can choose.

Not from pressure,  
but from awareness.

You may begin to sense  
what your words will carry—  
whether they will dry  
or nourish,  
whether they will close  
or open.

You are not asked  
to become silent in fear.

Only to speak  
from a place that is clear.

And as you do,  
even gently,  
you may feel it—  
a softening within,  
a quiet peace  
that remains.

Your words become  
like water—  
simple,  
life-giving,  
and free.
