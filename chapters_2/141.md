## 141 - The Wings of Trust

> *You are not sustained by striving alone*  
> *Life is given as well as gathered*

I saw a figure  
standing in the dimness—  
open,  
exposed,  
holding itself  
with quiet tension.

Its gaze moved downward—  
toward the ground,  
toward what could be gathered,  
secured,  
held close.

Its hands were busy.  
Its thoughts were full.

Everything in it  
leaned toward survival.

Then,  
above it,  
something stirred.

Not a demand—  
but a widening.

A presence  
like open sky.

From within it  
came a gentle lifting—  
as though something in the soul  
remembered  
how to rise.

Not away from the earth,  
but through it.

Wings formed—  
not seen with the eyes,  
but felt  
in the release of strain.

The figure grew still.

Its hands loosened.  
Its breath softened.

It looked again—  
and the same ground  
no longer felt  
like something to grasp,  
but something to receive.

And I saw  
that nothing had been taken away.

Only the weight  
of holding everything alone  
had lifted.

#### Living the Vision

Your thoughts gather  
around what you need.

Food.  
Provision.  
Security.  
The future.

It can feel like  
everything depends on you—  
on what you plan,  
what you do,  
what you hold together.

You may notice  
how heavy this becomes.

How your attention narrows.  
How your body tightens.  
How rest feels distant.

There is another movement  
available to you.

Not the absence of action—  
but a softening within it.

You can still plan.  
You can still work.

But you do not need  
to carry it all alone.

You may try  
a small release—  
a breath  
that lets go of just a little  
of the weight.

A quiet remembering:  
I am being sustained.

Even now,  
beneath your effort,  
something is holding you.

When you allow this  
to be true,  
something in you  
begins to lift.

Not away from life—  
but into a wider trust  
within it.
