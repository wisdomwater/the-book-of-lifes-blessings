## 179 - The Place of Rest

> *What remains begins to deepen*  
> *What is held in love finds its home*

I saw a gentle hillside  
resting in the warmth of midday.

The light was steady there—  
not shifting,  
not fading—  
as if it had found its place.

At the center  
was a quiet shelter.

Nothing grand,  
nothing calling attention to itself.

Only a stillness  
that welcomed without effort.

Nearby,  
a small flock gathered.

They did not wander far.  
They stayed close,  
moving slowly,  
feeding where the grass was soft and full.

There was no urgency in them.  
No searching beyond what was given.

They rested,  
then rose,  
then rested again—  
held within a simple rhythm.

And within the shelter,  
there was a presence—  
unseen,  
yet unmistakably near.

A place where nothing needed to prove itself.  
A place where being was enough.

The wind moved lightly across the hillside,  
but nothing was carried away.

All remained  
within a quiet belonging.

And I understood:

There is a place  
where the soul is fed—  
not by wandering,  
but by remaining  
where love is present.

#### Living the Vision

You feel the pull to move on.

To look elsewhere,  
to try something new,  
to leave behind what feels familiar.

It can seem like growth—  
like the next step  
is always somewhere else.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a quiet invitation  
to stay.

Not forever,  
not without movement—  
but long enough  
for something to take root.

You may sense it  
in the places you return to—  
a prayer,  
a practice,  
a way of being  
that feels simple  
and real.

It may not feel dramatic.  
It may not feel new.

But something in you  
recognizes it  
as nourishing.

You are not asked  
to search everywhere.

Only to notice  
where love is already feeding you.

And to remain there,  
even gently,  
until what is planted  
begins to grow.
