## 71 - The Gentle Rain

> *What opens, receives*  
> *What receives, is renewed*

I saw a dry hillside  
resting beneath a quiet sky.

The earth was firm,  
unbroken,  
as if it had long learned  
to hold itself closed.

Nothing grew there.

Only stillness—  
tight,  
unyielding.

Then, without sound,  
a light rain began to fall.

It did not rush.

It did not press.

Each drop landed softly,  
resting where it was given.

At first,  
the ground did not change.

It held its shape,  
resisting the touch.

But the rain remained.

Steady.  
Patient.

And slowly—  
almost imperceptibly—  
the surface began to soften.

Not all at once.

Just enough  
to receive a little more.

The water gathered,  
sinking deeper,  
touching places  
that had been long untouched.

And beneath the surface,  
something stirred.

A quiet loosening.  
A gentle opening.

The hillside did not lose itself.

It became alive again.

And the rain continued—  
not to overwhelm,  
but to restore.

#### Living the Vision

Something in you  
feels closed—

a tightening  
when you are challenged,  
a quiet resistance  
to what unsettles you.

It can feel protective.

Like holding your ground.

But you may also notice  
how it limits what can reach you.

There is another way  
that does not require force.

You may begin  
by simply allowing  
a small opening.

Not agreement.  
Not surrender.

Just a willingness  
to stay present  
a little longer.

To listen  
without turning away.

You may find  
that what once felt sharp  
begins to soften.

That something  
you could not receive before  
now rests more gently within you.

This is how change begins.

Not by breaking the surface,  
but by letting it soften  
over time.

And in that softening,  
grace finds its way—  
quietly,  
patiently—  
restoring what has been held too tightly.
