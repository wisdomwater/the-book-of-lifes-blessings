## 65 - The Quieting of the Flame

> *What is seen in the light loses its hold*  
> *The heart becomes whole when it rests again*

I saw a small flame  
flickering in a hidden place—

not wild,  
not consuming—  
but restless,  
searching for air.

It moved along the edges,  
touching what it could,  
testing what might catch.

Yet the space around it  
was filled with a soft, steady light.

Not forceful.  
Not urgent.

Simply present.

As the light remained,  
the flame began to change—

its movement slowed,  
its reaching softened,  
as though it no longer needed  
to grasp for anything.

The air grew still.

And in that stillness,  
the flame drew inward—  
no longer scattered,  
no longer hidden.

It became a quiet warmth.

Not something that burned,  
but something that could be held.

Around it,  
what had once felt fragile  
remained untouched—

gentle,  
unharmed,  
at peace.

And I saw  
that nothing had been forced away.

Nothing crushed or denied.

Only brought  
into a presence  
where it could no longer consume.

The light did not resist the flame.

It clarified it.

And what had once been restless  
found its place  
within a deeper calm.

#### Living the Vision

It happens fast.  
Something rises quickly in you—

a heat,  
a tightening,  
a surge that wants to move outward.

It can feel immediate,  
almost necessary.

But you may notice  
how it shifts  
when you pause.

When you do not follow it  
right away.

You might feel it more clearly—  
where it sits,  
how it moves,  
what it is asking for.

Not to be unleashed,  
but to be seen.

There is a quiet strength  
in staying present here.

In letting the feeling exist  
without becoming it.

You do not need  
to push it down.

You do not need  
to act it out.

You can simply remain—  
breathing,  
aware,  
held within something deeper  
than the moment itself.

And in that space,  
what once felt consuming  
begins to soften.

It becomes part of you—  
not something that rules you.

A warmth  
that can be guided,  
rather than a fire  
that must be feared.
