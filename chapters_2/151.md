## 151 - The Quiet Center

> *You are not meant to chase every wind*  
> *There is a place in you that remains*

I saw a wide, open field  
touched by a steady light.

A gentle wind moved across it—  
not forceful,  
but constant,  
shifting the grasses in soft waves.

At the center  
stood a single tree.

Its roots reached deep,  
hidden and sure.  
Its branches swayed,  
but did not break.

Around it,  
the air carried many currents—  
some warm,  
some restless,  
some calling the attention outward.

They passed through  
without taking hold.

The tree did not follow them.

It remained.

Not rigid,  
not unmoving—  
but quietly anchored  
in something deeper than change.

And beneath the surface,  
where no wind could reach,  
there was a stillness—  
cool,  
untroubled,  
alive.

The life of the tree  
flowed from there.

Not from the shifting air,  
but from the hidden depth  
that held it.

And in that place,  
there was no striving  
to become something else.

Only a steady growing  
into what it already was.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes everything in you  
wants to move on.

To adjust.  
To rethink.  
To begin again somewhere new.

Sometimes this feels like wisdom.

But you may also notice  
a quiet unease beneath it—  
a sense of never quite settling,  
never quite arriving  
where your feet already stand.

Something in you  
knows the difference.

There is a gentler way.

You may feel it  
when you return  
to something simple—  
a practice,  
a truth,  
a small act of presence—  
and remain there  
just a little longer  
than you want to.

Not forcing.  
Not proving.

Just staying.

In that staying,  
something begins to deepen.

Not dramatic.  
Not immediate.

But real.

You are not asked  
to hold everything in place.

Only to notice  
where life is already rooted  
within you—

and to remain  
long enough  
to feel it grow.
