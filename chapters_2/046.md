## 46 — Rooted Beyond the Bloom

> *What is beautiful can be received*  
> *but it cannot hold your life*

I saw a garden  
held in a quiet light—

soft,  
unrushed,  
open.

At its center  
stood a great tree,  
wide in its reaching,  
full in its flowering.

Its blossoms were many—  
delicate,  
fragrant,  
alive with color.

They opened easily,  
as if joy itself  
had taken form.

And near the branches,  
I sensed a presence—

reaching,  
gathering,  
drawing the blossoms close.

Not in harm,  
but in longing.

As though something within  
believed this beauty  
could be kept,  
could be held,  
could become  
a steady place to rest.

But beneath the surface,  
deeper than the eye could see,  
the roots stretched downward—

some touching living water,  
others searching  
through dry and silent ground.

And I felt the difference—

one drawing life  
from what does not fade,  
the other leaning  
on what was already passing.

The blossoms did not resist.  
They opened,  
they gave,  
they released.

And the garden remained—

quietly inviting  
a deeper rooting,  
a gentler way of being held.

#### Living the Vision

At times, something beautiful  
finds its way into your life—

a relationship,  
a sense of purpose,  
a moment of recognition  
that feels full and bright.

And something in you  
naturally leans toward it.

You may notice  
a subtle tightening—  
a desire to keep it close,  
to make it steady,  
to let it define  
what is secure.

It is a tender instinct.

But beneath that movement,  
there is another invitation—

to feel where your life  
is truly being drawn from.

Not from what opens  
and fades in its season,  
but from something quieter,  
deeper,  
already holding you.

You can still receive  
what is given.

You can still delight  
in what blooms.

But you do not have to  
anchor yourself there.

Something in you  
knows how to root  
in what does not pass.

And from that place,  
everything else  
can be gently held.
