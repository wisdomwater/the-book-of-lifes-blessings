## 122 - The Open Hand of Joy

> *What is given to one is not taken from another*  
> *Love makes room for every good thing to shine*

I saw a figure  
holding its hands close—  
tight,  
guarded,  
as though something precious  
might be taken away.

Its gaze moved outward,  
measuring,  
comparing,  
lingering on what others held.

And the more it looked,  
the more its own space  
seemed to narrow—  
as though the world itself  
was becoming smaller.

Then,  
very gently,  
a warmth entered the scene.

Not bright or overwhelming—  
but steady,  
like sunlight  
spreading across a field.

Where it touched,  
things began to open.

Flowers turned outward.  
Branches lifted.  
The air felt wider.

And I saw another figure—  
standing with open hands.

It did not grasp.  
It did not compare.

It simply received  
what was given  
and rejoiced  
in what was growing  
all around it.

There was no loss here.  
No scarcity.

Only a quiet abundance  
that did not need to be defended.

And in this openness,  
its own life  
seemed to deepen—  
not by taking,  
but by sharing  
in the goodness that moved  
through everything.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
another person’s goodness  
becomes hard to hold.

You may notice  
a tightening—  
a subtle comparison,  
a question that rises  
without invitation.

Why not me?

You do not need  
to push this away.

Only to see it clearly.

Something in you  
is afraid there is not enough—  
not enough goodness,  
not enough recognition,  
not enough life to share.

But there is another movement  
available to you.

A small opening.

You may try it quietly—  
a simple blessing,  
spoken even if your heart  
is not fully there yet.

A willingness to let  
another’s light remain  
without dimming it.

And in that moment,  
something shifts.

The tightness loosens.  
The space widens.

You begin to feel  
that goodness is not limited—  
that what flows through one life  
does not bypass your own.

You are still held  
within the same abundance.
