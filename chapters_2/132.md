## 132 - The Path That Gently Leads

> *You are not lost when you follow what is true*  
> *Even a small yes can guide your way*

I saw a wide expanse  
covered in shadow—  
thick,  
unclear,  
without direction.

Figures moved within it,  
walking without rest,  
their steps uncertain—  
as though the ground beneath them  
could not be trusted.

There was a heat there,  
not visible,  
but felt—  
a subtle unrest  
that made every step uneasy.

And still they moved,  
searching,  
turning,  
unable to find a place  
that held them.

Then,  
through the quiet,  
a narrow path appeared.

It did not force itself forward.  
It did not break through the darkness.

It simply lay there—  
steady,  
clear,  
unmoved.

Along it,  
the ground was firm.  
The air was lighter.

Not brighter,  
not dramatic—  
just enough  
to walk without strain.

One figure paused.

For a moment,  
it turned—  
not fully,  
just slightly.

And with that small turning,  
its next step changed.

Not all at once.  
Not everything resolved.

But the ground held.

And I saw  
that the path had always been there—  
waiting,  
unchanging,  
ready to receive  
even the smallest step of trust.

#### Living the Vision

You feel uncertain  
about the way forward.

You may move through your days  
carrying a quiet tension—  
as though something  
is not quite aligned.

You may already sense  
what is being asked of you.

A truth to acknowledge.  
A step to take.  
A turning you have delayed.

And yet,  
something resists—  
not loudly,  
but enough to keep you  
where you are.

You do not need  
to resolve everything today.

Only to notice  
where the next step feels clear.

It may be small—  
almost simple.

A word spoken honestly.  
A responsibility embraced.  
A quiet act of follow-through.

When you take that step,  
you may feel  
a subtle shift.

Not relief from everything—  
but a steadiness  
that was not there before.

The path does not rush you.

It only asks  
for your willingness  
to place your foot  
where it can be held.

And that is enough  
to begin again.
