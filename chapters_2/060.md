## 60 — The Clarity of True Joy

> *Not all that fills your senses will fill your soul*  
> *There is a joy that clears rather than clouds*

I saw a soft haze  
resting over a wide space—

not dark,  
but dimmed,  
as though the light  
had been gently covered.

Within it,  
there was movement—

laughter passing,  
echoes of delight  
rising and fading.

It came easily,  
one moment into the next,  
never quite settling,  
never quite remaining.

And the air felt full—  
yet not clear.

Each sound  
gave way to another.  
Each brightness  
softened into the haze.

But just beyond this,  
I noticed a quiet opening—

a place where the air  
was still,  
unclouded.

The light there  
was not louder,  
not stronger—

but clearer.

And as attention turned  
toward it,  
the haze began to lift—

not all at once,  
but slowly,  
like a veil  
gently drawn back.

The laughter did not disappear.

It changed.

It deepened—  
becoming softer,  
truer,  
able to remain  
without needing to continue.

And in that clarity,  
there was space—

to breathe,  
to see,  
to rest  
within a joy  
that did not fade.

#### Living the Vision

There are times when  
your attention is filled—

with sound,  
with movement,  
with things that bring  
quick delight.

It can feel full,  
even satisfying.

But you may notice  
how quickly it passes—  
how it asks for more,  
how it leaves  
a subtle restlessness behind.

There is another way  
of being with joy.

You may begin to sense it  
in quieter moments—  
when nothing is added,  
when nothing is required  
to keep the moment alive.

It feels clearer.

More grounded.

You do not have to  
turn away from joy.

But you can gently notice  
what brings you back  
to yourself.

What allows you  
to breathe more fully.

What leaves you  
more present  
than before.

That is the joy  
that remains.
