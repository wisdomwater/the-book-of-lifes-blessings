## 66 - The Healing That Fits

> *What is brought into truth can be made whole*  
> *The soul is restored by a mercy that understands*

I saw a quiet place  
set apart from all noise—

a wide, open space  
filled with a gentle, knowing light.

There were no harsh edges here.  
No urgency.

Only a deep stillness  
that seemed to listen.

In the center,  
there were many small vessels—  
each different in shape,  
each held with care.

Some were filled with clear water.  
Some with oil.  
Some with a soft, fragrant balm.

And I understood  
that each had been prepared  
for a particular need.

Those who came into this place  
were not turned away.

They were seen.

Not only in what had been done—  
but in how they had come to be.

Their wounds were not hidden.

Yet neither were they exposed  
to harm.

A gentle hand moved among them—  
not hurried,  
not uncertain—

choosing what would heal,  
what would cleanse,  
what would restore.

Some were washed slowly.  
Some were anointed.  
Some were simply held  
in the quiet light.

Nothing was forced.

Nothing exaggerated.

Only what was needed  
was given.

And in that careful tending,  
something began to return—

a softness,  
a steadiness,  
a life that had not been lost,  
only waiting  
to be restored.

#### Living the Vision

You become aware  
that something in you  
has caused harm.

It may come gently—  
a realization,  
a quiet knowing.

Or it may arrive  
with more weight.

And in that moment,  
you may feel the urge  
to turn away,  
to move past it quickly,  
to soften its edges.

But there is another way.

You may choose  
to remain present—  
to let the truth  
be seen without distortion.

Not to condemn yourself,  
but to understand  
what is needed now.

You may notice  
that not every situation  
calls for the same response.

Sometimes it is a word spoken.  
Sometimes a change in action.  
Sometimes a quiet returning  
to what you know is right.

There is a wisdom  
that meets you here—  
not harsh,  
not distant—

but deeply attentive.

And as you allow yourself  
to be guided by it,  
what once felt heavy  
begins to shift—

not erased,  
but transformed.

You are not asked  
to carry the wound forever.

Only to let it be healed  
in the way it needs.
