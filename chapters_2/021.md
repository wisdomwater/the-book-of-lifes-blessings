## 21 — The Boundary of Knowing

> *There is a place where striving quiets*  
> *And you are held without needing to understand*

I saw a vast expanse—

wide and open,  
without edge or measure.

Light stretched upward  
beyond sight.

Below, a quiet depth  
rested in stillness.

And between them,  
the world gently turned—  
held in a balance  
that needed no effort.

Nothing rushed  
to define itself.

Nothing reached  
beyond its place.

There was a natural belonging—  
as if all things  
rested within a greater knowing  
they did not need to contain.

I sensed a soft boundary—  
not a barrier,  
but a horizon.

A place where movement slowed,  
where thought became quiet,  
where the need to go further  
simply faded.

And there,  
nothing was lacking.

The unseen was not distant.  
The unknown was not empty.

It was full—  
held within a presence  
that did not need to be grasped.

And I understood:

what could not be reached  
was not withheld.

It was already  
within a wholeness  
that gently carried all things.

#### Living the Vision

At times, your mind reaches outward—

seeking to understand,  
to anticipate,  
to bring clarity  
to what lies ahead.

You may feel  
the pull to go further—  
to see more,  
to know more,  
to find something certain.

And yet,  
there are moments  
when that reaching  
grows tired.

When the questions remain,  
and the answers  
do not come.

You may notice then  
a quiet invitation—

not to push beyond,  
but to remain.

To soften  
at the edge of what you know.

There is no failure here.

Only a boundary  
that is gently given.

And within that boundary,  
you are not confined.

You are held.

You are free  
to live what is before you  
without needing  
to carry the whole.

And as you rest here,  
even briefly,  
a quiet peace  
begins to settle—

not from understanding,  
but from trust.
