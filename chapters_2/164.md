## 164 - Hands in Earth, Heart in Light

> *You can tend what is before you*  
> *and still remain turned toward heaven*

I saw a wide field  
resting beneath an open sky.

The soil was rich,  
dark with promise,  
ready to receive the work of hands.

A figure walked slowly  
behind a simple plow—  
steady,  
attentive,  
moving in quiet rhythm.

The earth opened  
line by line,  
yielding without resistance.

Nothing hurried.

Nothing strained.

And yet,  
there was movement—  
a faithful tending  
of what had been given.

Above,  
the sky remained clear—  
vast,  
gentle,  
filled with a soft and constant light.

And as the figure worked,  
there was a quiet awareness—  
not divided,  
not pulled in two directions—  
but held together.

The hands touched the soil.

The heart remained lifted.

Each step  
belonged to both.

And in that union,  
the work became lighter—  
not because it disappeared,  
but because it was no longer carried alone.

The field was still a field.

The labor was still real.

But something unseen  
flowed through it—  
a quiet trust  
that what was sown  
would be given life  
in its time.

#### Living the Vision

There are many things  
that ask for your attention.

Daily tasks.  
Responsibilities.  
Details that cannot be ignored.

You may feel  
how easily these fill your thoughts—  
how quickly concern  
becomes the center.

And yet,  
something in you  
longs to remain connected  
to what is deeper.

You are not asked  
to leave your work behind.

You are invited  
to hold it differently.

You may begin  
in a simple way—  
by noticing your breath  
as you move through your day.

By allowing a quiet awareness  
to remain  
even while your hands are busy.

Not dividing yourself—  
but gently remembering  
that you belong  
to more than what you are doing.

In this remembering,  
something softens.

The urgency eases.  
The weight lightens.

And what you do  
becomes part of a larger life—  
one that is held,  
guided,  
and quietly sustained.
