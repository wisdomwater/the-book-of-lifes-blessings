## 158 - A Life That Sings

> *When you return, something in you begins to sing*  
> *Your life becomes a quiet offering of praise*

I saw a garden  
opening in the early light.

Not arranged by force,  
but growing—  
each part in its place,  
each movement held in harmony.

The air was filled  
with a soft radiance,  
as though the light itself  
was aware.

Among the garden,  
a figure walked—  
unadorned,  
unhurried,  
simply present.

Where the figure moved,  
small blossoms opened.

Not all at once.  
Not in display.

But gently—  
as if responding  
to something unseen  
and deeply known.

Above,  
the space shimmered—  
not with sound,  
but with a kind of listening.

And in that stillness,  
every small act  
seemed to be received.

A hand lifted.  
A step taken.  
A moment of turning.

Nothing was lost.

Everything  
was gathered  
into a quiet offering.

And the garden itself  
became a kind of song—  
not spoken,  
not performed—  
but lived.

And in that living,  
there was a deep joy—  
as if all things  
were returning  
to their right place.

#### Living the Vision

Something in you  
wants to begin again.

Not in a grand way—  
just a small turning  
toward what is good.

You may feel it  
after noticing  
what no longer gives life—  
a quiet desire  
to move differently.

And when you follow that—  
even slightly—  
something shifts.

You may not see it clearly.

But there is a sense  
that your life  
is being received.

That your small choices  
are not unnoticed.

A word spoken gently.  
A step taken in honesty.  
A turning away  
from what once held you.

These are not small.

They carry a fragrance—  
something real,  
something alive.

You do not need  
to become perfect  
to begin living in this way.

You only need  
to keep returning.

And as you do,  
your life begins to gather  
into something whole—  
a quiet offering  
that does not strain  
to be seen.

It simply becomes  
what it was meant to be.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Let my life become praise  
>
> **Exhale:** I return to what is true
