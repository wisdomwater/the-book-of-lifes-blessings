## 7 — The Courage to Stand

> *Peace is not the absence of truth*  
> *Love gives the heart a quiet strength*

A small hollow rested beneath the roots of a great tree.

The earth curved inward,  
soft and dim,  
like a place made for hiding.

Within it,  
a form lay curled—  
drawn tightly into itself,  
as if the world beyond  
asked too much.

Above, the branches swayed gently,  
their leaves whispering  
with the movement of the wind.

Light filtered through in fragments,  
touching the ground  
but never quite reaching  
where the form remained.

There was no threat.

No storm.

Only a quiet distance  
between what was hidden  
and what was open.

Then, slowly,  
the light shifted.

Not brighter—  
but nearer.

It did not press.  
It did not demand.

It simply rested  
at the edge of the hollow,  
waiting.

And in that waiting,  
something within the curled form  
began to stir.

Not from fear,  
but from recognition.

A memory of standing.  
A memory of being whole.

The body did not rush to rise.

But it loosened.

Just enough  
to feel the warmth.

Just enough  
to sense the space beyond  
was not against it.

And in that small opening,  
strength returned—

not as force,  
but as presence.

A quiet readiness  
to unfold.

#### Living the Vision

Staying quiet can feel like the safest path.

When speaking  
might cost something—  
comfort, approval,  
a sense of belonging.

You may notice how the body responds—  
drawing inward,  
choosing stillness  
over movement.

There is a kind of peace here.

But it can feel small.

Contained.

As if something in you  
is waiting  
just beneath the surface.

There is another kind of peace  
that does not come from hiding.

It arises  
when you allow yourself  
to stand—  
even gently—  
in what feels true.

You may sense it  
in quiet decisions:

a word spoken honestly,  
a boundary held with care,  
a refusal to turn away  
from what matters.

This is not a call to force  
or to struggle.

It is an invitation  
to trust the strength  
already forming within you.

You do not have to become fearless.

Only willing  
to remain present  
where truth lives.

And in that presence,  
something steadier than fear  
begins to take root.
