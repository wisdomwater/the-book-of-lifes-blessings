## 102 - The Softening Flame

> *What has hardened can be warmed again*  
> *Mercy rises where the heart opens*

I saw a fire  
moving in many tones—

deep and dim,  
bright and sharp,  
restless in its shifting.

It did not settle.

It turned inward and outward  
at once—  
like something  
unable to find rest.

And within it,  
there was tension—  
a tightening,  
a holding—  
as though what moved there  
could not soften.

Then,  
from beyond the flame,  
a gentle warmth approached.

Not stronger—  
but steadier.

It did not compete.

It remained—  
quiet,  
constant—  
like a presence  
that did not withdraw.

For a time,  
nothing changed.

Then slowly,  
the edges of the fire  
began to soften.

The sharpness eased.  
The movement slowed.

What had burned  
with tension  
began to glow  
with warmth.

And in that change,  
something opened—

not all at once,  
but enough.

Enough for light  
to rest there.

Enough for the heat  
to become  
life-giving again.

And I saw  
that the fire  
was never meant  
to wound—

only to warm,  
when held  
in gentleness.

#### Living the Vision

Your heart begins to tighten—

a reaction,  
a judgment,  
a quickness in your words  
that feels sharp.

It may feel justified.

It may feel necessary.

But you may also notice  
what follows—  
a subtle distance,  
a closing,  
a loss of warmth.

You do not need  
to force yourself  
into softness.

Only to pause.

To notice  
what is rising within you.

And in that noticing,  
something begins to shift.

You may choose  
to speak more slowly.

To listen  
just a moment longer.

To hold back  
what would wound.

These are small movements.

But they open space.

And in that space,  
a different kind of strength  
begins to emerge—

one that does not harden,  
but remains open.

You are not losing anything  
by softening.

You are returning  
to what is true.

And over time,  
what once felt sharp  
becomes steady—  
a warmth  
that can be shared  
without harm.
