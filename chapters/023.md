## Day 23 - Restored by Holy Fire

> *What fell into darkness is not abandoned—God restores it with greater holiness.*

I saw the great figure turn toward the East—  
toward the place of rising light—  
and also toward the South,  
where warmth and fullness abound.

And I understood:

From the beginning,  
God brought forth His creation like the sun—  
radiant, ordered, alive.

He did not merely create—  
He perfected.  
He caused all things to grow,  
to multiply,  
to flourish in their appointed path.

Yet I saw the fall of humanity—  
how man, once bright,  
turned away  
and became clouded with darkness.

Still, God did not abandon him.

With great compassion,  
He turned again toward the fallen one  
and began a work of restoration.

Not returning man only to what he was—  
but lifting him higher.

I saw virtues poured out—  
not sparingly,  
but richly.

Holiness entered where corruption had been.  
Light was infused into what had grown dim.

It was as if the broken vessel  
was not merely repaired,  
but filled anew with living fire.

Thus God reformed man—  
not leaving him in ruin,  
but drawing him again toward the light  
from which he came.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the movement of redemption. Creation began in goodness and order, flowing from God like light from the sun. Humanity’s fall introduced distortion, but not final defeat.

God’s response is not simply to restore what was lost, but to infuse something greater—holiness. The fallen condition becomes the place where divine grace is most powerfully expressed.

The turning toward the East symbolizes renewal and awakening. The South reflects fullness and maturity. Together, they show that restoration is both a return and a transformation.

God does not abandon His creation to its brokenness. He actively works within it, reshaping and renewing it from within.

#### Living This Vision

There may be parts of your life that feel like they have fallen—places where you have lost clarity, strength, or direction. It is easy to believe those places are beyond repair. But this vision says otherwise.

God does not turn away from what is broken. He turns toward it. Ask yourself gently:

*Where do I feel like I’ve fallen away from who I was meant to be?*

That place is not the end of your story. It is the place where restoration can begin. You are not being asked to rebuild yourself alone. You are being invited to receive.

Even now, something holy is being offered to you—not because you are perfect, but because you are loved.

And what is given is enough to begin again.
