## 65 - The Inner Light

> *Seeing is more than looking outward. Light awakens when the heart opens.*

I saw a figure standing within a dim and shifting space. Its gaze moved constantly from one thing to another, searching every corner as though clarity might finally appear somewhere beyond itself. It looked carefully at everything around it, attentive to every detail, never allowing its attention to settle for long in one place.

And yet, despite all this searching, the space remained unclear.

The more the figure strained outward, the more distant peace seemed to become. The movement carried a quiet restlessness within it—a longing for certainty that could never quite arrive through observation alone. Everything was examined, yet nothing fully satisfied the deeper ache beneath the searching.

Then very quietly, another light began to appear.

It did not emerge from the world outside, nor from some distant horizon. It rose softly from within the figure itself, almost hidden at first, like a small glow patiently waiting to be noticed. It did not demand attention or overwhelm the darkness around it. It simply remained present.

And the figure paused.

For the first time, its gaze stopped rushing endlessly outward. It turned inward—only slightly at first—and within that gentle turning, the inner light became clearer. Not brighter than everything else, but steadier. More rooted. It did not reveal every answer at once or remove all uncertainty from the path ahead.

But it revealed enough.

A direction.

A quiet knowing.

A peace that no longer depended upon constant searching.

And I saw then that this light had always been there beneath the noise and striving, offering itself patiently to anyone willing to become still enough to receive it.

### Living the Vision

Much of life teaches us to search outward for clarity. We gather information, seek advice, compare perspectives, and try to understand the world through endless observation and analysis. There is wisdom in this searching, and yet there are moments when it leaves the soul exhausted—filled with knowledge perhaps, but still inwardly unsettled.

Sometimes the deepest guidance arrives differently.

Not through greater striving, but through quiet attentiveness to what is already moving gently within us. A subtle intuition. A calm sense of direction that cannot fully explain itself. A small inner clarity that feels steady even when certainty remains incomplete.

At first, this inner knowing may seem fragile compared to the louder voices surrounding us.

Yet over time we begin recognizing that not every truth arrives through forceful certainty. Some truths emerge slowly through presence, honesty, prayer, silence, and the patient opening of the heart. We discover that wisdom is not only about seeing more—it is about seeing from a deeper place within ourselves.

This requires trust.

Not blind certainty, but willingness to move gently with the light we have already been given instead of demanding total understanding before taking a step forward. The soul learns how to receive guidance one moment at a time.

And as we turn inward toward this quiet light, something begins to settle.

The urgency to grasp at every answer softens. The need to control the entire path loosens. We become more capable of living within mystery without fear, trusting that what is needed will continue revealing itself as we remain open.

And slowly, vision grows clearer—not because we have mastered everything, but because the heart itself has become more aligned with what is true.
