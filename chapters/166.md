## 166 - The Quiet Enough

> *What rests in enoughness is filled.*
> *What opens in gratitude overflows.*

I saw a wide expanse of water at dawn.

It was deep, yet completely still.

No waves strained against the shore.
No wind troubled its surface.

Light rested upon it like a gentle hand,
spreading softly across the waters.

And beneath that light, there was a quiet fullness—
not crowded,
not overflowing,
but complete.

Along the edge, a garden grew.

Each plant reached only as far as it needed.
Each leaf turned naturally toward the light.

Nothing grasped.
Nothing strained beyond its place.

And a presence moved there,
like a breath passing through all things.

Where it touched,
there was a deep settling.

The water did not seek to rise.
The garden did not seek to expand.

All things received what was given,
and in receiving,
they were filled.

And I understood:

There is a way of being
that does not hunger for more—
because it has learned to dwell
within what is already here.

#### Living the Vision

You notice a quiet contentment.

It may come unexpectedly—
in a simple breath,
in the warmth of a small thing,
in the absence of needing anything else.

And just as quickly,
something in you may begin to reach again.

To look elsewhere.
To wonder what is missing.

You may notice how subtle this movement is—
a leaning forward,
a quiet dissatisfaction,
even when nothing is wrong.

But there is another rhythm within you.

Something that knows how to pause.

Something that recognizes fullness
without needing to add to it.

It does not argue.
It does not force you to stop.

It simply waits—
steady,
present,
unchanging.

And when you turn, even slightly,
you may feel it:

a settling,
a sufficiency,
a gentle sense that this moment is not lacking.

Not everything is complete.
But something in you is at rest.

And from that place,
there is room to receive,
and even to give,
without losing yourself in the reaching.
