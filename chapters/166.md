## Day 166 - The Unfilled Hunger

> *Desire without measure devours the soul. What grasps at everything is never satisfied.*

After this, I saw other evil spirits who cried out:

“What good is there in one thing alone? We will search in every direction and take whatever we desire.”

And they stirred in human hearts a restless longing for worldly things, urging them to want everything.

Then I saw a vast body of water, stretching wide and deep.

It boiled with fierce heat.

Within it were horrible worms and many dark spirits. And there the souls were tormented—those who, while living, had given themselves to worldly desire in many forms.

They suffered in the boiling water because their desire had never been satisfied. They were tormented by worms because of the bitterness that grew within them. And evil spirits plunged them deeper with burning forks, for they had pursued these desires with great zeal.

And I saw and understood these things.

Then a voice from the living light spoke:

“The punishments you see are true. Those who wish to escape this fate must turn away from excess desire. Let them discipline themselves and give generously, that their hearts may be purified.”

And I understood further.

Worldly desire runs without rest.

It gathers what it can, grasping at things beyond its need. It has no measure—not in the heart, not in the body, not in its actions.

It is like dogs running here and there, never satisfied.

It is like an unclean bird, driven by restless hunger and anxious greed.

Such desire does not know God. It clings to what passes away.

But those who long for life turn away from excess. They moderate their actions. They shape their lives not only by what the world offers, but by what God gives.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals desire as something that can become distorted.

Desire itself is not condemned. But when it loses its measure—when it seeks fulfillment only in accumulation—it becomes a force that consumes rather than nourishes.

The boiling water shows the agitation of endless craving. The worms reveal the inner bitterness that grows when desire is never fulfilled. The plunging motion shows how willingly the soul dives deeper into its own unrest.

The comparison to animals is intentional. Without spiritual awareness, desire becomes instinct without wisdom—movement without meaning.

The invitation is not to suppress desire, but to redeem it.

#### Living This Vision

We live in a world that constantly invites us to want more.

More comfort. More security. More recognition. More experience.

But unchecked desire quietly erodes peace. It keeps the soul in a state of restless searching.

This vision asks us to notice:

What am I chasing that never satisfies?

To live differently is to begin practicing enoughness. To receive what is given with gratitude. To loosen our grip on excess. To give instead of grasp.

In this, desire becomes aligned with life rather than against it.
