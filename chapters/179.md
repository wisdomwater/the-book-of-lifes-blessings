## Day 179 - The Place of Rest

> *The restless soul wanders because it fears stillness. But wisdom asks Christ to show the place where love is fed and where the heart may rest.*

The second image was Frivolity.

It followed after Fatuity, for where foolish speech scatters the soul, frivolity carries it even farther into instability.

This image looked childish. It did not look joyfully toward heaven or carefully upon the earth. It did not discern what was right. It acted like a child in all its works.

Yet it had the face and beard of an old man, for frivolity often wishes to appear wise and venerable before others. It lacks wisdom, but borrows the appearance of devotion.

It hung in darkness, wrapped like an infant in a cradle, swinging back and forth in the wind.

So those ruled by this vice are trapped in their own will. They imagine they are resting, but they are carried here and there by temptation. They begin nothing rightly and finish nothing rightly. Like a troubled cloud, they wander without peace.

Sometimes the image rose from the cloth. Sometimes it hid within it.

So the frivolous soul sometimes tries to become reverent, but then hides its own will and continues seeking novelty.

It wants to be seen everywhere.

It wants to display itself proudly.

But Prudent Stability answered and called the faithful toward steadiness.

And wisdom speaks to Christ:

“Show me, Love of my soul, where You feed Your flock, where You rest at noon, so that I may not wander after the flocks of others.”

For the soul longs for the kiss of God’s grace.

When sin darkens it, repentance makes it beautiful again.

When it rises from sin, the daughters of heavenly Jerusalem rejoice in the fragrance of its renewal.

So the soul says to Christ:

“Show me Your humanity, born of the Virgin. Show me where You rested in death. Show me the path opened by Your resurrection, so I do not wander in crooked ways.”

#### What This Vision Means

Frivolity is not simple playfulness. It is spiritual instability.

It avoids depth. It moves from place to place, thought to thought, desire to desire, never remaining long enough to be formed.

The image of the cradle is striking. Frivolity wants comfort without maturity. It wants movement without growth. It wants to appear wise while remaining unrooted.

The response from the Song of Songs gives the remedy: ask Christ where He feeds and where He rests.

The wandering soul is healed not by shame, but by desire rightly directed. It must seek the place where love nourishes it.

Stability is not stagnation. It is rest in the Beloved.

#### Living This Vision

We often mistake movement for growth.

A new idea, a new project, a new practice, a new path—each can feel like life. But if we never remain, we never ripen.

This vision invites us to ask where Christ is feeding us now.

What place of prayer, service, honesty, or obedience have we been avoiding because it feels too ordinary?

The soul does not need to be everywhere.

It needs to be where love is real.
