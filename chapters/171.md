## Day 171 - The False Dominion

> *Power that seeks to control creation without honoring its source becomes deception. True wisdom leads back to God, not away from Him.*

Then I saw the third image.

It had the head of a wolf and the tail of a lion, and its body was like that of a dog.

It moved with the second image, saying, “We are one in all things.”

The winds roared in its ears, and it listened closely, studying them, as if they were its teachers.

It lifted its forelimbs, reaching toward the winds, gathering the elements as if they belonged to it.

Then it spoke:

“I have learned much from the wise—from those who have studied deeply and uncovered hidden things.

They have mastered the elements. They have gained knowledge from the heavens, from the stars, from the earth itself.

With this power, I rule wherever I wish—over the sky, over the earth, over all creatures.

Who can oppose me?

God created all things, but I do not harm them. I only reveal what is hidden.

What good is it if truth remains concealed?”

And its voice was filled with pride.

Then from the storm came a response:

“What is greater—to worship the Creator, or His creation?

The creatures cannot give life.

Man is given reason to know and speak truth, but you twist this gift.

You circle around creation, but you do not reach its center.

And in doing so, you will be cast down, for you have tried to take what belongs to God.”

And I understood the danger.

#### What This Vision Means

This image reveals a misuse of knowledge and power.

It is not knowledge itself that is condemned—but the attempt to control, possess, and elevate oneself above the source of all knowledge.

The creaturely forms—wolf, lion, dog—suggest instinct, dominance, and cunning.

The winds represent unseen forces, which this figure tries to master.

But the response makes clear: wisdom without reverence becomes distortion.

True knowledge leads to worship, not self-exaltation.

#### Living This Vision

We are drawn to knowledge.

To understanding how things work, how to influence outcomes, how to gain control.

But this vision asks:

Does my knowledge bring me closer to humility—or further into pride?

Do I seek understanding to serve life—or to elevate myself?

True wisdom is not just knowing.

It is knowing where knowledge comes from.

Let what you learn lead you back to reverence.
