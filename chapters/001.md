## 1 - The Man Between Heaven and Abyss

> *Between the heights of heaven and the depths of the abyss stands a mystery: a Presence who holds all creation together.*
> *From Him flow winds, voices, and the hidden movements of life.*

I, a simple and fragile mortal, received these visions not by my own strength but through the living light of God. From childhood I had been shown spiritual things—not with my bodily eyes, but inwardly through the Spirit. Yet the visions often left my body weak, as though my flesh struggled to bear what my spirit was shown.

For many years I endured both revelation and suffering. What was first given to me came gently, like milk given to a child. But as time passed, deeper mysteries were revealed—solid food for the soul.

Then a voice from heaven spoke:

“You who have received these visions from childhood—speak what you see and hear. Do not speak from yourself, but from Me. Write not from your own will, but from Mine.”

I trembled when I heard these words. My instinct was to hide, to disappear so that no one would see me. Yet the voice urged me forward, and so I began to write.

Then I was shown a vision.

I saw a Man of immense height. He stretched from the highest clouds of heaven down into the deepest abyss. His shoulders rose above the serene sky. From his shoulders to his thighs he stood within a bright cloud. From his thighs to his knees he moved through the air of the earth. From his knees to his calves he stood upon the ground. And from his calves downward he reached into the dark waters of the abyss itself.

He faced the East and looked toward the South.

His face shone with such brilliance that I could not look directly upon it.

From his mouth came a white cloud shaped like a trumpet. When he blew into it, three great winds were released.

One wind carried a fiery cloud.
Another carried a storm-filled cloud.
The third bore a cloud filled with radiant light.

The fiery cloud remained before the Man’s face, spreading from the East toward the South. Within it was a vast multitude of living beings, glowing with ardent life. They were united in purpose and harmony.

Before them stood a small table covered with feathers that moved according to the commands of God. Upon this table the wisdom of God had written secret mysteries. When the beings looked upon these writings, divine strength flowed into them, and they began to sound the trumpet in perfect harmony.

The second wind carried the stormy cloud from the South toward the West. Within that cloud was an immeasurable host of saints. Their voices roared like rushing waters.

They cried out:

“We dwell here according to the will of the One who sends the winds. But when will we receive our bodies again? For only then will our joy be complete.”

The beings in the fiery cloud answered with praise:

“When God sounds the great trumpet, lightning and fire will shake the earth. The fire within the sun will stir, and the world will tremble. Then the trumpet will call all peoples in every language, and those whose names are written will receive their bodies again.”

The third wind spread its radiant cloud from the East toward the North.

From the West came a terrifying darkness that tried to enter this shining cloud, but it could not pass beyond its brightness.

Within the cloud appeared the sun and the moon.

In the sun was a lion.
In the moon was a ram.

The sun moved across heaven, earth, and the depths below. As it advanced, the lion advanced with it, seizing its prey with fierce strength. When the sun set, the lion withdrew with triumphant roars.

The moon followed the path of the sun. And upon it the ram moved with steady resolve.

And the wind spoke:

“The woman who is with child will give birth, and the ram will contend against the North.”

#### What This Vision Means

Hildegard’s vision opens with a cosmic image: a towering Man who spans heaven, earth, and the abyss. This figure represents the divine order that holds all creation together. Nothing lies outside this living presence—not the heights of heaven nor the depths below.

The winds flowing from the Man’s trumpet reveal how God moves history and creation. Each wind carries a different cloud, representing different dimensions of spiritual reality.

The fiery cloud symbolizes the living spiritual powers who respond immediately to divine wisdom. Their unity shows that true knowledge of God leads to harmony rather than division. When they gaze upon the divine writings, they are strengthened to sound the trumpet together—suggesting that heavenly beings participate in the unfolding of God’s purposes.

The storm cloud represents the souls of the saints awaiting resurrection. Their cry reveals an important truth: the human soul longs not only for heaven but for the restoration of the whole person. Creation itself waits for renewal.

The radiant cloud symbolizes divine protection and light. Darkness attempts to invade it but cannot overcome it. Even when evil approaches, it cannot extinguish the light that flows from God.

The lion in the sun and the ram in the moon evoke strength and sacrifice—power and patience moving through the rhythms of time.

This vision reveals a universe alive with divine movement, where light ultimately holds back the darkness.

#### Living This Vision

Hildegard’s vision reminds us that our lives unfold within a vast spiritual reality we rarely see.

The Man who spans heaven and the abyss shows that God is present in every layer of existence. The highest joys and the deepest struggles both lie within His sight.

The three clouds also reflect the movements within our own spiritual lives.

Sometimes we experience the fiery clarity of divine truth—a moment when everything feels alive with purpose.

At other times we feel like the saints in the storm cloud, waiting. We long for restoration, for healing, for things to be made whole again.

And often we stand within the radiant cloud, where light quietly protects us even while darkness presses in from the edges.

The vision reminds us that darkness does not have the final word.

Even when we cannot see clearly, the winds of God are still moving. The trumpet has not yet sounded its final call.

And one day, as the saints declare, what is broken will be restored, and life will rise again.
