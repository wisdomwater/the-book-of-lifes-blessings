## Day 120 - The Light They Refuse to See

> *I heard of those who ask, “Who is this Lord?” even while they live beneath His light, breathe His air, and eat from the earth He waters with rain.*

I heard of certain hardened souls  
who should have borne no fruit at all,  
for in them there was only dryness—  
a barrenness made worse  
by empty superstitions  
and the dark arts of deceit.

They shaped all things  
according to greed and desire.  
And in their hearts and with their tongues  
they kept asking:

“Who is this Lord?  
What do we know of Him?  
What power does He have,  
if He remains hidden  
and we have never seen Him?”

Then the Lord answered them.

When you were called to do good,  
did you not see the light  
of the knowledge of good  
and the sun shining upon the world?

When you were called to avoid evil,  
did you not know the darkness  
of a troubled heart  
and the darkness of the night?

When justice was planted in you,  
did you not see the seed  
made fruitful by dew and rain?

When you placed seed into the ground,  
did you not watch it moisten, swell, and grow?

Who does this,  
if not the Creator of all things?

Every creature knows its Creator  
and longs toward Him.  
Each one understands, in its own manner,  
that it has life because He gives it.

But man, distracted and divided  
among many vanities,  
tries to limit the Creator  
as though God were another creature  
that could be measured, contained, or blamed.

And when man sins by his own will,  
he says God made him this way,  
as though the Creator were responsible  
for what the rebellious heart chooses.

Yet who gave wisdom to Scripture?  
Who spoke what no human mind  
could have imagined on its own?

The manifold wisdom in holy writings  
came from God.

So long as creation still serves  
the temporary needs of this present life,  
human beings do not yet behold  
the full perfection of eternal joy.

But when the world’s present form passes away,  
and creation is released from its labor,  
the elect will see their Creator  
and receive the reward of holy works  
in unending gladness.

Then they will need nothing from this passing world.  
They will shine like sparks in dry reeds—  
alive with the brightness of God,  
free from the heaviness of the flesh,  
in the joy that does not end.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision speaks to the spiritual blindness that demands a visible God while overlooking the traces of Him everywhere. The question “Who is this Lord?” is not asked from innocence here, but from a will already bent away from reverence.

Hildegard’s answer is deeply sacramental. God is not absent from the world. His signs are woven into light and darkness, seed and rain, growth and conscience, Scripture and wisdom. The problem is not that God gives no witness. The problem is that the heart can become too divided to recognize the witness already given.

The passage also confronts a common evasion: blaming God for what we freely choose. Sin wants to make the Creator responsible for the creature’s rebellion. But the vision insists that God made man good, gave wisdom, and set signs of truth in creation.

The promise at the end is beautiful: creation serves us now, but it is not the final joy. The elect are destined for a life beyond dependence on changing things—a joy full, direct, and unshadowed.

#### Living This Vision

There are times when God feels hidden.

And in those moments  
the heart may begin to say:  
If I cannot see Him clearly,  
how can I trust Him?

This vision answers gently but firmly:  
look again.

Look at the way goodness still calls to you.  
Look at the unease that warns you away from evil.  
Look at rain, seed, growth, hunger, conscience, beauty, sorrow, longing.

Not every sign will feel dramatic.  
But grace is often quiet.

This passage also asks for honesty.  
When I go astray,  
do I take responsibility?  
Or do I make excuses,  
blaming temperament, history, even God Himself?

The soul grows clearer  
when it stops accusing heaven  
for what it has chosen on earth.

Today, practice recognition.  
Name three places  
where the Creator has already made Himself known to you.
