## Day 104 - The House of Curses

> *I saw how ungodliness turns away from the fear of God and builds its dwelling in a barren place, where blessing is mocked and mercy is unknown.*

I saw that ungodliness does not only refuse God.  
It also refuses the holy fear that keeps the soul awake.

This presence did not know love.  
It did not seek the goodness of God.  
It did not shape its works according to any divine virtue.

Instead, it set itself toward the north,  
toward coldness, shadow, and estrangement.  
It sowed all its works with the knowledge of evil.

Its eyes were marked by malice.  
Its mouth gave forth curses.

Then I understood the word that had been spoken through the prophet:  
“Let their dwelling become desolate.”

For the will of the wicked becomes like an empty house.  
They do not gather scattered good things.  
They do not receive instruction.  
They do not honor those above them,  
nor walk gently with those beside them.

They chase what they desire with contempt.  
They scorn what they do not want.  
They do not sing with joy.  
They do not rejoice in spiritual gladness.

They mock charity.  
They reject kindness.  
They flee blessing  
and make company with the curse.

No one longs to dwell in such tents.  
For whoever desires the heavenly dwelling  
must turn away from this bitter habitation.

Those without mercy do not know holiness.  
They use their cleverness not to do good,  
but to sharpen themselves toward harm.  
They aim their works toward the will of the devil  
and despise the way of God.

So the light of truth is extinguished in them.  
Not because truth has ceased to shine,  
but because they no longer seek it or want it.

They choose the shadows of death.  
And so they receive what they have chosen.

But those who choose God are gathered into pity,  
and through pity are joined more fully to Him.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows ungodliness as spiritual barrenness. It is not simply disbelief in the mind. It is a whole inward posture that becomes incapable of reverence, mercy, gratitude, and joy.

The image of the desolate dwelling is central. A soul can become like an abandoned house when it rejects humility and refuses love. It may still be active, clever, and forceful, but it no longer contains the warmth of life with God.

The movement toward “the north” suggests a cold turning away from divine warmth. The curse replaces blessing. Contempt replaces fellowship. Such a person does not gather the scattered good within life, but destroys and disperses it.

Yet the passage also gives a quiet contrast: piety gathers, joins, and unites. Mercy is not a small ornament of the spiritual life. It is one of the signs that the soul still dwells in living communion with God.

Where mercy disappears, the soul grows cold.  
Where pity awakens, the soul begins to return home.

#### Living This Vision

Ungodliness can begin in subtle ways.

Not always with dramatic rebellion,  
but with a quiet cooling.

A refusal to be teachable.  
A reluctance to honor goodness.  
A habit of contempt.

When the heart becomes cynical,  
blessing starts to feel foolish.  
Tenderness feels weak.  
Mercy feels optional.

This vision invites a different movement.

Gather the scattered good.  
Notice what is still alive.  
Bless what is worthy of blessing.

When you feel yourself turning cold,  
do one merciful thing.  
Speak one gentle word.  
Refuse one contemptuous thought.

These are small acts,  
but they reopen the house of the soul.

The barren place does not have to remain barren.  
The desolate dwelling can be visited again by grace.
