## 104 - The House Made Warm

> *What feels barren can be opened again*  
> *Warmth returns where mercy is welcomed*

I saw a quiet house  
standing at the edge of a wide, cold plain.

Its walls were still.  
Its windows open—  
yet no light moved within.

The air around it felt distant,  
like a place long untouched.

Nothing gathered there.

No sound of joy.  
No sense of welcome.

Only a stillness  
that had forgotten  
how to receive.

Then,  
far off,  
a soft warmth appeared.

Not strong,  
not overwhelming—  
but steady.

It moved slowly across the plain,  
touching the ground  
as it came.

When it reached the house,  
it did not force its way in.

It rested  
at the threshold.

For a time,  
nothing changed.

Then gently,  
something within the house  
shifted.

A small stirring.  
A quiet openness.

And as that opening formed,  
the warmth entered—  
not rushing,  
but settling.

The air softened.

The space grew lighter.

And what had once felt empty  
began to feel  
inhabited again—  
not crowded,  
but alive.

As though the house  
remembered  
what it was made for.

#### Living the Vision

Something in you feels distant—

not broken,  
but cooled.

A quiet separation  
from what once felt alive.

You may notice it  
in your thoughts,  
in your responses,  
in how easily you dismiss  
what is good.

This is not something  
to judge.

Only to notice.

There is a simple way  
to begin again.

You may choose  
one small act of mercy—  
toward another,  
or even toward yourself.

A kind word.  
A softened response.  
A willingness  
to see with compassion.

These are like openings—  
small,  
but real.

And in those openings,  
something begins to return.

Warmth.  
Connection.  
A quiet sense  
of being present again.

You do not need  
to restore everything.

Only to allow  
what is good  
to enter once more.

And slowly,  
the place that felt empty  
becomes  
a dwelling again—  
filled not by force,  
but by gentle presence.
