## 68 - The Garden Within

> *You are not empty ground. You are a place where life is forming.*

I saw the earth resting in quiet fullness beneath a gentle sky. The soil was dark and rich, carrying within itself hidden depths no eye could fully see. Moisture moved softly through the ground, loosening what had once been firm and preparing it to receive life. Seeds lay buried there in silence, unseen yet held safely within the patience of the earth.

Nothing hurried in that place.

There was no striving, no anxious movement pressing growth forward before its time. And yet beneath the stillness, life was already unfolding in secret ways. Roots stretched quietly through hidden spaces. Small beginnings formed beneath the surface long before they would ever rise into the light.

Then I saw the soul reflected in this same earth.

Open.

Receptive.

Carrying within itself a depth greater than it often realizes.

At first, nothing outwardly appeared different. The surface remained calm and ordinary. But slowly, something within began to stir. A longing rose gently, soft as breath moving through still air. A prayer formed quietly, like dew settling over waiting ground. And where these touched the soul, something living began taking root.

Not forced into being.

Not shaped through pressure or endless striving.

Simply growing according to its own hidden wisdom, as though it had always known how to become what it was meant to be.

And I saw then that this life did not originate from the soul alone. It was given. Received. Held within grace and allowed to rise slowly toward fullness.

### Living the Vision

There are seasons when it feels as though little is changing within us. We look for visible signs of growth—for clarity, transformation, certainty, or emotional breakthrough—and when we cannot see them clearly, we begin to wonder whether anything meaningful is happening at all.

But much of spiritual life unfolds beneath the surface long before it becomes visible.

Sometimes growth appears first as a small willingness to return after wandering. A quiet desire to remain present. A subtle softening toward what is good and life-giving. These movements may seem insignificant compared to dramatic transformation, yet they are often the earliest signs that something sacred is already taking root within us.

The soul is not barren simply because growth is hidden.

Like seeds beneath winter soil, many things require darkness, stillness, and time before they emerge into visible life. We are not asked to force ourselves into immediate maturity or produce spiritual fruit through sheer effort alone. Instead, we are invited to remain open—to continue receiving what nourishes the deeper life within us.

This openness itself becomes fertile ground.

Prayer, silence, kindness, truthfulness, small acts of faithfulness—these gently water the hidden garden of the soul even when we cannot yet measure what they are doing. And over time, what has been quietly nurtured begins to rise naturally toward the light. Not all at once. Not perfectly. But truly.

You do not need to manufacture life within yourself.

You are simply invited to receive it.
