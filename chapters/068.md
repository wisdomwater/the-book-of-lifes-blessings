## Day 68 — The Swamp of Hollow Laughter

> *There is a joy that sparkles on the surface but leaves the soul dim beneath it.*

I saw a restless company of spirits, moving together in sly delight.  
They laughed with sharp voices and urged one another on.

“Lucifer is our lord,” they cried.  
“What does it matter if another is stronger?  
We will follow him and do as we please.”

Their joy was loud, but it rang hollow.  
It fed on defiance.  
It delighted in casting aside what is right.

And I saw what became of those who listened to them.

A vast swamp opened before me.  
Its waters were thick and unmoving.  
From it rose a dark smoke that spread like a heavy breath over the marsh.  
A dim fog settled over everything, dulling all light.

Within it were many souls—no longer clothed in bodies.  
They were immersed in that swamp as they once immersed themselves in foolish joy.

Small worms swarmed over them.  
The smoke choked them.  
The fog obscured them.

And I understood:

They were submerged because they had sunk into empty delight.  
They were choked by smoke because they had neglected the way of God.  
They were covered in fog because they had chosen what darkens the soul.  
They were bitten by worms because of the careless and vain words they had spoken.

Their laughter had become their burden.

And the living light spoke:

If those who are drawn to this false joy would turn,  
let them restrain themselves.  
Let them humble their bodies and quiet their desires.  
Let them seek guidance and measure their correction with sincerity.

For the tongue that once sharpened itself in mockery  
must learn again how to speak truth.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals a kind of joy that is not truly life-giving. It is a joy rooted in rebellion, distraction, and self-indulgence—a laughter that avoids truth rather than embracing it.

Foolish joy is not simply happiness. It is a distortion of joy. It delights in excess, in mockery, in saying what is clever rather than what is good. It resists the deeper call of the soul and instead clings to what is immediate and gratifying.

The swamp shows what happens when this becomes a way of life. The soul becomes heavy, obscured, and entangled. The smoke represents confusion and disconnection from God. The fog reflects spiritual blindness. The worms reveal how careless words and habits begin to consume from within.

Even the image of sharp teeth and cutting speech speaks to how words can wound—both others and the soul itself.

Yet the vision is not without hope. It points to the possibility of turning. Through awareness, restraint, and humility, the soul can begin to clear what has become clouded.

True joy is not loud or reckless. It is rooted in truth, and it brings light rather than dimness.

#### Living This Vision

There are moments when laughter feels like escape.

Not all laughter is harmful. But there is a kind that distances us from ourselves—where we avoid discomfort, dismiss what is sacred, or indulge in what we know is not aligned with who we are becoming.

This vision invites you to notice that line.

When does joy feel grounding and alive?  
And when does it leave a subtle heaviness behind?

Pay attention to your words.  
Are they building something true, or simply filling space?

Pay attention to your desires.  
Are they leading you toward clarity, or into fog?

You do not need harsh punishment to return.  
But you may need gentle restraint.  
A willingness to pause.  
A willingness to speak less and listen more.

Each small act of awareness clears the air.  
Each moment of honesty lifts the fog.

And slowly, the soul begins to breathe again.
