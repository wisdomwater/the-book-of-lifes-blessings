## Day 124 - The Fire of Self-Will

> *I saw disobedience rushing from place to place, stirring the darkness as it went. It called rebellion wisdom, yet its fire only multiplied the flames of other vices.*

Then I understood the fourth image more fully.

It is Disobedience.

It comes after vainglory  
because it serves vainglory  
and carries many other vices forward.

It prepares the way for them  
and opens that way with fire.

Its head is like a serpent’s,  
because its intention is joined  
to the ancient serpent  
who first persuaded man  
not to obey God.

Its chest is feathered like a seabird,  
because it helps men rise  
to a place where they trust  
their own understanding—  
not unto wisdom,  
but into folly.

Its legs and feet are like a viper’s,  
because it directs human steps  
according to desire,  
drawing them away  
from rightful submission  
and toward arrogant resistance.

Its back and tail  
and the rest of its body  
are like a crab’s.

For the strength it draws  
from rebellion  
does not carry a work  
straight to its proper end.

It moves sideways through excuses,  
forward in boldness,  
backward in cunning,  
never stable,  
never whole.

It rejects what is established  
in the commandments of God.  
With miserable excuses  
it mocks and tears down  
what is holy.

So it rushes here and there  
as though carried by the wind,  
and in moving  
it stirs up all the darkness.

Those who embrace disobedience  
do not rebel only once.  
They become restless in rebellion,  
moving from one refusal to another,  
stirring other vices into motion.

It turns toward the north  
and pours out much fire from its mouth,  
because in the north it finds  
the old defiance  
that first set itself against God.

From that source  
it kindles many fires in many sins,  
for disobedience burns with its words  
everything it can reach.

And so Obedience stands against it,  
calling the soul back to life.

#### What This Vision Means

Disobedience here is more than breaking a rule. It is a spiritual attitude that resists rightful order, distrusts goodness outside the self, and seeks to justify itself through restless cleverness.

The serpent’s head places it squarely in the drama of the Fall. Disobedience is not morally neutral independence. It repeats the old refusal: *I will not receive my life as gift and command.*

The crab-like body is especially vivid. This vice does not move cleanly toward truth. It sidesteps, retreats, darts, and disguises. It is unstable because it is cut off from the straightness of obedience.

Its link to vainglory matters too. Many acts of disobedience are fueled by the desire to preserve one’s own image, independence, or self-importance. Rather than yield, the soul would rather burn.

Obedience, by contrast, is not servility. It is alignment with reality as God has ordered it. It gives direction, peace, and coherence to the soul.

#### Living This Vision

There is a kind of resistance in us  
that does not want to be told.

It says:  
I know better.  
I will decide for myself.  
No one will direct me.

Sometimes that instinct protects freedom.  
But sometimes it is only pride in motion.

Disobedience often hides behind reasons.  
It sounds thoughtful, practical, even wise.  
Yet underneath may be a simple refusal to yield.

A good question is this:  
What do I resist,  
not because it is wrong,  
but because it asks me to surrender my self-will?

That is where disobedience often lives.

Practice one small act of willing obedience today.  
Receive a needed correction.  
Keep a promise.  
Follow through where you wanted to evade.

The soul becomes steadier  
when it stops living sideways.
