## Day 143 - The Chariot of Commandments

> *The world promises beauty through possession. Holy freedom begins when the soul no longer needs to be adorned by what passes away.*

Then I saw the seventh image.

It was like a woman down to the legs, but her legs and feet were sunk deep in darkness, so that I could not see them. Her head was covered like a woman’s head, and she wore a white garment.

And she spoke eagerly.

“I sincerely desire to gather wealth, honor, and beauty. I am always ready to receive a gift, even a small one. The more I possess, the more my knowledge grows.

“By my beautiful rings, necklaces, earrings, and riches, everyone can see that I am wise. In small things I clearly discern value.

“If I did not have all these things, I would not possess the quality and dignity I have. I would be like a rotten branch, without hardness or flexibility.

“But I am able to do good with God and with people. Others benefit from what I possess.”

Then the voice from the storm-cloud answered.

“You are a false argument. You arrange bodily things for the pleasure of the flesh.

“Many generations longed for riches and worldly honors. They sought signs in the sun and stars. They called themselves gods, or trusted in created things as gods.

“What did their vanity gain them? Where are their riches, honors, and possessions now?

“They did not keep the sign of the Holy Spirit. They did not desire heavenly goods. They desired what is bodily and passing away.

“But I live under the sign of the Holy Spirit. I ride in the chariot of God’s commandments. I walk in his ways. I call upon him as Father.

“I turn away from the desires of the flesh.

“When those desires trouble me, I keep watch in the fear of God and in the fiery wheel of the Holy Spirit.

“When people honor me and offer me possessions, I do not treasure them. I seek only what is necessary to sustain me with moderation.

“I say, ‘These things turn me away from the face of God, and I am ashamed.’

“When sin tempts me, I answer, ‘You did not create me, and you cannot free me from evil. Therefore I despise your deceit.’

“When the blazing flame of the Holy Spirit kindles me, all worldly things are consumed within me. Then I ride the heavenly chariot through the regions of heaven.”

#### What This Vision Means

Worldly desire presents itself beautifully. It speaks of wisdom, dignity, usefulness, and generosity. It claims that possessions give identity and honor.

But its feet are hidden in darkness.

This is the danger. The soul may appear clothed in white, adorned and respectable, while its foundation is sunk in shadow. Worldly desire often disguises itself as prudence or influence. It says, “With these things, I can be someone. With these things, I can do good.”

The virtue answers as holy detachment.

This does not mean hatred of the material world. It means freedom from being defined by it. The virtue receives only what is necessary and refuses to let possessions turn the soul away from the face of God.

The fiery wheel of the Holy Spirit consumes false attachments. What remains is not emptiness, but freedom.

To ride the chariot of God’s commandments is to move through life guided by divine love rather than worldly display.

#### Living This Vision

It is easy to measure ourselves by visible things.

What we own.  
How we look.  
How others honor us.  
Whether our life appears successful.

But this vision asks a deeper question: what are your feet standing in?

Are you rooted in God, or sunk in the need to be admired?

Holy detachment begins gently. We do not need to despise beauty or usefulness. We simply refuse to let them become our source of worth.

Use what you have.  
Share what you can.  
Receive what is needed.  
But keep your face turned toward God.

The soul becomes light when it no longer has to prove its value through possession.
