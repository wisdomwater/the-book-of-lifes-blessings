## Day 126 - The Mirror of Purity

> *I heard the apostolic call to draw near with a true heart, washed and made clean, holding fast to hope because the One who promised is faithful.*

Then I heard the witness of holy teaching  
rise up against unbelief.

The Spirit calls the soul  
to draw near to the supreme holiness  
with a true heart,  
in the fullness of faith.

Not with a divided heart.  
Not with a double mind  
that chooses one thing today  
and another tomorrow  
according to whim and self-will.

But with sincerity.  
With peace at the beginning of good works.  
With mercy in their completion.  
With the warmth of fraternal love.  
With the tenderness of pity.

For in this way  
the soul begins to imitate God  
in what is fitting for human life.

The heart must be purified  
from an evil conscience.

Scripture is like a sprinkling upon it,  
washing what has grown dull and dark.

The mind must not wander  
in endless private inventions,  
nor in the slyness that accepts one truth  
only to reject another.

It must learn again  
to ask in simplicity:

Who is God?  
What are His works?  
What has He spoken?

Then the conscience,  
which was shadowed by the fall,  
begins to clear.

The body too is washed with pure water.

For faith does not remain only inward.  
It enters the whole human life.  
It desires cleansing in thought,  
in memory,  
in desire,  
in habit,  
in action.

And then hope must be held firmly.

Not because the soul is strong in itself,  
but because the One who promised  
is faithful.

Faith does not rest  
upon the stability of man.  
It rests upon the faithfulness of God.

So the heart that once wandered  
through unbelief’s dark searching  
is gathered again.

It draws near.

It confesses.

It hopes.

And in that nearness  
the mirror of purity is lifted up,  
so that the soul may begin to see  
not merely the world,  
but God reflected in it.

#### What This Vision Means

This passage shifts from exposing unbelief to showing the path out of it. The answer is not coercion, nor shame, but purification and nearness. The soul is called to draw near to God “with a true heart,” which means with sincerity, singleness, and openness.

The contrast with unbelief is strong. Unbelief divides, hedges, and protects itself. Faith gathers and comes forward. Unbelief multiplies dark eyes; faith becomes a clear mirror. Unbelief asks questions in a way that evades surrender; faith asks in order to know and love what is true.

The sprinkling of Scripture is especially beautiful. The conscience is not healed by self-analysis alone, but by being washed in what God has spoken. The body washed with pure water suggests that faith is embodied; it touches the whole person.

And at the center is hope—not confidence in one’s own consistency, but trust in the fidelity of God. This is why the soul can come near even while still weak.

#### Living This Vision

Sometimes unbelief lingers  
not because a person has no light,  
but because the heart has grown divided.

Part of us wants God.  
Part of us clings to self-rule.  
Part of us wants truth.  
Part of us wants to remain untouched.

This vision invites a very gentle courage:  
come near anyway.

You do not need a perfect heart  
to begin.  
You need a true one.

Tell God the truth about your uncertainty.  
Let Scripture wash places in you  
that argument alone cannot heal.  
Let prayer become less about performance  
and more about nearness.

Try one simple act of drawing near today:  
sit quietly with a psalm,  
confess one hidden fear,  
or speak one honest word of hope.

The faithful God is not waiting  
for your flawless certainty.  
He is waiting for your consent.
