## 175 - The Deep Held in Peace

> *Even the depths are gently ordered*  
> *What feels vast is still within care*

I saw a great expanse beneath the earth—  
a hidden depth,  
wide and unseen.

It was not empty.

It held quiet strength,  
like a vast basin  
cradling all that rested above.

The waters moved within it,  
slowly,  
rhythmically—  
rising,  
settling,  
never breaking their bounds.

A soft breath passed over them.

Not seen,  
but felt—  
like warmth  
moving through stillness.

And at its touch,  
the waters became alive.

They flowed outward,  
spreading across the land,  
nourishing what had grown dry.

Then they returned again,  
gathered back into the deep,  
held without strain.

Nothing overflowed.  
Nothing was lost.

All moved  
within a quiet measure.

And above it all,  
there was a presence—  
steady,  
unshaken—  
holding the depths  
as easily as the surface.

The deep did not resist.  
It rested.

And I understood:

What lies beneath  
is not forgotten.

It is carried,  
shaped,  
and given its place  
within a greater peace.

#### Living the Vision

There are places within you  
that feel deep and unformed.

Thoughts that linger,  
feelings that rise without warning,  
currents you cannot fully trace.

At times,  
they may seem too much—  
too wide,  
too uncertain.

You may want to contain them  
or push them away.

But you may also notice  
that they do not move without limit.

Something in you  
already holds them.

A quiet steadiness  
that does not collapse  
under their weight.

You may sense it  
in a breath that comes  
even when everything feels full.

In a moment  
when the intensity eases,  
even slightly.

This is not control.

It is a deeper holding.

You are not asked  
to understand every depth.

Only to trust  
that what moves within you  
is not beyond care.

And that, in time,  
even the deeper places  
can begin to serve life—  
flowing outward,  
and returning again  
within a quiet, sustaining rhythm.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Breathe through my depths  
>
> **Exhale:** Hold me in Your peace
