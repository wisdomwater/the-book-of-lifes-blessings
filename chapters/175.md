## Day 175 - The Waters Beneath

> *The abyss is not beyond God’s power. Even the deep places are held, ordered, and made to serve the life of creation.*

I saw that the abyss itself revealed something of the strength of the Man.

For the abyss held what had been placed upon it. It sustained what was above the earth, what was on the earth, and what was beneath the earth, according to the order God had established.

It was like the workshop of the supreme craftsman.

Within it were the hidden materials of the world, both fiery and unburning. As an artisan gathers tools and shapes many works according to skill, so the abyss held the waters, the earth, and the substance of many creatures.

Yet the abyss did not hold these things by its own power.

It held them only by the power of God.

Its depths were like the bottom of a cistern. As the floor of a cistern carries the waters above it, so the abyss supported all that rested upon it.

Then I saw that from the calves of the Man came forth a kind of air, mingled with sweat, and it moved all the waters of the abyss.

By God’s power, the air came with sweetness and made the waters fluid. It spread them here and there, sending them across the face of the earth.

The waters advanced and withdrew. They grew and diminished. Yet they did not pass beyond the measure appointed to them from the beginning.

For the Spirit of God gave the waters their moisture and their path.

They became living movement.

Without that breath, they would remain fixed and hardened in one place. They would not water the land. They would not refresh the creatures.

Then I saw that the Man pressed down with His feet the forces of the elements—those above the earth, within the earth, and beneath the earth.

By His power, God held them in their order.

He restrained them until the time appointed for their movement, so that they would not break forth in terror before their hour.

And I understood: all things are strong only because God holds them.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision contemplates the hidden order beneath creation.

The abyss is not treated as mere emptiness or chaos. It becomes a foundation, a depth, a workshop. It holds the raw materials of creation, yet it does not possess independent power. It is sustained by God.

The waters are especially important. They are not static. They are moved by breath. They flow, rise, retreat, nourish, and remain within the bounds God has given them.

Water becomes an image of divine mercy and divine order. It is alive because the Spirit moves it.

The elements, too, are held back until their appointed time. Creation is powerful, but not abandoned to itself. It remains under the wise restraint of God.

#### Living This Vision

There are hidden depths beneath every life.

Some are fertile. Some feel frightening. Some seem beyond understanding.

This vision invites us to trust that even the unseen depths are not outside God’s keeping.

The waters move because God breathes upon them. The elements remain in place because God restrains them. What is deep and powerful is not greater than the One who holds it.

When your inner world feels like an abyss, remember: God can give movement to what feels stuck. God can place limits around what feels overwhelming. God can make even the deep places serve life.
