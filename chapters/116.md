## Day 116 - Beneath the Thunder of Despair

> *I saw a woman veiled in darkness, hemmed in by sulfur and trembling before thunder. She believed only death remained, yet Hope spoke from beyond the noise.*

Then I saw the sixth image,  
a womanly figure veiled in darkness.

Her head was covered like a mourning woman,  
and the rest of her body was wrapped in a dark garment.

Before her face burned a kind of sulfurous fire.  
At her right and her left stood mountains of sulfur,  
collapsing into the darkness with a great crash.

Behind her, near her back,  
rolled the sound of terrible thunder.

At this she was terrified.  
With groaning and trembling  
she pressed her hands against her chest  
and sank more deeply into the darkness, saying:

“I am utterly afraid.  
Who could comfort me?  
Who could help me  
and pull me out of the miseries pressing upon me?

Around me is the fire of Gehenna.  
The zeal of God has cast me toward hell.

What remains for me but death?

Good deeds give me no joy,  
nor does sin.  
I find no comfort.  
I find no good in any creature.”

Then Hope answered from the storm-cloud:

“O essence of the devil,  
you are the essence of sin,  
for you neither know nor consider  
the great good that is in God.

If you seek good,  
you will not find it apart from God.  
If you pursue evil,  
no one but God will judge it.

God created heaven and earth  
and all useful things.  
Even hell is subject to His command.

All rewards come from Him.  
All judgments belong to Him.

Why do you hand yourself over to ruin  
before any judgment has yet been spoken over you?

All creatures obey the command of God.  
Only the devil refuses,  
and so he was cast down.

But no one who desires to do good  
should imagine only destruction.

God is the highest good.  
He does not leave any good work without reward.

I sit upon the throne of God  
with holy desires.  
With faith I embrace His works,  
and by good deeds  
I draw myself across the whole earth.

But you do not trust  
in any goodness of God.  
So you gather punishments you do not see  
and waste your life in childish folly.”

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals despair as a spiritual captivity in which the soul can no longer perceive goodness. It is surrounded by threat—fire before, collapse on both sides, thunder behind. Despair feels hemmed in, with no path forward and no refuge.

What is striking is that despair here finds joy neither in good nor in evil. It is beyond temptation in one sense, yet not in freedom. It has become numb, convinced that nothing can help. This is why Hope does not answer with sentiment. Hope answers with reality: God still reigns. Judgment is not yet complete. Good still exists. Reward still belongs to the One who made all things.

Hope is not denial of pain. It is refusal to make pain the final word. Despair confuses present darkness with total ruin. Hope insists that the soul must not pronounce its own condemnation before God has spoken.

#### Living This Vision

Despair can feel very convincing.

Not dramatic, always—  
sometimes just heavy.  
Flat.  
Constricted.  
As though all roads are closed.

When despair deepens,  
even prayer can feel useless.  
Even beauty can feel far away.  
Even good things lose their taste.

This vision offers a crucial word:  
do not judge your whole life from within one dark hour.

You are not the final judge of your story.

When hope feels impossible,  
do not try to manufacture bright feelings.  
Instead, take one small step that refuses the lie.

Speak one honest prayer.  
Receive one act of kindness.  
Sit near one thing that still bears beauty.

Hope is often quieter than despair.  
But it is more real.

Even when you cannot feel it,  
God has not abandoned the field.
