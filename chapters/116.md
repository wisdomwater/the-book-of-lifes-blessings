## 116 - The Quiet Beyond the Storm
A stillness that remains beneath every sound

> *The storm may surround you*  
> *but it cannot silence what is held in God*

I saw a woman  
wrapped in shadow—  
not harsh,  
but heavy,  
like a night that would not lift.

Around her,  
the air trembled.

Sound moved like distant thunder—  
rolling,  
unsettling,  
never quite arriving,  
never fully passing.

She held herself close,  
as though bracing  
against something unseen.

And yet—  
beneath the trembling,  
there was another presence.

Not loud.  
Not forceful.

A stillness  
resting quietly  
at the center of her being.

It did not push the storm away.  
It did not answer the noise.

It remained.

Like a deep place  
untouched by the surface.

Like a hidden chamber  
where the air was clear.

For a moment,  
she grew still enough  
to notice it.

And in that small noticing,  
something softened.

The thunder did not cease.  
The darkness did not vanish.

But there was  
a place within her  
that was not overcome.

And I saw  
that this place  
had never left her—  
a quiet held  
within the life of God,  
waiting  
to be found again.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
everything can feel closed in.

Not always with intensity—  
sometimes just a quiet heaviness,  
a sense that nothing is opening.

You may notice  
how quickly the mind  
begins to draw conclusions—  
about your life,  
your future,  
your worth.

As though this moment  
defines everything.

But something in you  
remains untouched.

It does not argue.  
It does not rush to fix.

It simply waits—  
steady,  
present,  
unalarmed.

You may feel it  
in a pause,  
in a breath,  
in a small turning inward  
that does not demand clarity.

You do not need  
to escape the moment.

Only to remember  
that it is not the whole.

There is a deeper place  
where your life is still held.

And when you rest there—  
even briefly—  
something begins to open again.

Not all at once.  
Just enough.
