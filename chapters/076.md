## Day 76 — The Hunger Without Measure

> *When desire has no boundary, it devours what it was meant to enjoy.*

I saw a wolf crouched low.

It watched in every direction, ready to seize whatever it could.  
Its hunger had no rest.

And it spoke:

“Whatever I desire, I will take.

Why should I hold back?  
Why should I deny what I am?

If I feel pleasure, I will follow it.  
If I have the chance to speak, I will speak.

Everything in me is natural—  
why should I change?”

It justified itself completely.  
It knew no limit.

Then the voice from the cloud answered:

“You are without restraint.

You consume without understanding.  
You move without order.”

And another voice rose, clear and luminous:

“I am Discernment.

All things have measure.  
All things are held in balance.

The sun, the moon, the stars—  
each follows its path.

Nothing exceeds its place.

I walk within this order.  
I honor what is fitting.

And in this, I grow.”

And I saw:

The wolf was driven, never satisfied.  
But discernment moved with quiet clarity—  
aligned, steady, whole.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision speaks to unrestrained desire—the belief that whatever feels natural should be followed without question.

At first glance, this seems like freedom. But without limits, desire becomes consuming.

The wolf represents instinct without reflection. It takes, devours, and moves on—never satisfied, never at rest.

Discernment introduces something essential: measure.

It asks not only “What do I want?” but also “What is fitting? What leads to life?”

This is not about suppression.  
It is about alignment.

True freedom is not found in doing everything we desire.  
It is found in choosing what is good.

#### Living This Vision

Notice your impulses.

Not to judge them harshly—  
but to observe them.

When something rises—  
a craving, a reaction, a desire—pause.

Ask:

Is this leading me toward wholeness?  
Or away from it?

You do not need to deny yourself constantly.  
But you are invited to choose wisely.

Small acts of discernment build over time.

They create a life that feels ordered,  
clear,  
and deeply alive.
