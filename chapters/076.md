## 76 - The Strength That Listens

> *What does not need to prove can receive*  
> *Peace holds firm without resistance*

I saw a wide path  
stretching through open ground.

Along it moved many voices—  
quick,  
insistent—  
pressing forward,  
seeking to be heard.

Their sound filled the air—  
rising,  
meeting,  
colliding—  
never settling.

Then,  
just beside the path,  
there was a quiet place.

No noise gathered there.

Only a still presence—  
steady,  
open—  
as if it could receive  
without being moved.

It did not step into the voices.

It did not push them away.

It remained.

And in its stillness,  
something became clear—  
a deeper ground  
beneath all the movement.

A place  
that did not need to answer  
every call.

That did not need  
to defend itself  
to remain whole.

And I saw  
that this quiet place  
was not weak.

It was rooted—  
held by something  
greater than the voices around it.

And because it was held,  
it could listen  
without losing itself.

#### Living the Vision

You feel the urge  
to respond quickly—

to explain,  
to defend,  
to correct what is said.

It can feel necessary—  
as though silence  
would mean losing something.

But you may also notice  
another possibility.

A space  
where you do not react immediately.

Where you allow words  
to pass through  
without taking hold.

You may begin  
by listening—  
not to agree,  
not to yield—  
but simply to remain present.

In that presence,  
something steadies.

You are no longer driven  
to prove yourself.

You are free  
to respond from clarity  
instead of reaction.

This is not surrender.

It is strength  
that does not need  
to rise at every challenge.

And from this place,  
when you do speak,  
your words carry  
a different weight—  
grounded,  
measured,  
true.

You are not losing your voice.

You are finding  
where it rests.
