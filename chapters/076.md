## 76 - The Strength That Listens

> *What does not need to prove can receive. Peace holds firm without resistance.*

I saw a wide path stretching through open ground, and along it moved many voices at once. They hurried over one another—urgent, restless, determined to be heard. Their words rose like wind across dry fields, colliding and scattering without rest. There was movement everywhere, yet very little stillness. Each voice seemed to fear disappearing if it did not speak loudly enough to remain above the others.

And beside this path, almost hidden by its quietness, there was another place.

No noise gathered there. No striving reached into it. It held a steady stillness that did not withdraw from the world, yet neither did it become entangled in the noise around it. The air itself seemed softer there—as though the ground had learned how to remain at peace without needing to close itself off.

What struck me most was that this quiet presence did not resist the voices moving beside it. It did not argue with them or attempt to silence them. It simply remained rooted in something deeper. And in its stillness, another kind of strength became visible—not the strength of domination or control, but the strength of being inwardly held.

I saw then that peace is not weakness. The soul that no longer needs to defend itself at every moment becomes spacious enough to truly listen. It can receive without collapsing. It can remain open without losing its center. Beneath all the noise and reaction of the world, there is a deeper ground where the heart may rest—steady, clear, and undisturbed.

And because this place is held by something greater than fear, it does not need to prove itself in order to remain whole.

**Living the Vision**

Many moments in life awaken the instinct to react immediately. A criticism, a misunderstanding, a tense conversation, a feeling of being unseen or dismissed—these things can stir the nervous system so quickly that the soul begins defending itself before it has even taken a breath. We rush to explain, correct, justify, or protect the image we carry of ourselves.

Yet not every voice requires an answer.

There is a quiet maturity that slowly grows within us when we learn how to pause before reacting. This pause is not avoidance, nor is it passivity. It is the gentle practice of remaining grounded long enough to listen beneath the surface of our emotions. Sometimes what unsettles us most deeply is not the words themselves, but the old wounds they touch.

The contemplative life teaches the soul to stay present without becoming consumed. Over time, we begin to discover that silence can hold dignity, and that peace does not disappear simply because tension enters the room. We become less driven by the need to win and more drawn toward clarity, compassion, and truth.

And when words finally do come, they emerge from a quieter place within us. They carry less fear. Less urgency. More steadiness. More light.

You are not losing your voice by becoming still.

You are learning where your true voice lives.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Hold me in stillness

**Exhale:** Keep me in peace
