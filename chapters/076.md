## 76 - The Shape of Enough

> *Desire softens when it is held in light—  
and learns the beauty of enough.*

I saw a garden at the edge of dawn.

The air was clear, touched with a gentle warmth.  
Every plant grew in its own place—nothing crowded, nothing withheld.

A small stream moved through the center, steady and unhurried.  
It did not rush beyond its banks.  
It simply followed the path given to it.

Near the water, a living presence stirred.

Not driven.  
Not restless.

It moved with awareness, pausing often, as if listening to something deeper than hunger.

When it reached for fruit, it did so with care.  
Not grasping.

Receiving.

And I saw that the garden responded.

Where there was attention, there was life.  
Where there was patience, there was abundance.

Light rested over everything—  
not to control,  
but to reveal the quiet harmony already there.

Nothing strained.  
Nothing lacked.

All was held in a gentle, unseen order.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when something rises quickly within you.

A desire.  
A pull.  
A sense of wanting more, or wanting now.

It can feel urgent.

As if following it will bring relief, or fullness.

And yet, if you linger just a moment longer,  
you may notice something else beneath it.

A quieter awareness.

It does not push.  
It does not demand.

It simply waits to be noticed.

You may begin to sense that not every impulse needs to be followed.  
And not every longing is asking to be fulfilled in the way it first appears.

Something in you already knows how to move with care.

To receive rather than grasp.  
To choose what nourishes, not just what satisfies for a moment.

This knowing is gentle.

It does not take anything from you.

It shows you the shape of enough.
