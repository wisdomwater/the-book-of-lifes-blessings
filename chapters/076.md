## 76 - The Shape of Enough

> *Desire softens in the presence of light*  
> *and remembers the grace of enough*

I saw a garden  
resting at the edge of dawn.

The air was clear—  
warmed by a light  
that did not press,  
only revealed.

Each living thing  
grew in its place.

Nothing crowded.  
Nothing reached beyond its need.

A small stream moved quietly  
through the center—  
steady,  
unhurried—  
held within its gentle path.

Near the water,  
a presence moved.

Not restless.  
Not driven.

It paused often—  
as if listening  
to something deeper  
than hunger.

When it reached for fruit,  
it did so slowly.

Not grasping.

Receiving.

And the garden answered.

Where there was attention,  
life opened.

Where there was patience,  
fullness remained.

The light rested over all of it—  
not to shape,  
but to make visible  
the harmony already there.

Nothing strained.

Nothing lacked.

All was held  
within a quiet, unseen order.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
something rises quickly within you—

a desire,  
a reaching,  
a sense of wanting  
more  
or now.

It can feel urgent—  
as if following it  
will bring rest.

And yet,  
if you remain  
just a moment longer,  
you may notice  
another presence.

Quieter.

It does not pull.

It does not insist.

It simply waits.

You may begin to feel  
a space  
between the impulse  
and your response.

And in that space,  
something in you  
becomes clear.

Not every desire  
needs to be followed.

Not every longing  
is meant to be filled  
as it first appears.

There is a way  
of receiving  
that does not grasp.

A way of choosing  
that brings quiet steadiness.

This knowing  
is already within you.

It does not take.  
It reveals.

And gently,  
it shows you  
the shape of enough.
