## 136 - The Living Crown of Care

> *Nothing is beneath your attention*  
> *What you tend becomes alive*

I saw the earth  
resting beneath a gentle light—  
rich,  
open,  
quietly full of life.

Moisture moved within it.  
Seeds stirred unseen.  
Green things rose  
without striving.

And I saw a presence  
walking there—  
not above the earth,  
but with it.

Its steps were steady,  
its gaze attentive—  
not hurried,  
not distracted.

Where it looked,  
life seemed to gather.

Not because it controlled it,  
but because it noticed—  
with a kind of care  
that allowed things  
to be as they were  
and yet more fully alive.

Around it,  
nothing was ignored.

The small was not overlooked.  
The ordinary was not passed by.

Each thing  
was held  
in quiet regard.

And I saw  
that this attention  
was like a crown—  
not worn for display,  
but formed  
by the act of honoring  
what had been given.

A dignity  
that did not separate,  
but connected.

A presence  
that did not take,  
but tended.

#### Living the Vision

At times, it feels easier  
to turn away.

To decide something  
is not your concern.  
To pass by what feels small,  
or inconvenient,  
or beneath your attention.

But you may also notice  
a quiet invitation—  
to pause,  
to look again,  
to care.

It does not need  
to be dramatic.

A listening ear.  
A gentle word.  
A small act of tending  
toward what is near.

You may feel resistance—  
a sense that your attention  
should be reserved  
for what seems more important.

And yet,  
something shifts  
when you offer it anyway.

The world becomes less distant.  
More connected.  
More alive.

You begin to sense  
that what is given to you  
is not random—  
it is entrusted.

And in caring for it,  
you are not diminished.

You are made whole.
