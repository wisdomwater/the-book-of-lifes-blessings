## Day 130 - The Cave of Burning Envy

> *I saw a mountain with a narrow mouth, and within it a cave of boiling fire, fish, vipers, cold air, burning fog, and scorpions—the inward world of envy joined to hatred.*

After this I saw other evil spirits  
crying out in the crowd:

“What glory is this?  
What kingdom is this  
that no one can seize?”

These spirits persuade human beings  
not to love the gifts of God in another,  
but only to please themselves.

They stir envy  
against the prosperity  
and good works of others.

Then I saw a great mountain  
with a narrow opening.

Inside it was a cave full of fire,  
and in the fire boiled fish.  
There also writhed a multitude of vipers.

Along the mountain  
there moved a terrible mass of icy air.  
Above it hung a burning fog,  
filled with many scorpions.

In these punishments  
were the souls of those  
who had given themselves to envy and hatred.

They passed from the mountain  
to the torments of ice,  
and from the ice  
back again to the mountain.

They burned in the fire  
because their hearts had boiled with envy  
when they compared themselves to others.

They were tormented by the fish  
because they had spoiled the good success  
and actions of their neighbors.

They suffered from the vipers  
because they had wounded others  
with sharp and hostile words.

Because hatred had joined itself to their envy,  
they endured the icy air.

Because of the cruelty of that hatred,  
they were exposed to the burning fog.

Because of their piercing ferocity,  
they felt the stings of scorpions.

And since envy and hatred had fed each other,  
their torments also passed into one another.

Then the living light spoke:

“What you see is true.  
Those who wish to avoid these spirits  
and their punishments  
must humble themselves before God.  
For through envy they exasperated many,  
and through hatred they robbed many  
of their peace.”

Then I understood:

The envious cannot bear the good  
they see in others.

They cannot rest  
when another prospers.

So they imitate those  
who saw the light of Christ’s teaching  
and tried to darken it;  
who witnessed His miracles  
and sought to obscure them  
through the injustice of envy.

Envy does no good.  
It wounds even the one who carries it.

When joined to hatred,  
it stirs all the forces of the soul  
into bitterness.

It becomes like the devil,  
who lost the brightness of heaven  
because he opposed God  
through envy and hatred.

Therefore whoever longs to serve God  
must turn away from these evils,  
lest they darken the mirror of the soul.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows envy as a closed and burning interior world. The mountain with a narrow mouth suggests a soul trapped inside its own comparisons. Envy narrows perception. It makes life feel like a cave where another person’s good becomes unbearable heat.

The boiling fire is the agitation of comparison. The fish suggest spoiled fruitfulness—what should live in water is made to boil. The vipers reveal hostile speech. The icy air shows the coldness of hatred, while the burning fog and scorpions reveal cruelty and sharpness.

Envy becomes more dangerous when it binds itself to hatred. Then it is no longer only sadness over another’s good. It becomes active hostility, the desire to diminish, wound, or rob another of peace.

The reference to those who resisted Christ shows envy at its most tragic: seeing light and wanting to darken it. Envy does not disprove goodness. It attacks goodness precisely because it recognizes it.

Charity is the healing opposite. It rejoices where God gives life.

#### Living This Vision

Envy begins with comparison,  
but it rarely stays there.

At first it aches.  
Then it judges.  
Then it speaks sharply.  
Then it secretly hopes another will fall.

This is why envy must be met early.

When you feel it rise,  
do not feed it with stories.  
Do not rehearse why the other person does not deserve their blessing.

Instead, tell the truth:  
Their good is not my loss.

Then bless them.

This may feel false at first,  
but it is a holy act of resistance.

Pray for their prosperity.  
Thank God for the gift in them.  
Ask God to show you the gift in your own life  
without needing to steal light from theirs.

The mirror of the soul grows dark  
when envy is cherished.

But it clears  
when love learns to rejoice.
