## Day 160 - The Harmful Air

> *Lukewarmness does not rage against God; it simply stops caring. Yet the soul was made for fire, not numbness.*

Again I heard evil spirits crying in the crowd:

“Who or what God is, we do not know. We know only whom we see and what we see.”

These spirits led people into lukewarmness. They urged the soul to become dull in all things.

Then I saw a dark mass of air mixed with fire. Evil spirits ran through it, striking souls with fiery clubs. These were the souls of those who, while living in the body, had chosen spiritual dullness.

They remained in the dark air because their hearts had grown lukewarm. They endured fire because they had given little thought to God. They were tormented by spirits because, in life, they had refused the honest labor of the soul.

And I saw and understood.

Then the Living Light spoke:

“These things you see are true. Those who wish to escape the spirits that persuade the soul into lukewarmness must drive it out through fasting, prayer, and service to God in honest devotion.”

Lukewarmness lives like an animal that does neither good nor evil with intention, but lies inert in laziness.

It does not fear God.
It does not love God.
It does not sound in harmony with Him.

It does not reason like a human being, nor pray to God with the breath of the soul.

It is like useless and harmful air that dries the fruits of the earth.

It says to itself, “If God exists, then God is God. He does not need my work. I want only to live fully.”

And so it neglects the good.

But great is the folly of the person who does not seek to worship and love the One who created all things, whose kingdom has no end.

Wisdom shows another way.

Wisdom teaches the soul to look continually toward God and to hold body and soul in the mirror of the heart.


#### What This Vision Means

This vice is spiritual numbness.

It is not open rebellion. It is not loud hatred. It is the slow drying out of desire.

The dark air mixed with fire reveals the condition of the lukewarm soul: dimness without peace, heat without love, movement without purpose. The soul is afflicted because it has refused the holy work of becoming alive.

The harmful air that dries the fruit of the earth is a powerful image. Lukewarmness does not always destroy through violence. Sometimes it destroys by withholding life.

It dries what could have grown.

The phrase “God does not need my work” contains a half-truth. God does not need us from lack. But love invites participation. Good works do not complete God; they awaken us.

Wisdom calls the soul to look into the mirror of the heart and remember what it was made for.

Not mere survival.

Communion.


#### Living This Vision

There are seasons when you are tired and need rest. Rest is holy.

But lukewarmness is different.

It is the refusal to care.

It says:
“Why bother?”
“It does not matter.”
“I just want to be left alone.”

This vision does not shame exhaustion. It speaks to the places where the soul has stopped responding to love.

Begin small.

One prayer.
One honest act.
One movement toward what is good.
One moment of attention instead of numbness.

You do not need to become a flame all at once.

But let one ember glow.

The soul was not made to dry out in harmful air.

It was made to breathe God.
