## 96 - The Opening of the Tower

> *What has been closed can open again*  
> *Light waits at every window*

I saw a tall tower  
rising into a quiet sky.

It stood firm—  
well-formed,  
unshaken—  
its walls smooth,  
its height reaching upward.

Near the top,  
three windows opened outward.

They looked far across the land—  
clear,  
wide,  
watchful.

And yet,  
no light moved within.

The tower held itself—  
strong,  
self-contained—  
as though it needed nothing beyond its own height.

Then,  
softly,  
light approached.

Not from above alone,  
but from every direction—  
gentle,  
patient—  
like a presence  
that had always been near.

It did not press against the walls.

It waited  
at the windows.

For a time,  
nothing changed.

Then, slowly,  
something within the tower  
began to shift.

A stillness.  
A pause.

And in that quiet moment,  
the windows opened  
just a little wider.

Not forced.  
Not broken.

Simply willing.

And as they opened,  
light entered—  
not overwhelming,  
but steady.

It filled the space  
without resistance.

And the tower,  
still standing,  
began to feel different—  
no longer closed,  
but alive.

What had been held within  
was now connected  
to something greater.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you rely on what you can hold—  
your thoughts,  
your understanding,  
your sense of control.

It can feel steady.  
Secure.

And yet,  
you may notice  
a quiet separation—  
a sense that something  
is missing.

Not because what you have built  
is wrong—  
but because it is closed.

You may begin  
with something small.

A willingness  
to not have all the answers.

A moment  
of openness  
to something beyond yourself.

You may feel it  
as a softening—  
a shift  
from holding tightly  
to allowing.

This is how the windows open.

Not all at once.

Just enough  
for light to enter.

You do not need  
to lose what is strong.

Only to let it be touched  
by something greater.

And as you do,  
what once felt isolated  
begins to feel connected—  
held within a presence  
that brings clarity,  
and quiet life.
