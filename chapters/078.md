## 78 - The Dew of Living Desire

> *Desire is not the enemy*  
> *It is the garden waiting to be tended*

I saw a figure  
lying in stillness—  
as though at rest,  
yet the ground beneath it  
felt dry,  
tired,  
unable to hold life.

Its hands moved gently,  
touching what was near—  
petals,  
leaves,  
soft growing things.

But wherever it lingered,  
the greenness faded—  
not all at once,  
but slowly,  
as though something essential  
was being drawn away.

Then,  
like morning arriving  
without announcement,  
a different presence came.

A coolness in the air.  
A quiet settling.

Dew formed—  
light,  
clear,  
resting on every surface.

It did not strive.  
It did not take.

It simply remained,  
and where it touched,  
life returned—  
softly,  
naturally,  
without force.

The earth loosened.  
The petals opened again.

And I saw  
that this gentleness  
did not diminish desire—  
it restored it.

Not as hunger alone,  
but as something whole,  
capable of receiving  
and giving  
in the same breath.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes desire feels urgent—  
pulling you quickly  
toward something to grasp,  
to experience,  
to hold.

It can feel like life itself  
depends on following it.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
what remains afterward—  
a quiet thinning,  
a sense that something  
was taken too quickly  
to be fully known.

But there is another way  
desire moves within you.

Slower.  
Clearer.  
Less demanding.

It does not rush you.  
It invites you  
to stay present—  
to feel,  
to notice,  
to remain connected  
to what is real.

You do not need  
to reject desire.

Only to let it breathe.

To allow space  
between impulse and response.

In that space,  
something softens.  
Something clarifies.

And what once felt consuming  
becomes life-giving—  
a quiet exchange  
instead of a taking.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Let my desire be gentle  
>
> **Exhale:** Restore what is living
