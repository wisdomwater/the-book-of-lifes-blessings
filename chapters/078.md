## Day 78 — The Roar Before the Wind

> *The holy roar is not chaos. It is the sound of evil being brought to its end.*

Before the Man I saw a lion.

He turned toward the vices and stood against them.  
Then he opened his mouth and gave a mighty roar.

The sound went out into the wind.

“O devilish vices,” he cried,  
“I will destroy you with burning fire.  
I will lay you waste and bring you to an end,  
for you always rise against the justice of God and against me.”

And again I heard a voice from heaven:

“God opposed the hidden darkness of the devil  
with many signs and many judgments.

He overshadowed the confusion of the vices.  
They could not endure the holiness  
of the citizens of heaven.

Looking forth from heaven,  
He strengthened His faithful ones  
in every holiness.”

Then I understood why the Man faced the West and the North.

He stood firm against the devil  
like a brave combatant.  
And in the ancient decrees of God,  
he held this purpose:

to use every instrument of salvation—  
everything spoken in the old covenant,  
everything fulfilled in the new,  
every power of good works—  
to fight the darkness that destroys  
and the cold foolishness of ignorance.

The lion’s roar was not empty noise.  
It was judgment.  
It was cleansing.  
It was the cry that sends evil backward.

And I understood that the justice of God is not passive.  
It does not merely observe corruption.  
It confronts it.

The roar moved through the wind  
like the announcement of an end already determined.

#### What This Vision Means

The lion represents holy power—divine courage, kingly judgment, and the active opposition of God to all that destroys life. The roar is a revelation that evil is not ultimate. The vices may seem numerous, organized, and persistent, but they stand under sentence.

This vision also gathers together what came before. The struggle against vice is not left to human effort alone. God has provided “instruments of salvation” across the whole unfolding story—prophecy, covenant, baptism, Christ, grace, holiness, faithful works. The Old and New Testaments are presented not as disconnected things, but as one long resistance to darkness and ignorance.

The West and North symbolize places of decline, coldness, and obscurity. The Man facing them shows divine readiness. God turns toward what threatens us. He does not avert His gaze.

The lion’s roar is fierce, but it is not cruel. It is the sound of truth breaking the spell of evil.

#### Living This Vision

There are moments when you need gentleness.

And there are moments when you need to remember that God is fierce against what harms the soul.

Not fierce against your weakness.  
Not fierce against your longing to heal.  
But fierce against the things that devour, numb, deceive, and enslave.

When you feel overwhelmed by an old pattern,  
a numbing habit,  
a lie that has lingered too long,  
picture the lion.

Let that image remind you:  
evil is not a permanent resident.  
It can be confronted.  
It can be named.  
It can be driven back.

Sometimes spiritual life is tender rain.  
Sometimes it is a roar in the wind.

You do not need to create that roar.  
You only need to stand with the One who gives it.
