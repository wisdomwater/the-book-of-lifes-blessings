## Day 101 – Fire of the Hungry Belly

> *I saw a fire that fed on desire, and within it a darker flame that burned what had been loved more than the soul.*

I beheld a great and restless fire, surging upward with violent force.  
At its center was a darker core, more terrible than the flame itself, burning with a hidden intensity.

Within this fire were souls who, in their earthly life, had given themselves over to gluttony.  
They had loved the satisfaction of the body more than the well-being of the soul.

Around them moved spirits who cast sparks into the fire, striking the souls with burning fragments.  
They cried out with bitter mockery:  
“These are the ones who chose their bodies over their souls!”

The souls writhed in the heat.  
The excess they once welcomed now surrounded them.  
The indulgence that once felt like comfort had become their torment.

The outer fire burned because of their unrestrained appetite.  
But the dark core burned more deeply—  
for it was shaped from the cunning and deception that had accompanied their indulgence.

Then I heard the voice from the living light:  
“What you see is true.  
If a person would escape such suffering, let them learn restraint.  
Let them receive food and drink with measure, not excess,  
and seek wise guidance for the ordering of their life.”

I saw that many who chose this path had made their belly into a god.  
All their thoughts, desires, and efforts bent toward filling it.

They said within themselves:  
“Why should we not take what pleases us?”

But their pleasure became their deception.  
Their fullness became emptiness.

Gluttony was like a fire fed by bellows—  
and from it, many other evils were born.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals that gluttony is not merely about food, but about disordered desire. It is the turning of attention inward toward constant satisfaction, until the body’s cravings begin to rule the soul.

The “dark core” of the fire is especially important. It represents the hidden dimension of this vice—the rationalizations, the self-deception, the quiet voice that says, *this is harmless… this is deserved… this is enough.* What begins as comfort becomes compulsion.

To make the “belly a god” is to organize life around consumption. It is not only overeating, but the deeper habit of seeking relief, escape, or identity through intake—food, drink, or any form of excess.

The enemy, in this vision, does not attack by removing food, but by distorting its purpose. What is meant for nourishment becomes a doorway to imbalance.

Yet the vision does not condemn the body. It calls for right relationship—receiving what is needed, with awareness and restraint, guided by wisdom rather than impulse.

#### Living This Vision

This vision meets us in very ordinary moments.

The second helping.  
The late-night craving.  
The quiet thought: *just one more…*

Gluttony rarely feels like rebellion. It feels like comfort. Like relief after a long day. Like something small that doesn’t matter.

But over time, it forms a pattern.  
A turning inward.  
A habit of soothing instead of listening.

To live this vision is not to reject food or pleasure.  
It is to slow down enough to notice:

Am I nourishing my body—or escaping something within me?

There is a gentler way.  
A quieter strength.

To eat with gratitude.  
To stop before excess.  
To sit with the discomfort that arises when we do not immediately satisfy desire.

Even a small act of restraint becomes a flame of clarity.

Not a harsh denial—  
but a remembering of what truly satisfies.
