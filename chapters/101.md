## 101 - The Quieting Hunger

> *What seeks to consume can be softened*  
> *True nourishment restores the soul*

I saw a fire  
rising in restless waves.

It moved quickly—  
reaching,  
pulling—  
never settling  
into stillness.

At its center,  
there was a deeper heat—  
not brighter,  
but heavier—  
like something hidden  
beneath the flame.

And around it,  
there were traces  
of what had once been given—  
simple,  
good,  
meant to sustain.

Yet here,  
they had been drawn inward,  
taken again and again,  
until their form  
was no longer clear.

Then,  
beyond the fire,  
a quiet light appeared.

It did not oppose the flame.

It remained—  
steady,  
clear—  
like a presence  
that needed no striving.

For a time,  
the fire continued.

Then slowly,  
its movement changed.

The reaching softened.

The rising slowed.

Until at last,  
what remained  
was not a consuming flame,  
but a gentle warmth—  
held,  
contained,  
enough.

And I saw  
that what had been restless  
was not meant to burn endlessly—

but to be gathered  
into a quiet, sustaining heat.

#### Living the Vision

Desire rises within you—

not loud,  
but steady—  
a pull toward something  
that promises comfort.

It may feel small.  
Familiar.

A quiet reaching  
for more.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a deeper layer beneath it—  
a subtle unrest,  
a longing  
that is not quite met  
by what you take in.

You do not need  
to push this away.

Only to pause  
and become aware.

You may ask gently:

What is it  
I am truly seeking?

In that question,  
space begins to open.

The urgency softens.  
The need loosens.

And you may find  
that a smaller portion,  
a slower pace,  
a moment of gratitude  
begins to change  
how you receive.

This is not denial.

It is clarity.

A remembering  
that what nourishes  
does not overwhelm.

And as you return  
to this quiet awareness,  
desire itself  
becomes gentler—  
no longer consuming,  
but guiding you  
toward what is enough.

> **Inhale:** I receive with gratitude  
>
