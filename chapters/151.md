## Day 151 - The Turning Wheel

> *Inconstancy promises freedom, but leaves the soul spinning. Constancy teaches the heart how to remain.*

Then I saw a form lying in darkness, like a wagon wheel.

It turned as if driven by the winds, spinning like a millstone. It had no true beginning and no true end. It was heavy with excess and burdened with vanities. It could not rest in any honorable way, but ran here and there, always searching for something new.

On its spokes were four long rods, each the height of a standing person. These showed how deeply unstable habits can become rooted in the world. People take up one way, then another. They abandon the old, chase the new, and mistake constant change for wisdom.

Among the spokes was a human-like figure.

This was Inconstancy.

It turned with the wheel, unable to remain still. It chose one thing, then abandoned it. It searched the old ways, then rejected them. It praised the new ways, then grew tired of them.

Its hair was black and curled, as if it possessed many kinds of knowledge. But this was illusion. It did not have clear wisdom, only twisted vanity.

Its front limbs were like the hands of a monkey, imitating prudence while walking in foolishness. Its feet were like the claws of a hawk, sharp with bitterness, grasping only what pleased itself.

Its garment was decorated with black and white bands. At times it appeared beautiful, as if clothed in good reasons. It claimed to act for holiness. It claimed to avoid evil. Yet beneath this show, it walked against justice.

It cast a net to catch noble souls, but caught nothing.

For stable souls remain rooted in what is good.

Then Constancy answered.

Constancy exposed the folly of this spinning life and warned that those who follow it descend into misery.

For the soul cannot be healed while it is always fleeing the place where healing begins.


#### What This Vision Means

Inconstancy is the vice of restless instability.

It is not the same as growth. Growth may require change, but it moves with discernment. Inconstancy changes because it cannot remain.

The wheel is a powerful image. It suggests motion without pilgrimage, energy without direction. The soul is active, but not fruitful. It is busy, but not faithful.

The figure’s strange body reveals imitation without substance. Monkey-like hands suggest mimicry. Hawk-like feet suggest grasping. Curled black hair suggests the appearance of cleverness without the light of wisdom.

Even its clothing is deceptive—black and white bands, as if it can dress confusion in moral language.

Constancy is the healing virtue. It does not mean rigidity. It means faithful rootedness. It is the grace to remain with what is true long enough for it to form us.

A stable soul is not easily caught by every new wind.


#### Living This Vision

Many of us know the turning wheel.

We begin a practice, then abandon it.
We make a commitment, then question it.
We search for a better path before walking faithfully on the one already given.

Sometimes this is discernment.

But sometimes it is avoidance.

The question is not, “Should I ever change?”
The question is, “Am I changing from wisdom—or from restlessness?”

This vision invites you to choose one faithful place to remain today.

One prayer.
One act of service.
One commitment.
One truth you will not abandon simply because your feelings shift.

Constancy grows quietly.

It is built by returning.

Again and again.

Not with force, but with love.
