## Day 98 – Fire in the Foundations

> *When desire consumes what is meant to be life, the soul begins to feed on its own ruin.*
> *What should nourish becomes destruction when it is turned away from God.*

I heard a sorrowful cry carried through the voice of the prophet:

“The hands of the pitiful have cooked their own children…  
and they became their food in the destruction.”

And I saw what this meant.

There were souls who, in their weakness, gave themselves over to the fire of their own desires. What had been given to them as life—the fruits of their heart, their inner goodness, their capacity for love—they consumed in the heat of unrestrained appetite.

They fed on what was meant to grow.

Their desires became their food.  
Their cravings ruled them.  
And in this, they brought ruin not only upon themselves, but upon others who were meant to flourish among the people of God.

I saw them grow confused, unable to recognize truth. They said, “We hear many things, but we do not understand. We do not know what is true.” And in their uncertainty, they turned away.

The light within them dimmed.  
The virtues that once adorned them faded.  
Joy slipped from their grasp.

Then I saw a fire rise—not from them, but against them.

It burned at the foundations of what they had built. It did not flicker—it consumed. For what had been rooted in pride and false ascent could not stand.

They had believed they could climb higher than all others, reaching a place where none could challenge them. But this height was not real.

And so it was brought low.

The fire tore through the roots of their pride, uprooting it completely. What they had trusted collapsed. What they had built was undone.

For there is no lasting foundation apart from God.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals a tragic inversion: the soul consuming what it was meant to nurture.

The image of “eating their own children” is deeply symbolic. It speaks of misdirected desire—of turning inward in a way that destroys life rather than supports it. Our gifts, relationships, and inner capacities are meant to be cultivated, not consumed by unchecked appetite.

The confusion that follows is significant. When desire governs the soul, clarity fades. Truth becomes uncertain. The person no longer trusts what is real.

The fire of God’s zeal is not arbitrary punishment. It is the unveiling of what cannot endure. Pride builds upward without roots. It seeks height without humility. But such structures are inherently unstable.

The burning of the foundations shows that what is not grounded in truth must eventually fall.

This is not merely judgment—it is reality revealing itself.

#### Living This Vision

Consider where desire may be consuming rather than nourishing.

Are there places where you are using what is good in ways that diminish it?
- relationships strained by self-centered need  
- gifts used for recognition rather than service  
- habits that take more than they give  

Pause and reflect gently.

The invitation is not to suppress desire, but to reorient it.

Let desire become aligned with life:
- choose what builds rather than depletes  
- protect what is meant to grow  
- honor what has been entrusted to you  

And when you notice pride rising—when you feel the need to elevate yourself—return to humility.

Roots matter more than height.

For what is deeply grounded in truth will endure, even when all else falls away.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:**
> Ground me in truth  

> **Exhale:**
> Keep me from ruin
