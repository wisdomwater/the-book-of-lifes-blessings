## 98 - The Clear Window

> *What is watched with care remains clear. What is received in stillness stays true.*

I saw a quiet dwelling resting beneath the softness of morning light. Its windows stood wide and open toward the horizon, receiving the day without fear or resistance. Around the house a gentle mist drifted across the land, moving slowly over fields and grasses, touching everything in its path with delicate uncertainty.

At times the mist brushed against the glass.

It gathered lightly there for a moment, soft enough to almost go unnoticed. Yet the windows remained clear. From within the dwelling a steady presence kept watch—not tense, not guarded through force, but quietly awake. There was no fear in its attention. Light filled the room completely, meeting whatever passed near without becoming dimmed by it.

The mist did not enter and settle.

It moved on.

And through the clear glass, the world could still be seen as it truly was—open, whole, and undistorted. There was no struggle between the mist and the light, no battle for dominance. Only a quiet clarity that remained because it was gently tended.

And I understood then that confusion does not arise merely because thoughts, impressions, or emotions pass through us.

It deepens when we stop noticing what we are allowing to remain.

But what is held in patient awareness slowly becomes clear again.

### Living the Vision

Every day countless things move through human awareness. Words spoken by others. Images, fears, desires, opinions, memories, anxieties, temptations, and distractions pass continually before the mind like weather crossing an open sky. Some leave little trace. Others linger quietly, shaping the atmosphere within us before we fully realize it.

The soul becomes clouded not because thoughts exist, but because attention drifts unconsciously with everything that passes through it.

The contemplative life teaches another way of seeing.

Instead of reacting immediately to every thought or emotion, we begin practicing gentle awareness. We notice what arises without needing to become fused with it. A fear appears. An impulse rises. A judgment forms. Yet beneath all of this there remains a deeper stillness capable of observing without being overtaken.

This kind of watchfulness is not harsh self-control.

It is loving clarity.

We do not need to shut ourselves off from the world or become afraid of every passing influence. We simply learn how to remain inwardly awake. We pause before allowing impressions to settle deeply within us. We notice what nourishes peace and what quietly disturbs it. Over time, this awareness creates space between ourselves and the endless movement of reaction.

And in that space, freedom begins to grow.

The soul becomes less easily scattered. Words are spoken more carefully. Responses emerge from presence rather than compulsion. Inner life grows quieter, steadier, more transparent to light.

Not perfect.

But clear enough for truth to remain visible again.

Like a window gently tended each morning, the heart slowly learns how to receive the world without losing its clarity.
