## 169 - The Open Hand of Speech

> *Words that do not grasp can give life*  
> *What is spoken in truth rests in freedom*

I saw a quiet space  
filled with soft light.

The air was clear,  
undisturbed—  
as if it had never been pressed  
or shaped by force.

In the center,  
a figure stood  
with open hands.

Nothing was held tightly.  
Nothing concealed.

And when the figure spoke,  
the words did not rush outward.

They moved gently,  
like breath on a still morning.

Each word found its place  
without strain.

None reached to capture.  
None sought to persuade.

They simply revealed—  
clear,  
unhidden,  
at rest.

And as they moved through the air,  
they did not bind or linger.

They settled softly,  
like seeds falling  
into ready ground.

Silence followed,  
not empty,  
but full.

A silence that held  
what had been spoken  
without needing more.

And I understood:

There is a way of speaking  
that does not take.

It gives,  
and then releases—  
trusting what is true  
to remain.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes words rise quickly.

A thought forms,  
and with it,  
the impulse to speak—  
to explain,  
to respond,  
to shape how something is seen.

It can feel immediate,  
almost necessary.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a quieter space  
just before the words come.

A small opening  
where nothing has been said yet.

In that space,  
something in you is free.

Free to speak,  
or not.

Free to let the moment be  
as it is.

You may begin to sense  
the difference between  
words that reach outward  
and words that rest within.

One tightens.  
The other opens.

You are not asked  
to become silent.

Only to notice  
when your words are held loosely—  
when they arise from clarity,  
and do not need to control  
what follows.

And when you speak from there,  
something softens.

Not only in others,  
but within you.

Your words no longer bind.

They become  
a gentle offering,  
given freely,  
and allowed to rest.
