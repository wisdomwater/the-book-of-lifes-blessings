## Day 10 — The Sword That Judges

> *I saw a sword that moved with living zeal,
> revealing that every human work is known before the light of God.*

After these visions I saw that the great Man who stood between heaven and the abyss held a drawn sword.

The sword was unlike any blade I had seen before.

It had three edges.

The Man grasped it firmly by its hilt and swung it from side to side, as though cutting through unseen things.

Then the sword itself spoke:

“I burn with zeal against the North and against all who dwell there.

Who can overcome me?

Who can cast me down?

No one.

For I am not made from earthly matter. No man or woman created me.

Yet I judge every work that is done.

God formed humanity from the clay of the earth. And the works of humankind stand before me as though reflected in a mirror.”

Then I heard a voice from heaven speaking.

“Who can count the works of God?

Who can number the living beings within the kingdom of God?

Who knows how many there are, what they are like, or how they speak among themselves?

Yet many people spread the darkness of division. They turn toward the North and seek their own false gods there. According to their twisted desires they examine and destroy the good things God has created.

They say within their hearts:

‘This life is good. That life is bad.’

They believe they know more than God.

But they do not know what they are doing.

The works that God performs within a human soul endure into eternal life.

So it is written by the prophet Ezekiel when he spoke of the four living creatures.”

#### What This Vision Means

The sword represents **divine judgment and truth**.

Its three edges symbolize the fullness of God’s discernment—nothing escapes its awareness. Human actions, intentions, and hidden motives are all reflected in the mirror of divine wisdom.

The sword is not forged by human hands. It belongs entirely to God. This reminds us that ultimate judgment does not come from human opinion but from divine truth.

The “North” in Hildegard’s imagery often represents rebellion, deception, and spiritual darkness. Those who turn toward it reject the divine order and attempt to replace it with their own ideas of good and evil.

Yet the heavenly voice emphasizes that God’s works in the human soul endure beyond time.

Even when confusion spreads in the world, divine truth remains active and alive.

#### Living This Vision

Modern life often encourages us to become our own final authority.

We decide what is good, what is true, and what matters. Personal opinion can begin to feel like the highest court of judgment.

But this vision reminds us that a deeper wisdom exists.

There is a truth beyond our preferences—a truth that sees clearly, like a mirror reflecting the soul.

This does not mean living in fear of judgment. Instead, it invites us into honesty.

When we allow divine truth to examine our lives, we begin to see where we are growing and where we are drifting away from what is life-giving.

The sword of truth cuts away illusion.

And in doing so, it frees the soul.
