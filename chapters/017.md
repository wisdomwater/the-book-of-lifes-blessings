## Day 17 - Held in Hidden Strength

> *All creation is carried—sustained by a strength both unseen and tender.*

I looked again upon the figure,  
and from the thighs to the knees  
He stood within the air that surrounds the earth.

There was movement here—  
a quiet sustaining.

I was shown how the body is held together.  
From the thighs comes life.  
Through the knees comes support and motion.

So too with all creation.

God bears all things—  
not distantly,  
but intimately.

He sustains them with warmth and moisture,  
with the hidden forces that nourish life.

Nothing stands on its own.

All are carried.

Then I saw a new people arising—  
born not from corruption,  
but from purity.

They were marked by a quiet strength.  
Though they endured hardship,  
they were sustained from within.

A sweetness touched them—  
not always removing suffering,  
but giving it meaning.

This came through a great mystery:

Divinity joined itself to a virgin  
by the breath of the Holy Spirit.

And in this union,  
the Word became visible in human form.

Through Him, a new law was revealed—  
not separate from the old,  
but fulfilling it.

What was once divided  
was brought into harmony.

The sound and the Word became one.  
The beginning and the fulfillment met.

And in this union,  
life was strengthened.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision emphasizes God as the sustaining force behind all existence. Just as the body depends on its structure to move and live, creation depends entirely on God’s ongoing support.

The imagery of warmth and moisture points to the subtle, life-giving presence of God—often unnoticed, yet essential.

The emergence of a “new people” reflects transformation. Through Christ, humanity is not abandoned but renewed. This renewal is marked not by the absence of suffering, but by a deeper strength within it.

The union of old and new law signifies completion, not replacement. What was begun is brought to fullness.

Everything moves toward integration.


#### Living This Vision

It is easy to feel like you are carrying your life alone—holding everything together through effort and strain. But this vision gently challenges that belief.

You are being carried.

Even in moments when you feel weak or uncertain, something deeper is sustaining you.

Pause and consider:  
*Where have I been held, even when I didn’t realize it?*

There is also an invitation here toward inner alignment. Instead of living divided—pulled between competing desires—  
you are being drawn toward wholeness.

This does not happen all at once.  
It unfolds slowly. But as you lean into what is true, you begin to feel a quiet strength emerge. Not from striving, but from being supported.

And in that support, you find the courage to continue.
