## 17 - Joy That Lifts the Heart

> *Joy does not need another’s fall. It rises from what is whole within you.*

I saw a gentle light spreading across an open space, warm and steady like sunlight resting over quiet fields in late afternoon. Nothing about it hurried or demanded attention. It simply illuminated what was already there, allowing the world to breathe more freely beneath its presence.

Within the light there was movement—small gestures, passing expressions, quick laughter carried lightly through the air. At first the joy seemed bright and effortless, rising suddenly like sparks from a fire. It danced across the surface of things, touching moments briefly before moving on again. There was energy in it, but also restlessness, as though it needed constant motion in order to remain alive.

And beneath this movement, I sensed another kind of joy waiting quietly.

It did not rise from comparison or depend upon gaining advantage over another. It was not fed by attention, victory, or the fleeting relief that comes when circumstances temporarily bend in our favor. Instead, it resembled a still pool holding light within its depths—calm, rooted, quietly radiant from within itself.

Slowly, the sharper laughter began to soften. Its edge faded. Its urgency loosened. What had once rushed outward settled gradually into something deeper and more spacious. The movement slowed. The air itself seemed to widen with peace.

And what remained was not smaller than before. It was fuller.

This deeper joy did not disappear when the moment changed. It did not collapse when external pleasures passed through open hands. It remained present beneath changing conditions, alive in a quieter and more enduring way. Gentle. Rooted. Steady enough to hold sorrow without losing itself, and spacious enough to welcome beauty without needing to possess it.

And I understood then that true joy does not come from escaping emptiness through distraction or comparison. It rises from the soul becoming reconciled to itself within love.

**Living the Vision**

There are many kinds of happiness that move quickly through the human heart. The relief of being praised. The brief satisfaction of succeeding where others failed. The rush of distraction, excitement, or momentary pleasure. These experiences are not always wrong in themselves, but they often pass as quickly as they arrive. What rises from comparison or external validation rarely remains steady for long.

Most people eventually notice this pattern within themselves. Certain pleasures bring a temporary lift, yet leave behind a subtle restlessness once they fade. The soul begins searching again almost immediately, hoping the next moment, achievement, or affirmation will finally provide lasting fulfillment.

Yet beneath these shifting experiences there is another form of joy available to us—quieter, deeper, less dependent upon circumstance.

This joy often appears in simple moments of presence. Sitting quietly beneath trees. Sharing an honest conversation. Offering kindness without needing recognition. Feeling gratitude arise unexpectedly while washing dishes or watching evening light move across a room. These moments may seem small outwardly, yet something within them feels whole rather than consuming.

Deeper joy does not require comparison because it does not depend upon proving worth. It does not need constant stimulation because it grows from connection rather than distraction. It gathers the soul inward instead of scattering attention outward endlessly.

And perhaps this is why lasting joy often arrives most fully when we stop chasing it directly. The more we become rooted in truth, presence, compassion, gratitude, and love, the more joy begins rising naturally from within us—like water from a hidden spring already flowing beneath the surface of the soul.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Lift my joy

**Exhale:** Toward what is true
