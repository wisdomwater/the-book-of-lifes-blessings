## 69 - The Garden Made Gentle

> *What has been hardened can be softened again*  
> *Justice grows where the heart becomes tender*

I saw a field  
once tangled and overgrown—  
sharp with thorns,  
dense with neglect,  
untouched by care.

The ground felt tight,  
as if it had forgotten  
how to receive.

Nothing moved freely there.  
Even the air  
seemed to hesitate.

Then,  
without force,  
a quiet rain began.

It fell lightly—  
not to overwhelm,  
but to soften.

The earth slowly opened.  
The hardened places  
began to loosen.

And beneath the surface,  
something hidden stirred—  
roots long waiting  
for gentleness.

The thorns did not vanish at once.

But their hold weakened.

And in the spaces between,  
small green shoots appeared—  
fragile,  
yet sure.

The field was not remade  
in a moment.

But it was no longer closed.

It had begun  
to receive again.

And as it softened,  
a quiet harmony returned—  
not loud,  
not complete—  
but real.

Like a garden  
remembering  
how to grow.

#### Living the Vision

There are times  
when you may notice  
a tightening within you.

A quick judgment.  
A firm stance.  
A quiet unwillingness  
to bend or listen.

It can feel justified—  
even necessary.

And yet,  
something in you  
may also sense  
a loss of softness.

A distance  
from what feels whole.

There is a gentle way back.

Not by forcing change,  
but by allowing  
a kind of inner softening.

You may feel it  
when you pause  
before reacting.

When you listen  
a moment longer  
than usual.

When you let  
another perspective  
touch you—  
even slightly.

This is how the ground  
begins to open.

You are not asked  
to correct everything at once.

Only to notice  
where you have closed—  
and to allow  
a small kindness  
to enter there.

From this,  
something just  
begins to grow again.
