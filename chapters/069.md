## Day 69 — Wings Toward the West

> *Even in the shadowed directions, the Word of God stretches its wings to guide and gather.*

I saw the Man turn.

He faced the West and the North—those regions where light fades and cold winds rise.  
Yet he did not stand unguarded.

From his shoulders extended great wings, covering his arms.  
Another wing rested upon his back, and one upon his chest.  
All were lifted, as if ready for flight.

The wing behind him curved gently toward the left, never turning to the right.  
The wing upon his chest opened in two directions—one bending left, the other right—holding a quiet balance.

And within each wing, there was a book.

On the left, I saw pages of green and silver.  
The green spoke of Noah, lifted into the ark by God’s command, carried through the waters that both cleanse and renew.  
The silver spoke of the law—once written on stone, but later inscribed within the living heart.

On the right, I saw sapphire and gold.  
The sapphire held the word spoken into Israel, falling like a seed into a people.  
The gold shone with a deeper mystery: the Word that was with God in the beginning, through whom all things came to be.

Behind him, the wing bore a single page of white stone, like marble.  
It spoke of prophecy—of visions, music, and the breath of God moving through human lives, awakening wisdom.

Before him, on the wing of his chest, there was a dark book filled with stars.  
Its words, gathered by seekers and thinkers, were drawn from hidden depths.  
They labored to understand, drawing truth like water from a well, preparing a path even in shadowed lands.

And above him, I saw a luminous cloud.

It was filled with the souls of the righteous.  
They rose in a quiet procession, moving always forward, never turning back.

A voice like distant thunder, yet gentle, resounded:  
“Give praise to the King who rules all things.”

Some souls shone openly, adorned with light.  
Others remained hidden, like reflections in a bright mirror, unknown to the world yet radiant before God.

They sang with voices like many waters.  
They waited.

“How long?” they asked.  
“When will we see fully, without veil?”

And the answer came:

Not yet.  
Not until the winds are joined,  
the serpent is undone,  
and all things are gathered into their fullness.

So they waited—  
watching, longing, and moving ever forward.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals that even in the darker directions of life—the West and the North—God’s presence remains active, structured, and purposeful.

The wings represent divine movement and protection. They hold within them the unfolding story of God’s relationship with humanity. The books are not separate truths, but layers of one continuous revelation.

The green and silver pages speak of transformation—from external law to inward knowing. The sapphire and gold reveal the deepening of that revelation, culminating in the eternal Word that underlies all creation.

The marble page at the back reminds us that God has always spoken—through prophets, through music, through the quiet stirring of wisdom. Nothing is without meaning.

Even the dark, star-filled book on the chest shows that human seeking, though imperfect, participates in this unfolding. There is an honest reaching toward truth, even in obscurity.

The souls in the cloud reveal another layer: the journey is not yet complete. Even the righteous wait. They move forward, but fulfillment is still coming.

This vision holds tension—between what is revealed and what is still hidden. It invites trust in a process that is both unfolding and incomplete.

#### Living This Vision

There are seasons when life feels like the West or the North—  
uncertain, dim, or cold.

This vision reminds you that even there, something sacred is unfolding.

You may not always see clearly.  
You may feel like you are waiting—like those souls who long for completion.

But you are still moving.

The invitation is not to rush the process,  
but to remain aligned with what is true.

Let your heart become the place where the law is written.  
Let your life be shaped by the deeper Word that holds all things together.

Even your questions—your searching, your wrestling—can be part of the path.  
Draw carefully, like one drawing water from a deep well.

And when you cannot see the end,  
remember this:

You are not standing still.

You are being carried forward,  
into a fullness that is not yet visible.
