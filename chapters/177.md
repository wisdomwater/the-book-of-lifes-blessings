## Day 177 - Fruits of Turning

> *Repentance is not shame, but return. The heart that turns toward God becomes stone made living, hardness made fruitful, sin met by mercy.*

The Man, who represents God, calls to all who have ears to hear and hearts to understand:

Do penance for your sins.

He shows mercy through many signs and wonders, so that human beings may reject unrighteous works and turn to Him without delay.

But those who ignore His warning will be purified with severity, for they despise the call of the Judge.

Therefore, whoever wishes to be faithful in the house of God should discipline the body and turn the heart toward repentance.

And I heard the words:

“Breed of vipers, who warned you to flee from the wrath that is coming? Bear fruits worthy of conversion. Do not say within yourselves, ‘We have Abraham as our father,’ for God can raise up children for Abraham from these stones.”

And I understood.

Every vice passes through the sieve of truth according to its fault.

Every soul finds its place according to its works.

Who can assign the soul its dwelling after death except God, who gives each reward?

Yet God has placed something precious in His tabernacle: the forgiveness of sins for those who repent.

The Son of God was conceived without sin, born of the Virgin, and remained innocent. Therefore the Father gave Him power to forgive sins and receive those who turn back.

But those who remain in sin and wish to continue in it are called a brood of vipers. They follow the old deception, by which Adam and Eve lost the brightness of innocence.

Who can escape the justice of God without repentance?

Not the first angel.

Not Adam.

Not any child born into sin.

Therefore, do works of faith.

Believe in God.

Come to Him with tears and prayer.

Leave behind what corrupts you.

Amend what has been broken with good works.

And say sincerely:

“Alas, Lord, before You I have sinned.”

Do not trust in inherited holiness, old belonging, or borrowed righteousness.

Only God can free you.

For the Creator can raise children of faith from the hard stones of unbelief. The Son of God is the cornerstone, and from Him living stones are made.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision brings the call to repentance into sharp focus.

Repentance is not a religious performance. It is the real turning of the soul toward God.

Hildegard warns against false security. We cannot hide behind ancestry, belonging, tradition, identity, or outward appearance. No inherited name can replace a converted heart.

The image of stones becoming children is full of hope. Even hardness can be transformed. Even unbelief can become faith. Even what seems lifeless can be made living through grace.

Christ is the cornerstone—the holy foundation from which new life is built.

#### Living This Vision

It is easy to confuse spiritual belonging with spiritual transformation.

We may rely on what we know, where we come from, what we believe, or the communities we belong to.

But repentance asks something more honest.

Where do I need to turn?

What fruit is my life bearing?

This vision does not invite despair. It invites sincerity.

God can make living children from stone. That means no hardness in us is beyond grace.

The work is to stop pretending, turn toward mercy, and let our lives begin to bear fruit.
