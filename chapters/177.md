## 177 - The Living Stone

> *What turns begins to awaken*  
> *What was hardened can become alive*

I saw a field scattered with stones—  
worn,  
weathered,  
lying still upon the ground.

They seemed unmoving,  
untouched by time,  
as if nothing could enter them.

And yet,  
a quiet light began to rest upon them.

Not bright,  
not sudden—  
but steady,  
as if it had always been near.

The stones did not resist.

They simply received.

And slowly,  
deep within their stillness,  
something began to change.

A softness—  
not on the surface,  
but from within.

As if the center of each stone  
remembered something  
it had not lost.

The light remained.

And in time,  
what had been closed  
began to open.

Not breaking apart—  
but becoming.

Where there had been only hardness,  
there was now a quiet warmth.

Where there had been no movement,  
there was the beginning  
of life.

The field did not become a garden at once.

But something living  
had begun to emerge.

And I understood:

Turning is not loss.

It is the moment  
life begins again.

#### Living the Vision

You become aware  
of something within you  
that feels fixed.

A pattern,  
a response,  
a way of being  
that seems set in place.

You may wonder  
if it can truly change.

If something so familiar  
can become different.

And yet,  
there may also be  
a small turning within you.

A quiet recognition—  
not forced,  
not dramatic—  
just a gentle willingness  
to face what is there.

You may notice  
that this turning  
does not bring shame.

It brings space.

A soft opening  
where something new  
can begin.

You are not asked  
to undo everything at once.

Only to allow  
this small movement  
to continue.

To remain present  
to what is being revealed.

And to trust  
that even what feels most solid  
is not beyond transformation.

Life does not begin  
when everything is perfect.

It begins  
when you turn  
and allow yourself  
to be met.
