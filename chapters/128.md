## Day 128 - The Face of Fire

> *At the right hand of the great Man I saw a figure with a face of fire and a garment of steel. It cried out against the vices, for the zeal of God does not bargain with evil but burns to cleanse what destroys life.*

Then I saw, at the right side of the great Man,  
another image in human form.

Its face was fire.  
Its garment was steel.

And it cried out against the vices:

“O entrails of the devil,  
torrent of wickedness,  
you who pour death into the human race  
through your mortal deceit—  
you shall be confounded  
by the blood of Christ.

You shall perish  
in Him who is Alpha and Omega,  
for you are the worst death.”

Then I understood  
that this image is the zeal of God.

It is the righteousness  
and strength of divine judgment  
appearing against sin  
to purge it and blot it out.

It appears in human form  
because man, made in the image of God  
and breathed upon with the knowledge  
of good and evil,  
must be judged by the highest and just Judge  
who weighs all things in truth.

Its face is of fire,  
because the zeal of God consumes  
all that is unjust.

Not that He burns all things with earthly flame,  
but that by His holy judgment  
He reduces evil to nothing.

Its garment is steel,  
because His justice is firm,  
strong, and unbending.

He is clothed in justice  
because He judges by nothing else.

His cry against the vices  
is the refutation of all their illusions.  
They are like the entrails of the ancient serpent,  
streams of corruption  
sending deadly darts into the hearts of men.

Yet they will be put to shame  
by the Passion of the Son of God.  
They will be cast down  
by the eternal divine judgment.  
And when death itself is finally destroyed,  
their power will be destroyed with it.

For no one can resist God  
when He reveals His righteous vengeance against evil.

His wrath is never joined to wickedness.  
It does not share in evil.  
It opposes it  
and destroys it.

And even now  
He sets His judgments  
so that sin may be exposed by justice  
and sinners be brought  
to the hard grace of acknowledging their guilt.

This happens  
when a sinner condemns his own sin in repentance,  
takes up the cross of penance,  
and with piercing tears  
confesses before the priest.

For the true High Priest,  
the Son of God,  
was offered upon the cross,  
and by the shedding of His blood  
the sins of many were washed away.

Therefore, whoever longs for life  
must take these words  
into the depths of the heart.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows divine zeal not as cruelty, but as holy opposition to what destroys life. The face of fire reveals God’s intolerable love toward evil—not because He is arbitrary, but because evil deforms what He made for glory.

The steel garment is crucial. God’s zeal is not chaotic rage. It is clothed in justice. It is measured, incorruptible, and wholly aligned with truth. This makes it radically different from human anger, which is often mixed with ego, injury, and confusion.

The cry against the vices also reveals the victory of Christ. Evil may seduce, spread, wound, and counterfeit, but it cannot survive the Passion. The blood of Christ exposes and breaks its claim. God’s zeal is therefore both judgment and rescue: it destroys sin in order to save the sinner.

The passage also turns inward. Divine judgment is not only future. It is already at work whenever a person sees sin truthfully, grieves it, and turns in repentance. In that sense, the fire of God is already cleansing those who consent to be purified.

#### Living This Vision

Many people fear the zeal of God  
because they imagine it as harsh fury.

But this vision offers another way to see it.

God’s zeal is His refusal  
to make peace with what deforms you.

He does not hate you.  
He hates what is killing you.

That means His judgment, though severe,  
is also mercy.

When conscience burns,  
when truth exposes what you would rather hide,  
when repentance becomes unavoidable—  
this may be the fire-faced mercy of God  
drawing you back to life.

Do not run from that fire.  
Let it burn what is false.

Ask not only for comfort,  
but for cleansing.  
Ask God to make you willing  
to name your sin plainly,  
to stop defending it,  
and to let His justice heal what His love has uncovered.

The zeal of God is terrifying only to what is untrue.  
To the soul that longs for life,  
it is a fierce form of rescue.
