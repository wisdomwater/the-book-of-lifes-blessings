## 70 - The Heart That Rests

> *You do not need to follow every path. There is a place in you that can remain.*

I saw a wide landscape stretching outward beneath an open sky. Many paths moved across it in every direction. Some wound gently through the hills. Some shone brightly in the distance as though promising discovery and fulfillment. Others faded slowly beyond the horizon, hidden in uncertainty and possibility. Each path seemed to whisper softly to the traveler, inviting movement toward what might yet be found.

And among them, a figure wandered.

The traveler stepped first onto one path, then another, pausing often, turning frequently, never fully settling anywhere for long. Each direction carried its own appeal, its own subtle promise. Yet with every new step, the ground beneath the feet seemed to shift again. Nothing held firmly. No path brought rest. Movement itself had become its own kind of unrest.

Then, not far from the crossing of these many roads, there appeared a quiet place.

It was a small rise in the earth where soft light rested gently upon the ground. No path pointed directly toward it. No sign announced its importance. It simply remained there—still, steady, and deeply at peace within itself.

And slowly the figure began moving toward it.

Not driven by urgency or fear of missing something else, but drawn by a quieter longing beneath all the restless searching. As the traveler approached, the endless turning began to soften. The need to keep moving eased. One step came fully to rest.

And in that stillness, the ground held. Firm. Steady. Unchanging.

The many paths still remained at the edges of the landscape. Possibilities had not disappeared. Yet they no longer pulled at the heart with the same urgency. For within the quiet center, there was already enough—a sense of being held without the need to endlessly search for completion elsewhere.

And in that resting, the heart began listening again.

Not to every voice calling outward, but to the deeper voice that had patiently remained nearby all along.

### Living the Vision

There are seasons when life opens before us with endless possibilities. Many choices, many directions, many desires compete gently for our attention. We fear missing something important if we remain still too long. And beneath this abundance of options, a quiet pressure often forms—the pressure to keep moving, keep choosing, keep searching for the perfect path that will finally bring peace.

Yet constant movement can leave the soul weary.

We turn from one thing to another hoping clarity will appear through activity itself, but restlessness often deepens when we never remain present long enough to inhabit where we already are. The mind becomes scattered across possibilities while the heart quietly longs for grounding.

There is another way of living.

Not a life without freedom or choice, but a life rooted more deeply in presence than in endless pursuit. Sometimes this begins very simply: remaining with one small truth long enough for it to steady us. A moment of prayer. A practice of gratitude. A relationship we choose to nurture fully instead of constantly looking beyond it for something better.

These small acts of remaining begin creating inner stability.

We discover that wholeness does not come from keeping every possibility open forever. Peace grows when the soul learns how to inhabit the present moment without constantly fleeing toward imagined futures. The deeper self relaxes when it no longer believes it must grasp every path in order to be complete.

And slowly, a quiet center forms within us.

A place where the heart can rest.

A place where listening becomes possible again.

And from that stillness, what is truly ours to follow begins revealing itself naturally.

### Breath Prayer

**Inhale:** Root me in what is true

**Exhale:** I rest and remain
