## Day 141 - Wings Above the Stars

> *Earthly concern clings to survival as if God were absent. Heavenly desire receives creation as grace.*

Then I saw a fifth image.

It had the shape of a man with pale hair. It stood naked in the darkness, shaped like a barrel, exposed and hollow in its concern.

And it spoke.

“What concern is more important than the concern for life?

“Where do herbs grow? Where do fruit trees, vines, and all things necessary for life come from? Where do people receive nourishment and support?

“If I shed tears, beat my chest, sighed deeply, or bent my knees, I still would have neither food nor clothing. I would die.

“If I cried out to heaven and asked the sun, moon, or stars for what I need, nothing would come of it.

“So I will acquire everything I can by thought, word, and deed, so that I may live upon the earth.”

Then the voice from the storm-cloud answered.

“O predator of souls, what are you saying?

“Your mind is false, because you do not trust in God, who gives what is needed. Just as the body cannot live without the soul, no fruit of the earth grows without the grace of God.

“Look at the bones of the dead lying in their tombs. Consider what they can do. Nothing. They lie in decay.

“So you also do nothing but live in carelessness, because you choose to live without the grace of God. You do not seek God in your worries.

“But I dwell in the heights of heaven. By the grace of God, I find what I need in creation.

“I am life and vital force in every good work. I am a jewel among the virtues. I am the joy and understanding of the love of God. I am the root of holy desire.

“With the wings of good will, I fly above the stars of heaven. I fulfill the will of God. I climb the mountains of Bethel, where I behold the works of God face to face.

“I desire nothing except what is holy.

“I am psalmist and harpist for the joy of God. I am psalter and zither of divine joy. I belong to heavenly things.”

#### What This Vision Means

Earthly concern is not the same as responsible care. We all need food, clothing, shelter, and provision. But this vice reveals anxiety that has forgotten grace.

It believes survival depends entirely on its own grasping. It cannot imagine that creation itself is sustained by God. It sees herbs, fruit, vines, and labor, but not the hidden mercy beneath them.

Heavenly Desire does not reject the earth. It receives the earth differently. It sees creation as gift rather than possession. It works, but without worshiping its work. It seeks what is needed, but does not let need become its god.

The image of wings above the stars reveals a soul lifted by holy longing. Desire is not destroyed; it is purified. It rises from anxious acquisition into joyful participation in God’s will.

Heavenly Desire teaches the soul to trust the Giver within the gift.

#### Living This Vision

Worry often sounds reasonable.

It says, “I am only being practical.”  
“I have to take care of myself.”  
“No one else will provide.”

Sometimes these concerns are real. But when worry becomes our master, we begin to live as if God were absent.

This vision invites us to bring our needs into prayer without abandoning action.

Work, but do not worship work.  
Plan, but do not bow before fear.  
Receive what is needed, but do not let your soul become a storehouse of anxiety.

The earth still grows by grace.

Even today, beneath your efforts, something holy is sustaining you.
