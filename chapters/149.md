## 149 - Hands That Come Alive

> *What is alive reaches outward*  
> *What is rooted begins to give*

I saw a figure  
folded in on itself—  
quiet,  
still,  
as though it had forgotten  
how to move.

Its hands were hidden.  
Its steps were unseen.

It rested in a kind of calm,  
but the calm felt thin—  
like a surface  
without depth beneath it.

Nothing flowed from it.  
Nothing grew.

Then,  
nearby,  
I saw another presence.

Simple.  
Steady.

Its hands were open—  
not strained,  
not hurried—  
just ready.

It moved gently,  
touching what was near—  
lifting,  
preparing,  
offering.

Where its hands moved,  
something changed.

Small things came into order.  
What was scattered  
began to gather.

There was no urgency,  
only a quiet faithfulness—  
as though each small act  
carried a hidden life within it.

And I saw  
that this movement  
did not come from pressure.

It came from within—  
from a place already alive.

The hands did not force life.

They revealed it.

#### Living the Vision

There are times when  
you may feel withdrawn—  
not only physically,  
but inwardly.

A reluctance to act.  
A sense of distance  
from what needs doing.

It can feel like peace,  
but something in you  
may also recognize  
that it is not quite alive.

There is a gentle invitation here.

Not to do more—  
but to begin again  
with something small.

You may notice  
a quiet nudge—  
to reach out,  
to respond,  
to care in some simple way.

You do not need  
to summon great strength.

Only to allow  
your hands  
to come forward again.

One small act  
is enough to begin.

As you move,  
something within you  
begins to awaken.

Not all at once,  
but quietly—  
like life returning  
to a place  
that had grown still.

You are not meant  
to remain folded in.

You are meant  
to take part  
in what is living.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Awaken what is within  
>
> **Exhale:** Let my hands bring life
