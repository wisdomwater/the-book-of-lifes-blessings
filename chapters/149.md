## Day 149 - Wisdom’s Strong Hands

> *The soul that resists indolence becomes like a faithful woman—strong, gentle, and full of holy work.*

Then I saw the second image, the vice of spiritual dullness.

It followed Injustice, because when justice is neglected, the heart grows dim. The mind no longer fixes itself on God. It becomes clouded, lazy, and careless.

This figure had the face of a child and the hair of age. It was immature and weary at once. It did not seek discipline. It did not search with wisdom. It wanted only what was useful to itself.

It wore a pale tunic. Beneath it, its arms and hands were withdrawn. Its feet and body were hidden, so that its full shape could not be seen.

For those who give themselves to this dullness cover over the strength meant for good works. Their hands grow idle. Their footsteps vanish. They do not walk the path of righteousness, and so no trace of holy virtue can be found.

They say they want a quiet life.

But it is not peace.
It is numbness.

Then Fortitude answered this vice. Fortitude urged the soul not to sink into idleness, but to rise and serve—to help others in soul and body, and to use the hands for what is useful and good.

And Wisdom spoke through the words of Scripture:

“Who can find a strong woman? She is more precious than pearls.”

This woman is wisdom joined to strength.

She knows when to be gentle like wool and when to be firm like linen. She works with diligent hands. She gathers provision from afar like a merchant ship. She feeds those who are weary and prepares what is needed for the journey.

Wisdom does not sleep in laziness.

She protects the children of humanity, so they do not stand naked before God.

She gives food for the soul, so the faithful do not faint along the way.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision contrasts spiritual indolence with active wisdom.

The vice appears childish because it refuses maturity. Its white hair suggests weariness, but not holy age. It has lived long enough to know better, yet still avoids the work of transformation.

Its hidden hands are especially important. Hands symbolize action, service, and responsibility. When the hands disappear, the soul’s strength is unused.

Fortitude answers by calling the soul back into movement—not frantic busyness, but faithful usefulness.

Then Wisdom appears as the strong woman of Proverbs. She is not passive. She is discerning, practical, generous, and tireless in love.

Wisdom knows that contemplation and action belong together.

She looks toward God, but her hands remain open to the world.

True peace is not numb withdrawal.

It is strength made gentle, and gentleness made fruitful.


#### Living This Vision

There is a kind of tiredness that needs rest.

And there is a kind of tiredness that becomes avoidance.

This vision helps us discern the difference.

Sometimes we say, “I just want peace,” when what we really mean is, “I do not want to be disturbed.” But love often disturbs us. Growth asks something of us. Wisdom calls the hands back into service.

You do not need to do everything.

But you are invited to do the next faithful thing.

Wash the dish.
Answer with kindness.
Pray for the person you resent.
Take the walk.
Make the call.
Begin again.

Small works can become holy when they are done with love.

Wisdom is not far away.

She is found by those who keep seeking, even when the road feels long.
