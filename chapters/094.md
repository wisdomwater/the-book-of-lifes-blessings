## Day 94 – The Restless Sorrow

> *A soul cut off from God cannot find lasting joy, no matter how it searches.*
> *It moves from thing to thing, yet remains empty within.*

Then I saw a figure marked by deep sorrow.

Its skin was like that of a leper, set apart and afflicted. Its hair was black, heavy with the weight of its condition. Though it appeared human, it was separated from the life and beauty that belonged to God.

This was Unhappiness.

It stood apart from all that was good. It did not shine. It did not rejoice. It lived in the shadow of its own choices, drawn deeper into darkness by repeated turning away from God.

It wore no true garment. Instead, it covered itself with broad leaves—layer upon layer, shifting and unstable. These were the vanities it clung to, trying to find comfort, trying to create happiness through constant change.

But none of it satisfied.

Its hands struck its chest again and again, as though searching within itself. And what it found was sorrow—memories of its actions, the weight of its choices.

It had no hope in God.

It sighed.  
It grieved.  
It endured a quiet despair.

Yet even here, a voice stood against it—the call of true happiness, inviting the soul to turn back, to seek what endures.

But this figure remained restless, unable to find peace.

#### What This Vision Means

Unhappiness here is not simple sadness—it is a deeper spiritual condition.

It arises when the soul becomes disconnected from its source. Without God, the soul seeks fulfillment in external things, moving from one desire to another.

The leaves represent this instability. They are temporary coverings, constantly changing, never providing true shelter.

The striking of the chest reveals awareness. This soul is not unaware—it feels its condition. It recognizes its emptiness, but does not yet know how to return.

The lack of hope is central. Without hope, the soul remains trapped in its cycle, unable to lift its gaze toward something greater.

Yet the presence of the opposing voice shows that the path back is always open.

#### Living This Vision

If you feel a deep restlessness—an inability to feel satisfied—this vision speaks gently to you.

Notice where you are seeking fulfillment:
- constant change  
- new distractions  
- temporary comforts  

These may soothe for a moment, but they cannot sustain the soul.

Instead, turn inward—not to dwell on sorrow, but to become aware.

What is your soul truly longing for?

Allow yourself to sit with that question.

And even if hope feels distant, take one small step toward it:
- a quiet moment of stillness  
- a simple prayer  
- an act of gratitude  

Happiness is not found in constant movement.  
It is found in returning.
