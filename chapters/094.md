## 94 - The Warmth That Comes Near

> *You do not need to become strong first. Love comes close and makes you whole.*

I saw a great light rising gently across the horizon. It did not remain distant or unreachable, but moved near enough to be felt—like morning slowly unfolding across the earth after a long night. Its coming was steady and unhurried. It touched everything in its path without strain, offering warmth and brightness simply because it was its nature to do so.

Nothing called it down.

The light came freely, carrying no demand for worthiness or preparation. It rested upon the world with quiet tenderness, and wherever it touched, what had grown cold began to soften. Frozen places loosened. Dim places brightened. What had long remained hidden slowly emerged into visibility—not exposed with harshness or shame, but held gently within a compassionate radiance.

And I saw that the light moved especially toward the small and overlooked places.

It entered low ground where shadows lingered longest. It reached fragile edges where life seemed weakest. Nothing beneath its gaze was dismissed as unimportant. Nothing was passed by because it appeared broken, unfinished, or unready.

The light did not wait for perfection before drawing near.

It came because love moves toward what needs warmth.

And in its nearness, life began again.

Not all at once.

But enough for hope to awaken.

Enough for the soul to remember that healing often begins simply through being met with tenderness where we truly are.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when weakness feels difficult to bear. We become aware of our limitations, our exhaustion, our uncertainty, our inability to hold everything together the way we wish we could. The heart quietly concludes that transformation must wait until we become stronger, wiser, more disciplined, more spiritually mature.

And so many people spend their lives believing they must fix themselves before they are worthy of love.

But the deeper spiritual life reveals something very different.

Grace does not arrive only after healing is complete. Often grace is what begins the healing. Love moves toward weakness not to condemn it, but to sustain it. The soul does not become whole by hiding its fragile places. It becomes whole by allowing those places to be touched gently and truthfully.

The contemplative path invites us to stop resisting our own humanity long enough to receive what is already near.

Sometimes this nearness appears quietly: a moment of unexpected peace, a compassionate conversation, a small beauty that reaches the heart, a sense of being accompanied even in confusion, a prayer whispered without certainty yet somehow heard. These moments may seem small, but they carry immense tenderness within them.

We do not need to force transformation into existence.

Often the deepest changes happen through sustained nearness to love itself.

Like frozen ground softening beneath the warmth of morning light, the soul gradually opens where it once remained closed. What seemed lifeless begins to stir again. Fear loosens. Shame softens. The heart remembers it does not need to become worthy before it can be loved.

Love comes near first.

And in that nearness, healing begins.

### Breath Prayer

**Inhale:** Draw near to me

**Exhale:** Warm what is weak
