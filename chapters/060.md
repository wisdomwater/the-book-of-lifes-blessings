## Day 60 — The Smoke of Empty Laughter

> *What promises delight without depth leaves the soul wandering in shadow.*

I saw a black fog, thick and shifting, and within it a great fire that sent up heavy smoke. The air was clouded, unclear, as though everything had lost its brightness.

In this dimness, souls were held.

They moved within fire and smoke together, neither fully burning nor fully seeing. Their vision was obscured. Their breath was burdened.

These were the ones who had given themselves to vain and unbridled fun.

In life, they had chased laughter without truth. They delighted in excess, in constant amusement, in pleasures that shifted and changed with every passing desire.

Now that same inconstancy surrounded them.

The fog closed in.

The smoke confused them.

And the fire touched them, because they had warmed themselves by these fleeting joys.

Again, the Living Light spoke:

“What you see is real. But those who turn away from this while they live—those who discipline their desires, who refuse excess, who choose what is fitting—these may be spared such suffering.”

Then I saw something deeper.

Spirits moved among humanity, whispering of delight, pretending to sing the harmony of heaven. Yet their song was hollow. It could not endure.

For true joy is a song that does not end.

But vain amusement cannot sustain itself. It must always seek more, louder, different.

It becomes a mirror that reflects nothing true.

And those who follow it lose themselves in what fades.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision is not a rejection of joy, but of emptiness disguised as joy.

Vain fun is pleasure disconnected from truth. It is amusement pursued for its own sake, without depth, without grounding, without connection to what is real and lasting.

The fog and smoke symbolize confusion. When life is filled only with distraction and surface-level delight, clarity fades. The soul loses its sense of direction.

The fire represents the cost—subtle at first, but real. A life spent chasing fleeting pleasures leaves the soul unsatisfied and searching.

In contrast, true joy is stable. It is rooted in something deeper than momentary feeling. It aligns with truth, with goodness, with God.

The vision invites discernment: not all joy is the same.


#### Living This Vision

There is nothing wrong with laughter.

But ask yourself: what kind of laughter fills your life?

Is it restful? Grounded? Connected?

Or is it constant, restless, always needing something new?

Modern life offers endless distraction. Entertainment is always within reach. It is easy to fill every quiet moment with noise.

But the soul needs space.

It needs silence.

It needs depth.

Consider gently stepping back from excess. Not as a rejection of joy, but as a way of rediscovering it.

Choose what nourishes rather than what merely occupies.

Let your joy become quieter, deeper, more rooted.

There, you will find something that does not fade.
