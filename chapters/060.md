## 60 - The Gentle Waiting

> *Even fullness leans toward more. Love waits with quiet trust.*

I saw a soft radiance resting in deep stillness. It was peaceful and whole, bright with a quiet beauty that seemed untouched by fear or striving. Nothing there appeared empty or lacking. The light carried the fullness of something already complete within itself, like a place where all things rested safely in harmony.

And yet within that fullness, there remained a gentle turning.

It was not restless or anxious. No tension disturbed the stillness. Instead, there was an openness within the light itself—a quiet reaching toward something not yet fully revealed. Faces lifted softly, not in confusion or desperation, but in calm expectancy, as though they were listening for music already beginning to rise beyond the horizon.

They waited together within the same steady peace.

Their radiance did not diminish through waiting; it deepened. And within them moved a holy longing, tender and alive, like the breath drawn inward before it is released again. Nothing was hurried. Nothing was withheld. There was only the quiet certainty that what was already beautiful would continue unfolding into greater beauty still.

It felt like dawn before the fullness of sunrise.

Like a promise already present, yet still arriving.

And I saw then that this waiting was not emptiness at all. It was trust—gentle, patient, and unwavering. A resting within the assurance that what is meant to unfold will come in its proper time.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons when something within us feels unfinished. We sense a longing we cannot fully name—a quiet awareness that life holds depths not yet fully revealed. Sometimes this longing appears as a desire for healing, clarity, intimacy, peace, or a deeper sense of wholeness. The soul leans toward something more, even when it cannot clearly see what it seeks.

And often, our first instinct is to rush toward resolution.

We want certainty quickly. We want answers that remove ambiguity and fulfillment that quiets every ache. Waiting can feel uncomfortable because it asks us to remain open without immediate completion. Yet not all longing is a sign that something is wrong. Some longings are woven into the very movement of spiritual life itself.

There is a sacred kind of incompleteness that keeps the heart awake.

It draws us forward gently, stretching the soul beyond complacency and inviting us into deeper trust. The longing itself becomes part of the relationship between the soul and the Divine—not a punishment, but a form of participation in something still unfolding.

And so we learn another way of waiting.

Not with fear or grasping, but with quiet confidence. We discover that hope does not require constant certainty to remain alive. Like seeds resting beneath winter soil or dawn slowly brightening the horizon, much of what matters most grows invisibly before it fully appears.

The spiritual life often asks us to remain faithful to what we cannot yet completely see.

To stay open without demanding control.

To trust that what is unfolding in hidden ways will arrive when the soul is ready to receive it.

And slowly, waiting itself becomes peaceful.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** I wait in trust

**Exhale:** I rest in what will come
