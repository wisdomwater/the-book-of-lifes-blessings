## Day 24 - The Unsearchable Light

> *God’s brilliance cannot be contained—He is known, yet never fully grasped.*

I lifted my gaze toward His face—  
and I could not fully behold it.

It shone with such brilliance  
that my sight could not endure its fullness.

This was no ordinary light.

It was living fire—  
burning with goodness,  
radiant with justice.

And I understood:

No one can fully search God.  
No one can comprehend His depths.

There is no other like Him.

His works cannot be matched.  
His power cannot be measured.

From Him come the angels—  
burning and alive,  
carrying forth the light of His mysteries.

And these mysteries are without number.

They fill the heavens.  
They move upon the earth.  
They extend even into the hidden places below.

Everywhere, His works unfold—  
yet never exhaust who He is.

For God is not contained  
even by what He has made.

He remains beyond,  
even as He is present.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision emphasizes the transcendence of God. His nature is not fully accessible to human understanding. Even the attempt to “look upon His face” reveals the limits of perception.

God is described as both fire and light—symbols of purity, power, and life. Yet this fire is not destructive alone; it is illuminating and sustaining.

The countless mysteries flowing from God show that creation itself is an expression of divine depth, yet never a complete representation of it.

To know God is not to fully comprehend Him. It is to encounter what is real, while recognizing that it always exceeds our grasp.


#### Living This Vision

There is a desire within you to understand—to make sense of God, of life, of everything you experience. This desire is good. But it has limits.

There are moments when you will come close to something true—and yet feel that you cannot fully grasp it. Instead of frustration, this can become wonder. Ask yourself:

*Can I allow God to be greater than my understanding?*

You are not required to contain God in your thoughts. You are invited to encounter Him. Sometimes, the most honest response  
is not explanation—but awe.

Let yourself rest there.

You do not lose anything by not knowing everything. You gain space for something deeper.
