## 24 - The Breath of What Is Living

> *What is alive sustains you from within. You do not have to create your own life.*

I saw a quiet lifting within the soul, a gentle turning away from dimmed and weary edges toward something unseen yet unmistakably present. There was no form to grasp with the hands, no image clear enough to contain within thought. And yet, what I encountered there felt more real than anything visible—nearer than breath itself, steady as the pulse quietly moving beneath all living things.

This presence did not flicker or fade according to whether it was noticed. It did not struggle to sustain itself through human attention or effort. It simply remained—giving life continuously, holding creation together with a tenderness so constant it often passed unseen. Like air filling the lungs without demand, it offered itself freely and without exhaustion.

Around this living nearness I saw many other forms rising within the world. Some were carefully shaped, polished with meaning, strengthened by certainty and control. They appeared solid and dependable at first glance. People leaned upon them heavily, trusting them to carry the full weight of their lives—plans, identities, possessions, ideologies, achievements, even carefully constructed versions of themselves.

Yet when I looked more closely, these forms held no life within them.

They could not respond. They could not breathe. They could not nourish the soul beyond appearances. And those who clung to them completely began slowly taking on the same stillness—not the stillness of peace, but the heaviness of disconnection from the deeper current moving beneath life itself.

Then my gaze returned again to the unseen presence.

And immediately I felt it once more: living, spacious, quietly overflowing with life that could not be diminished or contained. It was not distant above the world, hidden behind unreachable heavens. It moved gently within and around all things, nearer than thought, sustaining even the breath that searched for it.

Always giving life.

Always enough.

### Living the Vision

There are many things people reach toward in order to feel secure. Plans. Routines. Accomplishments. Certainties. Roles. Control over outcomes. None of these are inherently wrong. In healthy measure they can help us navigate life wisely and responsibly. Yet the soul suffers when it begins depending entirely upon what was never meant to carry the full weight of human longing.

Often we do not realize how exhausted we have become until something cracks beneath us—a plan fails, an identity shifts, certainty disappears, or circumstances move beyond our control. Suddenly we discover how much effort it has taken to keep ourselves feeling stable through things that cannot truly sustain us forever.

And beneath this exhaustion, there may come a quieter invitation.

Not necessarily to abandon everything outwardly, but to loosen what we have made ultimate. To stop asking temporary things to provide the kind of life only deeper presence can give. The soul slowly learns that peace does not come from securing complete control over existence. It comes from remembering that we are already being held within a life larger than our own striving.

This awareness often returns in simple moments. A full breath taken slowly. Silence at the end of a difficult day. Sunlight through trees. The realization that even while we were anxious, life continued carrying us moment by moment. Something within us softens when we stop trying to manufacture our own existence through constant effort alone.

The Sacred does not ask us to create life from nothing. Life is already being given continuously.

And perhaps this is one of the deepest forms of rest available to the human soul: realizing that beneath all striving, something living has been sustaining us all along.

### Breath Prayer

**Inhale:** You are my life

**Exhale:** I rest in You
