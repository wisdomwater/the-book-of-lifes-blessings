## 24 — The Breath of What Is Living

> *What is alive sustains you from within*  
> *You do not have to create your own life*

I saw a quiet lifting—

a turning of attention  
away from the dimmed edges  
toward something unseen,  
yet deeply present.

There was no form  
to grasp,  
no shape  
to hold.

And yet,  
there was life—

nearer than breath,  
steady as the pulse  
beneath everything.

It did not flicker.  
It did not depend  
on being noticed.

It simply was—  
giving,  
sustaining,  
holding all things  
in a gentle continuity.

Around it,  
I saw many forms—

carefully shaped,  
polished,  
given weight and meaning.

They seemed solid.  
Defined.

But when I looked closer,  
they did not move.

They did not respond.

They held no life  
within them.

And those who leaned  
fully upon them  
grew still in the same way—

not at rest,  
but disconnected  
from the deeper flow.

Then my gaze returned  
to the unseen presence—

and I felt it again:

a living nearness,  
uncontained,  
unfading.

Not far above,  
but quietly within  
and all around.

Always giving life.

Always enough.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments you reach for something  
to steady you—

a plan,  
an idea,  
a sense of control  
that feels solid.

And for a time,  
it may seem to hold.

But something in you  
still feels the strain—  
as if what you are holding  
cannot quite carry  
what you need.

You may notice  
a quiet invitation  
beneath this—

not to let everything go,  
but to loosen  
what you have made ultimate.

To feel again  
what is already sustaining you.

You do not have to  
create your own life.

You are already  
being held within it.

You may sense it  
in a breath,  
in a pause,  
in a moment  
when you stop striving  
to secure yourself.

And in that space,  
something living  
meets you—

not distant,  
not fragile,  
but steady.

You can rest there.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** You are my life  
>
> **Exhale:** I rest in You
