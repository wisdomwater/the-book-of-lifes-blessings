## 173 - The Hidden Green

> *Even what feels barren can awaken*  
> *What is held in sorrow is not beyond life*

I saw a quiet grove  
in the dim light before morning.

At its center stood a tree—  
bare,  
still,  
its branches reaching without leaf or color.

It seemed to hold nothing.

And yet,  
as I watched,  
a soft light began to gather  
around its form.

Not from above alone,  
but from within the air itself—  
like a presence  
gently surrounding what appeared empty.

The branches did not move.  
The stillness remained.

But deep within the wood,  
something unseen stirred.

A faint greening—  
not yet visible,  
but alive.

The light lingered,  
patient,  
unhurried.

It did not force the tree to bloom.  
It simply remained near,  
as if reminding it  
of something it had not lost.

And slowly,  
almost imperceptibly,  
the hardness of the bark  
seemed to soften.

The tree was not yet full.  
Not yet restored.

But it was no longer closed.

It was being held  
in a quiet returning.

And I understood:

What feels lifeless  
may still carry  
a hidden life  
waiting to awaken.

#### Living the Vision

There are times when everything feels heavy.

When your thoughts return  
to the same place—  
a sense of absence,  
of distance,  
of something missing that you cannot name.

You may feel as though  
nothing is growing.

As though something in you  
has gone still  
and will not move again.

And in those moments,  
it can be hard to see  
anything beyond that weight.

But there may be  
a quieter awareness  
just beneath it.

Not loud,  
not insistent—  
but present.

You may notice it  
in a small pause,  
a breath that comes  
without effort,  
a moment that is not entirely empty.

Something in you  
is still alive there.

Not fully seen,  
not yet expressed,  
but real.

You are not asked  
to force yourself into light.

Only to notice  
what has not gone out.

And to remain,  
even gently,  
with that small presence.

In time,  
it may begin to open  
on its own.
