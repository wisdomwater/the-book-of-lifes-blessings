## Day 173 - Bound in Sorrow

> *When the soul forgets the goodness of God, even light appears as darkness. Despair binds what was meant to live freely.*

Then I saw the fifth image.

She appeared as a woman, but she was entangled completely in the branches of a dry and lifeless tree.

The tree had no leaves.

Its branches wrapped around her head, her neck, her arms, her body, her legs. Her hands hung downward, shaped like claws. Her feet had become like the wood of the tree itself.

She wore no garment but these bindings.

Around her, evil spirits moved through a foul black mist. She bent toward them, groaning.

And she cried out:

“Why was I created?

Why do I live?

Who will help me?

God has done nothing for me.

If He is real, why does He hide from me?

I see no good. I know no joy.

I was born in misery, and I live without consolation.

Why should I continue?”

And her voice was heavy with despair.

Then from the storm came a response:

“You do not see what is before you.

Look at the sun, the moon, the stars. See the beauty of the earth.

God has given much, yet you call it nothing.

You call day night.

You call blessing a curse.

You gather sorrow upon sorrow, because you refuse to see what is good.

But I rejoice in all that God has made.

Even in sorrow, there is hidden joy.

Even in joy, there is humility.

As day and night alternate, so do the experiences of life.

You are blind to this truth.”

And I understood the distortion of despair.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the sorrow that turns inward and becomes despair.

The dry tree represents lifelessness—hope cut off, vitality gone. The branches binding the woman show how despair entangles the soul, limiting movement and vision.

Her words reveal a deeper blindness. Not that suffering is absent—but that goodness is no longer perceived.

The response does not deny hardship. Instead, it restores perspective: life contains both joy and sorrow, and both have purpose.

Despair isolates. It narrows vision until only pain remains.

But truth widens the view again.

#### Living This Vision

There are seasons when life feels heavy.

When it is hard to see anything good.

When despair whispers that nothing will change.

This vision does not condemn those feelings—but it gently challenges their finality.

Is there anything, even small, that is still good?

Can you hold both sorrow and gratitude at the same time?

Even a small shift can begin to loosen the branches.

You are not as bound as you feel.
