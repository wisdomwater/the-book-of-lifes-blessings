## Day 21 - The Boundary of Knowing

> *There are edges the mind cannot cross—places where only God remains.*

I saw again the deep abyss—vast, silent, unreachable.

And I understood this:  
just as no one can descend to its end,  
so no one can know what will come  
after the fall of the son of perdition.

There is a boundary set for human knowing.

I heard the voice of Wisdom:

*“The height of heaven, the breadth of the earth, the abyss—who can measure them?”*

Then I beheld the world as a great sphere—  
held together by fire, wind, and air,  
filled with living creatures.

Above it stretched the heavens in their brilliance,  
far beyond the reach of human sight.

Below and around it lay the earth,  
broad and steady,  
wrapped in flowing waters.

And beneath, the abyss—  
deep and filled with hidden things.

No one could grasp it.

No one could reach its end.

Only God.

I saw that humanity lives within this vastness,  
like a small part within a greater whole—  
as the heart lives within the body,  
yet does not contain the whole of it.

So it is with creation.

All creatures exist within the power of God,  
yet none can comprehend Him.

He is beyond measure.  
Beyond containment.

And yet—  
He holds all things.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision emphasizes the limits of human understanding. The height of heaven, the breadth of the earth, and the depth of the abyss represent dimensions of reality that cannot be fully measured or grasped by human thought.

We live within creation, not above it. Our perspective is inherently partial. Like the heart within the body, we are vital, yet limited in scope.

The reference to the “son of perdition” and the unknown future underscores that even the unfolding of history has boundaries beyond human knowledge. God alone sees the whole.

This is not meant to diminish humanity, but to rightly place it. There is freedom in recognizing that we are not required to understand everything.

God’s transcendence does not remove His presence—it frames it.


#### Living This Vision

There are questions you may carry about the future—about what is coming, how things will unfold, or how certain events will resolve. It is natural to want clarity. But this vision invites you to release the need to know everything. Ask yourself:

*Where am I trying to see beyond what I’ve been given to see?*

There is a quiet peace that comes  
when you accept your limits. You are not responsible for the whole story. Only for your place within it. Instead of striving to grasp the future,  
you can rest in the One who already holds it.

You are not lost in uncertainty. You are held within a reality that is fully known by God.
