## 21 - The Light Within Knowing

> *Understanding rests in a deeper light and becomes clear without striving.*

I saw wide wings opened in perfect stillness, extending outward with quiet strength and gentleness. They did not beat against the air in an effort to rise higher or escape the earth below. Instead, they remained open like shelter—holding carefully what rested within them, protecting something sacred without enclosing it in fear.

Inside each wing rested a book.

The books were neither fully closed nor entirely opened. They seemed suspended in a state of patient readiness, as though waiting to be received in the proper time rather than forced into immediate understanding. Nothing within them demanded interpretation. They simply remained there, held within the quiet shelter of the wings, available to the one willing to approach slowly enough to see.

A soft light rested upon the pages.

On one side shimmered a gentle green radiance, like new life emerging from the earth after long winter rains. Beside it glowed a pale silver clarity, reflective and calm like still water before sunrise. On the other side rested a deep blue light, vast and quiet as the evening sky stretching beyond sight. And beside that, a warm gold radiated steadily—unchanging, grounded, full of hidden warmth.

The pages themselves did not speak through ordinary words. Their meaning unfolded differently, through presence rather than argument, through quiet illumination rather than forceful explanation. Nothing strained to be understood before its season had come. Everything rested within a patient clarity that allowed truth to ripen naturally.

And I understood then that the light did not arise from knowledge alone.

It came from what held the knowledge.

A wisdom deeper than thought itself, yet moving gently through thought like light through clear water. This wisdom did not overwhelm the mind or discard it. It illuminated slowly, revealing only what the soul was ready to receive in love, humility, and peace.

### Living the Vision

There are seasons when the mind longs intensely for understanding. We want clarity about our lives, our relationships, our pain, our future, our purpose. Questions rise naturally within us, and often we feel pressure to resolve them quickly—as though uncertainty itself were a failure we must overcome.

Yet the deeper movements of wisdom rarely unfold through force.

The mind can become exhausted when it tries to grasp everything immediately. Endless analysis, overthinking, and striving for certainty often leave the soul more restless rather than more grounded. We may gather information endlessly while still feeling inwardly disconnected from understanding itself.

There is another kind of knowing available to us—quieter, slower, more spacious.

This deeper wisdom often appears when we allow questions to remain open for a time rather than rushing to close them prematurely. We begin learning how to observe, listen, reflect, and remain present without demanding instant resolution. In these moments, insight sometimes arrives gently and unexpectedly: through silence, through lived experience, through honest conversation, through grief, through beauty, through prayer, through the slow unfolding of ordinary days.

This is not the rejection of reason. It is the healing of reason from the burden of trying to control every mystery.

The mind was never meant to carry truth alone. Understanding becomes fuller when it is held within humility, patience, intuition, compassion, and the quiet wisdom of the heart. Over time, what once felt distant or confusing begins to settle more deeply within us—not only as an idea we can explain, but as a lived knowing woven into the way we move through the world.

And perhaps this is the hidden grace within all genuine wisdom: that it arrives not merely as information, but as light slowly illuminating the whole of our being.
