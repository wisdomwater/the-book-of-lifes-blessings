## Day 79 — Wings Full of Hidden Books

> *What God has hidden is not absent. It is ripening toward revelation.*

Then I understood more deeply the mystery of the Man who faced the West and the North.

Each shoulder bore a wing that covered his arms.  
One wing was upon his back.  
Another was upon his chest.  
All were lifted, as though ready to fly.

And I understood that the strength of the divinity and humanity of the Son of God  
is a protection that never fails.

In that strength, God preserves all things.  
And within that same strength are hidden works—  
decrees not yet fully shown,  
mysteries still kept in silence.

For even though God works wonders every day,  
not everything has yet been made plain.

So too with the thoughts of a person:  
they are hidden within  
until they come forth in action.

The wing at the back signified the mysteries hidden before the birth of the Son of God.  
Ancient prophecies were veiled.  
They stood guarded in obscurity.

But now teachers draw from them  
as from a deep well.  
They search the hidden wisdom  
and bring forth what God allows to be known.

Still, they never empty the well.

For the wisdom of God cannot be exhausted.

And all the wings were lifted for flight,  
because the secrets of the old and new covenant  
are shown to the faithful  
not merely to admire them,  
but to practice them in good works.

The rear wing bent toward the left, not the right.  
And I understood:  
the old law defended the needs of those still bound to earthly things,  
those living according to the flesh.

But the wing upon the chest divided itself in two—  
one part bending left, the other right—  
for in the new covenant  
what was once hidden unfolds more fully.

What was once earthly and external  
now opens toward heaven.

What was once marked in circumcision  
is now renewed in baptism.

What was once written outwardly  
now moves inwardly and glorifies the soul.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the continuity and deepening of God’s work across time. The wings symbolize divine protection, but also divine revelation—truths carried, guarded, and gradually unfolded.

The back wing represents what was hidden and preparatory. The chest wing, opened in two directions, shows fulfillment and expansion. What was once external, partial, and bodily now moves toward inward transformation and spiritual life.

This does not mean the old was false. It means it was incomplete by design. It carried the seed of what would later flower more fully.

The image of the well is especially beautiful. Teachers draw from divine wisdom, but never drain it. God’s mysteries are not a puzzle to be mastered once and for all. They are a depth to be entered again and again.

This vision invites humility before revelation. We are given real truth, but never the whole ocean at once.

#### Living This Vision

You do not need to understand everything at once in order to live faithfully.

Some truths come early.  
Others unfold slowly.  
Some things that once seemed external or distant  
become inward and alive only after years of walking.

Let that be all right.

Draw from the well you have been given today.  
Take what is enough for obedience now.  
Let deeper understanding ripen in its season.

There is also comfort here:  
God’s hiddenness is not abandonment.

What you cannot yet see  
may still be under the shelter of His wing.

Stay teachable.  
Stay patient.  
Stay willing to let old meanings open into new life.

The wings are raised for flight.  
What is given to you is meant to become lived reality.
