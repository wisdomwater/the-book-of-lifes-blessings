## 79 - The Golden Thread Within

> *Nothing in you stands outside His care. Even what rises too high is gently bounded.*

I saw a figure lifting itself upward, reaching toward a height it could not sustain. There was brightness around it, but the light trembled and flickered, as though it depended upon being noticed in order to remain. For a moment it rose beautifully, full of motion and intensity, yet beneath its striving there was instability—as though it feared disappearing if it ceased reaching higher still.

Again and again it stretched upward, only to waver beneath its own effort.

And within the dimness surrounding this restless movement, I noticed something quieter.

A thread appeared—fine as gold woven into shadow, glowing softly with a steady light. It did not strain upward with the figure, nor did it react to the rising and falling around it. It simply remained where it was, calm and unchanging, tracing a hidden path through the midst of all unrest.

The figure continued its movements—reaching, collapsing, gathering itself once more. Yet the thread held its place. Nothing disturbed it. And slowly I understood that this thread was not a barrier meant to humiliate or confine. It was mercy.

A gentle boundary woven into the soul itself.

It preserved what striving alone could not sustain. It held open the possibility of return. Even when the figure lost itself in restless elevation, the thread remained nearby—quietly guiding the way back toward what was real.

And I saw that Divine Love does not abandon us when we drift into illusion. It remains present beneath our instability, holding together what fear and pride threaten to scatter.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when something within us begins reaching upward in unhealthy ways. We may long to feel important, exceptional, admired, spiritually advanced, or somehow beyond ordinary weakness. Often these movements arise quietly, hidden beneath sincere intentions. The soul begins comparing itself, measuring itself, searching for confirmation that it matters.

At first this can feel energizing—even beautiful. Yet anything built upon comparison eventually becomes fragile. When identity depends upon recognition, the heart grows anxious beneath the surface. Peace begins to depend on maintaining a certain image, and the soul slowly loses contact with its deeper ground.

But beneath all of this movement, there remains something steady.

The contemplative life teaches us to notice the quiet thread of truth within us—the place that does not need to prove itself in order to be loved. This deeper self does not rise and fall according to praise or failure. It rests in something more enduring than appearances.

And when we notice ourselves drifting into pride, fear, or self-importance, we do not need to collapse into shame. The path back is often very small. A breath. A moment of honesty. A soft relinquishing of the image we were trying to protect.

Grace does not disappear because we became lost for a while.

The thread remains.

And every gentle return strengthens our trust that we were never outside the reach of mercy to begin with.
