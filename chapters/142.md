## Day 142 - Dew of Repentance

> *Obstinacy refuses to ask. Repentance opens its mouth like dry earth receiving dew.*

Then I saw the sixth image.

It had the shape of a buffalo, heavy and hardened. It spoke with a stubborn voice.

“For me, there is no excess and no defect in anything. When I affirm something, I cannot speak with meekness or gentleness.

“If the earth were always soaked with rain and had no hardness, it would produce nothing. Its fruits would not ripen. If it were too soft, the waters would run over it and destroy it.

“Everything wounds me. Therefore I am not tender.

“A sudden shower can harm the earth. And if I do not sigh, so be it. If I do not weep, it means nothing to me. Many die from sadness. Many are exhausted by crying.

“God gives what he wants to give. Why should I struggle? Why should I labor for what I may never receive? It benefits no one to seek what cannot be found.”

Then the voice from the storm-cloud answered.

“What are you, bitter creature? You say you do not want to grow tired in life, yet birds, fish, wild animals, cattle, worms, and creeping things all labor for food.

“Even young creatures seek nourishment from their mothers. The earth receives its vital force from the air.

“Why is God called Father, except that when his children call upon him, he gives good things by grace, and they recognize him as God?

“Why then do you fight against God?

“I drink from the dew of his blessing. In repentance, I smile. With joyful words in the midst of tears, I say, ‘God, help me.’

“And the angels answer me with the sound of the organ. They praise God because I call upon him.

“Then the dawn of grace shines upon me, and God gives the food of life, because I sought it and did not want to be without strength.

“But because you ask nothing, nothing will be given to you.”

#### What This Vision Means

Obstinacy is hardened self-protection. It fears tenderness because tenderness can be wounded. It refuses tears because tears feel weak. It refuses to ask because asking admits need.

The buffalo shape reveals force without openness. It is strong, but not receptive. It can stand its ground, but it cannot receive grace.

Repentance answers with a surprising image: joy in the midst of tears.

Repentance is not despair. It is not self-hatred. It is the humble opening of the soul toward God. It says, “Help me,” and in that asking, the dawn begins to rise.

The dew of blessing is gentle. It does not flood the soul. It moistens what has become dry. It softens what has become hard. It restores life where pride has sealed the ground.

The angels rejoice not because the soul is perfect, but because the soul has turned.

#### Living This Vision

Sometimes we become hard because we have been hurt.

We stop asking. We stop crying. We tell ourselves we are being realistic. But beneath the realism, there may be fear—fear that no one will answer, fear that need will make us small.

This vision invites a simple prayer.

“God, help me.”

That may be enough.

Repentance does not require dramatic emotion. It begins wherever the heart opens, even slightly. A sigh can become prayer. A tear can become dew. A small turning can become dawn.

Do not fight God by refusing your own need.

Ask, and let grace find the dry places.
