## 142 - The Dew That Softens

> *You do not need to stay hardened*  
> *A small asking is enough to begin*

I saw a wide stretch of earth—  
dry,  
firm,  
holding itself closed.

Nothing moved within it.  
Nothing entered.

It was strong in its stillness,  
untouched,  
unaffected—  
yet unable  
to receive.

Then,  
in the quiet of early light,  
a softness came.

Not rain,  
not force—  
but dew.

It settled gently,  
resting on the surface  
without demand.

At first,  
nothing changed.

Then,  
slowly,  
the ground began to loosen.

Not breaking,  
not collapsing—  
but opening,  
just enough  
to receive what was given.

And where it opened,  
life stirred.

Not all at once—  
but quietly,  
as though something  
had been waiting  
for this very moment.

And I saw  
that the dew  
had always been near—  
waiting  
for the earth  
to welcome it.

#### Living the Vision

There are times when  
your heart feels closed.

Not in anger,  
but in protection—  
a quiet resistance  
to being vulnerable,  
to admitting need.

You may tell yourself  
that you are being strong,  
that you are simply  
accepting what is.

And yet,  
something in you  
remains untouched—  
unwatered,  
unmet.

There is another way  
that begins very small.

Not with effort,  
but with honesty.

A simple word:  
Help me.

You do not need  
to force emotion.  
You do not need  
to feel anything deeply.

Only to allow  
a small opening—  
a willingness  
to not hold everything alone.

You may notice  
how something softens  
when you do.

Not all at once—  
but enough  
to let something new  
begin.

You are not weakened  
by asking.

You are made open  
to what can restore you.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Soften what is closed
