## Day 30 – The Sound of True Judgment

> *When heaven beholds the will of God, it cannot remain silent—it becomes song.*

As I watched, the angels fixed their gaze upon the great board of divine mystery.  
They did not look casually.  
They leaned in with attention, as those who know they are standing before something holy.

And then, from within them, a sound began.

It was not a single note.  
It was not one voice rising above the rest.

It was like a trumpet—loud, clear, and filled with power.  
Yet within it, there were many tones, woven together in perfect unity.

A melody formed.

Not scattered, not chaotic—  
but whole.

I saw that this sound did not come from effort, but from seeing.  
As they beheld the will of God, something in them was filled—  
strength upon strength,  
joy upon joy.

And this fullness became praise.

They did not hesitate.  
They did not question.

They sang because they could not do otherwise.

Their song declared what they knew to be true:

That all the judgments of God are righteous.  
That nothing in Him is distorted.  
That every decision flows from truth itself.

Their praise did not strain to convince.  
It rested in certainty.

It was as though the very structure of reality resonated through them—  
and they gave it voice.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals that true praise flows from clear seeing.

The angels do not praise God out of obligation or fear. They praise because they perceive the reality of who God is. Their song arises from alignment with truth.

The image of music—many tones forming one melody—reflects harmony. Each voice remains distinct, yet contributes to a unified expression. This is what happens when beings are fully aligned with divine will.

To say that God’s judgments are “true and righteous” is not merely a statement of doctrine. It is a recognition that divine justice is not arbitrary. It is rooted in truth, love, and perfect understanding.

This vision suggests that confusion and resistance often come from limited perception. The more clearly we see, the more naturally trust and praise arise.

The angels do not force themselves to believe.  
They see—and in seeing, they rejoice.

#### Living This Vision

There are moments when life feels confusing or unfair.

You may question what is happening.  
You may struggle to trust.

This is human.

But this vision gently invites another posture—not blind acceptance, but openness.

What if your current understanding is only partial?

What if, over time, something deeper could become visible?

You do not have to pretend certainty.

But you can remain willing to see.

And perhaps, even now, you can find one small place—  
one moment, one breath—  
where something good is still present.

Let that be your beginning.

Praise does not always start as a song.

Sometimes, it begins as a quiet willingness to trust.
