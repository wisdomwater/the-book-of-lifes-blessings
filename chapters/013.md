## 13 — The Cry of Truth

> *Truth does not strive to rise*  
> *It stands, and all else settles*

A vast brightness stirred—  
not sudden,  
but ancient,  
as if it had always been  
just beneath the surface of things.

All around,  
light moved in quiet harmony.

Nothing strained.  
Nothing reached beyond its place.

Each presence rested  
in what it was given—  
whole,  
complete,  
at peace within the order of love.

Then, a subtle tension appeared.

Not loud.  
Not forceful.

But a reaching—  
a movement upward  
without root.

Like a shadow stretching  
toward a height  
it could not sustain.

For a moment,  
it seemed to rise.

And then—  
it thinned.

It could not hold.

Into the stillness,  
a sound emerged.

Not harsh,  
but clear.

Steady as truth itself.

A single cry  
woven through everything:

Who is like God?

The words did not push.  
They did not fight.

They revealed.

And in that revealing,  
what was not true  
fell away.

Not cast down—  
but unable to remain.

The light did not change.

It simply remained  
what it had always been.

And within that constancy,  
all things found their place again.

#### Living the Vision

Something in you wants to rise.

To be known.  
To be certain.  
To be seen as more  
than what is quietly forming.

You may feel the pull  
to move ahead of your own becoming—  
to claim what has not yet  
taken root.

It can feel urgent.

Convincing.

And yet,  
there is another movement  
you may begin to notice.

Slower.  
Truer.

A gentle returning  
to what is already real.

You may sense it  
in small moments of honesty—  
when you allow yourself  
to stand where you are,  
without reaching beyond it.

Nothing is lost here.

In fact,  
something steadier begins to grow.

You do not need to lift yourself  
into fullness.

You are invited  
to remain  
in truth.

And from that place,  
what is real  
naturally rises—  
not as effort,  
but as expression.

A quiet alignment  
that does not need to prove itself.

Only to be.
