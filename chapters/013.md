## Day 13 - The Cry of the Heavenly Host

> *The Lord rises like a warrior—not in rage alone, but in the fierce protection of what is good and true.*

Before time began, I beheld the Living One who brought forth all creation in strength and wisdom.  
All things came from Him, not scattered or uncertain, but whole and good, filled with the hidden seed of life.  

Nothing was lacking.  
Every virtue was woven into creation like threads of light.

Yet I saw another stirring.

There were those who, before learning to stand, sought to climb.  
Before being given a place, they tried to sit in honor.  
Their striving was empty—like shadows reaching for substance.  
And their pride, though it rose high for a moment, dissolved into ruin.

Then a great fire awakened.

I saw the angelic hosts blaze with living flame.  
Their voices rose like a storm wind, strong and unyielding.  
For the enemy had attempted to cover the heavens with darkness.

But he could not.

With a cry that shook the heights, they shouted:

“Who is like God?”

This cry was not only sound—it was truth itself.  
And in that truth, the enemy fell.

He was cast into darkness, stripped of light and joy.  
Yet even in his fall, I saw him prepare again—  
fitting arrows to his bow, seeking another battle.

Then I saw the wonder:  
God would not only conquer from heaven.

He would create a being in His own likeness—  
and through this living image,  
He would wage war again against deceit and shadow.

And so the struggle continues,  
not only above, but within the world—and within the human soul.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals that creation began in harmony, filled with divine intention and goodness. Nothing was made incomplete. Yet pride introduces disorder—not as a creative force, but as a distortion. It reaches before it is ready. It grasps without being given.

The fall of the enemy is not merely a distant cosmic event. It is the pattern of all pride: a desire to rise without grounding in truth. Such striving cannot endure, because it is not rooted in God.

The cry of the angels—“Who is like God?”—is the turning point. It is not only a declaration, but a realignment of reality itself. Truth exposes illusion. Light dissolves false elevation.

Most striking is that God chooses to continue this victory through humanity. The battle is no longer confined to heaven. It is carried into human life, where truth and deception meet daily.

Thus, each soul becomes a place of decision: whether to echo the cry of heaven or the grasping of pride.


#### Living This Vision

There are moments when something in us wants to rise too quickly—to be seen, affirmed, or established before the time is right. We reach for recognition. We claim understanding we have not yet lived.

This is the quiet echo of that ancient pride.

But the way of God is different. It is rooted, patient, and real. It does not rush upward. It grows.

When you feel that inner striving—pause. Ask gently:  
*Am I reaching, or am I receiving?*

The angels did not defeat darkness through force alone. They stood in truth.  
You are invited into that same posture.

Each small act of humility becomes a victory.  
Each honest step grounds you more deeply in what is real.

And in those moments, whether you realize it or not,  
you join the cry of heaven.
