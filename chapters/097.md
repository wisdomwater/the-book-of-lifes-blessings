## 97 - The Gentle Fire Within

> *The fire that sees you also loves you. What is brought into light is already being healed.*

I saw a quiet horizon just before dawn, where darkness had begun to thin and the first breath of light moved softly across the edge of the world. In that stillness there rested a circle of fire suspended in the air. It did not rage or spread wildly across the landscape. Its flames were steady and whole, alive with a quiet radiance that neither consumed nor threatened what came near.

The fire breathed gently along its edges.

Its movement felt almost tender—rising and falling like living breath, illuminating without violence. At the center of the fire there was complete clarity. Nothing hidden. Nothing obscured by shadow or confusion. And yet the light carried no harshness within it. There was no sense of accusation, no demand for perfection. Only an invitation to be seen truthfully.

Nearby stood a figure wrapped in shadow.

It did not appear trapped there, nor was it dragged unwillingly toward the fire. It simply waited, uncertain, as the steady light remained near without forcing itself forward. Then slowly, with a movement almost too small to notice, the figure turned and stepped closer.

Not all at once.

Just enough to allow the light to touch what had long remained hidden.

And where the light met these hidden places, it did not destroy them. It clarified. It softened. It loosened what could no longer remain once truth was welcomed. The fire itself did not grow more intense. It simply revealed what the soul had already become ready to release.

And what remained within the light stood lighter than before—more open, more honest, no longer afraid of being fully seen.

**Living the Vision**

There are places within every person that quietly resist being brought into the light. Old wounds, hidden fears, shame, compulsions, griefs, self-deceptions, unresolved longings—these parts of us often remain concealed not because they are beyond love, but because we fear what might happen if they are fully seen.

So the soul learns how to move around them carefully.

We distract ourselves. We stay busy. We keep certain truths hidden even from ourselves. Yet beneath all avoidance there often remains a quieter longing: the longing to live without hiding.

The contemplative life does not expose us in order to humiliate us.

It reveals us gently so healing can begin.

This is why honesty matters so deeply in spiritual life. Not harsh self-condemnation. Not endless self-analysis. Simply the willingness to become present to what is actually there. A moment of stillness. A prayer spoken truthfully. A compassionate acknowledgment of what the heart carries beneath the surface.

And often, when we finally allow these hidden places into awareness, we discover something surprising:

The light meeting us there is gentler than we expected.

Instead of rejection, there is spaciousness. Instead of destruction, there is release. The soul begins loosening its grip on what no longer serves life because it no longer needs to protect itself through hiding. What is false slowly falls away not through force, but through sustained nearness to truth.

This is the quiet work of healing.

Not becoming someone entirely different overnight, but allowing the light of love to reach places that have long remained unseen.

The fire that reveals you is not separate from the fire that loves you.

And what enters the light is already beginning to become free.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** See me in Your light

**Exhale:** Free what is not true
