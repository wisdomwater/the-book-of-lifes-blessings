## 75 - The Lightened Heart

> *What feels heavy can be lifted*  
> *Mercy loosens what has been bound*

I saw a dense weight  
settled over a quiet place.

It pressed downward—  
not violently,  
but steadily—  
like something long carried  
without rest.

The ground beneath it  
felt burdened,  
as though it had forgotten  
how to breathe.

Nothing moved easily there.

Then,  
a soft light began to appear—  
not from above alone,  
but from within the space itself.

It did not push the weight away.

It touched it.

Gently.

And where it touched,  
something began to change.

The heaviness loosened—  
not all at once,  
but enough.

Small openings formed—  
spaces where the weight  
no longer held as tightly.

And through those openings,  
the ground softened.

The air lightened.

Movement returned—  
slow,  
but real.

And I saw  
that what had been pressing down  
was not meant to remain.

It could be lifted—  
not by force,  
but by a quiet release.

By a turning  
toward something softer.

Something more open.

And the light remained—  
patient,  
steady—  
restoring ease  
where there had been strain.

#### Living the Vision

At times, something within you  
feels heavy—

a tightening,  
a closing,  
a quiet hardness  
that makes it difficult  
to respond with care.

You may notice it  
in how you see others—  
quickly,  
without space—  
as if there is no room  
for understanding.

And yet,  
there is a gentler way  
that is always near.

You may begin  
with a simple shift.

A pause  
before reacting.

A willingness  
to look again.

Not to excuse—  
but to see.

In that small movement,  
something begins to loosen.

The weight lightens.

The heart softens.

You may not feel it  
immediately.

But over time,  
these moments gather—  
opening space  
where there was once pressure.

You are not asked  
to carry everything.

Only to release  
what was never meant  
to remain.

And as you do,  
your inner world  
becomes lighter—  
freer to move  
in kindness.
