## Day 75 — The Weight of Sorrow

> *When the soul clings to despair, it forgets how to receive light.*

I saw a figure like a leper.

Its body was bare, covered only with wide leaves.  
It struck its own chest again and again.

And it spoke:

“What hope is there for me?  
What life remains but pain?

There is no help.  
Only sorrow.  
Only loss.”

Its voice sank into itself,  
as if it could see nothing beyond its grief.

Then the voice from the cloud answered:

“You cling to sorrow  
and do not seek anything else.

You do not call out to God.  
So how will you receive anything?”

And another voice rose, bright and alive:

“I am Joy.

I call, and I am answered.  
I seek, and I find.

I trust in the goodness of God.

And so I sing—  
like a zither before Him.”

This joy was not shallow.  
It was rooted in trust.

It rested in something deeper than circumstance.

And I saw:

Despair closed in on itself.  
But joy opened outward—  
toward something greater.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision distinguishes between sorrow and despair.

Sorrow is human. It moves through us and can even deepen us.

But despair becomes a dwelling place. It convinces the soul that nothing good remains—that no help is possible.

The figure’s repeated lament shows a mind trapped in its own conclusions.

Joy, in contrast, is not denial of pain. It is trust in something beyond it.

It seeks.  
It asks.  
It remains open.

This openness allows grace to enter.

The vision suggests that despair is not only about circumstances—it is about whether the soul still reaches outward.

#### Living This Vision

There are moments when everything feels heavy.

In those moments, you do not need to force happiness.

But you can choose not to close completely.

Try this:

Name one small good thing.  
One small light.

Reach for it—not as escape,  
but as connection.

You can also ask—quietly, simply:

“Help me.”

That is enough.

Joy often begins not as a feeling,  
but as a direction.

A turning.

And even the smallest turning  
can begin to open the way.
