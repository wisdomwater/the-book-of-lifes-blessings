## 75 - The Lightened Heart

> *What feels heavy can be lifted. Mercy loosens what has been bound.*

I saw a great heaviness resting over a quiet landscape. It did not arrive with violence or noise. Instead, it pressed downward slowly and steadily, like a burden carried for so long that it had become woven into the very atmosphere of the place. The ground beneath it seemed tired, compressed beneath the weight, as though it had forgotten what it felt like to breathe freely.

Nothing moved easily there.

The air itself felt dense and strained. Every motion carried effort within it. It was the kind of heaviness that settles gradually over the soul—quiet enough to go unnoticed at first, yet powerful enough to make tenderness and openness feel difficult over time.

Then a soft light began to emerge.

It did not descend only from above, nor did it force itself into the darkness. The light seemed to rise gently from within the very space burdened by the weight, as though something hidden beneath the heaviness had never fully disappeared. It approached the burden without aggression. It simply touched it.

And where the light touched, something began to loosen.

Not all at once.

Not dramatically.

But enough.

Small openings appeared where the weight no longer held so tightly. The ground beneath it softened. The air grew lighter. Space returned where everything had once felt compressed. And slowly, movement began again—tentative at first, but real.

And I saw then that the heaviness had never been meant to remain forever.

What burdened the landscape could be lifted—not through force or denial, but through gentle release. Through turning toward what softens instead of what hardens. Through allowing mercy to enter places long bound by tension and strain.

And the light remained there patiently, restoring ease where weariness had once settled deeply into the earth.

**Living the Vision**

There are times when the heart begins carrying a hidden heaviness. Sometimes it forms through old hurt, disappointment, resentment, fear, or exhaustion. Sometimes it appears as judgment—toward others, toward ourselves, or toward life itself. The soul grows tight around its pain, and slowly tenderness becomes more difficult to access.

We may not even realize how much we are carrying.

The heaviness can shape the way we speak, the way we react, the way we see other people. We begin responding quickly, defensively, without space for understanding or compassion. Everything feels more compressed, more pressured, more difficult to hold gently.

Yet mercy is always nearer than we think.

Not as denial of truth, but as a softer way of seeing it. Sometimes the shift begins with something very small: a pause before reacting, a willingness to look again, a moment of curiosity instead of immediate judgment. These small openings matter deeply because they create space where light can begin entering what has grown hardened within us.

Over time, the heart begins to loosen.

Not because pain is ignored, but because it no longer needs to be carried in the same way. Compassion gradually releases what bitterness once held tightly. Understanding softens what fear tried to protect. The soul learns that it was never meant to carry endless heaviness alone.

And slowly, life becomes lighter again.

Not perfect.

But freer.

The heart remembers how to move gently through the world once more.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Open my seeing

**Exhale:** Let mercy move
