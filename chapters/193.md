## 193 - The World Made Bright

> *What is hidden will be brought into light*  
> *What is wounded will not remain broken*

I saw the world  
held within a great stillness.

The horizon stretched in every direction,  
reaching toward the farthest edges of the earth.

And through all of it,  
a living presence moved—  
quiet,  
vast,  
holding everything within itself.

Nothing was outside that movement.

Mountains, waters, fields, and skies—  
all seemed to awaken  
as it passed.

What had been dim  
began to brighten.

What had carried long shadows  
slowly filled with light.

And near the center,  
I saw a radiant creature  
white as morning—  
gentle,  
strong,  
untouched by fear.

It rested close to the living presence  
without resistance,  
as if all purity  
naturally returned there.

Then the air itself  
seemed to clear.

The heaviness that had covered the world  
lifted like ash carried away by wind.

The earth did not disappear.

It became luminous.

And all things once hidden  
stood openly in the light—  
not exposed in shame,  
but seen clearly  
and made whole.

And I understood:

Nothing true is lost.

All that belongs to life  
is being drawn  
toward renewal.

#### Living the Vision

There are times when life feels unfinished.

You may notice  
how much remains unclear—  
old wounds,  
unfinished healing,  
parts of yourself  
still waiting for light.

At times,  
it can feel as though  
the world itself carries this heaviness.

And yet,  
something deeper continues to move.

You may sense it  
in quiet moments of hope—  
small glimpses  
that not everything is lost,  
that goodness still remains,  
that healing is still unfolding.

You are not asked  
to complete the work yourself.

Only to remain open  
to the light already coming.

There is a renewal  
moving through all things,  
even where you cannot yet see it.

What feels dim  
will not remain dim forever.

What feels divided  
is being gently gathered.

And as you trust this,  
even softly,  
you may begin to feel  
a deeper peace—  
not because everything is finished,  
but because love  
has not stopped restoring  
what it has made.
