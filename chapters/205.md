## Day 205 - The Voice Before Time

> *The One who speaks is before all beginnings—the source, the breath, the fire within all things.*

Then I heard a voice.

It came softly, like drops of balm falling upon the soul—gentle, steady, and filled with quiet authority.

It said:

“I am the power of divinity before eternity and before time. I have no origin.

I am the strength by which all things were formed and known. I am the mirror through which all is seen in its truth. I am the Word that sounded when all began.”

And I understood:

This was the voice through whom all things came into being.

“I spoke,” the voice continued, “and all things came forth. I divided time and ordered what is and what shall be. I breathed life into the form made from earth, and I clothed it in living flesh through the Spirit of fire.

I entrusted creation into human hands.

And I watched.”

The voice grew deeper.

“When humanity was deceived by the serpent, I descended.

Like a flame, I entered the Virgin. I took on flesh untouched by corruption. I came forth in strength, to overcome what had been broken.”

I saw a great turning.

The voice spoke of restoration—of water washing, of fire purifying, of a great wheel set in motion to return humanity to its original beauty.

“I established order,” it said. “I gave boundaries, union, restraint, and devotion. I formed paths for those who live in the world, and for those who step beyond it.

I completed my work against the ancient serpent.

Though it does not yet fully know its defeat, it will be brought to confusion.”

And the voice rested in certainty:

“I restore the glory of paradise to those who turn toward me in truth.”


#### What This Vision Means

This passage reveals the identity of the divine Word—the eternal presence that exists before time and sustains all creation. It speaks as both creator and redeemer, emphasizing continuity: the one who formed humanity is the same one who restores it.

The imagery of breath, fire, water, and the “wheel” reflects an ongoing process of transformation. Creation is not static—it is dynamic, guided, and continually being brought back into alignment with its original purpose.

The serpent represents deception, fragmentation, and the misuse of human freedom. The response of the divine is not abandonment, but descent—entering into the human condition to restore what was lost.

The “wheel” symbolizes divine order in motion: laws, relationships, disciplines, and spiritual paths all serve the purpose of healing and reorientation.

This vision presents salvation not as escape, but as restoration—bringing humanity back into harmony with its original design.


#### Living This Vision

There is a part of you that feels disordered at times.

Pulled in different directions.

Confused, distracted, or disconnected.

This vision speaks into that place.

It reminds you that your life is not abandoned to chaos. There is a deeper order at work—sometimes unseen, but always present. Even your struggles can be drawn into that movement of restoration.

The “wheel” is still turning.

Every small act of honesty, restraint, kindness, or devotion becomes part of that returning. You do not need to fix everything at once. You simply step into alignment, one moment at a time.

There is also comfort here:

You are not the one who initiates healing.

You respond to it.

The same presence that breathed life into you is still at work, gently drawing you back to yourself—and beyond yourself.

Trust that movement.

Even when you cannot see the whole.
