## Day 174 - The Rod of Judgment

> *What opposes life cannot endure. The forces that distort and deceive are not ultimate—they will be brought to nothing.*

Then before the Man, I saw a great mace of bronze.

It was fixed into the abyss like a weapon of judgment.

It moved from place to place, striking with force. And as it moved, it spoke:

“You who oppose God and rise in pride—

I strip away your honor.

I cast you into confusion.

I drive you into decay.

You rise from the mouth of the serpent to deceive, but I will strike you down.

You wound others with your actions, leading them away from truth.

But I will pursue you.

I will show you the strength of God.

I will bring you to nothing.”

And I felt the weight of its authority.

Then I heard a voice from heaven:

God has ordered all things.

He has placed waters across the earth—not only for the body, but for the soul.

Through water, cleansing comes.

Through water, life is renewed.

Those who turn toward God are washed, purified, and drawn toward eternal joy.

And I saw that all people, whether they know it or not, live under the protection of God.

Even those who resist Him are still sustained by Him.

All things are held together by His power.

As water nourishes the earth, so the soul gives life to the body.

And I understood:

Nothing exists apart from Him.

#### What This Vision Means

The mace represents divine justice—not cruel, but restorative.

It exposes what is false and brings it to an end.

The connection to water is important. Judgment and mercy are not separate. What is broken is struck down, but what is living is cleansed and renewed.

Water becomes a symbol of both physical and spiritual life. It nourishes, cleanses, sustains.

The vision expands outward: all creation is under God’s care, even when it does not recognize Him.

#### Living This Vision

It can feel as though chaos or injustice has the final word.

But this vision offers a different truth.

What is false will not endure.

What is rooted in deception will eventually collapse.

At the same time, what is good is being sustained—often quietly, like water nourishing the earth.

Your task is not to control everything.

It is to remain aligned with what is life-giving.

To trust that what is true will endure.
