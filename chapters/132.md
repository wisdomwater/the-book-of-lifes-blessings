## Day 132 - Wandering Over Burning Ground

> *I saw disobedient souls wandering in thick darkness over a floor of fire, bitten by worms, because they had refused the path of humble submission.*

After this I saw other evil spirits  
crying out in the crowd:

“God must not be the only God.  
Another besides Him will be almighty.”

These spirits urge people toward disobedience.  
They teach them to oppose those set over them.

Then I saw very thick darkness.  
Within it, souls wandered  
over burning ground.

The earth beneath them was fire,  
and dreadful worms tormented them with bites.

These were the souls  
who had stained themselves with disobedience  
during life.

They wandered in darkness  
because they had been blind,  
refusing to follow the commands  
of those rightly placed over them.

They walked on burning ground  
because they had acted arrogantly,  
choosing their own way  
according to self-will.

They were bitten by worms  
because in life they had fought stubbornly  
against their superiors.

Then the living light spoke:

“What you see is true.  
Those who are bound by disobedience,  
if they wish to be freed from these spirits  
and escape this punishment,  
must seek forgiveness  
with humble words.

Let them kneel before those they have resisted.  
Let them withdraw for a time into solitude.  
Let them discipline themselves  
with fasting, scourging, and rough clothing.

Then they will be counted,  
with justice and mercy,  
among the children of the Father.”

And I understood:

Disobedience is a terrible evil.  
It does not fear God.  
It does not honor man.

Those who give themselves to it say within their hearts:

“What do we see?  
What can we do?  
We do not know why this command is laid upon us.  
Our judgment is better and more useful than theirs.  
They only want to be above us.”

But whoever places hope in God  
takes on the submission  
that leads to holiness.

Such a soul obeys right commands  
and does not follow the one  
who fell from heaven into hell  
because he would not be subject  
to his Creator.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals disobedience as blindness joined to arrogance. The thick darkness shows that refusal to obey eventually clouds discernment. The burning ground reveals the painful instability of self-will. The worms show the inner corruption of stubborn resistance.

Hildegard is speaking of rightful obedience, not servile submission to abuse or injustice. The vice here is the rebellious refusal to receive guidance, correction, or order because the self wants to remain supreme.

The inner words of disobedience are revealing. It assumes that command is domination, that guidance is envy, and that submission is humiliation. This posture cannot imagine holy authority, because it interprets everything through suspicion and self-defense.

The remedy is concrete humility: seeking forgiveness, kneeling, solitude, discipline. These actions re-teach the soul what disobedience forgot: that we are not self-created, self-governed gods. To obey what is good and true is not degradation. It is return.

#### Living This Vision

Disobedience often sounds reasonable.

Why should I listen?  
I know better.  
They just want control.  
My way makes more sense.

Sometimes resistance is necessary.  
But sometimes it is only pride defending itself.

The question is:  
Am I refusing because the command is unjust,  
or because obedience wounds my ego?

That question requires courage.

Today, look for one place  
where you have resisted what is good, wise, or necessary.

Maybe it is an apology.  
A responsibility.  
A discipline.  
A promise.  
A truth you already know.

Take one step of humble obedience.

The fire of self-will burns beneath the feet.  
But the path of obedience, though narrow,  
leads toward peace.
