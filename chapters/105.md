## 105 - The Steady Stream

> *Truth gathers what has been divided*  
> *The heart finds rest in what is real*

I saw a wide space  
where two currents moved side by side.

One was warm—  
restless,  
rising—  
never settling.

The other was cold—  
still,  
withdrawing—  
pulling inward upon itself.

Between them,  
the ground felt uncertain—  
as though it could not decide  
where to rest.

Movement passed from one to the other—  
back and forth,  
without peace.

Then,  
from a quiet distance,  
a third way appeared.

A clear stream—  
steady,  
gentle—  
neither rushing  
nor retreating.

It flowed  
without strain—  
held within its path.

It did not oppose the currents.

It remained.

And where it moved,  
something changed.

The ground beneath it  
grew firm.

The air around it  
became still.

And slowly,  
the restless movement  
began to ease.

What had shifted back and forth  
found a place to rest.

Not forced—  
but drawn  
into something whole.

And I saw  
that this quiet stream  
had always been present—  
unchanging,  
faithful—  
waiting  
to be followed.

#### Living the Vision

At times, you can feel divided within—

pulled in different directions,  
uncertain what is true,  
unsure where to stand.

You may notice  
how easily this happens—  
a word spoken  
that does not quite match your heart,  
a small adjustment  
to protect yourself,  
a hesitation  
to be fully honest.

These movements  
may seem small.

But over time,  
they create unrest.

You do not need  
to correct everything at once.

Only to begin  
with what is simple.

A clear word.  
An honest response.  
A quiet refusal  
to distort what is real.

These are like steps  
into the steady stream.

And as you remain there—  
even imperfectly—  
something begins to settle.

The tension eases.  
The movement slows.

You are no longer pulled  
between extremes.

You begin to feel  
a quiet alignment—  
a sense of being  
where you truly are.

Truth does not rush.

It holds.

And in that holding,  
you find rest.
