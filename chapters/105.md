## Day 105 - The Fire and the Frozen River

> *I saw souls passing between burning fire and icy water, for falsehood divides the heart and leaves it unable to rest in what is real.*

After this I saw evil spirits boasting with a loud and blasphemous voice.  
They praised Lucifer as though he were enthroned above the stars,  
as though his will ruled over all things.

From their pride they stirred up falsehood in human hearts.  
They urged people toward lies, slander, deception,  
and the twisting of speech against God and neighbor.

Then I saw a fearful place of punishment.

There was a fire burning in total darkness.  
Beside it ran a river of ice water.  
Near them moved two dragons, striking at both flame and water,  
stirring the river whenever they plunged into it.

Above these was a mass of hot air,  
touching both the burning fire and the freezing stream.

In these torments were the souls of those  
who had defiled themselves with falsehood.

Some had lied without oath.  
Others had bound their lies with oaths and perjury.

The souls passed from fire into ice water,  
and from ice water back into fire.  
The dragons tormented them.  
And those who had sworn falsely  
also suffered beneath the oppressive heat above.

I understood the meaning.

They endured the fire because they had spoken many lies.  
They suffered the frozen water because deception had sunk deep within them.  
They were tormented by dragons because they had heaped one lie upon another.  
And those who lied under oath were scorched by the burning air  
because they had wrapped falsehood in solemn words.

Then the living light spoke again:  
“What you see is true.  
Those who would turn away from such evil  
must learn to hate deception  
and submit themselves to cleansing discipline.”

I saw that falsehood does not rejoice in truth.  
It enlarges what is empty.  
It makes bold claims that have no substance.  
It speaks with wicked delight.

Liars wrap themselves in lies  
as a snake hides in its burrow.  
For a time they may seem great in the world.  
But before the judgment of God,  
their splendor is nothing.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals falsehood as instability of soul. The liar cannot remain in what is solid, simple, and true. Instead, the soul moves between opposites—heat and ice, agitation and concealment, boldness and fear.

The fire shows the destructive force of lies once spoken aloud. The icy river shows the inner coldness of deceit buried within the heart. The dragons suggest the violence and cunning of compounded lies, since one lie often demands another. The burning air above those who swore falsely points to a deeper corruption: using sacred language, promises, or solemnity to give weight to what is untrue.

Falsehood is portrayed here as anti-human. To be weak is human. To be tempted is human. But to deliberately serve what is unreal is to move against the truth in which life itself is held together.

Truth brings unity.  
Falsehood fragments.

This is why lies do not merely distort facts.  
They divide the speaker from their own soul.

#### Living This Vision

Most falsehood does not begin with grand deception.

It starts with exaggeration.  
With image-management.  
With saying what protects us instead of what is true.

Sometimes we lie to avoid shame.  
Sometimes to gain approval.  
Sometimes because we no longer know how to stand plainly in the light.

This vision calls for simplicity.

Let your words become cleaner.  
Let your speech match your heart.  
Do not decorate what is empty.  
Do not swear certainty where you are uncertain.

Truthfulness is not harshness.  
It is a quiet alignment.

When you tell the truth gently,  
something inside you steadies.

The soul no longer has to run  
between fire and ice.

Even small honesty heals.  
A confession.  
A correction.  
A refusal to slander.

Truth may humble us,  
but it also makes us whole.
