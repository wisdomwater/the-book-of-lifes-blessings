## Day 125 - The Black Eyes of Unbelief

> *I saw the image of unbelief: many dark eyes searching everywhere, and yet unable to see the light that was already shining before it.*

Then I understood the fifth image more deeply.

It is Unbelief.

It comes after Disobedience,  
for when a person chooses not to obey,  
the heart soon begins to deny the One  
who commands in love.

This image looked like a man,  
except for its head.

For unbelief often knows, in some hidden way,  
that God is.  
Yet it refuses Him reverence.  
It turns its mind away from faith,  
and so loses the straight impulse  
by which the soul comes to know God.

From the knees to the soles of the feet  
it was sunk in darkness.

For unbelief does not bend toward the true faith,  
nor does it walk in its path.  
In word and deed it remains in refusal,  
preferring the dark to the light.

Its head had no clear human features,  
but was filled with black eyes.  
And near the front was one eye  
that at times flashed like burning fire.

There was no wisdom in that mind,  
only the folly of human cleverness  
separated from God.

In its hidden thoughts  
it searched in every direction.  
It practiced the works of unbelief,  
and carelessly rejected  
the light of truth even while seeing it.

Sometimes those without faith  
claim they possess the truest faith.  
Yet their lives are tangled in deceit.

And sometimes, among all their darkened intentions,  
a spark seems to break through—  
as if an eye of faith were glowing for a moment.

Then they search the powers of nature,  
the motions of the stars,  
the arrangement of created things,  
hoping to find there  
what can only be received from God.

But they do not find holiness there.  
They do not find the light of life.

Its right hand rested on its chest,  
while its left held a staff.

So do those who prefer unbelief:  
lazy toward good and holy works,  
yet confident in their own vain undertakings.

They divide God in two,  
trying to investigate Him  
in higher and lower creatures,  
without ever reaching the truth of life.

And it was wrapped in a black cloak,  
for unbelief defends the darkness  
of its own arts and arguments,  
instead of seeking the blessed life.

But Faith rises against it,  
calling the human being  
to behold God in the mirror of purity.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows unbelief as a restless searching that cannot truly see. The black eyes are powerful symbols: scrutiny without surrender, investigation without trust, curiosity without worship.

Hildegard is not condemning thought, study, or wonder. The problem is not that the mind looks carefully at the world. The problem is that unbelief looks everywhere except toward humble faith. It wants knowledge without reverence, explanation without obedience, and certainty without surrender.

The flashing front eye is especially meaningful. Even within unbelief there may still be moments of illumination—brief glimpses, stirrings, questions, intimations of truth. But if the heart insists on remaining closed, those flashes are not enough to guide the soul home.

The black cloak suggests that unbelief often protects itself. It wraps itself in self-made explanations and dark arts of reasoning rather than allowing itself to be purified. Faith, by contrast, is called a mirror of purity because it reflects what it receives from God.

#### Living This Vision

Unbelief is not always loud.

Sometimes it sounds like this:  
I’ll trust only what I can master.  
I’ll believe only what I can reduce to my own terms.

Or this:  
I am still searching,  
but I do not want to be changed by what I find.

There is nothing wrong with questions.  
Questions can be holy.

But the deeper issue is posture.  
Do my questions lead me toward humility,  
or do they guard me from surrender?

This vision invites a simple prayerful honesty:  
Lord, where am I searching everywhere but You?

Sometimes the soul exhausts itself  
looking for certainty in systems, signs, intellect, or control.  
And all along, faith waits quietly—  
not asking us to stop thinking,  
but to let our thinking bow.

Truth is more than information.  
It is relationship.
