## 125 - The Eye That Grows Still

> *Not all searching leads you home*  
> *Some sight awakens when you rest*

I saw a figure  
filled with many eyes—  
turning,  
shifting,  
searching in every direction.

Nothing escaped its notice.

It traced the patterns of light,  
followed the movement of air,  
studied the smallest changes  
in the world around it.

And yet,  
for all its seeing,  
there was no rest.

The more it looked,  
the more it seemed to reach—  
as though something essential  
remained just beyond its grasp.

Then,  
without sound,  
a quiet presence drew near.

Not another thing to observe—  
but a stillness  
that did not ask to be examined.

It did not compete  
with all the movement.

It simply remained.

And slowly,  
one of the many eyes  
grew calm.

It no longer strained outward.

It softened—  
as though learning  
a different way of seeing.

Not by searching everywhere,  
but by receiving  
what was already near.

And in that softening,  
the light that had always been present  
became clear—  
not brighter,  
but unmistakable.

#### Living the Vision

You look for clarity  
in many places.

You think,  
you analyze,  
you follow every thread  
that might lead you closer to truth.

This movement is not wrong.

But you may notice  
how it can become restless—  
as though each answer  
leads to another question,  
and nothing fully settles.

There is another kind of seeing  
that begins differently.

Not with more searching,  
but with a quiet pause.

A willingness to stop  
just long enough  
to notice what is already here.

You may feel it  
as a gentle invitation—  
to trust,  
even without having everything resolved.

This is not the end of thought.

It is the softening  
that allows thought  
to become clear.

You do not have to stop asking.

Only to let your questions  
rest in something deeper  
than your own effort.

And in that resting,  
something begins to open—  
not all at once,  
but enough to guide you forward.
