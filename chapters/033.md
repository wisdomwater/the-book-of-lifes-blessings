## 33 — The Mountain Beyond Seeing

> *There is more than you can hold*  
> *And it is still holding you*

I saw a mountain  
rising in quiet light—

not harsh,  
not distant,  
but steady  
and deeply present.

Its base was wide,  
rooted in the earth  
like something ancient  
and sure.

Paths moved along its sides—  
gentle,  
winding,  
drawing the gaze upward.

And yet,  
as the eye followed its height,  
it began to soften—

not disappearing,  
but becoming  
too luminous  
to fully see.

The peak was hidden  
in brightness.

Not concealed in shadow,  
but in fullness.

There was no urgency here.  
No striving.

Only a quiet invitation  
to walk,  
to notice,  
to be.

The air itself  
felt alive with depth—

as if something vast  
was near,  
yet not pressing to be known.

And I sensed  
that the mountain  
was not asking to be climbed  
to its end—

but to be received  
as it revealed itself,  
step by step.

Always more,  
yet never withholding.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you reach for understanding—

wanting to see clearly,  
to make sense  
of what feels just beyond you.

You may feel the pull  
to keep climbing—  
to arrive,  
to finally grasp  
what has been unclear.

But something in you  
already knows  
the gentle edge  
where knowing softens.

You may notice it  
as a quiet pause—  
when answers do not come,  
and yet something deeper  
does not feel absent.

Just still.

Just present.

This is not a lack.

It is a different kind of nearness.

You are allowed  
to stand here—  
to breathe  
without resolving everything.

To let what is unseen  
remain kind.

To trust that what is hidden  
is not far away,  
but simply  
beyond what can be held  
all at once.

And as you remain,  
without forcing,  
something within you  
begins to rest—

not in certainty,  
but in wonder.
