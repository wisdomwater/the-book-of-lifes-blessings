## Day 119 - The Groaning of the Elements

> *I heard the elements lament before their Creator, for human sin had troubled their courses; yet God declared that He would cleanse what had been defiled and call hearts back through judgment.*

Then I understood the flowing of the elements  
from the marrow of the great Man’s loins.

They came forth  
and returned again,  
as breath goes out from a man  
and is drawn back in.

From the Creator’s power  
the virtues of the elements proceed.  
They give heat, moisture, firmness, and fertility.  
They bring creatures to birth and growth.  
And when, by God’s command,  
their work is withdrawn,  
creatures wither and pass away.

So it is also in the soul.

From the hidden strength of life within,  
the virtues arise.  
Good fame springs forth from holy action.  
Contemplation rises from prayer.  
Contrition moves the heart to tears before God,  
and compunction, in its own time,  
draws those tears back into silence.

Then I understood the cry of the elements.

The voice I had heard  
was not human speech,  
but the wild lament of creation itself.  
The elements do not speak as we speak,  
yet when they exceed their proper bounds  
they reveal their distress.

They are troubled by the sins of men.

Because human beings are bound to the elements,  
and the elements are bound to human beings,  
the disorder of one touches the other.

Thus they become fetid  
with the stench of vice.  
They hunger for justice.  
At times they even take on the smoke of punishment  
that rises from the shameful deeds of men.

And God answered that He would cleanse them.

With His broom—  
that is, with His judgments and chastisements—  
He afflicts sinners with scourges and calamities  
until they turn back in penitence.

For He desires all things to be pure before Him.

So the winds are hindered by decay.  
The air vomits filth.  
Moisture grows disordered.  
Fog and snow come wrongly.  
Parasites and blights devour the fruit of the earth.

Why?

Because men close heart and mouth to justice.  
They will not open themselves to truth.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision presents creation as deeply entangled with moral life. Hildegard does not imagine the elements as neutral machinery. They are living signs of order, and when human beings descend into corruption, the world itself bears the strain.

The complaint of the elements is not literal speech but symbolic groaning. Storms, blights, pestilence, and disorder in the natural world reveal that something is out of joint. Humanity is not standing above creation as a detached observer. We are woven into it. Our disorder echoes outward.

The comparison to breath is important. Just as breath goes out and returns, the elements proceed from God and remain dependent on Him. The same is true of virtue in the soul. Good works, prayer, tears, contemplation—all arise from divine life and return to it.

God’s “broom” shows that judgment is not merely vengeance. It is cleansing. God afflicts in order to purify, awaken, and restore. His purpose is not ruin, but the recovery of what has been defiled.

#### Living This Vision

This passage asks us to take seriously  
that sin is never private.

What we nurture inwardly  
spills outward.

A bitter heart affects a room.  
A cruel culture affects a city.  
A disordered people affect the land itself.

That may sound overwhelming,  
but it also means your goodness matters more than you think.

Prayer matters.  
Repentance matters.  
Truthfulness matters.  
The tears you shed before God  
are not wasted.

When you feel the world is heavy,  
begin where you can.

Open your heart to justice.  
Open your mouth to truth.  
Confess what needs cleansing.  
Do one act that aligns your life  
with God’s order rather than chaos.

Creation is still groaning.  
But it is also still being held.

And the One who cleanses  
has not laid down His broom.
