## Day 22 - Wisdom Governs All Things

> *The eye of God sees all, guides all, and gently calls the human heart toward the good.*

Then I saw Wisdom—  
not as an idea,  
but as a living presence.

It flowed from God,  
ordering all things with quiet authority.

Nothing was outside its reach.

I saw how all things were shaped by this Wisdom—  
the heavens given their course,  
the earth made fruitful,  
the waters held in their bounds.

Nothing was left unattended.

Like a careful steward,  
God entered even the hidden places—  
ensuring that nothing was lost.

Wisdom was like an eye—  
seeing all things clearly.

Yet it was also gentle—  
a friend,  
an embrace.

For God governs not with excess,  
but with perfect measure.

Then I saw humanity within this order.

Man was given something remarkable:  
the ability to desire,  
to choose.

He could turn toward the good—  
and receive help from God.

Or he could turn toward the enemy—  
and receive suggestions of darkness.

Yet even this choosing  
was not entirely his own.

For nothing happens  
without the permission of God.

Still, the human heart moves—  
seeking, desiring, reaching.

And I saw how easily it can be turned.

As the enemy once deceived the first man,  
so he continues to whisper.

When a person turns fully away from the good,  
their desires deepen into darkness—  
like sinking into the abyss.

But when one holds even a small desire for good,  
though they stumble,  
they are not abandoned.

God preserves them.

I saw the human soul like the sky—  
sometimes clear and bright,  
sometimes covered in storm.

Not fixed,  
but changing.

Yet still held.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision presents Wisdom as the active, ordering presence of God in the world. It is both powerful and gentle—governing creation while also inviting relationship.

Human freedom is real, but not absolute. We are able to choose, yet our choices exist within the sustaining will of God. This creates a dynamic tension: we are responsible, yet dependent.

The contrast between turning toward God or toward the enemy highlights the importance of desire. What we incline ourselves toward shapes what we receive.

Importantly, this vision offers hope. Even imperfect striving toward the good is honored by God. A small desire for goodness is enough to keep a person from being lost.

The instability of the human condition is acknowledged—we are not constant. Yet we are not abandoned in that fluctuation.


#### Living This Vision

You carry within you the ability to choose—but also the experience of being pulled in different directions.

Some days, your desires feel clear and aligned. Other days, they feel scattered or conflicted. This vision invites you to pay attention  
not to perfection, but to direction. Ask yourself:

*What am I moving toward right now?*

Even a small turning toward the good matters. You do not need to eliminate all struggle to be held by God. If there is even a quiet desire within you  
for what is true, that desire is already being met with help.

Do not be discouraged by inconsistency. Like the sky, you will have both clarity and storm. What matters is that you continue to turn—  
again and again—toward the light.

And you will be guided.
