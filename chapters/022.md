## 22 - The Place That Opens Again

> *Even what feels empty can receive. A small opening is enough for light.*

At the edge of the vision, beyond the brightness of the fields and the warmth of the living light, there was a quieter place resting in stillness. Nothing there shimmered or blossomed outward. It appeared simple, almost bare, turned inward upon itself like a room long left untouched. Yet the stillness did not feel abandoned. It felt waiting.

The place was not sealed shut in resistance. It carried more the feeling of earth lying dormant beneath winter skies, or air holding its breath before the first light of dawn begins to spread across the horizon. There was no movement stirring there yet, no visible warmth rising from the ground. And still, something within it remained quietly open beneath the surface.

Its simplicity revealed everything plainly. Nothing distracted the eye. Nothing covered over the emptiness or tried to disguise it with noise and activity. There was only space. Silence. A kind of honest stillness that did not pretend to be more alive than it presently felt.

And within that stillness, I sensed something almost imperceptible—a softness hidden deep inside the quiet, a place where light could enter if welcomed gently enough.

Not through force.

Not through urgency.

Only through reception.

The light did not rush toward the emptiness demanding to transform it immediately. It waited patiently at the threshold, ready to enter wherever even the smallest opening appeared. And I understood then that nothing within the soul is ever beyond return. Some places are not dead. Only unopened. Only waiting for the slightest turning toward what gives life.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons when the inner life feels dry or distant. Prayer feels quiet. Meaning feels harder to touch. Emotions flatten into numbness or fatigue. We may begin wondering whether something essential has been lost within us, whether the warmth we once knew has disappeared completely.

The instinct during these seasons is often to force movement again—to fix ourselves quickly, to manufacture feeling, to escape the discomfort of stillness. Yet not every quiet season is a sign of failure. Some forms of stillness are preparing the soul in ways we cannot yet see.

Like winter soil resting beneath the surface, the heart sometimes enters periods where outward growth slows so that deeper roots can quietly form. These spaces may feel empty at first because we are accustomed to measuring life only through visible movement and emotional intensity. But beneath the silence, something more patient may already be unfolding.

The invitation during such seasons is often very small and gentle. Not dramatic transformation. Not immediate certainty. Simply openness.

A willingness to pause instead of fleeing. A breath that stays present for one more moment. A quiet honesty about what we are truly feeling. A small act of tenderness toward ourselves when we would rather harden. These small openings matter more than we realize.

The Sacred rarely forces its way into the soul. Light enters where it is received. Even the smallest crack in our guardedness becomes enough for grace to begin moving again. And slowly—often more slowly than we expect—what once felt hardened or distant begins to soften beneath the warmth of renewed presence.

You do not need to become radiant overnight. You only need to remain open enough for life to find you again.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Open me to receive

**Exhale:** Let light enter gently
