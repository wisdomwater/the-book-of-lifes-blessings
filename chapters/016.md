## 16 - The Clarity Beneath the Noise

> *What is true does not strain to be seen. Clarity waits beneath the surface.*

I heard a voice moving through the air—not loud or violent, but persistent in a quieter way, like a thread weaving itself unnoticed through every conversation, every thought, every inward movement of the heart. It did not create anything entirely new. Instead, it reshaped what already existed. What had once seemed simple became tangled. What had once felt clear slowly turned uncertain beneath its touch.

At first the change was subtle, almost impossible to notice. A light mist began gathering over the landscape, soft enough to seem harmless. Yet gradually it thickened, blurring the edges of things once easily recognized. Familiar paths became harder to distinguish. Shapes shifted at their boundaries. Nothing appeared entirely false, yet nothing felt fully grounded either.

Within the mist came many whispers—questions, suggestions, partial truths turning endlessly upon themselves. Some sounded wise at first hearing. Others stirred fear, urgency, or confusion. The mind moved quickly from one thought to another, searching for certainty but finding only more movement. Everything seemed to shift just enough to unsettle the soul without ever fully revealing why.

And yet beneath all the noise, something deeper remained unchanged.

I sensed a quiet steadiness resting below the confusion, like the hidden strength of roots deep beneath winter soil. This clarity did not struggle against the mist or attempt to overpower it through force. It simply remained present—whole, grounded, untouched by the turbulence moving above it. The deeper truth had no need to shout for attention. It waited patiently beneath the surface.

And wherever this quiet steadiness was noticed, even briefly, the fog began to thin. Not suddenly, not dramatically, but gently—like morning light lifting mist from a field little by little until the shape of the world became visible again.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons when the inner world feels crowded with competing voices. Thoughts multiply. Questions circle endlessly. One fear contradicts another. Opinions, expectations, anxieties, and possibilities pull the mind in different directions until even simple decisions begin to feel overwhelming. In such moments, confusion can seem more convincing than clarity simply because it speaks more loudly.

Yet most people, deep within themselves, already know the difference between confusion and truth.

Confusion often creates urgency, fragmentation, and exhaustion. It keeps the soul spinning endlessly without rest. Clarity feels different. Even when difficult, it carries a certain groundedness. It does not need to manipulate or overpower. There is a quiet steadiness beneath it—a sense of alignment that brings space to the heart rather than contraction.

Part of spiritual maturity involves learning that not every thought deserves equal trust or attention. The mind produces countless voices each day, many shaped by fear, old wounds, cultural noise, or exhaustion. We do not need to follow every impulse simply because it appears. We are allowed to pause. Allowed to breathe. Allowed to let thoughts pass through us without building a home for them inside the soul.

As we become quieter inwardly, deeper clarity begins emerging naturally. Often it does not arrive through dramatic revelation, but through simple returning—to honesty, stillness, grounded presence, compassion, and what feels quietly life-giving rather than chaotic. Truth rarely demands frantic striving. It waits patiently beneath the surface until we become still enough to notice what has never truly moved.

And perhaps this is one of the hidden mercies of the spiritual life: that clarity does not depend upon controlling every thought, but upon learning how to remain rooted beneath the noise.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Clear my seeing

**Exhale:** I rest in what is true
