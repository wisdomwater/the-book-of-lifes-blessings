## Day 16 - The Sound Before the Word

> *God speaks in shadows and clarity—guiding the soul from echo into fullness.*

I saw the great figure again,  
and from His shoulders downward  
He was veiled in a bright cloud.

Within this cloud, mysteries moved—  
some revealed,  
others hidden in radiant secrecy.

From the beginning of creation,  
God had worked wonders.

Some were made visible,  
like flashes of light breaking through the veil.  
Others remained concealed—  
kept in the quiet depths of divine knowing,  
guarding and shaping the souls who seek Him.

For not all things can be understood at once.

Then I saw something like a gathering—  
a people formed under His authority.

They were given a law.

But this law was like a sound—  
not yet the full word.

It was heard,  
but not fully understood.

Like a voice echoing before meaning becomes clear,  
it prepared the way.

For the sound comes first,  
and then the word.

And so it was.

The law was a shadow,  
a hint,  
a preparation.

Until at last,  
the Word Himself appeared.

Not spoken only—  
but living.

And in Him,  
what was once separate came together.

The sound and the Word were united.  
The shadow and the reality became one.

And the hidden meaning  
was made known.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the unfolding nature of divine revelation. God does not reveal everything at once. Instead, truth is given in stages—first as shadow, then as clarity.

The “cloud” represents mystery—not confusion, but depth. Some things are shown, while others remain hidden for a time. This is not withholding, but protection and preparation.

The old law is described as “sound”—something real, yet incomplete. It points toward something greater, but does not fully contain it. It trains the ear before the heart can fully understand.

Christ, then, is the “Word”—the full expression of what was previously only hinted at. In Him, the partial becomes complete.

This movement from sound to Word reflects the journey of the soul itself.


#### Living This Vision

There are times in life when things feel unclear—when you sense something is true, but cannot fully grasp it. You may feel like you are hearing an echo without yet understanding the message.

This is not failure.

It is formation.

You are learning to hear.

Be patient with the process.  
Not all clarity comes at once.

Ask yourself:  
*What “sounds” am I hearing in my life right now? What might they be pointing toward?*

Instead of forcing understanding,  
stay attentive.

Over time, what feels like fragments  
begins to form meaning.

And when clarity comes,  
it often arrives not as new information,  
but as recognition.

You realize:  
*This is what was being said all along.*

The Word has a way of finding you—  
right where you are.
