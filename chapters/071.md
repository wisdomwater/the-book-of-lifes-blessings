## 71 - The Returning Harmony

> *What has been scattered can be gathered. What listens deeply begins to come home.*

I saw a wide valley resting beneath a quiet and spacious light. At first the air there felt unsettled. Winds moved across the landscape in conflicting directions, crossing and circling without rest. Nothing seemed fully grounded. The grasses bent first one way and then another, as though uncertain which movement to trust or follow.

And the sound within the valley carried a subtle strain.

It was not loud or violent, but restless—like many small currents moving without harmony. Everything remained in motion, yet nothing seemed fully at peace within itself. The valley held beauty still, but it had lost its rhythm.

Then slowly, a deeper stillness began rising beneath the movement.

It did not descend from above as command or force. It emerged quietly from within the valley itself, like a low and steady note sounding beneath all the scattered winds. The stillness did not erase the motion surrounding it. Instead, it began gently gathering the movement into something more whole.

The winds did not disappear.

They began to align.

One by one, the currents softened into a shared rhythm. The air cleared. The grasses lifted and moved together again, no longer pulled endlessly in opposing directions. What had once felt fragmented slowly became unified—not through sameness, but through harmony deeper than difference itself.

And I saw that the valley did not strive to hold itself together.

It simply yielded to the deeper order already present beneath the unrest.

And within that yielding, peace returned.

Not the peace of suppression or control, but the peace of belonging.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when the inner life feels divided. Thoughts pull in different directions. Emotions rise before we fully understand them. One part of us longs for peace while another clings tightly to fear, anger, or self-protection. The soul can begin to feel scattered within itself, as though no single voice inside us fully knows how to lead.

Often our instinct is to force resolution quickly.

We try to silence uncomfortable emotions, reject certain parts of ourselves, or regain control through pressure and certainty. Yet this kind of inner force rarely creates lasting peace. More often, it deepens the fragmentation already present within us.

There is another way.

A quieter and more compassionate way that begins with listening instead of suppression. We pause long enough to notice what is moving within us before deciding what must be fixed. We allow conflicting emotions, fears, longings, and questions to be seen without immediately condemning them.

This gentle attention creates space.

And within that space, something deeper begins to emerge—a steadiness beneath the inner noise. The soul slowly discovers that peace is not created by denying parts of ourselves, but by allowing them to be gathered into a deeper wholeness rooted in truth and compassion.

Not everything resolves immediately.

Yet the urgency softens.

The scattered movements within us begin slowly aligning around something quieter and more trustworthy than fear. And from this growing harmony, our words, actions, and presence begin carrying a different tone into the world. Less reactive. Less divided. More grounded in love.

Not because we have mastered ourselves completely, but because we have allowed peace itself to meet us where we are and gently gather us home.
