## Day 157 - The Spinning Wheel

> *The soul turns like a wheel until it remembers where it came from. Then repentance becomes the road home.*

The faithful soul, when it has felt the correction of God, begins to tremble.

It knows it cannot escape healing unless it turns back. So it speaks with a heavy heart:

“O God, You know all things. You have made all things in perfection.

When I fall into the desire of my sins, fear rises in me. When repentance shows me my condition, I tremble. And if I do not repent, I fear even more.

I walk the paths of my own will. I follow the tracks of pleasure. I grow weary of my sins, yet cannot fully free myself from them.

I am like a spinning wheel.

I turn toward the North, then the East, then the South, then the West. From the wound of original sin, I draw sins into thought, word, and deed.

Yet when my soul remembers where it came from, I begin to separate barley from wheat.

Still, I cannot do this completely, for I am flesh and blood.

When pleasure stirs me, I leap like a fawn. I do not restrain myself. And when age or sorrow makes sin bitter to me, I long for more time to amend my life.

So I turn in all these things like an unstable wheel.

Therefore, O God, I detest my sins. I know You do not forgive the arrogance that opposes You without repentance. You cast down the first disobedient angel. You exiled humanity after the first fault. You gather all iniquity into the fold of contrition.

But I trust You.

You tore open heaven.

You clothed Yourself in flesh.

You took upon Yourself the crimes and sins that Your mercy purifies through penance.

Therefore I, a sinner, purified by You, shall live.”


#### What This Vision Means

This reading gives voice to the repentant soul.

It is honest about instability. The soul does not pretend to be pure, steady, or finished. It admits its turning—toward temptation, fear, regret, longing, and hope.

The wheel returns as an image of human inconsistency. But here, unlike the vice of inconstancy, the turning soul begins to awaken. It remembers its origin. It begins to discern wheat from barley, what nourishes from what merely fills.

The prayer also holds the mystery of mercy. The soul knows judgment is real, yet it trusts that God has entered flesh to heal flesh.

Repentance is not despair.

It is the place where truth and hope meet.

The soul says, “I have sinned,” and also, “I shall live.”

That is the miracle of return.


#### Living This Vision

You may feel divided.

Part of you wants healing.
Part of you wants the old pleasure.
Part of you fears judgment.
Part of you trusts mercy.

This does not mean you are lost.

It means you are awake enough to see the struggle.

The invitation is to keep turning toward God, even if you have turned away many times before.

Name the sin.
Name the fear.
Name the longing to live.

Do not wait until you feel pure to pray.

Pray from the spinning wheel.

God knows how to meet you there.
