## Day 19 - Strength That Does Not Collapse

> *Even when shaken, what is rooted in God does not fall.*

I saw the great figure again,  
and from the knees to the calves  
He stood firmly upon the earth.

The knees bore weight.  
The calves gave strength.

And I understood:  
as these sustain the body,  
so God sustains the world.

The earth itself was like this—  
a place of support and movement,  
holding all creatures in their place.

Waters were guided.  
Air was held in balance.  
All things were ordered by His strength.

Yet I saw something else.

At times, the knees would bend.  
They would tremble under strain.

And so it is with creation—  
and with humanity.

There are moments when the right path is forgotten,  
when creatures move in ways that distort what is good.

But the earth does not abandon its purpose.  
It is restored.

For the power of God corrects what is bent.

Then I saw the people of the new way—  
those formed by the living Word.

They endured trials.

Their knees were shaken.  
Their strength was tested.

A great pressure came upon them,  
like a force seeking to make them collapse.

Yet they did not fall.

For their strength did not come from themselves.

It came from their head—  
the Lord.

Even as the Church itself seemed to bend at times,  
it did not break.

What was shaken  
was strengthened again.

And it remained.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision portrays resilience grounded in divine strength. The imagery of knees and calves highlights both vulnerability and endurance. Knees bend—they are not rigid. Yet they are supported by strength that prevents collapse.

The earth reflects this same dynamic: though disrupted by human failure, it is continually upheld by God’s sustaining power.

The trials of the “new people” point to the reality that faith does not remove suffering. Instead, it anchors the soul within it. Even when shaken by forces of deception or opposition, those rooted in God remain standing.

The Church, too, shares in this pattern—bending under pressure, yet never ultimately overcome.

Strength, in this vision, is not the absence of struggle. It is the ability to endure without losing foundation.


#### Living This Vision

There will be seasons when you feel unsteady—when your confidence weakens, your clarity fades, and your strength seems insufficient.

This is not a sign that you are failing. It is part of being human.

The question is not whether your knees will bend. They will. The question is:

*What is holding you up?*

If your strength depends only on your own effort, you will feel constant pressure to remain unshaken. But if your strength is rooted in something deeper, you can bend without breaking.

In moments of strain, turn your attention to the source of your support. You are not meant to hold yourself together alone. Even now, beneath the surface of your struggle, there is a strength carrying you.

Lean into it.
