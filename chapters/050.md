## Day 50 – The Fire of Anger

> *I saw a man whose mouth burned like poison and flame, turning thought into destruction.*

Then I beheld another image rising from the shadow—a man in form, yet disfigured by fury.

His face was human, but his mouth was like that of a scorpion, filled with deadly threat. From it came not life, but poison—words that struck and wounded like venom. His eyes were distorted, the white overtaking their clarity, as though sight itself had been overwhelmed. He looked, but did not truly see.

His arms were strong like a man’s, yet what they carried was not wisdom but the power to harm. His hands were twisted, clawed, reaching always to tear, to grasp, to destroy. His body curved like a crab, moving not forward in truth, but sideways along crooked paths.

His legs were like those of a lobster, and his feet like serpents, driven by pride and envy. And I saw that he was caught within a great turning wheel, spinning endlessly. He grasped its upper spokes with his hands and pressed his feet against the lower rim, as if sustained by his own will.

Yet he went nowhere.

He only turned.

His body was naked, stripped of all righteousness. His head bore no hair, for anger had robbed him of clarity and honor. And from his mouth burst flames—great tongues of fire—words spoken in wrath, burning everything in their path.

In this fire, he forgot God.

And in his forgetting, he consumed both others and himself.

#### What This Vision Means

Anger, as I saw it, is not merely emotion—it is a force that distorts perception, speech, and action.

The scorpion’s mouth reveals how anger poisons communication. Words spoken in rage do not simply express—they wound, often beyond repair. The distorted eyes show how anger blinds judgment, replacing clarity with reaction.

The wheel is the most revealing image. Anger traps the soul in cycles. It feels powerful, even justified, yet it leads nowhere. The one caught in it believes they are acting with purpose, but they are only repeating patterns shaped by hurt, pride, or fear.

The fire represents the outward expression of this inner storm. It spreads quickly, consuming relationships, peace, and truth.

Anger becomes most dangerous when it aligns itself with righteousness—when it convinces the soul that destruction is justified.

#### Living This Vision

Anger will come.

It rises quickly, often before we are aware of it. It feels sharp, energizing, even clarifying. But this vision invites you to pause and look deeper.

What is beneath the anger?

Is it fear?  
Hurt?  
A desire to control?  
A longing to be seen or heard?

Anger itself is not the enemy. But when it takes control—when it speaks unchecked—it begins to shape the soul in its own image.

The invitation is not suppression, but transformation.

To notice the fire before it spreads.  
To hold the heat without letting it burn.  
To choose words that heal rather than wound.

There is a strength greater than reaction.

It is the strength to remain grounded when the storm rises.
