## 50 — The Fire Becoming Light

> *The fire within you is not against you*  
> *It can become a warmth that gives life*

I saw a great turning—

a wheel moving in quiet rhythm,  
carrying within it  
a hidden fire.

At times it flared—  
bright,  
sudden,  
reaching outward  
with intensity.

It held a fierce energy,  
alive and awake,  
like something that  
longed to be seen,  
to be heard,  
to be known.

And I felt its heat—  
not only as destruction,  
but as power.

A force that could move,  
that could speak,  
that could change what is.

Yet when it rushed outward  
without resting,  
it scattered—

its flame stretching too far,  
losing its center,  
touching everything  
without truly holding anything.

The wheel continued to turn,  
but it did not carry the fire forward.  
It only circled.

Then I saw another way—

the same fire,  
gathered inward.

Held.

Not extinguished,  
not denied—

but received  
into a deeper stillness.

And there,  
it changed.

Its sharpness softened.  
Its brightness steadied.

It became warmth—  
a quiet, living heat  
that did not consume,  
but sustained.

And from this warmth,  
something new emerged—

clarity without force,  
strength without harm,  
a presence that could remain  
even in the rising heat.

#### Living the Vision

It happens. Something rises quickly in you—

a surge of heat,  
a tightening,  
a sudden clarity  
that something is not right.

It can feel powerful,  
almost necessary.

You may feel the urge  
to move with it,  
to speak,  
to act,  
to release what is building.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
how quickly it can carry you—  
how easily it can move  
beyond what you intended.

But within that same fire,  
there is another possibility.

You can feel it  
without becoming it.

You can hold it  
without letting it speak for you.

There is a deeper strength  
that does not rush.

A steadiness  
that allows the heat  
to settle,  
to gather,  
to become clear.

And from that place,  
what needs to be said  
can be spoken  
without wounding.

What needs to be done  
can be done  
without harm.

The fire does not need  
to be lost.

It only needs  
to be held.
