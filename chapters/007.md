## 7 — Wisdom Governs All Things

> *You are seen more clearly than you see yourself*  
> *And quietly guided toward what is good*

I saw a presence moving through all things—

not separate,  
but within.

It flowed like light  
through open space,  
like water  
finding every hidden place.

Nothing was untouched.

The air settled in its rhythm.  
The waters remained in their bounds.  
The earth opened and received.

All things moved  
in a quiet harmony—  
not forced,  
but gently held in place.

And at the center of this movement,  
there was a seeing—

clear,  
steady,  
unhurried.

Like an eye  
that did not search,  
but simply knew.

Yet this knowing  
did not weigh upon anything.

It rested—  
like a kindness  
that understood  
without pressing.

Then I saw the human heart—

open,  
shifting,  
alive with motion.

It turned  
as if drawn  
by many small currents.

Some led toward stillness.  
Some toward unrest.

And yet,  
within all this movement,  
there remained a quiet pull—

subtle,  
persistent,  
gentle as breath.

It did not demand.

It invited.

And even when the heart wandered,  
even when it clouded or grew restless,  
this presence did not withdraw.

It remained near—  
guiding,  
holding,  
waiting for even the smallest turning.

For even the faintest movement  
toward what is good  
was met with care.

And nothing was lost.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
your inner world feels divided—

when one part of you  
longs for what is steady and true,  
and another part  
pulls in a different direction.

You may notice  
how subtle these movements are—  
how a thought,  
a feeling,  
or a desire  
can gently shape your path.

And it can feel uncertain.

But beneath all of this,  
there is a quiet guidance  
already present.

You may sense it  
in the smallest inclinations—  
a pause before reacting,  
a soft pull toward kindness,  
a desire to return  
to what feels real.

These are not insignificant.

They are the ways  
you are being led.

You do not need  
to become perfectly clear  
to be guided.

Only to notice  
what is already drawing you.

And when you turn—  
even slightly—  
toward what is good,  
you may feel something meet you there.

A support.  
A steadiness.

You are not navigating alone.

You are being gently led  
from within.
