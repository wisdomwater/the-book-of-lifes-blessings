## Day 7 — The Worm Who Avoids Courage

> *I saw how fear disguises itself as peace,
> and how the soul can shrink until it no longer stands for truth.*

In the vision I saw a fifth image.

Its head resembled that of a man, but its left ear was like the ear of a hare—long and wide, so large that it covered the entire head like a heavy flap.

The rest of the body was strange and pitiful.

It was like a worm without bones, curled tightly within a hole, as a small child might curl beneath blankets. It seemed weak and hidden, unwilling to stretch itself into the light.

From this creature came a harsh voice.

“I do not wish to offend anyone. If I anger someone, I might lose comfort or help.

If I offend a person, they might turn against me, and then I would have no friends.

So I will honor the noble and the rich. But I will not concern myself with the holy or the poor, because they cannot benefit me.

I prefer to be agreeable with everyone so that I may live without suffering.

If I strike someone, they might strike me harder. If I harm someone even a little, they might repay me with something worse.

So I will keep quiet.

Whether people do good or evil, I will say nothing.

Sometimes it is better to lie and deceive than to tell the truth. It is better to gain something than to lose it. It is better to run away from a fortress than to fight against it.

Why begin a struggle that you cannot finish?

Even if the strong and the wise mock me, they only possess what chance has given them. I prefer the safety of my own house.

Those who speak the truth often lose their possessions.

And those who fight sometimes die.”

Then I heard a voice from the stormy cloud answer him.

“Your first error was to raise your voice against God by refusing to follow justice.

You wandered far from truth and deceived others whenever it was convenient. You have no integrity.

But I hold the sword of the strongest virtues of God. With it I cut down injustice.

I will strike you with this sword, for you are nothing but ashes of ashes.

The things you gather will become small and empty.

I refuse to dwell among the ashes and lies of the world. Instead I seek the fountain of living water.

I fight against the ancient serpent and destroy his defenses through the mysteries of God’s wisdom.

For this reason I will always dwell with the true God.”

#### What This Vision Means

This strange creature represents **cowardly complacency**, a form of spiritual laziness that avoids truth in order to preserve comfort.

The hare’s ear symbolizes excessive caution and fear. A hare constantly listens for danger, always ready to flee. In the same way, this spirit is always listening for threats to its safety or reputation.

The worm-like body reveals the deeper condition of the soul: it has lost its backbone. It curls inward, hiding rather than standing upright.

This vice does not openly rebel against God. Instead, it quietly refuses responsibility.

It refuses to speak truth.
It refuses to defend justice.
It refuses to risk loss.

Peace becomes its excuse, but the peace it seeks is merely the absence of conflict.

Divine Victory answers with the image of the sword. The sword represents courage rooted in divine truth. It cuts through deception and refuses to live among the ashes of compromise.

Where cowardice retreats, divine courage stands.

#### Living This Vision

There is a temptation that feels very reasonable.

It whispers, *Stay quiet. Do not risk anything. Protect your comfort.*

Sometimes this voice even sounds wise. It tells us that peace is better than conflict, that survival is better than courage.

But the soul slowly shrinks when it never stands for truth.

We see injustice and remain silent. We watch deception and say nothing. We choose safety instead of integrity.

Over time, the spirit curls inward like the worm in the vision.

Divine courage does not mean constant conflict or reckless bravery. It means being willing to stand where truth stands—even when it costs something.

Sometimes courage is as simple as speaking honestly, refusing a lie, or defending someone who cannot defend themselves.

The sword of virtue is not held in anger.

It is held in fidelity to what is right.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:**
> Give me courage for truth

> **Exhale:**
> Let me stand in Your justice
