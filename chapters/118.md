## Day 118 - Clothed in the Living World

> *I heard that the glory of God cannot be stolen, for all creation is held in Him, and every righteous work returns its brightness not to the self, but to its Source.*

Then I heard the voice from heaven speak again.

It said that the Creator made the world,  
fastened it together with the elements,  
and adorned it with many creatures  
for the service of humankind.

The devil, envious of this honor,  
never ceases to seduce the human race through vice,  
hoping to strip man entirely  
from the dignity prepared for him.

Yet he cannot carry away the glory of God.  
He cannot diminish it.  
This vision itself makes that plain.

Then I understood more deeply  
why the great Man was turned toward the north  
and looking also toward the east.

God teaches the human being  
not to sink into the confusion of blindness and sin.  
Man must reject that darkness with strength  
and prepare himself for the honor of the true light.

For within man there is knowledge  
of good and evil,  
and he must discern,  
like one turning a wheel,  
which way he will go.

I saw that the winds, the air,  
and the fruitfulness of the world  
clothed the Man like a garment.

The flight and breadth of the winds,  
the gentle moisture of the air,  
the piercing fecundity of grasses and trees—  
all these are upheld  
by the higher powers with which God works.

As they proceed from Him  
and return glory to Him,  
they obey Him in all things.

The fire that warms creatures  
and the light that illumines them  
also adorn Him.

So it is with the soul.

At the beginning of good desire,  
the soul moves like wind.

The taste of a good will  
spreads like air.

And the full completion of holy works  
makes the soul fruitful  
as the earth becomes fruitful in season.

All true good in man  
comes forth from God  
and is strengthened by God.

Therefore it is fitting  
that when man does good,  
he should return the glory not to himself,  
but to the One from whom the life of goodness came.

#### What This Vision Means

This passage reveals a profound harmony between creation and the soul. The elements serve as the clothing of the divine Man because all creation bears witness to God’s life, power, and order. The world is not separate from praise. It is already participating in it.

The soul mirrors this same pattern. Good desire begins like wind, invisible but moving. Intention spreads like air. Then, when a work is fully carried out in love and truth, it becomes fruitful. Hildegard is showing that the spiritual life is not static. It unfolds like a living ecology.

The central insight is that goodness should not terminate in the self. Human beings are tempted to claim their virtues as personal possessions. But just as the elements return glory to God, so the soul is called to return all honor to its Source. The adornment belongs to God, even when it shines through us.

This vision invites a holy humility: our works matter deeply, yet they are never self-generated. Grace gives them life, strength, and completion.

#### Living This Vision

It is easy to do something good  
and quietly make it about ourselves.

We help.  
We endure.  
We speak wisely.  
We create something beautiful.

And then, almost without noticing,  
we begin to feed on the recognition it brings.

This vision gently reorders that impulse.

Your good works matter.  
They are real.  
They bless the world.

But they are not self-born.

When a kind desire rises in you,  
when you choose the true,  
when you persevere in what is right,  
God is already at work beneath it.

That does not make your effort meaningless.  
It makes it sacred.

A simple practice can help:  
when you do good, give thanks immediately.  
Return the brightness.  
Do not clutch the adornment.

Let your life become like the elements—  
moving, nourishing, fruitful,  
and always turning its praise toward God.
