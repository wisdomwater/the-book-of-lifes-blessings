## Day 111 - The Cry Above the Mountains

> *I saw the great Man turned toward the north and east, clothed with the world itself, and I heard the elements groaning because human sin had disordered their song.*

I saw again the great Man, and now He had turned toward the north while also gazing toward the east.

The winds and the air, and the fruitfulness of the world beneath the firmament of heaven, clothed Him from thigh to knee like a garment.  
The fire and brightness of the air adorned His clothing.  
From the center of His loins the powers of the elements poured outward and returned again, as breath goes forth from a man and then is drawn back within him.

Then I heard the strong voice of the elements crying out to Him:

“We cannot follow the course set for us by our Maker.  
Human beings, through their evil deeds, twist us like a mill wheel.  
Because of this we reek with pestilence, and we hunger for all righteousness.”

But the Man answered them:

“I will cleanse you with My broom.  
Meanwhile I will afflict humankind until they return to Me.  
Then I will prepare many hearts according to My own heart.  
As often as you are defiled, so often will I purify you by punishing the one who has contaminated you.”

And He said that the winds stink with decay, and the air vomits filth, because human beings do not open their mouths to righteousness or receive it as food.  
The living force of creation dries up when false religion rules—when people act only according to their own desires and say, “Who is this Lord whom we do not see?”

Yet He answered them:

“Do you not see Me in day and night?  
Do you not see Me when you sow, and when the rain moistens the seed so that it may grow?  
Every creature knows its Creator.  
Only man rebels, withholding from the Creator the honor due Him.”

Then, in the fog where many vices moved, I saw another image.

It had the face of a woman.  
Its eyes were of fire.  
Its nose was smeared with mud.  
Its mouth was shut.

It had no arms or hands.  
From each shoulder stretched a wing like a bat’s—one spread toward the east, the other toward the west.  
Its chest was like a man’s.  
Its legs and feet were like a lobster’s.  
It had neither belly nor back.  
Its head was bare, and it wore no garment.  
Darkness surrounded it completely, except for a very thin thread like a golden circle, running from the crown over the cheeks to the chin.

And this image cried out:

“I shout over the mountains.  
Who can compare with me?  
My cloak is spread over hills and fields,  
and I will allow no one to surpass me.  
No one is like me.”

Then from the storm-cloud I heard another voice answer:

“I am a pillar of cloud.  
Why should I endure your foul insults, when the Creator Himself came down from heaven to lead humanity back to Himself?

I dwelt in the heights with Him, and when He came down to the earth, I also dwelt to the ends of the world.  
I do not speak deceitfully, pretending to be what I am not.  
If I did, I would not be sunlight driving away darkness.

But with God I shine into all darkness.  
No storm can overthrow me,  
for I stand with God in the fullness of His goodness.”

#### What This Vision Means

This vision opens the third part by showing that human sin is not private. It reaches outward into the very elements. Creation itself groans when humanity turns from righteousness. The disorder of the soul becomes a disorder in the world.

The first image is the voice of self-exaltation. It wants to tower over hills and fields, to be unmatched, to be seen as singular and unsurpassable. Yet its form is broken and incomplete: no hands, no belly, no back. This suggests a soul that wants greatness but lacks wholeness. Its mouth is closed, yet it boasts. Its nose is smeared with mud, showing that pride, however lofty its claims, remains earth-stained.

The fine golden thread hints that even here, divine order has not fully withdrawn. God still sets a limit. Against this image stands Humility, speaking not from weakness but from radiant stability. Humility does not need to invent itself. It shines because it abides with God.

#### Living This Vision

It is easy to imagine pride as loud and obvious.  
But often it is quieter.

It appears whenever we need to be above others.  
Whenever we cannot bear being ordinary.  
Whenever we secretly insist: no one is like me.

This vision invites us to notice how exhausting that posture is.  
Pride must always compare, always defend, always elevate itself.

Humility is different.  
It does not mean pretending to be small.  
It means refusing the lie.

You do not need to become larger than others to belong to God.  
You do not need to shout over the mountains.

When your heart begins to strain toward superiority,  
return to what is real.  
Thank God for the good in others.  
Receive your life as gift, not conquest.

Humility is not humiliation.  
It is rest.  
It is the soul standing in truth, bright enough to endure every storm.
