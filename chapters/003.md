## 3 - The Sound Before the Word

> *Before clarity, there is a quiet calling. Before the word, there is a sound.*

I saw a soft radiance resting like a luminous cloud—not concealing in darkness, but gently holding what had not yet fully emerged into sight. It moved with the tenderness of breath across still water, quiet and unhurried, carrying within itself the feeling of something alive yet not completely formed. There was no sharp outline, no immediate explanation. Only the sense that beneath the surface of silence, something sacred was slowly awakening.

Within the radiance, tones began to stir. They were not words in the ordinary sense, yet they carried the beginning of meaning within them, like music heard before dawn or the distant echo of a bell moving through mist. The sound did not demand interpretation. It did not force understanding upon those who heard it. Instead, it called gently and repeatedly, inviting the soul not into certainty, but into deeper listening.

And those who remained within that quiet Presence did not struggle to grasp what was unfolding. They listened with patience. They allowed the sound to move through them like rain sinking slowly into dry earth. In their listening, something within them began to soften and open—not through effort, but through recognition. It was as though the resonance itself was shaping an inner space where truth could gradually take root.

Then, almost imperceptibly, the light deepened. What had first been sensed only faintly became inwardly known. Not as knowledge imposed from outside, but as something revealed from within the sound itself, as though meaning had always been hidden there waiting patiently to unfold. The cloud did not vanish once clarity arrived. It became luminous from within, and what had once seemed distant or mysterious now stood quietly present—whole, alive, and gently understood.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons in life when something begins stirring beneath the surface of awareness long before we can explain it. A longing arises. A feeling lingers after a conversation. A quiet pull remains in the heart without clear direction or language. Many people rush to define these moments immediately, anxious to resolve uncertainty before it has had time to ripen into wisdom. Yet the soul often grows differently than the mind expects.

Some truths arrive slowly, like dawn spreading across a field before the sun itself becomes visible. We begin by sensing rather than knowing. By listening rather than concluding. In these moments, spiritual life becomes less about mastering answers and more about learning to remain present to what is quietly unfolding within us.

You may notice how often the Sacred speaks through subtle things—a line from a book that stays with you unexpectedly, a moment of stillness while driving home at dusk, a tenderness that appears in the middle of grief, a sudden clarity that comes only after long waiting. None of these moments are incomplete simply because they are unfinished. They are invitations into deeper attentiveness.

Over time, the soul learns that clarity is rarely forced into existence. It emerges gently through faithful listening. What once felt hidden begins to glow from within. And sometimes we discover that the meaning we sought was already present from the beginning, carried quietly inside the sound before the word.
