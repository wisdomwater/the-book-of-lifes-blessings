## 3 - The Bitter Smoke

> The soul hardens quietly.  
> A heart that refuses love slowly forgets how to live.

In the dark fog where the vices gathered, I saw another image appear.

This fourth figure had the height of a man, but its form was like thick smoke. It had no arms or legs, no movement or strength. Only two great black eyes stared out from the shadow. The figure did not walk or rise. It simply remained fixed in place within the darkness.

And it spoke:

“I did not create the world. I did not bring anyone into existence. Why should I wear myself out working for others? I will not labor unless it benefits me.

“If I show kindness to someone, what does that give me? If I spend my time caring for others, what peace will I gain?

“So I will do nothing—neither good nor evil. I will take care of myself and let every person take care of their own life.”

Then a voice answered from the stormy cloud above.

It was the voice of Mercy.

“O heart of stone, what are you saying?

“The flowering plants give their fragrance freely. Even the silent stone releases moisture from its depths. All creation shares what it has been given.

“The creatures of the world serve one another. Yet you stand outside this living harmony.

“You do not truly deserve the human form you wear, for nothing flows from you but coldness. You are like bitter smoke rising from corruption.”

The voice continued, gentle yet strong:

“But I am a living plant filled with soft and vital strength. I grow in the air and in the dew. My life overflows so that I may help others.

“When the Creator spoke the word that brought all things into being, I was there. I was part of the harmony that called creation into service and care.

“My eyes see every need. I make the suffering of others my own.

“I lift up those whose spirits are crushed. I soothe pain like healing balm.

“My words bring comfort.

“But you are only smoke—dark and bitter.”

Then another figure appeared.

This fifth image had a strange and distorted body. Its head was human, but one ear was enormous like the ear of a hare, covering almost its entire head. The rest of its body twisted like a boneless worm curled inside its burrow, hiding as though wrapped in itself.

And it spoke in a harsh voice:

“I do not want to offend anyone. If I anger people, I might lose their help or protection.

“So I will honor the rich and powerful. I will not trouble myself with the poor or the holy who cannot benefit me.

“I will remain agreeable to everyone so that I can live in comfort. Whether people do good or evil, I will remain silent.

“Sometimes it is better to lie than to speak the truth. Better to gain something than to lose. Better to flee from conflict than to fight for anything.

“Those who speak truth often lose everything. Those who fight sometimes die.

“So I will stay safe in my own house.”

Again the voice from the cloud responded.

This time it was the voice of Divine Victory.

“You wandered from justice when you lifted your voice against God and refused to imitate righteousness. Because of this, you deceive others whenever the opportunity arises.

“You have no integrity.

“But I carry the sword of God’s strength. With it I strike down injustice.

“I will shatter your lies and reduce your false gains to dust.

“I do not live among ashes and empty words. I seek the spring of living water.

“With the wisdom of God’s truth I fight against the ancient serpent, and I will always remain with the living God.”

Then I saw a sixth image.

Its form was monstrous and twisted.

Its face was human, but its mouth resembled a scorpion’s sting. Its eyes were distorted. Its hands ended in long claws. Its body looked like a crab, its legs like lobsters, and its feet like vipers.

It was caught inside a turning mill wheel. With its hands it grasped the upper spokes, and with its feet the lower ones as the wheel spun endlessly.

From its mouth burst words like burning sparks:

“I will crush anyone who offends me. Why should I endure insult?

“If someone does wrong to me, I will strike them with my sword. I will beat them with my staff.”

And the wheel continued to turn.

#### What This Vision Means

Hildegard reveals how different forms of spiritual darkness take shape inside the human heart.

The fourth vice—Hardness of Heart—refuses compassion. It does not actively harm others. Instead, it simply refuses to care. This indifference appears calm, even reasonable, yet it slowly suffocates the soul. Mercy answers by reminding us that all creation is built on mutual care. Life flourishes through generosity.

The fifth vice—Cowardly Laziness—hides behind comfort and safety. It flatters the powerful and ignores the vulnerable. It prefers peace at any cost, even if that peace requires silence in the face of injustice. Divine Victory reminds us that truth sometimes demands courage and conflict.

The sixth vice—Anger—appears as a creature trapped in a spinning wheel. This image reveals something profound: anger often traps the one who carries it. The more it spins, the tighter the soul becomes bound within its cycle of retaliation.

Each vice begins with a familiar thought: *Why should I bother? Why should I risk? Why should I endure?*

Yet Hildegard’s vision shows that the virtues answer with a different question: *What kind of life do you wish to live?*

A life of fear, indifference, and resentment slowly collapses into darkness. But a life shaped by mercy, courage, and truth participates in the harmony of creation itself.

#### Living This Vision

These vices are not distant monsters. They whisper quietly in everyday moments.

Hardness of heart appears when we see suffering but scroll past it. When compassion feels inconvenient.

Cowardly laziness appears when we avoid speaking truth because it might cost us something.

And anger appears whenever we replay old wounds and imagine revenge.

Hildegard’s vision invites us to pause when these impulses arise. Instead of reacting automatically, we can ask a deeper question: *What virtue is being invited here?*

Where hardness appears, mercy can soften the heart.

Where fear seeks safety, courage can take a small step toward truth.

Where anger burns, patience can break the spinning wheel.

None of this happens instantly. The virtues grow slowly, like plants nourished by quiet rain.

But every small choice toward compassion, courage, and restraint pulls the soul out of the fog and back toward the light where the hymn of praise is still being sung.
