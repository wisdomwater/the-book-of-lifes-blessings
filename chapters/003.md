## 3 — The Garden That Remains

> *Beauty passes through your hands*  
> *Love roots itself within you*

A garden opened in quiet abundance.

Branches stretched wide,  
heavy with blossoms—  
soft colors unfolding  
in the gentle light.

Petals drifted on the air,  
resting briefly on the skin  
before falling  
back to the earth.

Beneath the tree,  
a figure stood  
gathering flowers close.

Not hurried—  
but intent.

As if holding them tightly  
might keep them from fading.

For a moment,  
everything felt full.

Color.  
Fragrance.  
Warmth.

But slowly,  
almost imperceptibly,  
the blossoms began to loosen.

Their edges softened.  
Their brightness dimmed.

One by one,  
they returned to the soil  
from which they came.

The branches grew still.

Not empty—  
but quiet.

And beneath the tree,  
something shifted.

The hands that once reached outward  
began to open.

The gaze, once fixed on what could be held,  
softened inward—

where something deeper  
had always been growing.

Not a garden of petals,  
but of roots.

Hidden.  
Steady.  
Unfading.

A life not gathered,  
but given.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when beauty feels like something you must hold onto.

A season.  
A feeling.  
A version of yourself  
you wish would remain.

You may notice how quickly the hand closes—  
how gently, and yet how firmly,  
you try to keep what is already changing.

Not out of greed,  
but out of longing.

Something in you recognizes beauty  
and wants to stay near it.

But there is another movement,  
quieter,  
less urgent.

A soft invitation  
to receive  
without grasping.

To enjoy what is here  
without asking it  
to remain.

You may begin to sense  
that what you truly long for  
is not the flower itself—

but the life  
that gives rise to it.

And that life  
has never been outside you.

It is already taking root  
beneath the surface,  
growing in ways  
that do not fade with time.

You are not losing beauty.

You are being led  
into a deeper way of seeing it.
