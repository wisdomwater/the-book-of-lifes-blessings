## 134 - The Warmth That Gives Life

> *What burns without rest can be made gentle*  
> *What reaches can learn to receive*

I saw a fire  
moving without stillness—  
rising,  
falling,  
reaching for more to consume.

It flickered with intensity,  
never satisfied,  
always searching  
for something to draw into itself.

And around it,  
the air felt strained—  
as though everything near  
was being asked to give  
without rest.

Then,  
as quietly as dawn,  
another presence came.

A warmth—  
steady,  
even,  
unhurried.

It did not leap.  
It did not grasp.

It simply remained.

And where it rested,  
things were not consumed—  
they were nourished.

The ground softened.  
The air grew clear.

What had been restless  
began to settle.

The fire did not vanish—  
it changed.

Its movement slowed.  
Its heat deepened.

It became a gentle flame—  
one that could be drawn near,  
one that could give life  
without taking it away.

And I saw  
that the same fire  
that once consumed  
could also illuminate—  
when it was held  
within what is true.

#### Living the Vision

Desire can feel urgent—  
as though it must be followed  
without pause.

It can pull you inward,  
narrowing your attention,  
making everything else  
feel distant.

You may notice  
how quickly it asks  
to be satisfied—  
and how little space  
it leaves for anything else.

But there is another way  
to meet it.

Not by pushing it away,  
but by slowing down  
long enough  
to feel it fully.

What is this desire seeking?

Connection?  
Comfort?  
Relief?

When you allow the question  
to remain open,  
the urgency begins to soften.

Desire becomes clearer—  
less demanding,  
more honest.

You can hold it  
without being carried by it.

And in that space,  
it begins to change.

What once felt consuming  
becomes something you can guide—  
something that can serve life  
instead of draining it.

You are not losing desire.

You are learning  
how to let it become  
a light.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Steady what is restless  
>
> **Exhale:** Let my desire give life
