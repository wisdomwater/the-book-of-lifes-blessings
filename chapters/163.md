## Day 163 - The Blind Wandering Heart

> *Inconstancy promises freedom, but leaves the soul scattered. The heart that will not be rooted becomes like an idol, unable to hear the living voice of God.*

After this, I saw other evil spirits among the crowd I had seen before.

They cried out with loud voices:

“We will search with our own skill. We will investigate everything. We will grasp whatever we can.”

These spirits stirred human beings toward inconstancy. They urged them to move from one thing to another, never resting, never becoming steady.

Then I saw a great fire.

Within it were many worms of many kinds. There the souls were punished who, while living in the body, had been inconstant in word and deed.

They burned because their fickleness had deceived many. They were tormented by worms because their many treacheries had joined themselves to vanity.

And I saw and understood these things.

Then I heard a voice from the living light, saying:

“The punishments you see are true. Those who wish to reject the spirits of fickleness must turn away from this vice. Let each person take up the discipline suited to their life, so that inconstancy may be driven out.”

Then Inconstancy itself spoke:

“How can I walk on only one path when there are so many choices before me? The pastures where I feed are not mine. Those I think are friends become enemies. Those I meet as friends are only near me for a moment.

So I can make no stable covenant.

As others treat me, so I treat them. If I submit too much, they will trample me. I will praise what I wish and despise what I find worthless. I will hide from what I fear. I will keep my own will.”

Inconstancy spoke of searching and testing, of grabbing one thing and then another, afraid to fall, yet never knowing who it was.

“Heaven does not speak to me,” it said. “The earth does not help me. No creature invites me to remain.”

Those ruled by fickleness speak this way because their eyes are blind to faith. Their ears are deaf to truth. Their tongues turn away from the law. Their hearts are stone toward the love of God.

They trust neither God nor human beings.

They become like idols—without living knowledge of God, yet whispering many deceptions to others.

So let those who desire God flee from inconstancy. Let them reject false appearance and deception. Let them draw near to what is stable and firm in God.

#### What This Vision Means

Inconstancy is not simple curiosity. It is the refusal to become rooted. It is the restless will that wants every path open, but commits to none. It fears being trapped, but becomes imprisoned by its own motion.

Hildegard shows this vice as a scattered, suspicious voice. It cannot trust friends. It cannot trust God. It cannot trust creation. It keeps its own will as a fortress, but that fortress becomes loneliness.

The punishment mirrors the condition of the soul. Fire reveals the burning unrest beneath fickleness. Worms reveal the hidden decay of divided loyalties and small betrayals.

The tragedy of inconstancy is spiritual numbness. The eyes no longer see faith. The ears no longer hear truth. The heart becomes like stone.

#### Living This Vision

Many of us know this restlessness.

We change direction when commitment becomes uncomfortable. We question every path when obedience begins to cost us something. We call it freedom, but sometimes it is fear.

This vision invites us to become steady—not rigid, but rooted.

To be faithful is not to know everything. It is to remain with what is true long enough for love to grow. It is to stop grasping at every pasture and begin trusting the Shepherd.

Today, choose one good thing and remain with it.
