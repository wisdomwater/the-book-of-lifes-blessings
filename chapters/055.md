## Day 55 – The Fire That Cleanses

> *I heard that the flames I saw were not only for torment, but for healing what had not yet been made whole.*

And a voice came from the Living Light, speaking with clarity:

“These things are true, as you see them—and even more than you can see.”

The sufferings I had witnessed were not without purpose. They were not empty punishments, nor were they given in cruelty. They were cleansing.

I understood then that some souls, having left the world too soon, had not yet been fully purified. Others had struggled, had begun to turn, but had not completed their transformation. And so, what remained unfinished was brought into completion.

These flames refined them.

They passed through what they had not yet surrendered.

They were washed in what they had once resisted.

Yet even here, mercy was present.

For I saw that the works of the living—the prayers, the love, the offerings of those still walking the earth—could reach these souls. The virtues of the saints also moved like light, touching them, easing their burden.

Nothing done in love was lost.

Everything offered in grace found its way into the hidden places.

And God was present in all of it.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals purification as an extension of divine mercy.

Not all transformation is completed within the span of earthly life. Some souls depart still in process—still learning, still being freed from attachments and distortions.

The fire represents not rejection, but continuation.

It is the completion of what was begun.

The role of the living is also profound. Acts of love, prayer, and intercession are not isolated—they participate in a greater communion. They become channels through which grace flows beyond what is seen.

This vision reshapes the idea of judgment. It is not simply a verdict—it is a process that restores what is incomplete.

God does not abandon what He has begun.

#### Living This Vision

There is something deeply hopeful here.

You do not need to be perfect.

You are invited to be sincere.

The work of transformation is ongoing, and even what feels unfinished in you is not wasted. Every small turning, every act of love, every moment of repentance matters.

And beyond that—your love for others matters more than you know.

Your prayers, your compassion, your quiet offerings… they ripple outward.

They reach places you cannot see.

The invitation is to live with this awareness:

Nothing done in love is ever lost.
