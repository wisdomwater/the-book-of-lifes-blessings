## 165 - The Softened Ground

> *What has closed can open again*  
> *The breath of God meets what is willing to soften*

I saw a stretch of earth  
hardened by long dryness—  
cracked,  
unyielding,  
holding its shape  
against all touch.

Nothing grew there.

The surface resisted  
even the lightest step,  
as if it had forgotten  
how to receive.

And in that stillness,  
a quiet presence drew near—  
not with force,  
not with demand.

Only a nearness  
that carried warmth.

For a time,  
nothing seemed to change.

The ground remained firm.  
The surface held.

Then,  
softly,  
a mist began to settle—  
barely visible,  
like breath upon the earth.

It did not rush.

It lingered.

And slowly,  
almost imperceptibly,  
the edges of the cracks  
began to soften.

Not broken open—  
just eased.

The hardness loosened  
its hold.

And beneath the surface,  
something stirred—  
a memory of life,  
waiting.

The ground did not become  
a garden in a moment.

But it was no longer closed.

It had begun  
to receive again.

#### Living the Vision

There are places within you  
that may feel set  
and unmoving.

Thoughts you return to.  
Responses that come quickly.  
A sense of holding your ground  
no matter what is offered.

It can feel like strength.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a quiet distance—  
a place where nothing new  
seems to take root.

Something in you  
may be ready  
for a different movement.

Not sudden.  
Not forced.

You may begin  
by allowing a small pause—  
a moment  
where you do not defend,  
do not resist,  
do not close.

Just for a breath.

In that space,  
you may feel  
a gentle softening—  
not losing yourself,  
but becoming available  
to something more.

You are not asked  
to surrender all at once.

Only to notice  
where you can soften  
even slightly.

And to trust  
that life  
enters through openness.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Soften what is closed  
>
> **Exhale:** Breathe life in me
