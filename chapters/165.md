## Day 165 - The Hardened Heart

> *A heart that refuses to soften cannot receive the breath of God. What resists love becomes barren, even while it believes itself strong.*

After this, I saw among the crowd other evil spirits who cried out loudly:

“Who is this God who causes such trouble?”

And they stirred the hearts of human beings toward stubbornness, urging them to harden themselves and refuse to yield.

Then I saw a place of darkness, thick and burning with pitch and sulfur.

Within it, souls were tormented with great lamentation—those who, while living in the body, had been obstinate in their minds.

They rose within the darkness, straining against God as if their strength could oppose Him.

They suffered the burning pitch because they had not shared in virtue. They were scorched by sulfur because they had rejected kindness. And they cried out in anguish because they had not lifted their hearts in longing toward God.

And I saw and understood these things.

Then a voice from the living light spoke:

“The punishments you see are true. Let those who have been stubborn turn away from this hardness. Let them humble themselves, and seek God with sincerity, lest they suffer the same fate.”

And I understood more deeply.

Those who cling to stubbornness are like the dead. They do not see. They do not hear. They do not move by the breath of God.

Stubbornness is like a mole, digging blindly, casting up the earth and overturning what is good.

It is like a spark that leaps upward for a moment, only to fade into nothing.

It refuses to be softened. It refuses to be changed.

But I was shown another way.

I remembered Job, the servant of God, who endured suffering without turning his heart against the Lord. His patience rose like a fragrant offering. His trust remained, even when all else was stripped away.

But stubbornness has no such patience. It is hard as rock and barren as dry land.

Those who turn away from it and join themselves to God will receive a great reward.

#### What This Vision Means

Stubbornness is not strength. It is resistance to transformation.

In this vision, stubbornness appears as a kind of inner death. The soul loses its ability to respond—to hear truth, to receive love, to be moved by grace. What once could grow becomes hardened and closed.

The images are striking: the mole that blindly disturbs the ground, the spark that briefly rises but cannot endure, the barren land that produces nothing. Each reveals a life cut off from living connection.

In contrast, Job stands as a living witness. He suffers deeply, yet remains open. His heart does not close. His patience becomes fertile ground where grace can still take root.

The difference is not suffering or ease—but openness.

#### Living This Vision

Stubbornness often disguises itself as certainty, independence, or strength.

But inwardly, it may be a refusal to listen, to change, or to be softened. It can appear when we cling tightly to our own way, even when it leads us away from love.

This vision invites a gentle question:

Where has my heart become hard?

To live this vision is not to force ourselves into weakness, but to allow ourselves to be softened again. To remain teachable. To remain open—even when it is uncomfortable.

Softness is not defeat. It is the beginning of life.
