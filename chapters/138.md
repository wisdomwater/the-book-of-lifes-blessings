## Day 138 - The Zither of Prayer

> *Forgetfulness of God begins when the soul turns only toward what it can see, touch, and control.*

Then I saw a third image.

Its head was like a salamander, and the rest of its body was like a lizard. Before it appeared a black cloud, turbulent and gloomy, mixed with a dense white cloud. The figure placed its front feet upon the cloud and spoke.

“If God ignores me, and I do not want to know him, why should I turn away from my own will?

“When God does not love me, I do not feel him. So I will consider only what favors me, what I desire, what I understand, and what pleases me.

“Many speak to me of another life, strange and unseen. I do not know it. I do not feel it. No one can teach it to me.

“Many say, ‘Do this,’ or ‘Do that.’ They point me toward God, toward life, toward reward. But many tyrants also come with wonderful promises, and their agreements turn out false.

“What I do must fit my own plan.

“I do not want many gods, many teachers. If there is a God, surely he knows me.”

Then a voice answered from the storm-cloud.

“O unbridled perdition, what are you saying? Who created you? Who gave you life?

“God.

“Why do you not recognize that you did not make yourself?

“I invoke God. I ask him for all that I need. I know his commandments, and I follow them with reverence.

“My impulse is the knowledge of the good. Through it I perceive God. In it I play the zither of prayer when I worship him. Through it I recognize him.

“If I return to what is worn out and passing away, I turn away from God.

“It is not the earth by itself that gives food, clothing, and all that humanity needs. It is God. People see things appear, but they do not see how they grow. They only know that life rises by the power of God.

“No one but God gives life, even to the smallest thing in the world.

“So the human being serves God with devotion in all works and turns away from evil, lest the will become corrupted.

“I wear the belt of abstinence. I remain in the joyful flower of holiness. Under the banner of God, I lead the ordered army of the King where God works.”

#### What This Vision Means

Forgetfulness of God speaks in the language of self-enclosure. It does not deny God with argument as much as with indifference. It says, “I do not feel him, so I will follow only myself.”

This is a familiar temptation. The unseen life feels distant. The visible world feels urgent. Conflicting voices surround us. Some voices promise holiness. Others promise power, pleasure, or escape. The soul grows weary and says, “I will trust only what fits my plan.”

Holiness answers by returning to origin.

Who made you?  
Who gives life?  
Who causes growth beneath the surface?

The virtue does not offer a complicated defense. It remembers. It prays. It listens. It wears abstinence like a belt—not as rejection of life, but as a way of staying gathered and awake.

The zither of prayer is a beautiful image. Prayer becomes music in the soul. It tunes the inner life toward God, so the soul can recognize what is good.

Holiness is ordered remembrance.

#### Living This Vision

Forgetting God rarely happens all at once.

It happens when life becomes crowded. When fear becomes loud. When disappointment makes us numb. When we stop asking what is good and ask only what is useful.

This vision invites us back into remembrance.

Not frantic devotion. Not forced certainty. Just a return to the source.

A breath.  
A prayer.  
A moment of gratitude.  
A choice to do the good you already know.

When you cannot feel God, you can still turn toward God. When you cannot see growth, you can trust that life is still rising beneath the soil.

Holiness is not escape from the world. It is seeing the world as held by God.
