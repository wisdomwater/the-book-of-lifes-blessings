## 44 - The Wings That Hold You

> *Even where light feels distant, you are not alone. Love stretches wider than the places you fear.*

I saw a figure standing quietly at the edge of a vast horizon where the light had begun to soften into evening stillness. The air there felt spacious and calm, as though the world itself were pausing between what had already been known and what had not yet fully appeared. The figure turned gently toward the dimmer places ahead—not with fear or hesitation, but with a quiet and steady intention.

From him extended great wings.

They were broad and living, filled not with force but with presence. One wing curved softly behind him, resting like memory held in peace—everything carried, nothing denied. Another opened before him toward the unseen future, welcoming what had not yet arrived without anxiety or resistance. And from his shoulders the wings stretched outward in both directions, holding past and future, sorrow and hope, all things within a deep and quiet balance.

The wings did not strain against the wind or beat frantically through the air.

They simply remained open.

Steady.

Sheltering.

Beneath them there was a profound sense of safety—not the safety of escape from uncertainty, but the safety of being held within it. What might otherwise have felt exposed or abandoned rested gently beneath their covering.

And within the shelter of these wings, something else unfolded.

It felt like pages turning silently within a great and living story. Lives were being carried there—not as burdens to be endured, but as wisdom gathered tenderly into light. Every joy, every grief, every unfinished longing seemed held within a compassion vast enough to contain it all without being overwhelmed.

Above the figure gathered a soft radiance, luminous like a cloud filled with hidden life. Within it moved a quiet procession, a continual rising toward something beyond ordinary sight. Nothing about the movement felt hurried. It unfolded with the patience of eternity itself.

And though the horizon ahead remained open and unfinished, there was no fear within it.

Only the deep knowing that nothing was lost.

Everything was still being held.

Still being guided.

Still being drawn gently forward beneath the wide and faithful shelter of love.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons in life when clarity feels far away. We stand at the edge of circumstances we do not fully understand—uncertain about the future, uncertain about ourselves, uncertain about where the path ahead might lead. In these moments the soul can feel exposed, vulnerable, almost untethered.

And yet beneath the uncertainty there is often another presence quietly waiting to be noticed.

Not loud.

Not controlling.

But steady.

Many people imagine they are alone whenever answers fail to appear quickly. But the deeper spiritual life reveals that accompaniment does not disappear simply because clarity has not yet arrived. Love remains present even within the dim places of our journey. Sometimes especially there.

This presence does not always remove uncertainty immediately. Instead, it holds us gently within it. We begin to sense that we are being carried by something larger than our own understanding—a wisdom patient enough to accompany us through incomplete seasons without demanding immediate resolution.

The soul slowly learns that it does not need to see the entire horizon in order to continue walking.

There is a kind of trust that grows not from certainty, but from repeated experiences of being sustained even when life feels unfinished. A quiet realization emerges: I am still held here. I am still being guided. I am still moving, even when I cannot yet see where the path is leading.

And over time, this awareness softens fear.

Not because all questions disappear, but because the heart begins to recognize the faithful presence beneath them. We discover that the wings of divine love stretch wider than our confusion, wider than our sorrow, wider even than the places we most fear losing ourselves.

You do not have to carry yourself alone into the unknown.

Even now, something unseen and compassionate continues drawing you gently toward fullness.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Carry me forward

**Exhale:** Into Your fullness
