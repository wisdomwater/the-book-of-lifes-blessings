## Day 44 – The Serpent’s Voice

> *The enemy does not create—he distorts, multiplying shadows to confuse the light.*

Then I heard the voice of the ancient serpent.

It did not speak in weakness.

It spoke with defiance.

“I will use my power,” it said,  
“and I will fight.”

And I saw what this meant.

From its mouth poured corruption—  
thick, foul, spreading.

It cast out vices like seeds—  
scattering them among humanity.

And as they took root, they grew into distortions.

False teachings.  
Twisted desires.  
Confusion dressed as truth.

It stirred division.  
It mocked what was good.

It took what was meant to shine—  
the lives of the faithful—  
and sought to stain them.

To make what was pure appear suspect.  
To turn admiration into disgust.

And a fog spread over the earth.

Dark.  
Heavy.

Within it, voices rose:

“Why worship what you cannot see?  
Why honor what you do not know?”

And I saw how this persuasion worked—  
pulling the mind away from what is invisible yet true,  
toward what is visible yet empty.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the strategy of the enemy: not creation, but distortion.

The serpent cannot make something new—it can only twist what already exists. It takes truth and bends it, making it harder to recognize.

The fog represents confusion—a state where clarity is lost and discernment is weakened.

The persuasion to worship only what is seen reflects a deeper temptation: to trust only what is immediately tangible, rejecting the unseen reality of God.

This leads to idolatry—not only of physical objects, but of ideas, desires, and self-made truths.

This vision calls for discernment—the ability to see beyond appearances.

#### Living This Vision

You are constantly being shaped by what you listen to.

Voices.  
Ideas.  
Assumptions.

Some of them sound convincing.

But not all lead to life.

Pause and ask:

Does this draw me toward truth?  
Or away from it?

Does it deepen clarity?  
Or create confusion?

You do not need to engage every voice.

You can choose what you receive.

And what you release.
