## 44 — The Clarity Beneath the Noise

> *What is true does not strain to be seen*  
> *Clarity waits beneath the surface*

I heard a voice  
moving through the air—

not loud,  
but insistent,  
like a thread  
that wove itself  
into everything.

It did not create.  
It reshaped.

It took what was simple  
and made it tangled—  
what was clear  
and made it clouded.

A mist began to gather—

soft at first,  
then thickening,  
blurring the edges  
of what had once been easy to see.

Within it,  
many whispers—

questions,  
suggestions,  
half-formed truths  
that turned upon themselves.

Nothing felt solid.

Everything shifted  
just enough  
to unsettle.

And yet,  
beneath it all,  
something remained—

quiet,  
unchanged,  
steady as the ground  
beneath unseen roots.

A deeper clarity.

It did not argue  
with the mist.

It did not force its way through.

It simply was—  
present,  
whole,  
untouched.

And where it was noticed,  
the mist began to thin—  
not all at once,  
but gently,  
like morning  
lifting the fog.

#### Living the Vision

Your thoughts feel crowded—

pulled in different directions,  
filled with voices  
that seem convincing  
yet leave you uncertain.

You may try  
to sort through them,  
to make sense  
of every thread.

But something in you  
already knows  
the difference  
between clarity  
and confusion.

You may notice it  
as a quiet grounding—  
a sense of steadiness  
beneath the movement.

Not loud.  
Not demanding.

Just present.

You do not need  
to follow every voice.

You can pause.

You can let the noise pass  
without giving it a home.

And gently,  
your attention can return  
to what is simple,  
what is real,  
what brings a quiet ease.

Clarity does not rush you.

It waits  
for you to notice  
what has never moved.

> **Inhale:** Clear my seeing

> **Exhale:** I rest in what is true
