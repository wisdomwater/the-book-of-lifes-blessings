## Day 150 - The Cloud Before God

> *Forgetfulness begins when the soul stops turning toward God and begins trusting only its own restless thoughts.*

Then I saw the third image: Forgetfulness of God.

It came after spiritual dullness, because when the heart grows numb, it slowly loses its desire for God. The soul becomes distracted by many questions, many plans, many restless suggestions. It begins to live as if it knows God, yet no longer wishes to be united with Him.

This figure had the head of a salamander, and the rest of its body was like a lizard. It was cold, quick, and difficult to hold. Its heart was stubborn. Its will resisted God. Its works were hurried and unrestrained.

Before it stood a cloud—black, turbulent, and dark—mixed with a thick white cloud.

This cloud revealed the confusion of those who forget God. Their thoughts become blackened by evil, stirred by unbelief, and darkened by changeable desires. Yet these things are mixed with the appearance of something good, like white cloud blended into shadow.

They make plans according to their own pleasure. They do more than desire asks, and less than love requires.

The figure placed its front feet upon the cloud.

And I understood: those who forget God place their first steps not on the path of salvation, but on the path of confusion. Their lives divide into two roads—forgetfulness of God and hardness of heart.

They listen only to what rises within themselves.

But Holiness answered this vice.

Holiness called the soul back.

It urged humanity not to abandon God in forgetfulness, but to desire Him truly. To remember not only with the mind, but with the whole life.

For the soul that remembers God becomes steady again.

The cloud begins to clear.

And the heart learns where to place its feet.


#### What This Vision Means

Forgetfulness of God is not simple distraction. It is a gradual separation.

The soul does not always reject God loudly. Sometimes it simply stops remembering. It becomes preoccupied with its own plans, fears, arguments, and desires. Slowly, God becomes distant—not because He has moved, but because the soul has turned elsewhere.

The lizard-like body suggests coldness and quick movement without depth. The mixed cloud shows how confusing this vice can be. Not everything appears evil. Some thoughts seem reasonable. Some plans seem wise. But when they are detached from God, they become unstable.

The danger is not thinking, planning, or questioning. The danger is doing these things without love, humility, or remembrance.

Holiness answers by calling the soul back into union.

To remember God is to return to the center.


#### Living This Vision

It is easy to forget God while still speaking about spiritual things.

We can think about life, worry about choices, analyze people, and make plans—yet never become quiet enough to return to the Presence.

This vision invites a gentle pause.

Before you step onto the cloud of anxious thought, ask:

Where are my feet resting?
Am I moving from fear or from trust?
Am I listening only to myself?

You do not need to solve everything before remembering God.

Remember first.

A single breath can become a doorway.
A whispered prayer can clear the cloud.
A moment of attention can bring the soul home.

God is not far.

But the heart must turn.
