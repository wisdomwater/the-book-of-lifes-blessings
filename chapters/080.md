## 80 - The Quiet Path of Yes

> *You do not lose yourself in surrender*  
> *You come home to what is true*

I saw a figure  
moving quickly—  
turning,  
shifting,  
never settling in one place.

Its path was uncertain,  
as though each step  
led into another direction.

The ground beneath it  
felt stirred,  
unsettled—  
like dust carried by restless wind.

And though it moved often,  
it did not seem to arrive.

Then,  
not far away,  
I saw a path.

Simple.  
Clear.

It did not call out.  
It did not demand to be followed.

It simply remained—  
steady beneath the light.

Beside it stood another presence,  
quiet,  
unhurried.

There was no force in it.  
Only a gentle readiness—  
as if waiting  
for a willing step.

When the figure paused—  
just for a moment—  
the movement within it softened.

The turning slowed.

And in that small stillness,  
it saw the path.

Nothing had changed around it.

But something within  
had become open enough  
to recognize  
what had always been there.

#### Living the Vision

At times, something in you resists.

A quiet tightening—  
a turning away  
from what is being asked.

It may come with reasons,  
with explanations,  
with a sense of certainty.

And yet,  
you may also feel  
a subtle unrest—  
a lack of clarity,  
a sense of moving  
without arriving.

There is another way  
that sometimes feels quieter,  
almost hidden.

A small willingness.  
A simple yes.

Not forced.  
Not pressured.

Just a gentle turning  
toward what you already sense  
is true.

You may notice  
that this yes  
does not diminish you.

It steadies you.

It brings a kind of coherence—  
where your steps  
begin to follow  
a clearer direction.

You do not need  
to resolve everything.

Only to take  
one honest step  
in alignment  
with what you have been given.

And let that be enough  
for now.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Open me to Your way  
>
> **Exhale:** I say yes in trust
