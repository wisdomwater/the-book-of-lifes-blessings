## Day 170 - The Wandering Voice

> *Restlessness that seeks to be everywhere finds no true home. What chases visibility loses depth and peace.*

Then I saw the second image.

It appeared childish, yet its face bore the beard of an old man.

It had no hair upon its head.

It hung in the darkness, wrapped in a cloth like a cradle, swinging back and forth in the wind.

At times it rose up.

At times it hid again within the cloth.

And it spoke:

“It is foolish to remain in one place.

I will be seen everywhere. My voice will be heard everywhere. My face will be known in all places.

The grass grows and the flowers bloom—should not man also display his glory?

I am like the grass in my thinking, and like the flower in my beauty.

So I will make myself known in all places.”

And I saw how it moved—never still, never rooted.

Then a voice from the storm replied:

“You will fall like a fading flower.

You will be trampled like dust on the road.

Your voice is empty. Your vision is distorted.

Your words are not tested by reason. You scatter yourself like snow, without purpose.

You do not partake of wisdom or discernment.

You live like birds with no dwelling.

You are ash and decay, and you will not find peace.”

And I understood the instability of this spirit.

#### What This Vision Means

This image reveals the desire for recognition without substance.

It is restless, unstable, and scattered. It seeks to be everywhere, to be seen and heard, but lacks grounding.

The contrast between youth and age shows confusion—immaturity cloaked in the appearance of wisdom.

The swinging motion reflects instability. There is no center, no rootedness.

The response exposes the emptiness beneath the display. Without depth, visibility becomes vanity.

#### Living This Vision

This speaks directly to our age.

The desire to be seen, known, validated—across many places, by many people.

But without grounding, this leads to exhaustion and emptiness.

This vision invites us to choose depth over visibility.

To be rooted rather than scattered.

To grow quietly rather than perform loudly.

Where can you become more present instead of more visible?
