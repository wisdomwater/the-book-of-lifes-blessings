## 109 - The Gathering Center

> *What has been scattered can return*  
> *Peace grows where life is gently held*

I saw a wide expanse  
like a restless sea.

Its surface moved without pause—  
waves rising,  
turning,  
spreading in every direction.

Nothing held.

Nothing remained  
where it was.

The water reached outward—  
again and again—  
as though searching  
for a place to rest.

But it did not find one.

Then,  
beneath the movement,  
something deeper appeared.

A stillness—  
quiet,  
unseen—  
like a center  
that had never been lost.

It did not stop the waves.

It held them.

And as it held them,  
their motion began to change.

Not all at once—  
but gradually.

The rising softened.  
The reaching slowed.

Until the surface itself  
began to settle—  
not empty,  
but gathered.

And I saw  
that the sea  
was not meant to scatter.

It was meant  
to rest within its depth.

To move  
without losing itself.

And the stillness remained—  
steady,  
anchoring—  
drawing everything  
back into place.

#### Living the Vision

Life can feel full—  
but not whole.

So many movements.  
So many thoughts.  
So many things  
pulling your attention.

You may feel  
as though you are everywhere at once—  
yet not fully present  
anywhere.

This is the feeling  
of being scattered.

And yet,  
there is a deeper place within you  
that does not move this way.

You may sense it  
when you pause—  
even briefly—  
and allow everything  
to settle.

A breath.  
A moment of stillness.  
A choice  
to do less.

You do not need  
to stop everything.

Only to return  
to what gathers you.

One step at a time.

You may begin  
with a small boundary—  
a gentle limit  
that creates space.

And in that space,  
something begins to come back together.

Your attention steadies.  
Your energy returns.  
Your heart feels more whole.

You were not made  
to be everywhere.

You were made  
to be present.

And in that presence,  
peace begins to grow.
