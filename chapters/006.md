## Day 6 — The Smoke of the Hard Heart

> *I saw how a heart can grow so cold that it no longer moves toward anyone.
> Yet mercy still speaks like living dew upon the earth.*

In the vision I saw a fourth image.

It had the stature of a man, yet it did not appear solid like the others. Its form was like thick smoke gathered into a human shape. It had no arms and no legs.

Only two great black eyes stared out from the darkness.

The figure did not rise or move. It remained fixed in the same place within the shadow I had seen before.

Then it spoke.

“I did not create anything. I brought no one into existence. Why then should I labor and wear myself out?

I will not do it.

I will not work for anyone unless it benefits me. God created everything and should care for it Himself.

If I am kind to someone and concern myself with their troubles, what gain do I receive?

I will do nothing—neither good nor evil.

If I spend my time showing compassion, what peace will that bring me? What kind of life would I have if I must pay attention to everyone who is joyful or suffering?

No.

I will care for myself, and each person can care for themselves.”

Then I heard a voice answer from the stormy cloud.

“O heart of stone, what are you saying?

The flowering plants release their fragrance. Even the stone gives moisture from its hidden depths. Every creature offers something of its fullness.

All creation serves humankind willingly.

Yet you are scarcely worthy of the human form you pretend to have. From you comes only a cruel gaze without mercy. You are like bitter smoke darkened by evil.

But I am a living plant filled with gentle vitality. I grow in the air and in the dew.

My heart is so full that I can help others.

I was present when the Creator spoke the word that brought all creatures into service for humankind. But you were not there.

With my eyes I see every need and make it my own. I help those who are crushed in spirit to rise again.

I am balm for pain.

My words bring healing.

But you—are only bitter smoke.”

#### What This Vision Means

The fourth image represents hardness of heart.

Unlike the previous figures, this one is almost formless. It lacks limbs because it refuses to act. It exists like smoke—present but without substance or life.

The large black eyes suggest a cold awareness. The figure sees others, but it refuses to respond.

This is the spiritual condition of indifference.

Hardness of heart does not always appear violent or openly cruel. Sometimes it simply withdraws from responsibility. It says, “That is not my concern,” or “Let others handle their own suffering.”

The figure believes it has found peace by avoiding involvement.

Mercy responds with a powerful image of creation itself. Every creature gives something of itself. Flowers release fragrance. Even stone releases moisture.

The universe is built upon generosity.

Mercy describes itself as a living plant nourished by dew. This image reflects life that continually gives and renews itself. Compassion does not drain life—it flows from a heart already filled by divine grace.

Where hardness withdraws, mercy moves toward.

#### Living This Vision

In a world full of suffering, the temptation toward indifference is strong.

We see so many problems—so many voices asking for help—that the heart can become numb. To protect ourselves from feeling overwhelmed, we may begin to close the door of compassion.

At first this feels like relief.

But over time something inside begins to grow cold.

The spiritual life invites us into a different posture. Mercy does not mean solving every problem or carrying every burden. Instead, it means remaining open to the humanity of others.

Sometimes mercy appears in very small acts: listening, offering kindness, speaking gently, or simply refusing to turn away.

These moments are like drops of dew upon dry ground.

They remind the soul that we were not created to live as isolated smoke drifting through darkness.

We were created to share life.

> Fill my heart with mercy
