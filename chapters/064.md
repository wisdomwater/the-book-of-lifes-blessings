## Day 64 — Fires of Blood and Blindness

> *When anger becomes action, it wounds both the other and the soul that carries it.*

I saw many forms of fire.

One burned with both heat and cold together, an unbearable tension. Within it, souls were tormented by every kind of worm. These were those who, in the fury of anger, had taken life.

They burned for the violence they had committed.

They froze for the blindness that had overtaken them, for they had not seen what they were doing.

And the worms pierced them, for they had acted without reverence for the life before them.

Then I saw another fire.

Here, two great and terrible worms exhaled into the flames. The souls within had killed for gain—for desire, for possession, for what they wanted to acquire.

The fire consumed them.

The worms tormented them.

For their actions had been driven not only by violence, but by greed.

Then I saw a deep pit, filled with boiling pitch and sulfur.

Frogs and scorpions surrounded it, striking fear into the souls within. These were the ones who had killed in fear—those who had acted out of the impulse to defend themselves before being harmed.

They burned from the heat of their action.

They were tormented for the haste that had ruled them.

They trembled, remembering the moment when fear overtook wisdom.

And I saw yet more.

Souls who had killed without intention were carried in restless winds, unsettled and without grounding. Others who had taken life in secret ways burned in fire, tormented by grotesque worms.

And near a clear well, I saw souls who had extinguished life before it had fully begun. Fire consumed them, and yet they could see their suffering reflected in the water, as if shown what had been lost.

And I understood:

Every act carries its imprint.

Nothing is without weight.

And yet all of this was shown not for despair—but for awakening.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision unfolds the many faces of destructive anger.

Not all violence is the same, yet all share a common root: a loss of awareness, a disconnection from the sacredness of life.

The fire represents the consequence of that disconnection. The cold reflects blindness—the inability to see clearly in the moment of action. The worms symbolize the lingering effects of choices made without reverence.

Even fear-driven or unintentional harm carries weight. The soul must come to terms with what has been done—not as condemnation, but as truth.

The most striking image is the clear well. It reflects not comfort, but recognition. The soul is shown what it has done, and in that seeing, transformation becomes possible.

This is not a vision of punishment alone.

It is a vision of accountability that leads to awakening.


#### Living This Vision

Most people will never commit physical violence.

But anger still has consequences.

Words can wound.

Silence can harm.

Neglect can diminish.

This vision invites you to take your inner life seriously.

To notice where anger begins to shape your actions—even in subtle ways.

And when you see it, do not turn away.

Look clearly.

Not with shame, but with honesty.

This is how change begins.

You are capable of harm—but also of healing.

You can choose differently.

You can slow down, breathe, and remember the sacredness of the person before you.

Even now, you can become someone who brings life rather than takes it.
