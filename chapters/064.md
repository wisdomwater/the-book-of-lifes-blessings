## 64 - The Returning to Reverence

> *Every life is held in quiet holiness*  
> *To see clearly is to move with care*

I saw a wide, open space  
bathed in a steady, gentle light.

Nothing burned here.

Nothing strained.

The air was clear—  
soft to breathe,  
free of tension.

At the center,  
a spring rose from the earth—  
pure,  
untroubled—  
its waters lifting upward  
before flowing outward  
in quiet streams.

Those who came near  
did not rush.

They slowed.

As if something within them  
remembered  
how to approach  
what is sacred.

When their hands touched the water,  
they grew still.

Not from fear—  
but from recognition.

The light rested on each face  
without judgment,  
revealing not what condemned,  
but what could be seen  
at last.

And in that seeing,  
something shifted.

Movements softened.

Breath deepened.

Where there had been tension,  
there was now space.

No voice instructed them.

No force restrained them.

Only the presence—  
clear,  
unwavering—  
inviting a different way  
of being.

To move gently.

To remain aware.

To honor what stands before them  
as something alive  
and worthy of care.

And the water continued to rise—  
unceasing,  
offered freely—  
restoring what had been forgotten.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
your actions come quickly—

before you have time  
to fully see.

A word spoken.  
A reaction formed.  
A movement made  
from impulse.

It is human.

And yet,  
you may notice afterward  
a quiet awareness—  
a sense that something  
was missed in the moment.

Another way begins here.

Not in regret,  
but in noticing.

You may find yourself  
pausing more often—  
just enough  
to feel what is present  
before you act.

A breath.

A moment of seeing.

And in that small space,  
something opens—  
a deeper recognition  
of the life before you,  
and the life within you.

You are not asked  
to be perfect.

Only to become aware.

To let your actions  
flow from presence  
rather than reaction.

And over time,  
what once felt automatic  
begins to soften—  
becoming more aligned  
with the quiet reverence  
that has always been there.
