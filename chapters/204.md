## Day 204 - The Dazzling Hidden Joy

> *There are joys beyond seeing, beyond hearing, beyond imagining. They are real, though hidden, waiting beyond the limits of the body.*

I beheld a radiant light—clear and powerful—and within it I perceived another light, far greater and without limit. It stretched beyond all boundaries, deeper than anything I had yet seen.

I longed to look into it.

But its brilliance overcame me.

It shone with such intensity that my sight faltered. I could not gaze into it, nor grasp its fullness. It was beauty beyond ornament, delight beyond delight, joy overflowing into joy. It carried a fullness so complete that no human eye has seen it, no ear has heard it, and no heart has fully held it while bound in mortal flesh.

Before me there appeared something like a seal.

Behind it were joys still hidden—greater and more numerous than all that had come before. I knew they were there, yet they were veiled from me, as if kept in sacred reserve.

My vision trembled under the weight of such glory.

And the Living Light spoke:

“These joys you glimpse are true. They are as they are shown—and there are more beyond them.”

So I stood, suspended between knowing and not knowing.

Held in wonder.

Drawn forward by a promise my body could not yet bear.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the limits of human perception when faced with divine reality. The layered lights symbolize increasing depths of spiritual awareness, each one surpassing the last. What is most striking is not only the beauty of what is seen—but the greater beauty that remains hidden.

The “seal” represents a sacred boundary. It is not a denial, but a protection. The fullness of divine joy cannot yet be received by the soul still bound to a fragile body and limited understanding. And yet, the glimpse is enough to awaken longing.

The voice affirms that what is seen is real—but incomplete. This is a central truth of the spiritual life: what we experience of God is genuine, yet always partial. There is always more.

This vision invites humility. It reminds us that the unseen is not empty—it is overflowing. And that what we cannot yet perceive is not absent, but lovingly withheld until we are ready.


#### Living This Vision

There are moments when you sense something deeper—an unexplained peace, a quiet joy, a pull toward something beyond words.

And then it fades.

It is easy to think nothing was really there.

But this vision suggests otherwise.

What you glimpse in fleeting moments may be a real participation in something far greater—something your current life cannot yet sustain fully. The longing itself is part of the gift.

Instead of grasping or trying to force clarity, you are invited to trust the hiddenness.

Not everything must be understood to be real.

There is a kind of faith that rests not in possession, but in anticipation. A quiet knowing that what you have tasted is only the beginning.

Let the glimpse be enough for now.

Let it draw you forward gently.
