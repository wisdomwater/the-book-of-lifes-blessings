## 20 - The Tower of Quiet Strength

> *What rises in light cannot be overcome. The soul is held where love abides.*

I saw a tower standing in the early light, rising from the earth with quiet dignity. Nothing about it felt hurried or defensive. It had not been built through striving or force, but seemed to have emerged slowly through patience and stillness, like stone shaped over many years by wind, weather, and faithful hands. The morning light rested gently upon its surface, revealing strength without hardness.

Its walls were open in places—not broken, but welcoming. Light moved freely through the spaces within it, illuminating rather than exposing. Near the summit, three clear windows faced outward toward the horizon, uncovered and receptive to the coming dawn. They seemed less like openings for watching the world below and more like places prepared to receive light without obstruction.

From within the tower, a quiet presence extended outward, like arms resting gently across its edges. There was no grasping in the movement. No desire to possess or control. Only offering. A steady kindness reaching outward without strain, like warmth shared from a hearth on a cold evening.

And the light resting upon those outstretched arms carried a healing softness. It did not burn or overwhelm. It restored.

Far below, movement stirred in the distance. Restless currents moved across the land, searching and striving, as though trying to rise upward through effort alone. There was tension in their motion—a reaching that never seemed fully at rest. Yet none of it could disturb what stood above in quiet stability.

For the tower did not resist the movement below.

It simply remained.

Its strength came not from opposing the storm, but from being rooted in something deeper than the storm could touch. And I understood then that true strength does not emerge from domination, fear, or endless self-protection. It rises from what dwells quietly within the soul—a living presence grounded in love, peace, and trust.

### Living the Vision

There are seasons when life feels inwardly unsettled. Thoughts race. Emotions pull in different directions. Anxiety searches constantly for certainty or control. At times it can feel as though something within us is always reacting—always bracing against pressure, disappointment, fear, or exhaustion.

And yet beneath this movement, many people eventually discover another place within themselves.

A quieter place.

It does not argue with every fear or attempt to overpower every difficult emotion. It simply remains present beneath them, steady as deep roots beneath shifting weather. We often become aware of this deeper center in very simple moments: pausing long enough to breathe fully, sitting quietly in stillness, walking slowly beneath trees, praying without needing immediate answers, or remembering—even briefly—that we are already held by something larger than our own striving.

This inner steadiness does not mean we never struggle. The winds of life still move around us. Restlessness still rises. Fear still visits the heart. But we slowly learn that not every movement within us requires complete identification or reaction. We are allowed to return, again and again, to the deeper place where peace remains quietly available beneath the surface.

Spiritual maturity is not about becoming emotionally invulnerable. It is about becoming rooted deeply enough that we no longer collapse beneath every passing storm. The soul grows stronger not through hardness, but through abiding in what nourishes life most deeply—love, presence, truth, compassion, and trust.

And perhaps this is the hidden mystery of quiet strength: that it does not need to announce itself loudly in order to endure. It remains because it is already resting within something greater than itself.
