## 43 - Beneath the Sheltering Wings

> *Love does not withdraw from what is broken*  
> *It gathers, restores, and holds all things in light*

I saw a vast expanse—  
creation spread wide and living,  
held in a quiet, radiant order.

Light moved through all things—  
through air and water,  
through stone and growing leaf—  
a harmony so gentle  
it could almost be missed.

And over this,  
I saw a great presence  
like outstretched wings—

not distant,  
not looming—

but near,  
curving inward  
as if to gather everything  
into a place of rest.

Beneath these wings,  
there was warmth.

A nearness  
that did not press,  
but invited.

Where the ground had been disturbed,  
it was covered.  
Where something had been torn,  
it was held.

Nothing was hurried back  
into place.

Nothing forced into wholeness.

Only gathered.

And in the shelter,  
something began to return—

not all at once,  
but gently—

like breath finding its rhythm again.

I saw that the light  
did not avoid what had been wounded.

It entered it.

It remained there.

Until even what had been broken  
could rest  
within its care.

And the wings did not close.

They stayed open—  
wide enough  
for all to come near.

#### Living the Vision

At times, you become aware  
of what has gone wrong—

within yourself,  
or in the world around you.

It can feel heavy.  
Irreversible.

You may wonder  
if anything can truly be restored.

But there is something deeper  
than what has been lost.

You may sense it  
in quiet moments—  
a presence  
that does not turn away  
from what is difficult.

It does not rush you.  
It does not demand.

It simply remains  
with you.

You do not need  
to fix everything  
before you come near.

You can bring  
what is unfinished,  
what is unclear,  
what still aches.

And as you do,  
you may begin to notice—  
a softening,  
a steadiness,  
a sense of being held  
even in what is unresolved.

This is how healing begins.

Not by escaping the wound,  
but by allowing it  
to be gathered  
into a love  
that does not leave.
