## 43 — The Quiet Return to Stillness

> *Not every voice leads you home*  
> *Stillness reveals what is true*

I saw a space  
filled with movement—

not the gentle flow of life,  
but a restless drifting.

It shifted  
without direction,  
never settling,  
never at ease.

Forms moved through it—  
light at first glance,  
yet unable to remain.

They wandered  
from one place to another,  
touching, leaving,  
never belonging.

And in their passing,  
they left traces—

subtle threads,  
barely seen,  
like patterns  
that invited attention  
but did not give rest.

Nothing was loud.  
Nothing demanded.

It was quieter than that—

a constant pull  
away from stillness.

Then I felt another presence—  
soft,  
steady,  
unmoving.

Where it touched the space,  
everything slowed.

What had been scattered  
began to gather.

What had been restless  
grew aware  
of its own movement.

And in that awareness,  
there was a choice—

not forced,  
not urgent—

but real.

To remain scattered,  
or to return  
to what is whole.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
your attention begins to scatter—

moving quickly  
from one thought to another,  
from one desire to the next.

Nothing seems clearly wrong,  
and yet  
something feels unsettled.

You may notice  
a quiet pull  
toward what is immediate,  
what distracts,  
what keeps you moving.

But there is also  
another presence within you—

steady,  
unhurried,  
patient.

You may feel it  
in a pause,  
in a breath,  
in a small awareness  
that invites you  
to stop.

Not to withdraw,  
but to return.

You do not need  
to analyze every thought.

You only need  
to notice  
what brings you into rest  
and what pulls you away.

And gently,  
again and again,  
you can come back—

to stillness,  
to clarity,  
to what is real.
