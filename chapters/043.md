## 43 - Beneath the Sheltering Wings

> *Love does not withdraw from what is broken. It gathers, restores, and holds all things in light.*

I saw a vast and living expanse stretched wide beneath a quiet radiance. Creation itself seemed held within a gentle and harmonious order—air moving softly over water, light resting upon stone, leaves opening toward the warmth that sustained them. Nothing strained to remain alive. Everything belonged within the great rhythm moving quietly through all things.

The light flowed everywhere.

Through rivers and fields, through the hidden roots beneath the earth, through the smallest living breath. Its presence was so gentle it could almost be overlooked, yet nothing existed outside its reach. It held creation together not through force, but through intimacy.

And above this living world, I saw a great presence like outstretched wings.

The wings did not loom over creation with power meant to intimidate. They curved inward with tenderness, drawing close as though desiring to gather everything beneath them into safety and rest. There was warmth beneath these wings—not the heat of pressure or demand, but the warmth of nearness freely offered.

Where the ground had been disturbed, it was covered gently.

Where something had been torn open, it was held carefully.

Nothing was forced back into wholeness before it was ready. Nothing broken was treated as a problem to solve quickly. Everything wounded was simply gathered first—received fully into compassionate presence.

And within that shelter, something began slowly returning to life.

Not dramatically.

Not all at once.

But gently, like breath finding its rhythm again after long fear or sorrow. The trembling eased. The guarded places softened. What had once expected abandonment slowly discovered it could rest without needing to defend itself constantly.

Then I understood something deeper still.

The light did not avoid woundedness.

It entered it.

It remained there patiently, refusing to leave until even what had been broken learned again how to rest within love. And the wings above never closed themselves away. They remained open—wide enough for every weary and unfinished soul to draw near.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when the brokenness of life becomes impossible to ignore. We see it within ourselves—in old wounds, repeated failures, grief, fear, exhaustion. We see it in the world around us—in injustice, loneliness, violence, and all the ways human beings struggle to love one another well. Sometimes the weight of this brokenness feels so large that restoration itself seems impossible.

And yet beneath this ache there remains another reality quietly holding us.

Many people imagine divine love as something available only after healing is complete, after clarity arrives, after wounds are hidden or resolved. But the deeper spiritual life reveals something very different. Love does not wait for perfection before drawing near. It moves toward woundedness precisely because woundedness longs to be held.

Healing often begins not through escaping pain, but through discovering we no longer carry it alone.

There is a profound transformation that happens when a person feels truly accompanied within what hurts most deeply. The nervous system softens. Defenses loosen. The soul begins slowly trusting that it does not need to earn tenderness through achievement or self-erasure. Even unresolved places within us can rest when they are no longer abandoned.

This does not mean every wound disappears immediately. Some forms of healing unfold slowly over years. Yet something essential changes the moment we realize we are still held within love even while unfinished.

You do not need to fix everything before coming near to the sacred.

You may bring your confusion, grief, anger, longing, weariness, and uncertainty exactly as they are. And within the shelter of compassionate presence, what once felt fractured begins gradually learning the shape of wholeness again.

This is how restoration often unfolds.

Not through force.

But through remaining long enough beneath the sheltering wings of love that the soul remembers it belongs there.
