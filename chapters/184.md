## Day 184 - The Poison of Scorn

> *Foolish speech dries the fruit of the soul. What mocks truth cannot lead us into joy.*

After this, I saw other evil spirits in the crowd.

They cried out loudly:

“Is what God does so great? Lucifer is great, and we will always be with him.”

These spirits urged human beings toward fatuity. They taught them words and actions of scorn toward everything.

Then I saw a flame of fire.

Within it swarmed monstrous worms.

There the souls were tormented who had lived in folly and had shown no respect for others.

They burned in the flame because their passion for foolishness had wounded many. They were tortured by worms because, loving this vice, they had forgotten God.

And I saw and understood.

Then I heard the voice of the living light:

“The punishments you see are true. Those who have consented to fatuity, and wish to drive away the spirits that suggest it, must discipline themselves and learn silence with measured correction.”

Then I understood the nature of this vice.

Fatuity is full of forgetfulness of God.

It wants to shatter truth with false words.

It is like a stinking cloud that dries up every fruit.

It does not rise in honesty. It is not clothed with the garment of shame or holy discipline.

Instead, it tries to uproot what is true beneath the shadow of scornful speech.

It hisses like a serpent.

It becomes tangled in blasphemous words against God and humanity.

It corrodes the foundations of the law as a moth destroys a garment.

It deceives the human soul through mockery and leads it toward death.

But those who choose to love God with a pure heart and disciplined mind reject the poison of scorn.

They refuse the laughter that leads to grief.

They will not begin in mockery and end in mourning.

#### What This Vision Means

Fatuity is not harmless silliness. It is foolishness sharpened into scorn.

It mocks what is holy. It treats truth as something to be toyed with. It refuses reverence, and in doing so, dries up the soul’s fruitfulness.

The stinking cloud is a powerful image. Words create atmosphere. Scorn can fill a room, a heart, or a community with spiritual dryness.

The remedy is silence, discipline, and reverence. Not silence born of fear, but silence that heals the tongue and restores respect for truth.

#### Living This Vision

Mockery can feel powerful.

It lets us stand above others. It lets us avoid humility. It lets us laugh at what we do not want to face.

But the soul cannot live on scorn.

This vision asks us to notice where our words have become corrosive.

Do they dry up fruit?

Do they wound what is tender?

Do they hide pain behind contempt?

Choose reverence. Choose measured speech. Choose words that help truth grow.
