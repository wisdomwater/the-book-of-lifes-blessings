## Day 131 - The Swamp of Praise

> *I saw vainglory sink into a broad swamp of filth, for the soul that lives to be admired eventually becomes trapped in the mire of its own self-regard.*

After this I saw other evil spirits  
crying out in the crowd:

“There is no god to call upon but Lucifer,  
and with him we will enjoy magnificent glory.”

These spirits teach vainglory to human beings  
and urge them to pursue it with zeal.

Then I saw a wide and sprawling swamp  
filled with filth  
and many kinds of worms.  
From it rose a terrible stench.

There the souls were tormented  
who, while living in the world,  
had given themselves with all their strength  
to vainglory.

Because they had worried endlessly  
over their own praise and pride,  
their souls were sunk in the dirt of the swamp.

Because they believed themselves superior to others,  
they were tortured by worms.

Because in every circumstance  
they lived this way without restraint,  
they endured the stench  
that rose around them.

Then I heard the voice of the living light:

“What you see is true.  
Those who wish to overcome the spirits  
that suggest vainglory  
must grieve over this vice,  
fast, pray, and flee from it completely.”

And I understood this:

Vainglory looks toward the left.  
It considers what it can do  
by its own irrational impulse  
and wants, in everything,  
to have its own will.

Before others it decorates itself.  
It longs to be honored and praised.

When it adorns itself,  
it does not fear God.

When it seeks praise,  
it does not want God.

When it becomes anxious for admiration,  
it leaves the righteousness of God.

Those who follow vainglory  
think everything they learn by themselves  
is best.

They do not give God reverence.  
They do not desire the Son of God.  
They mock what is holy and just.

But those who long to serve God in faith  
must reject this vanity firmly.  
They must keep contrition in the spirit  
and walk in works that lead to holiness.

#### What This Vision Means

Vainglory is the hunger to be seen, admired, praised, and honored. It turns the soul outward toward applause and inward toward self-importance. Over time, the person becomes trapped in a swamp of image, comparison, and performance.

The swamp is a fitting image. Vainglory feels elevated at first, but spiritually it sinks. The filth represents the uncleanness of self-obsession. The worms show the corruption that grows when someone believes themselves superior. The stench reveals that vanity eventually becomes noticeable; what was meant to impress begins to repel.

This vice is especially dangerous because it can attach itself to good things. A person can pursue knowledge, virtue, beauty, or service while secretly wanting to be praised. When admiration becomes the goal, even good works become muddied.

The remedy is not self-hatred, but contrition and holy hiddenness. Vainglory wants the self displayed. Humility lets the soul become truthful before God.

#### Living This Vision

Notice where you hunger to be noticed.

It may be in your work.  
Your wisdom.  
Your kindness.  
Your suffering.  
Your spirituality.  
Your ability to be good.

Vainglory whispers:  
Make sure they see.  
Make sure they know.  
Make sure you are admired.

This vision invites a quieter path.

Do one good thing today  
without announcing it.

Let one act remain between you and God.  
Let one kindness be hidden.  
Let one accomplishment pass without being polished for display.

This may feel like loss at first.  
But it is freedom.

The soul does not need applause  
to be alive.

God sees in secret.  
And what is rooted in Him  
does not sink into the swamp.
