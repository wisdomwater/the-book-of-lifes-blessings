## 72 - The Quiet Surrender

> *What loosens its grip begins to rest*  
> *The heart finds peace when it no longer stands alone*

I saw a solitary figure  
standing in a wide and open place—

the horizon stretched far,  
unbroken,  
untouched.

The figure held tightly  
to what was within reach,  
as if everything depended  
on its grasp.

The air around it  
felt tense—  
not from danger,  
but from effort.

Nothing moved freely.

Then, slowly,  
a soft breeze began to pass through.

It did not push.

It did not take.

It simply moved—  
steady,  
gentle,  
unafraid.

The figure remained still.

And then—  
almost imperceptibly—  
its hands began to open.

Not fully.

Just enough  
to feel the air.

And in that small opening,  
something shifted.

The tension eased.  
The breath deepened.

The breeze moved closer—  
not as a force,  
but as a presence  
that could now be received.

The figure did not disappear.

It did not lose itself.

It became less alone.

And the space around it  
no longer felt empty—  
but held,  
alive,  
connected to something  
wider than itself.

Nothing was taken.

Everything was shared.

And in that quiet exchange,  
peace began to settle—  
not as control,  
but as trust  
gently taking root.

#### Living the Vision

You feel the need  
to hold everything together—

to manage,  
to protect,  
to ensure  
that nothing slips beyond your reach.

It can feel necessary.

Even wise.

But you may notice  
the weight it carries—  
the quiet tension  
of always needing to remain in control.

There is another way  
that does not ask you  
to abandon yourself.

Only to loosen,  
just slightly.

You may begin  
by allowing a small openness—  
a willingness to receive  
what is not coming from you.

A breath.  
A pause.

And in that space,  
you may sense  
that you are not alone  
in holding your life.

Something steadier  
is already present—  
not demanding,  
not overwhelming—

simply available.

You do not lose yourself  
by trusting this.

You find a deeper ground  
beneath what you carry.

And from there,  
peace begins to grow—  
quietly,  
without force—  
as something shared  
rather than something you must secure.

> **Inhale:** I release control

> **Exhale:** I rest in trust
