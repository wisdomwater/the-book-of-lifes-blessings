## Day 72 — The Voice That Refuses

> *The will that seeks only itself becomes its own prison.*

I saw a third figure.

Its form was like a man, yet not fully so.  
Its head resembled a wild beast—fierce, distorted.  
Its eyes burned with restless light.  
From its mouth came flames.

Snakes hung from its jaws,  
and a strange veil circled its head.

It knelt, yet its posture was defiant.

And it spoke:

“I will not obey God or man.  
If I obey, I lose myself.  
Others will command me, use me, dismiss me.

No—I will serve only myself.

If I am wronged, I will repay it.  
I will secure my place so none can oppose me.

Why should I lie beneath another?  
I will follow my own advantage.”

Its voice was sharp with pride and fear.

Then the voice from the cloud answered:

“You are cruel and filled with darkness.

If God allowed you to act without limit,  
what would remain of order?

Where would justice be?”

The voice revealed its condition:

“You dwell in insult.  
You rest in confusion.  
You live where all are against one another.”

And I saw:

What it called strength was isolation.  
What it called freedom was unrest.

It had made itself its own ruler—  
and in doing so, lost peace.

#### What This Vision Means

This figure represents a will turned entirely inward—a refusal to trust, to yield, or to participate in anything beyond personal gain.

At its core, this is not just pride. It is fear disguised as control.

The belief is: “If I do not protect myself, no one will.”  
And so the soul resists all authority—divine or human.

But this resistance creates fragmentation.  
Relationships break down.  
Trust dissolves.  
Peace disappears.

The imagery of flames and serpents shows the inner turmoil of such a state. There is constant agitation, constant defense.

Piety, in this vision, is not weakness. It is alignment.  
It is the ability to trust what is good, to participate in something larger than oneself.

True strength is not domination.  
It is rootedness in what is true.

#### Living This Vision

Consider where you resist being guided.

Are there places where you feel you must control everything?  
Where trust feels dangerous?

That instinct may come from real wounds.  
But if it governs everything, it will isolate you.

You are not meant to carry life alone.

Try loosening your grip—just slightly.

Allow for cooperation.  
Allow for guidance.  
Allow for something beyond your own perspective.

This does not mean losing yourself.  
It means finding a deeper grounding.

Peace does not come from control.  
It comes from alignment.

And alignment requires trust.
