## 72 - The Gentle Center

> *What returns to the Source is made whole. True knowing rests in quiet reverence.*

I saw a vast sky filled with gentle movement. Soft currents crossed through the open air, circling and weaving together in patterns too subtle to fully grasp. The winds carried a quiet intelligence within them, like fragments of a language spoken beneath the surface of ordinary hearing. Everything moved with complexity and beauty, yet nothing remained still for long.

And beneath all this movement, there was a center.

A quiet point of stillness resting within the heart of the sky itself. It did not move with the changing currents or strain to gather them into order. It did not attempt to direct the winds or impose itself upon their paths. It simply remained—steady, grounded, and deeply at peace within its own being.

From this stillness, a different kind of awareness spread outward.

It was not grasping or possessive. It did not seek control through understanding. Instead, it carried a quiet knowing rooted in presence itself. The winds passed through this center freely, and nothing there was disturbed. The more the stillness remained within itself, the wider and more harmonious the sky became around it.

And I understood then that there is a way of knowing that does not dominate what it sees.

It listens.

It receives.

And it gently returns all things to the deeper mystery from which they arise.

**Living the Vision**

There are moments when understanding begins to grow within us. We notice patterns in life. We learn how people move, how emotions unfold, how systems operate, how spiritual truths begin connecting together. This kind of insight can feel powerful, even comforting. Knowledge offers clarity in a world that often feels uncertain.

Yet something subtle can happen alongside this growth.

The mind begins tightening around what it knows. We start identifying ourselves with our understanding, quietly seeking security through certainty. Knowledge becomes something we hold onto rather than something we humbly participate within. And slowly, without meaning to, we can drift away from wonder itself.

The soul longs for another kind of wisdom.

A wisdom that remains open even while understanding deepens. A way of seeing that does not separate us from the mystery of life, but draws us more deeply into reverence for it. True spiritual knowing does not place us above others or above the unfolding of existence. It softens us into humility.

This begins whenever we pause long enough to loosen our grip on certainty.

We allow what we know to remain incomplete and alive. We remember that truth is not merely information to possess, but a reality we are continually being invited into. The deeper we grow, the more gently we begin holding our understanding.

And in that openness, something beautiful happens.

Knowledge becomes less about mastery and more about participation. We stop standing outside life trying to control it, and instead begin resting within it—held, included, and shaped by something far greater than ourselves.

Wonder returns there.

So does peace.

The soul becomes whole not by knowing everything, but by remaining close to the Source from which all true wisdom flows.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Source of all knowing

**Exhale:** Keep me in wonder
