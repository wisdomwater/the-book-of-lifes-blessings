## 72 - The Gentle Center

> *What returns to the Source is made whole*  
> *True knowing rests in quiet reverence*

I saw a wide sky  
filled with moving currents—  
soft winds crossing,  
circling,  
whispering through the open air.

They carried patterns,  
subtle and intricate,  
as if holding a language  
just beyond hearing.

And beneath them,  
there was a still point.

A quiet center  
that did not move  
with the shifting winds.

It did not reach to gather them.  
It did not try to direct their paths.

It simply remained—  
steady,  
present,  
at rest within itself.

From this center,  
a gentle awareness spread—  
not grasping,  
not claiming,  
but knowing.

The winds passed through it,  
and nothing was disturbed.

The sky opened wider,  
not controlled,  
but held in a deeper harmony.

And I understood:

There is a knowing  
that does not take hold of things.

It listens,  
it receives,  
and it returns  
all things  
to the One  
from whom they come.

#### Living the Vision

You begin to understand something—  
how things move,  
how patterns form,  
how life unfolds in quiet ways.

And with that understanding,  
you may feel a subtle shift.

A desire to hold it,  
to shape it,  
to make it your own.

It can feel like strength—  
like clarity,  
like rising above uncertainty.

But you may also notice  
how quickly this becomes tension.

A tightening around what you know,  
a need to be certain,  
a quiet distance from the mystery itself.

There is another way  
already within you.

A way of knowing  
that does not separate you  
from what is known.

You may sense it  
when you pause—  
when you let what you understand  
remain open.

In that openness,  
something softens.

You are no longer above  
or outside.

You are within—  
held,  
included,  
part of a greater unfolding.

And from there,  
knowledge becomes something different.

Not a place to stand over,  
but a path that leads you  
back into wonder.

Back into quiet belonging.
