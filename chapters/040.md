## Day 40 – Following the Light

> *To follow Christ is to move with Him—toward what is eternal, and away from what fades.*

I watched as the moon moved—steady, faithful.

Within it, I saw the form of a sheep.

Gentle.  
Attentive.

And it followed the ram without turning aside.

Where the ram went, it went.

Whether the sun rose or declined,  
it remained near.

And I understood—this is the Church.

Not standing apart,  
but following.

It moves with the mystery of God—  
not clinging to the world,  
but turning toward what is eternal.

When it advances, it moves in spiritual strength.  
When it withdraws, it releases what is worldly.

It does not hold tightly to what passes away.

It follows what endures.

I saw that this path is not without trial.

Those who follow must pass through many ordeals.

As Christ Himself did.

But they do not walk alone.

They walk in His steps.

They live what He taught.

They carry His life within them.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision portrays the Church—and by extension, the faithful—as followers of Christ’s movement.

The sheep represents humility, trust, and attentiveness. To follow Christ is not to lead, but to remain close, responsive, and aligned.

The motion of the sun reflects the unfolding of divine mystery through time. The Church participates in this movement, advancing in spiritual things while loosening its attachment to what is temporary.

The trials faced by the faithful are not signs of abandonment, but participation in the life of Christ.

This vision emphasizes faithfulness—not as rigid adherence, but as living alignment.

#### Living This Vision

You do not need to have everything figured out.

You do not need to lead the way.

You are invited to follow.

To stay close to what is true.  
To listen.  
To respond.

This may mean letting go of things that feel secure but are not lasting.

It may mean walking through difficulty.

But you are not alone in this path.

You are walking a way that has already been walked.

And it leads somewhere good.
