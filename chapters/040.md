## 40 — The Nearness That Follows

> *You do not have to lead the way*  
> *Staying near is enough*

I saw a soft light  
moving across a wide horizon—

steady,  
unhurried,  
faithful in its path.

Within it,  
a gentle form walked—

attentive,  
quiet,  
drawn by something deeper  
than sight.

It did not rush ahead.  
It did not fall behind.

It remained near.

Where the light turned,  
it turned.

Where the light rested,  
it stayed.

There was no strain  
in its following—

only a simple nearness,  
like breath  
keeping time  
with a hidden rhythm.

And as it moved,  
something subtle shifted—

what once felt necessary  
began to loosen,  
like leaves  
falling without effort.

Nothing was forced away.

Only what could not remain  
in that nearness  
quietly released.

The path was not always smooth.

There were places  
of narrowing,  
of shadow,  
of quiet testing.

But the light  
did not disappear.

And the one who followed  
did not walk alone.

There was a sense  
of being carried  
even while walking—

held within a movement  
already sure  
of where it was going.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you feel the pressure  
to know where you are going—

to decide,  
to lead,  
to have clarity  
before you take the next step.

But something in you  
longs for a simpler way—

not to control the path,  
but to stay close  
to what is true.

You may notice  
how this feels—

a quiet pull  
toward what is steady,  
a gentle awareness  
that asks you to remain  
rather than rush.

At times,  
this may mean letting go—

not dramatically,  
but softly—

releasing what no longer fits  
as you move forward.

You may not see  
the whole path.

But you can sense  
the next step.

And that is enough.

You are not being asked  
to lead your life alone.

You are being invited  
to follow  
what is already  
guiding you.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Keep me near  
>
> **Exhale:** I follow in trust
