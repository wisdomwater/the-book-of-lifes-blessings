## Day 37 – The Light That Cannot Be Overcome

> *Darkness gathers, but it cannot pass the boundary of what is held in Christ.*

I saw the wind again move across the glowing cloud—  
from the east toward the north.

It carried with it a purity.

A strength born not of force,  
but of holiness.

I understood this as the work of Christ—  
the rising of truth,  
the flowering of a life untouched by corruption.

In this, there was a new power.

Something the old ways could not fully accomplish.

The enemy, once strong,  
could not stand against it.

For what had been partial was now complete.

And yet, from the west, I saw darkness gather.

Thick.  
Heavy.  
Filled with intention.

It pressed forward—  
seeking to cover, to overwhelm, to undo.

It was the force of all that opposes truth.

All that resists light.

It came against the glowing cloud.

But it could not pass.

It could press.  
It could trouble.  
It could cause suffering.

But it could not overcome.

The light remained.

Unbroken.

And within that light, I saw something wondrous.

The sun and the moon appeared.

The sun blazed with hidden mystery—  
the Incarnation,  
God made flesh through the purity of the Virgin.

A brilliance that illuminated all things.

And the moon followed—  
reflecting that light.

I understood this as the Church,  
receiving and carrying the light into the world.

Within the sun, I saw a lion.

Strong.  
Radiant.  
Unfathomable.

This was the power of God made visible—  
walking a path no one else could walk.

A mystery no one else could fulfill.

And though it was hidden,  
it shone.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the victory of divine light over darkness.

The movement from east to north symbolizes the rising of truth in Christ—a purity and completeness that surpasses what came before.

The gathering darkness represents the persistent reality of evil. It opposes, pressures, and seeks to overwhelm. Yet it is limited. It cannot ultimately overcome the light rooted in Christ.

The sun symbolizes the Incarnation—the divine entering human existence. The moon reflects the Church, which carries and reflects that light into the world.

The lion represents strength, authority, and the hidden power of God revealed in Christ.

This vision affirms that while darkness is real, it is not final.

#### Living This Vision

You will encounter darkness.

Resistance.  
Discouragement.  
Moments that feel heavy and uncertain.

It may press against you.

But it cannot define you.

Not if you remain rooted in what is true.

There is a light within reach—  
steady, unwavering.

You do not have to create it.

Only remain in it.

And when you cannot see clearly,  
borrow the light.

Reflect it.

Carry it in small ways.

Even that is enough.
