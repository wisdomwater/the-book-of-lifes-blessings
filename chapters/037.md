## 37 - Rooted Beyond the Bloom

> *What is beautiful can be received, but it cannot hold your life.*

I saw a garden resting beneath a soft and quiet light. Nothing within it strained to become more than it already was. The air carried a gentle stillness, and everything seemed to unfold at its own unhurried pace. At the center stood a great tree, wide in its reaching and abundant in its flowering.

Its blossoms opened freely across the branches—delicate, fragrant, alive with color and movement. They seemed to embody joy itself, unfolding effortlessly toward the light. The garden felt full of beauty that did not need to announce itself. It simply bloomed.

And near the flowering branches, I sensed a presence reaching toward the blossoms.

Not with cruelty.

Not with violence.

But with longing.

It gathered them close as though believing this beauty could somehow be made permanent—held tightly enough to become a lasting refuge against change. There was tenderness within the reaching, yet also a quiet fear beneath it: the fear that what is beautiful might fade if not grasped firmly enough.

Then my attention was drawn beneath the surface of the garden.

Far below what the eye could see, the roots of the great tree stretched downward through the earth. Some roots reached living water deep beneath the soil, drawing nourishment from something steady and enduring. Others strained through dry and silent ground, searching desperately where no lasting life could be found.

And I understood the difference.

One way of living draws from what does not fade.

The other leans entirely upon what is already passing.

The blossoms themselves did not resist the seasons. They opened fully. They gave their fragrance freely. And when their time came, they released themselves gently back to the earth. Their beauty was real precisely because it was never meant to be possessed.

Still the garden remained.

Quietly inviting every soul toward a deeper rooting, a gentler way of being held by life itself.

**Living the Vision**

There are moments when something beautiful enters our lives and awakens deep joy within us. A relationship begins to flourish. A season of purpose opens before us. A moment of connection, creativity, or recognition touches the heart in ways that feel luminous and alive. Naturally, something within us leans toward these experiences with gratitude and longing.

And often, without realizing it, we begin asking them to carry more than they were meant to hold.

We hope a relationship will secure our identity. We ask success to quiet our fears. We cling to moments of happiness as though they could protect us from all future sorrow. This instinct is deeply human. The soul longs for permanence because somewhere within it remembers the ache for eternal belonging.

Yet everything that blooms within this life also changes.

Love deepens and transforms. Seasons shift. Beauty opens and releases itself again. When we anchor our entire being to what is temporary, fear quietly enters alongside our joy. We begin grasping instead of receiving. Protecting instead of participating.

But beneath these changing experiences there is another invitation.

The deeper life asks us to root ourselves not in what fades, but in what quietly sustains us through every season. Divine love remains beneath the blossoms like hidden water beneath the earth—steady, nourishing, unseen yet always present. From this deeper grounding, we become free to delight fully in what is beautiful without needing to possess it forever.

We can love deeply without turning love into fear.

We can receive joy without demanding permanence from it.

And as the soul learns to root itself in what does not pass away, everything else in life can be held more gently—with gratitude, openness, and peace.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** You are my root

**Exhale:** I rest beyond the fading
