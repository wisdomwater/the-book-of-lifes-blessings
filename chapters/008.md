## Day 8 — The Wheel of Wrath

> *I saw how anger traps the soul inside its own fury,
> yet patience moves like quiet air that outlasts every storm.*

In the vision I saw a sixth image.

Its face resembled that of a man, yet its mouth was shaped like the mouth of a scorpion. Its eyes were distorted so that the whites were larger than the dark pupils.

Its arms appeared human, but its hands twisted into long claws.

The creature’s chest, belly, and back were shaped like the shell of a crab. Its legs resembled those of a lobster, and its feet were like writhing vipers.

The figure had no hair upon its head and no clothing upon its body.

And it was bound within a great mill wheel.

The creature gripped the upper spokes of the wheel with its clawed hands while its feet clutched the lower spokes. It seemed trapped within the turning circle, unable to escape the motion.

From its scorpion mouth came words that flashed like burning torches.

“I trample and crush anyone who offends me.

Why should I endure an insult?

If someone does not wish harm from me, then let them not harm me first.

But if they strike me, I will strike them harder. I will smite them with my sword and beat them with my staff.

No one will insult me without paying for it.”

Then again I heard a voice from the stormy cloud.

“My voice has been heard in the heights of heaven, and it descends to the earth like balm poured upon a wound.

You are a fraud that thirsts for blood, always rising from the cold North.

But I am the vital breath of gentle air that brings forth the flowers and fruits of virtue.

I build steadily in the hearts of human beings. What I begin, I bring to completion, because I remain constant.

I trample no one. I remain calm, and no one can condemn me.

But if you raise a tower of fury, I will destroy it with a single word.

Your works will scatter like dust.

You will pass away.

But I will remain forever.”

#### What This Vision Means

The monstrous figure represents **wrath**—anger that consumes the soul.

Its body is made of creatures known for sharpness, poison, and hardness: scorpion, crab, lobster, serpent. These images reveal the defensive and aggressive nature of uncontrolled anger.

The mill wheel is especially important.

Wrath traps the soul in a cycle. One insult leads to retaliation. Retaliation invites another injury. The wheel keeps turning, grinding relationships, communities, and even the heart itself.

The creature clings to the wheel, believing its fury gives it power.

But the voice of Patience reveals a deeper strength.

Patience is not weakness. It is described as “the vital breath of gentle air” that produces the fruits of virtue. Like the steady rhythm of nature, patience quietly builds what anger constantly destroys.

Wrath shouts.

Patience endures.

Over time, endurance outlasts violence.

#### Living This Vision

Anger is a powerful human emotion.

Sometimes it arises from genuine injustice or pain. But when anger becomes our guiding force, it traps us inside the same wheel seen in the vision.

We replay offenses in our minds. We imagine retaliation. We wait for the chance to prove ourselves right or punish someone who wronged us.

The wheel keeps turning.

Patience offers another path.

Patience does not deny pain or pretend that injustice is acceptable. Instead, it refuses to let anger become the master of the heart.

It creates space between the wound and the response.

In that space, wisdom can grow.

The soul learns that strength does not always appear in loud retaliation. Often it appears in the quiet decision not to let bitterness rule our lives.

Patience does not collapse under pressure.

It outlasts the storm.
