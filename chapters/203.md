## Day 203 - The Song of Virginity

> *Purity is not emptiness, but fullness offered wholly to God. The soul that belongs undividedly to love sings a song no one else can sing.*

Again, in the radiance, I saw as through a mirror a mass of air purer than the clearest water and brighter than the sun.

Within it was a breeze carrying all the living strength of herbs and flowers of heaven and earth. It was filled with fragrance, like summer rich with sweetness.

In that air I saw souls wearing garments of dazzling whiteness, woven through with gold. From chest to feet, their garments were adorned with bands of precious gems and breathed out intense perfume.

They wore belts beyond human understanding, decorated with gold, gems, and pearls.

On their heads were crowns braided with gold, roses, and lilies, encircled with precious stones shaped like little tubes.

Whenever the Lamb of God made His voice heard, a gentle murmur flowed from the hidden mystery of divinity. It passed through those jeweled tubes, and the melody sounded like every kind of harp and organ.

Only those who wore these crowns sang this song.

Others listened and rejoiced, as a person rejoices when seeing the sunlight after long darkness.

Their shoes were bright, as if springing from a living fountain.

Sometimes they walked on golden wheels, carrying harps in their hands and playing them.

They understood, knew, and spoke an unknown language that no one else could use.

These were the souls who served God in virginity and celibacy. They imitated the angelic ranks. They offered themselves with holy works, chastity, mercy, praise, meditation, and love.

They had gone beyond what was required and given themselves wholly to God.

And the Lamb rejoiced with them.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision portrays virginity not as absence, but as radiant fullness.

The air is pure, fragrant, and alive. The garments are white and gold. Roses and lilies crown the soul. The song is unique, intimate, and known only to those who have offered this particular gift.

Hildegard sees celibate virginity as a consecrated form of love—an undivided belonging to God. It is not contempt for the body, but the offering of body and spirit in a special way.

The unknown language reveals a mystery of love that cannot be translated into ordinary speech.

#### Living This Vision

Not everyone is called to this exact path.

But every soul is invited into some form of undivided love.

We all have places where desire is scattered, where devotion is mixed, where love is divided by fear, pleasure, or distraction.

This vision asks:

What part of me longs to belong wholly to God?

Purity is not coldness. It is love gathered into one flame.

Let your life, whatever your calling, become more whole in love.
