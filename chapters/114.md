## Day 114 - The Swift Scales of Self-Will

> *I saw a creature rushing through the darkness, serpent-headed and wind-tossed, and I heard how disobedience calls self-will wisdom while refusing the life-giving command.*

Then I saw the fourth image.

Its head was like a serpent.  
Its chest was feathered like a seabird.  
Its legs and feet were like a viper’s.  
Its back, tail, and the rest of its body were like a crab.

It moved quickly here and there,  
as though the wind carried it.  
And as it moved,  
it stirred all the darkness around it.

Then it turned north  
and cast much fire from its mouth.

And it said:

“Why should we obey the commands of others?  
If we do, we do not honor what we are.

We pay proper attention to philosophy  
and are wiser than many.  
Should we not act according to our own knowledge?  
Of course we should.

Many teachers establish rules  
from their own preferences and judgments.  
Must we live according to what pleases them?

If I knew the trees,  
if I understood the birds,  
if teachers still laid commands upon me,  
I would not know what to do.

But what I decide for myself,  
I know to be useful.  
It is better to do what I know  
than what I do not know.

So I will do what I can see, touch,  
and understand with my own senses.  
I will search creation  
for what benefits or harms me.  
And since God has subjected creatures to me,  
why should I not determine their use for myself?  
I will think about what pleases me.”

Then from the storm-cloud I heard the voice of Obedience answer:

“I who obey God  
know union with Him.

When God created all things by His Word  
and said, ‘Let it be,’  
I was there,  
watching the fulfillment of His command.

When the first angel rebelled against God,  
I knew that his works had no life in them,  
because he desired to be what he was not.

I am in the sun,  
the moon,  
the stars,  
the springs of water.

I am the root in all the works of God,  
as the soul is in the body.

As a human will carries out its desire,  
so I am the will of God in action,  
fulfilling what He commands.

I desire nothing apart from Him.

But you transgress the Creator’s command.  
You speak as though you belonged to God,  
yet you respect no one  
and do only what pleases you.

Where is the heaven you made?  
Where is the earth?  
Where are the mountains and seas you formed?

You created nothing.  
Yet you despise what God has made.

You are like dry leaves from a tree  
and like scales from fish.  
You are made to fall,  
for your name leads not to usefulness,  
but to death.”

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals disobedience as restless self-rule. It is not merely breaking rules. It is the deeper conviction that the self should be answerable only to itself.

The image is fractured and unstable—serpent, bird, viper, crab—moving rapidly and stirring darkness wherever it goes. This is the inner shape of self-will when it rejects divine order. It becomes clever but unrooted, swift but directionless, fiery but not life-giving.

Disobedience claims independence as wisdom. It says, *I will do what I can see and understand for myself.* This sounds practical, even intelligent. But it refuses the truth that life is received before it is mastered. It forgets that creatures are not ours to interpret merely for our own pleasure or advantage.

Obedience speaks as a cosmic harmony. It is woven into creation itself—the stars, waters, and living order of the world. Obedience is not humiliation. It is participation in the divine “Let it be.” To obey God is to come into accord with reality.

#### Living This Vision

There is a part of us that resists being told.

It says:  
I know enough.  
I can judge for myself.  
Why should anyone guide me?

Sometimes that resistance protects us from harm.  
But sometimes it becomes a habit of soul.

Then even wise guidance feels threatening.  
Even God’s commands begin to feel restrictive.

This vision asks a searching question:  
Do I want truth,  
or do I only want freedom on my own terms?

Obedience begins where trust begins.  
It does not require blindness.  
It requires humility.

There are moments when the holiest act  
is simply to say,  
Not my impulse.  
Not my preference.  
Let me align myself with what is true.

In daily life, this may look like restraint,  
teachability,  
or choosing what is right  
before it feels pleasing.

The self-willed soul scatters.  
The obedient soul becomes rooted.
