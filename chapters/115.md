## Day 115 - The Head Full of Eyes

> *I saw a figure whose head was crowded with darkened eyes, and one fierce eye burned in front of them all. It looked everywhere, yet could not see the way home.*

Then I saw the fifth image.

It looked like a man in body,  
but from the knees down it was swallowed in darkness.  
Its head had no familiar human features.  
Instead it was filled with many black-colored eyes,  
and in the front was one great eye  
sending out flashes like fire.

Its right hand rested on its chest.  
In its left it held a staff.  
It was wrapped in a black cloak.

And it said:

“I know no life but this one—  
the life I can see and touch,  
the life I can feel with my own hand.

What reward could be promised me  
for a life that remains uncertain?

Of this material life I can say:  
this exists, or this does not exist.

So I ask, I investigate,  
I see, I hear, I learn—  
and yet I find nothing beyond.

If among visible things  
I find something that favors me,  
why should I refuse it?

I walk only where I know.  
I enter only the discipline I understand.

When I try to fly on the winds,  
I am thrown back to the earth.  
When I ask the sun and moon what I should do,  
they answer only a little.

I hear many teachings, rumors, and doctrines,  
yet I do not know them.

So I will do whatever seems most useful to me.”

Then I heard Faith answer from the storm-cloud:

“You are the deceit of the devil,  
denying in your heart what is just.

Your blackened eyes cannot see  
the path of salvation that leads to heaven.

You flee the brightness of faith  
and hide within your own dark reasoning.

But I praise God with the angels.  
I desire what belongs to God.

With the Cherubim I receive His judgments.  
Through prophets, the wise, and the scribes  
I shine through the kingdoms of the world  
with the justice of God.

I am a mirror in God,  
for I shine in all His commandments.”

#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows unbelief not as simple ignorance, but as a narrowing of reality to only what can be grasped by the senses. The many black eyes suggest constant investigation without true vision. Unbelief can be intensely observant and still remain blind.

The great burning eye at the front is especially striking. It suggests a fierce, self-trusting scrutiny—a mind that insists on seeing everything for itself, yet cannot receive what is given in light from above. It looks everywhere except toward trust.

Faith does not reject the world of sight and touch. Rather, it refuses to let visible things become the whole horizon of meaning. Faith is called a mirror here because it reflects a greater light. It receives, rather than invents, the truth.

So the contrast is not between thinking and not thinking. It is between closed reasoning and illuminated perception. Unbelief says, *I will only live by what I can verify.* Faith says, *I will live by the truth that God reveals, even where my senses cannot reach.*

#### Living This Vision

Many people know this voice.

I can only trust what I can prove.  
I can only rely on what I can hold.  
If I cannot see it clearly, I will not lean on it.

There is honesty in that impulse.  
But if it hardens, the soul becomes closed.

Not everything most real can be touched by the hand.  
Love cannot.  
Hope cannot.  
Grace cannot.  
Yet they shape a life.

This vision does not ask you to abandon reason.  
It asks you not to imprison yourself within it.

You can ask questions and still remain open.  
You can search and still stay humble.  
You can admit mystery without collapsing into confusion.

Faith often begins as a small consent:  
There may be more light than I can presently see.

That is enough to start.
