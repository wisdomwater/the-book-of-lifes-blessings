## Day 182 - The Dry Tree

> *Worldly sorrow binds the soul in its own branches. But heavenly joy teaches the heart to see goodness even in sorrow.*

The fifth image was the Sadness of Life in the World.

It followed Avarice, for when those who grasp cannot have what they desire, they fall into sadness and cannot easily escape.

This image appeared as a woman.

Behind her stood a tree completely dry, without leaves or life. She was tangled in its branches as though the tree had grown around her.

One branch crossed over her head, showing how sorrow presses upon the mind from beginning to end.

Another wrapped around her neck and throat, choking the strength by which the soul should bear the yoke of God and receive the food of life.

Branches bound her right arm and left arm. Her arms were crossed and could not stretch outward. For this sorrow makes people fear both spiritual work and earthly work, until they do neither. They remain in heaviness, unable to move toward good.

Her hands hung like raven claws, for her works had become rough and dark.

More branches wrapped around her belly and legs, tightening around the strength of soul and body. Her feet were made of the wood of that tree, for she no longer walked in hope, correction, or trust.

She wore no clothing except the branches that bound her.

Then evil spirits came toward her with a foul black mist. They attacked her, and she bent toward them, groaning.

For this sadness steals consolation from the mind. It leads the soul toward despair, self-loathing, and the belief that happiness can never be reached.

But Heavenly Joy answered.

It called the soul to reject bitterness and cling to God with faith and gladness.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision is tender and severe.

The sadness described here is not ordinary grief. Grief can be holy. Tears can cleanse. But this sorrow becomes a prison. It turns the soul against itself and blinds it to God’s goodness.

The dry tree shows life cut off from hope. Its branches become excuses, fears, anxieties, and contradictions. They bind the mind, throat, arms, body, and feet—thought, speech, work, strength, and direction.

The raven claws reveal work twisted by despair. The person can still act, but without tenderness or trust.

Heavenly Joy does not deny suffering. It calls the soul back to God’s goodness within it.

#### Living This Vision

There are sorrows that need compassion, time, and care.

But there is also a sorrow that begins to speak falsely. It says, “Nothing good remains. I cannot move. I cannot change. God has forgotten me.”

This vision asks us not to believe that voice completely.

Where has sadness wrapped itself around your life?

What small branch might be loosened today?

A prayer. A walk. A confession. A word of gratitude. A hand extended.

Joy may return slowly, but even the smallest movement toward God is already life stirring in the dry tree.
