## 182 - The First Green

> *What feels bound can gently loosen*  
> *What seems lifeless may begin again*

I saw a tree in the quiet of early light.

Its branches were still,  
bare,  
woven tightly around themselves—  
as if holding something they could not release.

Near its trunk,  
a figure stood close,  
almost hidden within the wood.

Nothing moved at first.

The air was heavy,  
as though waiting.

Then,  
a soft warmth began to gather—  
not from above alone,  
but from within the stillness itself.

It did not break the branches.  
It did not force them apart.

It simply remained.

And slowly,  
something subtle began to change.

A loosening—  
barely seen.

A space  
where there had been none.

And deep within the wood,  
a faint green appeared.

Not yet a leaf.  
Not yet life in full.

But a beginning.

The branches did not fall away.  
They softened.

The tree did not become whole at once.

But it was no longer closed.

It was being awakened  
from within.

And I understood:

Even what feels bound  
can hold the seed of life—  
waiting,  
not for force,  
but for gentle return.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when sorrow feels close.

Not passing,  
not light—  
but steady,  
as if it has settled into the shape of things.

You may notice  
how it touches everything—  
how it narrows what you can see,  
what you can hope,  
what you can begin.

It can feel as though nothing moves.

And yet,  
there may be  
a quieter presence  
beneath it.

You may sense it  
in something small—  
a breath that softens,  
a moment that opens,  
a thought that is not entirely heavy.

It does not remove the sorrow.

But it sits with it  
without being overcome.

You are not asked  
to escape what you feel.

Only to notice  
what has not been taken.

There is still  
something alive within you.

Not fully seen.  
Not fully formed.

But real.

And as you remain  
even gently aware of it,  
you may feel  
a slight loosening—  
a space where life  
can begin again.
