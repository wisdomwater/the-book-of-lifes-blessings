## Day 181 - The Burden of Hoarding

> *Greed promises safety but gives only heaviness. The soul that grasps everything loses the freedom to receive.*

The fourth image was Avarice.

It followed after the dark art of enchantment, for greed also serves idols. It lives in the womb of the devil and is never filled. It cannot complete what it desires.

It appeared as a man because it wanted the things of earth, not the things of heaven.

Its head had no hair, for there was no honesty in its will. It wore the beard of a goat, as if clothed in shameful desire.

Its pupils were small, and the whites of its eyes were large. It could not rejoice in the good of others. Envy glared from its face.

It breathed with great force through its nose, drawing in worldly craving, then casting it out, only to gather it again with greater hunger.

Its hands were iron, because its works were hard and rough. Its legs were bloody, because it spent its strength in violence and harm for its own gain. Its feet were like a lion’s, for every step moved toward ferocity and plunder.

Its robe was woven in black and white, for greed gathers riches whether justly or unjustly, caring nothing for where they come from.

Upon its chest clung a black vulture. Its claws pierced the breast, feeding the conscience with the darkness of greed.

Before it stood a tree rooted in Gehenna, bearing fruit of pitch and sulfur. Avarice stared at it, plucked its apples with its mouth, and devoured them eagerly.

Around it moved horrible worms, stirring the darkness like fish striking water with their tails.

For greed does not allow peace. It disturbs even good souls, tempting them to hoard what should be shared.

But Total Detachment answered:

“All the good of God is enough for me.”

#### What This Vision Means

Avarice is not simply wanting possessions. It is wanting without trust.

Its body shows the spiritual cost of grasping. Iron hands cannot receive gently. Bloody legs reveal the harm done in pursuit of gain. The lion’s feet show predatory movement. The vulture on the chest reveals a conscience being eaten alive.

The tree rooted in Gehenna tells us that greed bears poisonous fruit. What is gathered from fear cannot nourish the soul.

Total Detachment is not hatred of creation. It is freedom. It rejoices in God’s gifts without needing to possess everything.

#### Living This Vision

Greed often hides beneath the language of security.

We tell ourselves we are only being wise, only preparing, only protecting ourselves. But sometimes the heart has crossed from stewardship into fear.

This vision asks:

Can I rejoice when others receive good?

Can I trust God with what I do not control?

Can I recognize enough?

Detachment does not mean having nothing. It means nothing owns the soul.

Let God’s goodness be enough.
