## Day 144 - The Voice That Divides

> *A voice that tears apart cannot create. It only spins what already exists into confusion.*

I saw a strange and unsettling figure suspended in darkness, hanging upside down as if cut off from all order. Its head was like a leopard—restless, cunning, alert. But its body was like a scorpion—armed with poison, bent toward harm.

It turned toward the South and the West and began to speak with restless agitation.

“I reject the East,” it said. “I refuse the South. Each part seeks its own gain. The East wants everything. The South clings to everything. The West falls into darkness. The North grasps what moves in shadow. Each keeps its strength for itself.”

Its voice spun like a wheel.

“Birds, beasts, and fish do what they can. So do I. I overturn everything—rich and poor, rulers and common people. I stir them, twist them, tire of them. I keep what pleases me and discard the rest. All act as they are able—and so do I.”

Its words scattered like seeds of unrest.

Then from a storm-filled cloud came a voice—clear, steady, and unshaken.

“You miserable deceiver,” it said, “what power do you truly have?”

“You cannot create even the smallest life. You cannot shake the heavens. You cannot dim the sun or command the stars. Even the dust in a beam of light stands beyond your reach.”

“You rage and accuse, but you are bound within creation. You are no master—only a servant, as an ox to its owner.”

“You do not know your place. You belong neither to strength nor to harmony. You are only a stumbling block.”

“And yet, even your rebellion cannot diminish God.”

The voice faded, but its truth remained—like light that cannot be swallowed by shadow.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the nature of discord—not as true power, but as fragmentation.

The inverted creature symbolizes a being cut off from divine order. Its upside-down posture reflects disorientation. Its leopard head suggests restless cunning, while its scorpion body reveals hidden harm. Discord often appears intelligent, even persuasive—but it carries poison beneath its surface.

Its speech exposes its nature. It does not create unity. It divides. It exaggerates differences. It frames the world as competing forces, each grasping for its own survival.

But the voice from the cloud reveals a deeper truth: discord cannot create reality—it can only distort it.

It has no generative power. It cannot bring life, light, or order. It can only rearrange what already exists, stirring confusion and instability.

True power belongs not to the one who disrupts, but to the One who sustains.

Even chaos exists within the limits of divine order.


#### Living This Vision

Discord often enters quietly—not as obvious evil, but as subtle division.

It shows up in thoughts like:
“Everyone is against me.”
“No one understands.”
“Each person is only out for themselves.”

These thoughts feel true in the moment. They carry a strange energy. But they fragment the heart.

This vision invites you to pause when you feel that inner spinning.

Ask gently:
Is this thought creating clarity—or scattering it?

You do not need to fight discord with force. You can step out of its motion.

Return to stillness.
Return to what is whole.
Return to what is quietly true.

Unity does not demand uniformity. But it does refuse the lie that everything is against everything else.

You are not held together by your own effort.

You are held.
