## Day 15 - The Shoulder of Power

> *God bears His strength not to dominate, but to restore what has been broken.*

I saw again the great figure whose measure spans all things.  
And I heard the word: *“His power rests upon his shoulder.”*

This power was not hidden.

It appeared in a man.

For God Himself took on flesh,  
and in that living body, He carried divine strength into the world.

With the weight of His being, He pressed against the works of darkness.  
He shattered the designs of the enemy.  
He broke open the depths that held humanity captive.

And I saw how this healing spread—  
carried outward through many,  
like streams flowing from a single source.

For through His messengers,  
waters of cleansing were poured over the earth.

Then I was shown the wound of humanity.

The first man had been like a tender bud,  
just opening to life.  
But he was deceived.

The enemy reached into him,  
stirring what was not meant to rule—  
and from this came a bitter seed,  
sown into all who followed.

A poison moved through the generations—  
not seen with the eyes,  
yet deeply felt in the soul.

But God did not leave humanity in this condition.

I saw a new forming—  
a body shaped in purity,  
within the stillness of a virgin womb.

No corruption touched it.  
No poison entered.

The warmth of the Holy Spirit gave it life.

And through this man—  
pure, yet fully human—  
the sickness was met.

He did not turn away from the wounded.  
He entered into their condition.

And by His own wholeness,  
He cleansed what had been defiled.

Thus the one without stain  
became the healer of all who were stained.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals Christ as the turning point in the story of humanity. The “shoulder” represents both strength and responsibility—the place where burdens are carried. God does not remain distant from human brokenness; He takes it upon Himself.

The imagery of the poisoned seed speaks to the deep distortion within human nature—what we might experience as disordered desire, inner conflict, or a pull away from what is good. This is not merely individual failure, but something inherited and shared.

Yet the response of God is not condemnation—it is incarnation. By entering human life in purity, Christ becomes both contrast and cure. He reveals what humanity was meant to be, and restores what has been damaged.

The cleansing is not external only. It is a deep renewal of the human condition itself.


#### Living This Vision

There are parts of you that feel tangled—habits, desires, or patterns that seem to run deeper than your conscious will. It can feel as though something within you moves on its own, pulling you away from who you want to be.

This vision does not ask you to deny that struggle.

Instead, it invites you to bring it into the presence of the One who heals.

You do not cleanse yourself first and then come to God.  
You come as you are—and are cleansed in the coming.

Consider this gently:  
*Where do I feel “poisoned” or out of alignment within myself?*

Do not meet that place with shame.  
Meet it with openness.

Healing begins not with force,  
but with allowing light to enter what has been hidden.

And slowly, what was once heavy  
begins to be carried for you.
