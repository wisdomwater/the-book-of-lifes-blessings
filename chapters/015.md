## 15 — The Strength That Carries

> *You do not bear yourself alone*  
> *Love carries what you cannot*

A great stillness stood at the center of all things.

Not distant—  
but near,  
like a presence that had always been  
quietly holding everything together.

Its strength was not sharp  
or forceful.

It rested—  
like a steady shoulder  
beneath a weight  
that did not need to be named.

Nothing strained.

Nothing was pushed away.

What was heavy  
was simply carried.

And from that place of carrying,  
something began to flow—

not in sudden movement,  
but like water  
finding its way outward.

Gentle.  
Clear.

Touching what had been burdened  
without resistance.

There were places  
where the ground felt tight,  
as though something beneath  
had been held too long.

Places where life had grown tangled,  
where the movement inward  
felt difficult to follow.

And yet,  
where the water passed,  
something loosened.

Not forced open—  
but softened.

Not erased—  
but restored.

The presence did not stand apart  
from what was wounded.

It moved within it.

And in that nearness,  
what had once felt too heavy to hold  
was lifted—

not removed from life,  
but carried within a strength  
that did not falter.

#### Living the Vision

There are places within you  
that feel difficult to untangle.

Patterns that return.  
Responses that feel deeper  
than your own choosing.

You may notice  
how easily the weight gathers—  
how quickly you try  
to manage it,  
to carry it alone.

And yet,  
there is another way  
already present.

You may sense it  
in quiet moments  
when you stop striving  
just long enough  
to feel what is there.

Not to fix it.  
Not to push it away.

But to allow it  
to be seen  
within a greater holding.

There is a strength  
that does not demand  
your perfection.

It meets you  
within what is unfinished.

You are not asked  
to cleanse yourself  
before being received.

Only to come  
as you are.

And in that coming,  
you may begin to feel  
that what once seemed yours alone  
is no longer carried alone.

Something steadier  
has taken hold.

And slowly,  
what was heavy  
begins to lighten—  
not by effort,  
but by being shared.
