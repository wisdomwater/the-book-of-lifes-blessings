## 15 - The Nearness That Follows

> *You do not have to lead the way. Staying near is enough.*

I saw a soft light moving slowly across a wide horizon, steady and unhurried as evening light passing over open fields. It did not flash or call attention to itself through spectacle. Its faithfulness was quieter than that. It simply continued forward with calm assurance, illuminating the path one gentle step at a time.

Within this light walked a solitary figure, attentive and still within themselves, as though guided by something deeper than sight alone. The figure did not rush ahead in anxious certainty, nor lag behind in fear. It remained near the light with simple trust. When the light turned, it turned. When the light rested, it paused. There was no striving in its movement, no desperate need to control the direction of the journey.

The nearness itself seemed to become its own form of guidance.

As the figure continued walking, something subtle began to change. What once felt urgent or necessary slowly loosened its hold, like autumn leaves falling quietly from branches when their season has ended. Nothing was torn away violently. Nothing was rejected in shame. Certain things simply could not remain within the deeper rhythm of this nearness, and so they released naturally in time.

The path itself was not without difficulty. There were narrow places where vision became uncertain, stretches of shadow where the next step was not fully visible, quiet seasons that felt like testing rather than clarity. Yet even there, the light did not disappear. And the one who followed was never truly alone.

Beneath the walking there remained another mystery still—the sense of being gently carried even while moving forward. As though the path itself already rested within a wisdom far steadier than human certainty. The journey did not depend entirely upon the strength of the traveler, but upon the faithfulness of the light leading them onward.

### Living the Vision

Many people carry a quiet pressure to have life completely figured out before they can move forward. We want certainty about where we are going, confidence that our decisions are correct, reassurance that we are not making mistakes we cannot undo. The mind often believes peace will arrive once the entire path becomes visible.

Yet the soul longs for something simpler and more human than perfect control.

There are moments when we begin sensing that spiritual life is less about mastering the future and more about remaining close to what feels deeply true in the present moment. Guidance often comes this way—not through overwhelming certainty, but through a gentle steadiness that quietly invites us toward the next faithful step.

This kind of following requires trust. Not blind denial of reality, but trust that we do not need to carry the entire map of our lives at once. Often we are only given enough light for the step directly before us. And strangely, that small light becomes enough when we stop demanding guarantees about every horizon ahead.

Along the way, certain attachments naturally begin loosening. Old identities, habits, fears, ambitions, or ways of protecting ourselves may no longer fit the deeper movement unfolding within us. This release is rarely dramatic at first. More often it feels like a soft unfolding, a gradual realization that some things no longer nourish the life we are being called toward.

The path will still contain uncertainty. There will be seasons of shadow and waiting. Yet even there, the deeper Presence does not withdraw. We are not asked to force our way through life alone. We are invited to remain near—to stay attentive, honest, open, and willing to keep walking with the light we have been given today.

And perhaps this is enough: not perfect foresight, but faithful nearness.

### Breath Prayer

**Inhale:** Keep me near

**Exhale:** I follow in trust
