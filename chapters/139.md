## 139 - The Still Center

> *You are not made for endless turning*  
> *There is a place in you that can rest*

I saw a great wheel  
turning in the dimness—  
swift,  
unceasing,  
driven by a force  
that never paused.

A figure moved within it,  
grasping,  
adjusting,  
reaching for what passed by.

Its motion was constant,  
yet nothing settled.

Each turn brought it  
to the same place again—  
different in form,  
yet unchanged in depth.

Then,  
as the wheel continued,  
I noticed something hidden.

At its center—  
a quiet point  
that did not move.

While everything around it  
spun without rest,  
this place remained  
still,  
unaffected,  
untroubled.

It held the wheel  
without joining its motion.

The figure,  
in one brief moment,  
grew aware of it.

Its hands loosened.  
Its gaze softened.

For a breath’s space,  
it did not reach.

And in that pause,  
the turning lost its hold—  
not because it stopped,  
but because something deeper  
had been found.

And I saw  
that the stillness  
had always been there—  
waiting  
to be noticed.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when your life feels full of motion.

One thing leads to another—  
tasks, thoughts, concerns—  
each asking for attention.

You move through them,  
often without pause.

And yet,  
you may sense  
a quiet weariness—  
as though something in you  
is always turning,  
never quite arriving.

There is a place within you  
that does not move  
in the same way.

You may feel it  
in a pause between breaths,  
in a moment  
when you stop reaching  
for what comes next.

It does not require  
everything to stop.

Only a small turning  
of your attention.

You can return to it  
gently—  
again and again.

Not to escape movement,  
but to no longer be carried by it.

From this place,  
your actions begin to change.

They become less reactive,  
more grounded.

Less scattered,  
more whole.

You are not the turning.

You are held  
at the center.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Bring me to stillness  
>
> **Exhale:** I rest at the center
