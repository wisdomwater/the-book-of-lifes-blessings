## 188 - The Hidden Spring

> *Even the barren ground can receive*  
> *What seems empty may still be held*

I saw a wide, quiet land  
stretching into the distance.

The earth was dry,  
unbroken by streams,  
untouched by green.

Nothing moved.

The air was still,  
as if waiting  
for something it could not yet see.

And yet,  
beneath the surface,  
there was a softness—  
a hidden depth  
not yet revealed.

Then,  
very gently,  
a small drop appeared.

It did not fall from above.  
It rose from within—  
clear,  
quiet,  
unforced.

Where it touched,  
the ground did not resist.

It received.

And though the land  
did not change all at once,  
something had begun.

The dryness was no longer absolute.

A presence  
had found its way  
into the barren place.

It did not overwhelm.  
It remained.

And I understood:

Even where nothing seems to grow,  
there can be a hidden spring—  
waiting to be noticed,  
waiting to be trusted.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when life feels empty.

Not in appearance,  
but within—  
a dryness that touches  
your thoughts,  
your prayers,  
your sense of hope.

You may feel  
as though nothing is moving.

As though what once gave life  
has grown distant.

And in those moments,  
it can be easy to believe  
that nothing remains.

But there may be  
a quieter truth  
beneath that feeling.

You may notice it  
in something small—  
a breath that continues,  
a moment that softens,  
a faint awareness  
that you are still here.

It may not feel like much.

But it is not nothing.

You are not asked  
to create fullness.

Only to remain open  
to what is already present,  
even if it feels hidden.

Trust does not always begin  
with certainty.

It begins  
with a small turning—  
a willingness to stay  
even here.

And in that staying,  
something may begin to rise—  
slowly,  
quietly,  
but truly.
