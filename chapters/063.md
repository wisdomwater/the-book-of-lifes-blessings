## 63 - The Still Waters

> *What stirs in unrest can be quieted. Peace deepens where you choose to remain.*

I saw a deep place where movement never ceased. Currents turned endlessly upon themselves, rising and falling in restless circles that seemed unable to find rest. The waters pulled downward and surged upward again, repeating the same motion over and over as though trapped within their own turbulence.

The air above them trembled with unease.

A warm and unsettled wind moved across the surface, carrying the feeling of something unable to become still. Beneath the swirling currents, a hollow space seemed to open and close endlessly, drawing things downward only to release them again into the same exhausting motion. Nothing remained steady there. Everything was caught within repetition.

Then at the edge of this unrest, I noticed a quiet pool.

Its waters were still—not frozen or rigid, but deeply calm. The surface held its shape effortlessly, reflecting light without distortion. The pool did not struggle against the currents nearby, nor did it attempt to force the waters into silence. It simply remained grounded within its own stillness.

And where that stillness touched the edge of the circling waters, something began to change.

The movement did not vanish immediately, but it softened. The urgency weakened. The currents slowly lost their grip upon themselves, as though the deeper peace beside them revealed another possibility they had forgotten. And I saw then that stillness was not fragile or passive. It was deeper than the unrest surrounding it.

And because it was deeper, it endured.

**Living the Vision**

There are moments when life pulls us quickly into reaction. A difficult conversation, a sharp comment, a misunderstanding, or an unexpected frustration can stir something immediate within us. Thoughts race. Emotions rise. The impulse to respond quickly feels almost irresistible, as though something urgent must be defended or resolved right away.

This movement is deeply human.

Yet beneath the first wave of reaction, there is often another presence waiting quietly within us. It does not shout or demand attention. It simply invites pause. A breath before speaking. A moment of inward stillness before returning anger for anger or urgency for urgency.

These pauses matter more than we realize.

For within them, the soul begins remembering that it does not have to be carried away by every emotional current that passes through it. We can remain present without becoming consumed. We can speak truthfully without surrendering ourselves to heat, fear, or defensiveness.

Stillness is not avoidance.

It is groundedness.

It allows us to respond from clarity instead of compulsion. Over time, as we return again and again to this quieter center, we begin noticing that situations which once controlled our inner state no longer hold the same power over us. The currents may still move around us, but something deeper within remains steady.

And from that steadiness, peace slowly begins flowing outward into the spaces that once unsettled us most.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Settle what moves within

**Exhale:** I remain in peace
