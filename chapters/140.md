## Day 140 - The Ladder and the Lake

> *The soul that spins without discernment mistakes motion for freedom. But every desire leads either downward into confusion or upward toward God.*

Then I heard the voice from the storm-cloud answer the turning image.

“You are foolish and vain,” the voice said, “abandoned by the power of God’s gifts.

“Consider carefully. The devil did what he wanted, and he fell into hell. Adam did what pleased him, and all his children became mortal. Goliath trusted in what he could do, and he was struck down by a child. Nebuchadnezzar also trusted in his own greatness, and what became of him?

“God gave humanity the ability to act, but he also gave the knowledge to discern what is honorable and what is not.

“He placed in the human being the sword of good knowledge and the staff of evil knowledge. When the flesh gives itself to desire and the soul agrees, a struggle begins. Good knowledge raises its sword against evil, and evil knowledge strikes back against the good.

“So the human being must consider what is truly useful.

“Before evil knowledge, God placed a lake. Before good knowledge, he placed a ladder rising toward heaven, because good knowledge is a virtue of God.

“But you choose the knowledge that leads downward. You descend toward the lake, and you despise the ladder that climbs to heaven.”

And the wheel continued to turn in darkness.

But now its motion was revealed.

It was not freedom. It was descent disguised as choice. It was a soul carried by impulse, refusing the stillness needed to discern the way.

#### What This Vision Means

Constancy answers inconstancy by calling the soul back to discernment.

The vice claimed honesty: “I do what I can. I follow what is before me.” But the virtue reveals that not every capacity should be obeyed. Just because we can do something does not mean it leads to life.

The sword and staff show the inner conflict of the human person. Within us is knowledge that cuts through confusion and knowledge that excuses it. One defends the good. The other strikes against it.

The lake and ladder are simple but powerful images. Evil knowledge leads downward into heaviness, murk, and loss of direction. Good knowledge becomes a ladder—step by step, choice by choice, lifting the soul toward God.

Constancy is not rigidity. It is faithful direction. It keeps choosing the ladder, even when the wheel of impulse wants to spin.

#### Living This Vision

We often call ourselves free when we are merely following whatever desire is loudest.

Constancy asks us to pause.

Where is this choice leading?  
Is it carrying me toward clarity or confusion?  
Toward God or away from God?

The ladder is climbed through small acts of faithfulness. A truthful word. A restrained impulse. A promise kept. A prayer whispered before reacting.

You do not need to climb the whole ladder at once.

You only need to choose the next rung.
