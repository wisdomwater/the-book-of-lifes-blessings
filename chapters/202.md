## Day 202 - The Blood of the Lamb

> *The martyrs join their suffering to the Lamb. What was meant to silence them becomes a new song in heaven.*

Then I saw others in the radiance.

They stood upon something like a sea, but it was not liquid and had no moisture like water. It poured forth an intense brilliance.

They wore garments of red hyacinth, decorated with precious gems upon the shoulders and bordered near the feet. Their heads shone with crowns of pure gold, surrounded by circles of bright mirrors.

Their shoes were adorned with emeralds and beryl.

In their hands they held palm branches, transparent as clear crystal water. In these palms, as in mirrors, many miracles of God appeared.

Then I heard a voice from heaven like thunder:

“The old serpent opposed God and was cast into hell. He drew human beings toward himself and persuaded them to kill one another. But to erase the sins of humanity, the Lamb, the Son of God, took flesh and was killed.

Now let those who died for the Lamb come, and let the blood of the Lamb be joined with the blood of their bodies.”

When these words were spoken, the souls rose from the shining sea and poured drops of blood upon the land.

Then all the heavenly harmony answered with a new song to the testimony of the slain Lamb.

Each time the voice spoke, the martyrs poured out their blood, and new songs rose again.

These souls had despised bodily safety for the love of God. They endured torments and did not yield. They gave their members, one by one, even unto death.

And their suffering became praise.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision is filled with the imagery of martyrdom: red garments, palms of victory, blood, thunder, and song.

The martyrs do not suffer meaninglessly. Their blood is joined to the blood of Christ, the Lamb who was slain. Their witness renews the heavenly praise of His passion.

The shining sea shows that they stood firm above the anxiety of death. The palm branches reveal victory. The mirrors in their crowns and palms reveal that God’s miracles are reflected in their perseverance.

Their suffering was meant to erase them, but in God it becomes testimony.

#### Living This Vision

Most of us will not face martyrdom in blood.

But we do face moments when faithfulness costs us something.

Reputation.

Comfort.

Approval.

Control.

This vision asks us to remember that suffering offered in love is not wasted.

When you endure for what is true, when you refuse to surrender love to fear, when you bear pain without becoming cruel, your life joins the song of the Lamb.
