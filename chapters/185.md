## Day 185 - The Swamp of Wandering

> *Frivolity promises freedom, but leaves the soul without root or rest. True delight is found when the heart turns toward God.*

After this, I saw other evil spirits in the crowd.

They cried out:

“Our lord is Lucifer. He penetrates everything and knows all things.”

These spirits inspired frivolity in human beings and persuaded them not to desire stability.

Then I saw a great swamp.

It was filled with rotting, foul-smelling filth. From it rose a terrifying fog that covered the whole place.

There the souls were punished who, while living in the world, had given themselves to frivolity and wandered constantly from one thing to another.

Their souls were held in the swamp because of the disordered wandering by which they had sinned.

Because they had delighted in this vice, they tasted the stench of corruption. Because they had always searched for new and different sensations, they were wrapped in the fog.

And I saw and understood.

Then the living light spoke:

“The punishments you see are true. Those who wish to overcome the spirits of frivolity must humble themselves, mourn this vice, and restrain its excesses.”

Frivolity does not fear God.

It does not love God.

It lacks wisdom because it rejects holy fear and will not remain within love.

It is the daughter of disobedience and often the servant of lust. At first it may not seem fully joined to them, but it seeks them as companions and becomes bound.

It has no true joy and no true sorrow.

It is like unseasoned food.

It has no root and no fruitful branches.

No dew of prophecy nourishes its roots. No noon-day wisdom rises in its leaves.

It attracts idleness and laziness. It forgets what belongs to God and often neglects even the body’s true needs.

But those who serve God with steady devotion reject this vanity. They restrain body and spirit and lift their hearts to God.

For the soul that delights in the Lord is filled with good things that do not dry up.

#### What This Vision Means

Frivolity is restless distraction.

It is not the same as joy. Joy has depth. Frivolity avoids depth. It moves constantly because it cannot bear stillness.

The swamp reveals where this wandering leads. What first feels light becomes heavy. What first feels entertaining becomes foul. The fog shows how constant novelty clouds perception.

The phrase “no true joy and no true sorrow” is especially piercing. Frivolity numbs the soul. It keeps us from both deep gladness and honest grief.

The remedy is not grimness. It is holy delight—delighting in God until the heart becomes steady.

#### Living This Vision

Our world trains us in frivolity.

Scroll, click, taste, watch, move on.

We can become full of stimulation and empty of nourishment.

This vision asks:

What am I using to avoid stillness?

Where has distraction replaced delight?

God does not call us into a joyless life. He calls us into deeper joy—the kind that can take root, grow branches, and bear fruit.

Return to what nourishes.
