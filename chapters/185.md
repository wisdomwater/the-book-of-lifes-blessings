## 185 - The Rooted Delight

> *What remains begins to nourish*  
> *What is held in love becomes full*

I saw a quiet garden  
after a long wandering wind.

The air had once been thick—  
moving here and there,  
never settling,  
carrying fragments without rest.

But now,  
a stillness had come.

The ground lay open,  
softened,  
ready.

At the center,  
a small spring rose—  
clear,  
unhurried,  
constant.

It did not rush outward.  
It did not seek to fill everything at once.

It simply flowed,  
giving what it had,  
moment by moment.

Around it,  
roots reached downward.

Not scattered,  
not searching everywhere—  
but growing deep  
where the water was.

And as they deepened,  
life began to rise.

Leaves unfolded.  
Branches formed.  
Fruit appeared—  
quietly,  
without strain.

Nothing here hurried.

Nothing here wandered.

All was held  
within a gentle fullness.

And I understood:

There is a joy  
that does not move from place to place—  
it grows  
where it remains.

#### Living the Vision

You feel the pull to move on.

To shift your attention,  
to seek something new,  
to leave behind what feels ordinary.

It can feel like freedom—  
like you are following life  
wherever it leads.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a quiet emptiness  
beneath the movement.

A sense that nothing  
has time to take root.

But there may be  
another invitation  
gently present.

You may notice it  
in what quietly nourishes you—  
a place,  
a practice,  
a turning of the heart  
that feels simple  
and real.

It may not be exciting.  
It may not change quickly.

But something in you  
recognizes it  
as life-giving.

You are not asked  
to give up joy.

Only to allow it  
to deepen.

To remain  
long enough  
for something true  
to grow.

And in that remaining,  
you may discover  
a delight  
that does not fade—  
because it is rooted  
in what does not pass.
