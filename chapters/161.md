## Day 161 - The Lost Light

> *Forgetfulness of God wraps the heart in darkness, yet even after the fall, a little light remains.*

Again I saw evil spirits crying out:

“Come, come. Let us hurry where we need to go.”

These spirits led people into forgetfulness of God. They persuaded them not to remember their Creator or the works of His hands.

Then I saw a great valley, wide and long, filled with enormous fire and an unbearable stench. Within it writhed many worms, terrible to look upon.

There the souls were punished who, while living in the body, had surrendered to forgetfulness. They had forgotten the fear and love of God. They did not want to know or understand what they were doing.

They were placed in the valley because they had rejected faith in their hearts.

They burned in fire because they had desired impiety.

They endured the stench because they had opposed God.

They were tormented by worms because they had repeatedly raised up new deceits, full of cunning.

And I saw and understood.

Then the Living Light spoke:

“These punishments are true. Therefore, those who have forgotten God should flee the spirits that lead them into oblivion. Let them restore their hearts. Let them look again toward their Creator and His works.”

For Forgetfulness of God wraps the heart in the darkness of unbelief.

It whispers, “How can we know God, whom we have never seen? How can we remember what our eyes have never held?”

But when humanity fell, all creation was dimmed with it.

God had made the human being full of light. The first human could see the pure brightness of heaven and knew the song of angels. Radiance clothed him in splendor.

But when he broke the command of God, he lost this brilliance, and creation fell with him into shadow.

Yet not all light was lost.

A little light remained.

And by that light, the human heart can still know its Creator.


#### What This Vision Means

Forgetfulness of God is portrayed as darkness, fire, stench, and worms because it disfigures the soul from within.

To forget God is not merely to misplace an idea. It is to lose contact with the Source of life. When the soul forgets God, it forgets itself. It no longer understands what it is doing or what it was made for.

The valley suggests descent. The fire reveals disordered desire. The stench shows corruption. The worms represent thoughts and deceits that keep multiplying in the dark.

Yet the vision carries hope.

Even after the fall, a little light remains in humanity. The soul is wounded, but not erased. It still carries the capacity to remember. It can still look at creation and recognize the One who made it.

Forgetfulness is powerful, but it is not final.

Memory can return.

The heart can be restored.


#### Living This Vision

Sometimes we forget God not because we deny Him, but because life becomes loud.

Anxiety, distraction, pain, pleasure, work, and fear crowd the heart until remembrance grows faint.

This vision invites you to recover the little light.

Look at one created thing today with reverence.

A tree.
A cup of water.
Your own breath.
The face of someone you love.
The ground beneath your feet.

Let it remind you: I did not make myself.

Then turn that remembrance into prayer.

God is not absent simply because you cannot see Him directly.

The little light within you still knows how to seek Him.
