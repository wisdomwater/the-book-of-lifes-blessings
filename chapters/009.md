## 9 — The Quiet of What Is True

> *What is real does not strain to hold you*  
> *It remains, steady and clear*

I heard a voice  
not loud,  
but carrying  
through everything—

like a tone  
that does not fade.

It did not press.  
It did not demand.

It simply was.

And in its presence,  
all things became clearer—

not sharper,  
but more whole.

Nothing needed to be adjusted.  
Nothing needed to be hidden.

What was true  
rested in itself.

I saw a wide expanse—  
open,  
untroubled.

Light moved across it  
like morning over water.

There were no storms here.  
No breaking.

Only a quiet order—  
a rhythm  
that did not force itself.

And within this stillness,  
I felt a deep steadiness—

as if everything  
was being gently held  
in a knowing  
that could not be shaken.

There was no fear  
in this seeing.

Only a kind of reverence—  
a soft turning  
toward what is real.

And I knew  
this was not something  
to achieve.

It was something  
already present—  
waiting  
to be noticed.

#### Living the Vision

There's a quiet tension within—

a subtle pulling  
between what is easy  
and what is true.

You may notice  
how the mind tries to soften things,  
to reshape what you feel  
so it becomes more comfortable.

This is a gentle instinct.

But something deeper in you  
remains steady.

It does not argue.  
It does not rush.

It simply stays—  
like a quiet knowing  
beneath the surface.

You may sense it  
in small ways—

a pause before a decision,  
a feeling that something  
does not quite settle,  
or a soft clarity  
that asks for your attention.

This is not here  
to correct you.

It is here  
to guide you  
back into alignment  
with what already is.

You do not need  
to force yourself into truth.

You only need  
to become still enough  
to recognize it.

And as you do,  
something within you  
begins to rest—

not because everything is resolved,  
but because you are no longer  
turning away.

> **Inhale:** Steady my heart
