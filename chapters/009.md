## Day 9 — The Naked Joy of the Flesh

> *I saw a joy that trusted only the pleasures of the moment,
> and a deeper longing that reaches toward a life that never fades.*

In the vision I saw a seventh image.

From the head to the back it appeared like a man, but its hands were shaped like those of a monkey, restless and grasping. From the loins downward its body resembled that of a goat. Its feet were hidden deep within the darkness I had seen before, so that I could not see them clearly.

The figure stood naked and uncovered.

Then it spoke boldly:

“I live a sweet and pleasant life. Why should I deny myself?

God gave me this life. Why should I not give pleasure to my flesh?

Copper may first appear dark and rough, but later it shines like gold. If there is some stain in my flesh, that is no fault of mine.

Many people live blindly without knowing what they do.

But I know this life.

And I will enjoy it fully.”

When the figure had spoken, I again heard a voice from the stormy cloud answering it.

“You stand naked and yet feel no shame for living a blind and speechless life instead of the life where there is no darkness.

Your actions violate justice and truth. You do not live as a rational being.

But I know that the life of this world withers like dry grass.

Therefore my longing reaches toward what can never fade.

Within me I keep the harmony of heaven, the joy of angels, and the delight of the spirit.

These treasures do not weary me, for I have made a covenant with them, and I will never abandon them.”

#### What This Vision Means

This figure represents **the seduction of sensual pleasure** when it becomes the center of life.

The mixed human and animal body reveals a soul divided between reason and instinct. The monkey hands symbolize restless grasping for pleasure, while the goat-like lower body reflects indulgence in physical appetites.

The hidden feet suggest that this way of life stands upon uncertain ground.

The figure argues that pleasure is natural and therefore harmless. It even appeals to God as the creator of life and beauty.

But the voice of God’s Desire reveals the deeper issue: the problem is not pleasure itself, but the narrowing of the soul’s longing.

When the heart seeks only bodily satisfaction, it forgets the deeper joy it was created to receive.

God’s Desire speaks of another kind of joy—one rooted in divine harmony and spiritual communion. This joy does not fade like grass in the sun.

The soul that discovers it no longer needs to chase fleeting delights.

#### Living This Vision

Human life contains many beautiful pleasures—food, music, companionship, creativity, rest.

These gifts are part of the goodness of creation.

Yet when pleasure becomes our ultimate purpose, the soul quietly loses its direction.

We begin to organize our lives around comfort, stimulation, and personal gratification. Slowly, the deeper hungers of the spirit are forgotten.

But the heart was made for something larger.

There is a joy that comes not from consumption but from communion—from love, truth, creativity, compassion, and the presence of God within the soul.

This joy does not require constant stimulation.

It grows quietly, like a spring that never runs dry.

The invitation of this vision is not to reject the beauty of life, but to discover the deeper beauty that gives every other joy its meaning.
