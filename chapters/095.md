## Day 95 – The Devouring Wolf

> *When the soul abandons balance, it becomes consumed by its own desires.*
> *What it gathers does not satisfy—it only deepens the hunger.*

Finally, I saw a figure like a wolf, crouched low, watching, waiting.

This was Immoderation.

It bent inward upon itself, relying only on its own strength and will. It did not look upward. It did not seek guidance. It trusted only its own impulses.

It lurked everywhere, ready to seize whatever it could. It gathered endlessly, driven by a belief that accumulation would bring security.

But it was deceived.

For all that it gathered led only to ruin.

Its nature was restless, shifting, without stability. It followed every desire, bending itself toward whatever seemed pleasing in the moment. It rejected moderation, seeing it as limitation rather than wisdom.

And so it consumed—  
not only things, but meaning, order, and beauty.

It stripped away balance and replaced it with excess.

Yet nothing it gained could hold.  
Nothing it built could stand.

For what is against God cannot endure.

And I saw clearly: this path leads downward, not upward. It promises fullness, but delivers emptiness.

Still, a quiet and steady presence stood against it—the voice of true discernment, calling the soul to balance, to restraint, to a wiser way.

#### What This Vision Means

Immoderation is the loss of balance in the soul.

It is not simply indulgence, but a deeper reliance on self without reference to God or truth. The wolf image reveals its predatory nature—always seeking, always consuming, never satisfied.

The crouched posture shows how the soul turns inward, trusting only itself. This creates a closed system, cut off from wisdom and guidance.

Immoderation often feels like freedom. It promises the ability to do whatever one desires. But in reality, it leads to bondage—being driven by impulses rather than guided by truth.

The result is instability and eventual collapse.

Moderation, by contrast, is not restriction. It is alignment. It creates space for life to flourish in a sustainable way.

#### Living This Vision

Consider where your life feels out of balance.

Where are you consuming more than you need?  
Where are you acting without reflection?

Immoderation often hides in:
- habits that feel hard to control  
- patterns that repeat despite consequences  
- desires that feel urgent and overwhelming  

The path forward is not harsh denial, but gentle realignment.

Pause before acting.  
Create space between desire and response.  
Invite wisdom into your choices.

Balance is not something forced—it is something cultivated.

And as you practice moderation, you will find a deeper freedom—one that does not collapse, but endures.
