## Day 11 — The Four Living Faces

> *I saw that the human soul is shaped by many forces,
> and each bears the mark of God’s living wisdom.*

Then I heard the words of the prophet speaking of the four living faces:

The face of a man.
The face of a lion.
The face of an ox.
The face of an eagle.

And I understood their meaning.

The face of a man reveals that humanity is the work of God alone. In this face is the tenderness of divine mercy, which gives to the human heart the capacity to feel, to respond, and to be moved with compassion.

The face of the lion stands to the right. It reveals the strength and virtue of God. From it comes reason, courage, and the power to act rightly. This strength proclaims the greatness of good works done through divine inspiration.

The face of the ox stands to the left. It shows the mystery of sacrifice. In it I saw how humanity is called to offer itself to God. Some offerings are accepted, others are not. Yet when a person turns their will toward God, they are drawn upward even while still living among earthly things. And God, in His goodness, gives gifts so that the soul may offer itself more fully.

Above them all is the face of the eagle.

This face reveals the knowledge of God—the awareness that sees from above and understands according to divine will. It stretches everywhere, like life itself, seeing far and wide. In the soul, this knowledge shines like stars in the firmament.

And I understood that these are not separate things.

They live together within the human soul, forming its life in God.

#### What This Vision Means

The four faces describe the fullness of human spiritual life.

Each face represents a dimension of how the soul reflects God:

* The human face: compassion and sensitivity
* The lion: strength, courage, and reason
* The ox: sacrifice and devotion
* The eagle: spiritual insight and divine awareness

Together, they form a balanced and integrated life.

If one aspect is neglected, the soul becomes distorted. Compassion without strength may become weakness. Strength without compassion may become harshness. Sacrifice without understanding may become empty ritual. Knowledge without humility may become pride.

The eagle is placed above because spiritual awareness gives direction to all the others. It lifts the soul beyond immediate desires and helps it see life from a higher perspective.

This vision reveals that the human being is not simple.

It is a living harmony shaped by divine design.

#### Living This Vision

In daily life, we often feel pulled in different directions.

Sometimes we are called to be gentle. Other times we must be strong. Sometimes we are asked to give, and at other times to understand.

These tensions are not signs of confusion.

They are part of the soul’s design.

The invitation is not to choose one and reject the others, but to allow them to grow together in harmony.

You may ask yourself:

* Where am I being invited to show compassion?
* Where must I stand with courage?
* What am I being asked to offer or release?
* What deeper understanding is unfolding within me?

When these movements begin to align, life becomes less fragmented.

The soul begins to move with a quiet coherence.

Like the stars in the sky, each part finds its place.
