## 51 — Gathered Into Wholeness

> *You were not made to live divided*  
> *Something in you longs to be gathered again*

I saw a wide expanse  
touched by a restless wind—

light things lifted easily,  
carried here and there,  
never settling,  
never remaining.

They moved without direction,  
caught in currents  
they did not choose.

And within this movement,  
I felt a scattering—  
a sense of pieces  
no longer held together.

Thoughts drifting.  
Desires pulling apart.  
Attention divided  
across many directions at once.

Nothing was anchored.

Nothing was whole.

But then,  
beneath the movement,  
I noticed something quieter—

a center  
that had not been lost.

It did not chase the wind.  
It did not resist it.

It simply remained.

And slowly,  
as awareness turned toward it,  
the movement above  
began to soften.

What had been scattered  
was not forced back together—

it was gently drawn inward,  
as though remembering  
where it belonged.

The wind did not disappear.

But it no longer carried  
everything away.

Something deeper  
was holding it now.

And within that holding,  
there was peace.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
your inner world  
feels pulled in many directions—

a thought that won’t settle,  
a feeling that returns  
again and again,  
a tension that lingers  
beneath everything else.

You may notice  
how easily your attention  
is carried—  
from memory to reaction,  
from one possibility  
to another.

And somewhere within,  
a quiet exhaustion forms.

But beneath all of this,  
there is something  
that has not been scattered.

A place in you  
that remains whole.

You do not have to  
force yourself back together.

You can begin  
by simply noticing  
what is already still.

A breath.  
A pause.  
A soft return  
to what is here.

And in that returning,  
something begins  
to gather—

not by effort,  
but by presence.

You may feel  
a gentle coherence forming—  
a sense that you are  
no longer pulled apart.

You are being held  
from within.
