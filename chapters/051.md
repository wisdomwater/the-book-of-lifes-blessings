## Day 51 – Scattered by Fury

> *I heard a warning: anger divides the soul until it can no longer stand in peace.*

Then a voice spoke, recalling ancient words:

“Simeon and Levi are instruments of violence… cursed be their anger, for it is fierce.”

And I understood.

Anger and hatred bind themselves together, forming tools of destruction. They do not build—they tear down. They do not unite—they scatter.

I saw how those consumed by anger cannot stand among the righteous without disturbance. Their thoughts are hidden and unstable. They do not root themselves in what is firm and true, but move through shadows, seeking advantage, setting traps.

They cannot remain in the light.

Their speech turns sharp, filled with accusation and slander. Even when they speak of justice, their hearts burn with something else. They resist correction. They refuse to release what they hold.

And so they are scattered.

Not only outwardly, but inwardly.

Their thoughts are divided.  
Their desires are fragmented.  
Their peace is lost.

Like dry leaves caught in the wind, their passions carry them from place to place. They find no rest among those who seek God. Nor do they find rest among those who chase the world.

They belong nowhere.

For they have chosen division within themselves.

#### What This Vision Means

The scattering described here is both spiritual and psychological.

Anger, when held and fed, begins to fragment the inner life. It separates the person from others, but also from themselves. There is no longer unity between thought, intention, and action.

The inability to stand among the righteous symbolizes a deeper incompatibility. Anger resists humility, correction, and truth. It prefers justification over transformation.

The image of being scattered like straw reveals instability. Without grounding in love or truth, the soul becomes reactive, easily carried by emotion, unable to settle.

This is the quiet consequence of unchecked anger: not only conflict with others, but the loss of inner coherence.

#### Living This Vision

You may have felt this before.

The way anger lingers.  
The way it replays conversations.  
The way it pulls your thoughts in circles.

Over time, it becomes exhausting.

This vision invites you to consider a different path—not by denying anger, but by refusing to let it define you.

Where are you holding onto something that is dividing you inside?

Where has anger taken root and begun to scatter your peace?

The way forward is not through force, but through release.

To loosen your grip.  
To entrust justice to something greater than yourself.  
To return, slowly, to wholeness.

You were not made to live divided.

You were made for peace.
