## 48 — The Gentle Return to Presence
where joy becomes whole

> *Not all that delights will nourish you*  
> *There is a joy that gathers you inward*

I saw a gathering  
filled with light laughter—

soft voices,  
easy movement,  
a sense of brightness  
passing between many.

Among them moved  
a figure  
light in step,  
quick in response.

Wherever attention faded,  
something new appeared—

a story,  
a gesture,  
a flicker of delight  
to lift the moment again.

And those around  
leaned toward it,  
welcoming the ease,  
the lightness,  
the way it kept  
everything moving.

But beneath the surface,  
I felt a thinning—

as though the moment  
could not deepen,  
only continue.

Each spark  
gave way to another,  
never resting long enough  
to become still.

Then,  
just beyond the movement,  
I noticed a quiet space—

unfilled,  
unadorned,  
yet full in a different way.

The laughter did not reach here.

But something else did—

a gentle presence,  
steady,  
unhurried.

And in that space,  
joy was not lost—

it became whole,  
rooted,  
able to remain.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you reach for something light—

a distraction,  
a bit of noise,  
a way to soften  
what you are feeling.

It can seem harmless,  
even helpful.

A way to rest  
without having to go deeper.

But you may notice  
how quickly one thing  
leads to another—

how the quiet  
keeps being postponed,  
how the stillness  
never quite arrives.

And somewhere beneath it,  
a quieter longing remains.

Not for more stimulation—  
but for something  
that can hold you  
without needing to change.

You do not have to  
push anything away.

But you can gently notice  
what nourishes  
and what only fills.

There is a space in you  
that does not need  
to be entertained.

It only waits  
to be entered.

And when you do,  
you may find  
that joy has not disappeared—

it has been waiting  
to deepen.
