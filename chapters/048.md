## 48 - The Word Within

> *What was written at a distance draws near and begins to breathe within you.*

I saw a wide stone path stretching across a quiet and open landscape. Its surface was marked with lines and forms carefully carved into the stone—clear, enduring, unchanged by time or weather. The markings carried the weight of wisdom preserved faithfully across generations. They spoke of direction, order, and truth.

And yet the path remained empty.

No footsteps moved upon it. No living presence walked its length. It seemed to wait in stillness, as though something essential had not yet fully arrived.

Then slowly, light began gathering within the stone itself.

Not descending from above, but awakening from inside what had once appeared cold and rigid. The radiance moved gently beneath the surface like warmth spreading through earth after winter. What had once seemed fixed and distant softened beneath its quiet presence.

And from within the light emerged something living.

Not separate from the path, but flowing through it like breath through a body. Where there had once been only form, there was now movement. Where there had once been instruction observed from afar, there was now intimacy.

The markings upon the stone did not disappear.

They became alive.

No longer merely words to obey externally, they unfolded inwardly as living wisdom carried within the heart itself. The path was no longer something outside the traveler demanding adherence from a distance. It appeared wherever the living presence moved.

And I understood then that true guidance does not merely command.

It awakens.

The nearness of the light did not pressure or control. It invited the soul gently into harmony with what was already breathing quietly within it. The path became less about striving toward perfection and more about learning to walk in union with the life already present beneath the surface.

### Living the Vision

Many people spend years trying to do what is right while inwardly feeling disconnected from the life beneath their actions. They follow expectations, rules, habits, or responsibilities faithfully, yet something within them remains distant and weary. Goodness can begin to feel external—a performance carried outwardly rather than a living reality flowing from the heart.

Eventually, the soul begins longing for something deeper than correctness alone.

It longs for nearness.

For authenticity.

For a way of living where love no longer feels imposed from the outside, but arises naturally from within.

This longing is sacred.

For the deeper spiritual life is not merely about conforming behavior to ideals. It is about awakening to the living presence already moving quietly inside us. Divine wisdom is not only something written in sacred texts, traditions, or teachings—though it may certainly come through them. It is also something breathed into the soul itself, gradually becoming embodied through awareness, compassion, trust, and relationship.

Often this awakening happens subtly.

A kind response arises without calculation.

Forgiveness becomes possible where bitterness once ruled.

An action emerges naturally from love rather than obligation.

These moments reveal something important: the heart is learning to move from inner communion rather than external pressure alone.

This does not mean guidance is unnecessary or that wisdom becomes self-created. Rather, it means the truths once held at a distance begin taking root within us until they become part of how we breathe, speak, love, and walk through the world.

Over time, what once felt like burden slowly becomes life.

The path is no longer only something we follow.

It becomes something living within us.
