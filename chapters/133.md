## 133 - The Clearing of the Waters

> *Even when the heart feels heavy*  
> *there is still a way back to light*

I saw a wide expanse  
dimmed by shadow—  
not empty,  
but unclear,  
as though something  
had settled over it.

Beneath the surface  
was a thick stillness,  
like water that had lost  
its movement.

Nothing reflected clearly.  
Nothing seemed alive  
in the way it once had been.

And yet,  
above it,  
light remained.

Soft.  
Unhurried.  
Patient.

It did not force its way in.  
It simply rested,  
waiting.

Then,  
very gently,  
the surface began to stir.

Not by effort—  
but by a quiet openness,  
a small yielding  
to what was already present.

The heaviness shifted.  
The water loosened.

What had been clouded  
began to clear—  
slowly,  
almost imperceptibly.

And in that clearing,  
something returned—

a reflection,  
a depth,  
a sense of life  
that had never fully left.

And I saw  
that nothing had been destroyed.

Only covered.

Only waiting  
for the heart  
to turn again  
toward the light.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
everything can feel dull.

As if meaning has thinned,  
as if what once stirred you  
no longer reaches you in the same way.

You may wonder  
if something has been lost.

But you may also notice  
a quiet invitation—  
not to force yourself  
to feel differently,  
but simply to remain open.

To let your attention rest  
on one small thing  
that still carries life.

A breath.  
A word of prayer.  
A moment of gratitude.

You do not need  
to clear everything at once.

Only to allow  
what is heavy  
to soften,  
even slightly.

Faith can begin here—  
not as certainty,  
but as a turning.

A willingness  
to look again  
toward what is true,  
even if it feels distant.

And in that small turning,  
something begins to move.

Not all at once.  
But enough  
to remind you  
that light is still present—  
and has never truly left you.
