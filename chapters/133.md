## Day 133 - The Mud of Unbelief

> *I saw a great fire surrounded by darkness, and beneath it a vast pool of stinking mud, for unbelief turns the joy of God’s creatures into falsehood and sorrow.*

After this I saw other evil spirits  
crying out in the crowd:

“God will come to an end,  
and another will take His place.”

These spirits suggest unbelief to human beings  
and drive them away from trust in God.

Then I saw a vast and terrible fire  
surrounded by darkness.

Beneath it lay a pool of stinking mud,  
extraordinary in width, length, and depth.

Around both the fire and the mud  
horrible worms ran back and forth.

There the souls were tormented  
who, while living,  
did not trust God  
and held Him in their minds as nothing—  
as though He were an idol without life.

They burned in the fire  
because of their wickedness.

They were surrounded by darkness  
because in unbelief  
they would not look toward God.

They were soiled by the mud  
because disbelief had changed  
the righteous joy of God’s creatures  
into falsehood and pain.

They endured the worms  
because they were obstinate  
in refusing hope in God.

Then I heard the living light say:

“These things are true.  
Those consumed by unbelief,  
if they wish to overcome the spirits  
that suggest it,  
must fast, pray, and kneel  
until divine power drives from them  
the deeds and words of disbelief.”

And I understood:

Unbelief stands against God  
and against humanity.

It trusts neither God nor man.

It says within itself:  
“Who am I?  
What have I been?  
What will I be?”

Those who speak this way  
are blind,  
because they have no hope of salvation in God.

They do not want to know day or night.

When they refuse to know God,  
they deny the day.  
When they do not fear His judgments,  
they reject the night.

And because they are unjust toward human beings,  
they despise the creation of God.

Their thoughts become blind.  
Their life loses order.

But the good souls  
who wish to flee disbelief  
join themselves to God  
by faith and good works,  
preferring to be with Him  
rather than with the devil.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows unbelief as a deep corruption of perception. It does not only deny God intellectually. It changes how the soul experiences reality. The joy of creation becomes mud. The living God is treated like a lifeless idol. Day and night lose their meaning.

The fire shows the active harm of unbelief. The darkness shows the refusal to look toward God. The stinking mud reveals how unbelief soils the world, making what should awaken gratitude feel empty, false, or painful. The worms suggest obstinate thoughts that keep moving through the soul, repeating distrust.

The questions “Who am I? What have I been? What will I be?” are not condemned as honest searching. They become destructive when severed from hope. Without trust in God, self-questioning can collapse into confusion and despair.

Faith restores order. It does not answer every mystery immediately, but it turns the face of the soul toward God again. And in that turning, day becomes day, night becomes night, and creation begins to speak of joy once more.

#### Living This Vision

Unbelief can make the world feel flat.

Beauty no longer lifts the heart.  
Prayer feels pointless.  
The future feels sealed in fog.  
Even the question “Who am I?”  
becomes heavy.

This vision invites you not to despise your questions,  
but to bring them into hope.

Ask:  
Who am I in God?  
What have I been given?  
What might grace still make of me?

Faith is not pretending certainty.  
It is turning toward God  
even when the inner landscape feels muddy.

One small act of faith matters.

Kneel.  
Pray badly but honestly.  
Give thanks for one creaturely joy.  
Do one good work even before you feel belief return.

Unbelief isolates the soul.  
Faith reconnects it.

And sometimes the first light of faith  
is simply the willingness  
to look toward God again.
