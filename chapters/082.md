## 82 - The Open Field of Blessing

> *Another’s good does not close your path. It reveals how wide the field truly is.*

I saw a narrow place where everything seemed pressed inward upon itself. The air felt tight and unsettled, as though it could no longer move freely through the space. Heat rose suddenly and then disappeared into coldness. Nothing rested long enough to become peaceful. There was constant motion there—thoughts circling endlessly, measuring, comparing, reacting to every movement nearby.

The place felt small not because it lacked space, but because something within it had forgotten how to look outward.

And beyond this narrowness, I saw an open field.

It stretched wide beneath a quiet sky, unbounded and still. The air moved gently through tall grasses and growing things. Light rested easily upon the earth without needing to choose where it would fall. Nothing in the field struggled against what stood beside it. Each living thing remained in its own place—rooted, nourished, receiving what it needed without fear that another’s flourishing would diminish its own.

There was no urgency there. No competition.

Only abundance.

And I saw then that the narrow place had never truly been closed. Its walls were formed from inward turning—from the anxious belief that goodness was scarce and that love must be earned through comparison. The open field had always existed beyond it, patient and unchanged.

The moment the gaze lifted, even slightly, the tightness began to loosen.

Space returned.

Breath returned.

And with them came the quiet freedom to rejoice in life again.

**Living the Vision**

Comparison has a way of shrinking the soul. Another person succeeds, receives recognition, finds joy, healing, beauty, or belonging—and suddenly something within us tightens. The mind begins measuring worth in secret ways. We compare timelines, gifts, opportunities, appearances, achievements, or spiritual progress, often without even realizing we are doing it.

And slowly, the heart begins to live inside a smaller world.

In this narrowness, another person’s goodness can feel threatening, as though their flourishing somehow leaves less life available for us. Yet the deeper spiritual life reveals a different truth: Divine Love does not move through scarcity. Grace is not rationed. The flourishing of another soul does not close the door to your own becoming.

The contemplative path gently teaches the heart to widen again.

Sometimes this widening begins with a very small movement—a conscious blessing offered toward someone we envy, even before our emotions fully agree. Not forced positivity. Not denial of our feelings. Simply a willingness to step beyond the reflex of comparison long enough to remember that we belong to the same living field of grace.

And when we do, something within us softens.

The nervous urgency eases. We begin to see beauty without needing to possess it. Another person’s light no longer feels like evidence of our absence from love. Instead, it becomes a reminder of how generous life truly is.

There is room for many forms of flourishing.

There is room for your life, too.

The field has always been wide enough to hold you.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Widen my heart in love

**Exhale:** I bless what is good
