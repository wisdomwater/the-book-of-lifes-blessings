## Day 82 — The Marble of Prophecy

> *What is seen dimly in shadow prepares the soul to recognize the light.*

And I saw the book upon the wing at the back of the Man.

It appeared as a single piece—  
white like marble,  
solid and unbroken.

And I understood:

This was the strength of prophecy.

Those who spoke before the coming of the Son  
saw only from behind—  
as one who sees the back of a figure  
and wonders at the face.

They perceived truth,  
but not yet its full form.

They spoke what they were given—  
nothing more,  
nothing less.

Their words were firm,  
like stone.

They did not bend to please others.  
They did not scatter their speech.

They remained steady  
in what they had seen.

And yet, there was a simplicity in them.

Like children,  
they spoke plainly.

They did not pretend to know what was hidden.  
They did not add to what was revealed.

They stood in truth  
without embellishment.

And I saw:

Though their vision was partial,  
it was faithful.

Though they saw in shadow,  
they spoke with clarity.

And what they foretold  
would later be revealed in fullness.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision honors the role of prophecy as both limited and trustworthy.

The prophets did not possess complete knowledge. They saw glimpses—shadows of what was to come. Yet those glimpses were real.

The marble imagery reflects both strength and restraint. Prophecy is not fluid or speculative. It is grounded, exact, and resistant to distortion.

The idea of seeing “from behind” is deeply important. It acknowledges that revelation unfolds progressively. What is unclear at one time becomes clear later.

This vision also emphasizes integrity. The prophets did not attempt to go beyond what they were given. They did not shape their message to fit expectations.

They spoke what they saw.

This is a model not only for prophecy, but for all truth-telling.

#### Living This Vision

You are not required to see everything clearly.

There will be moments when you only understand partially—  
when something feels true,  
but not yet fully formed.

That is not failure.

It is part of the path.

Speak what you know—  
but do not pretend to know more.

Live what has been revealed—  
and let the rest unfold.

There is strength in this kind of simplicity.

It keeps you grounded.  
It keeps you honest.

Over time, what once felt like shadow  
will take on form.

But even then,  
you will still be invited to walk  
with humility.

For the fullness of truth  
is always greater  
than what you can hold at once.
