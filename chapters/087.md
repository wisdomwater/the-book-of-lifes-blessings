## Day 87 — The Hidden Choir of the Saints

> *There are souls so given to God that even now they live like a quiet echo of heaven.*

In the brightness of that blessed cloud,  
I saw deeper still—  
into a hidden place of joy.

There were souls there  
who had come to the fullness of holiness.

While they lived in the body,  
they had given themselves to contemplation—  
turning inward and upward toward God.

Now they were adorned  
with virtues beyond counting,  
clothed in a radiance not of earth.

And they sang.

Their voices were like trumpets—  
clear, resounding, filled with praise.

Their music flowed like harps,  
like waters of the sea,  
like many streams joining into one great sound.

It was a harmony so vast  
that the human heart could scarcely bear it.

And I understood:

These songs were the breath  
of prophets, sages, and all who had been touched by God.

The Spirit had moved through them in life—  
and now that same Spirit  
filled their praise.

These were the ones  
who had risen above the pull of the flesh.

They had not lived for passing things.  
They had turned from the weight of the world  
as though it could not hold them.

They became, even on earth,  
like angels among men.

Through the windows of faith,  
with the simplicity of children,  
they had found God.

And now they remained in Him—  
steady, joyful, and full.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals a deeper level of spiritual maturity—a life fully oriented toward God.

These souls are not separate because they were superior in a worldly sense. They are separate because they were wholly given. Their attention, desire, and devotion were directed toward the divine rather than toward the passing concerns of earthly life.

Contemplation here is not escape. It is alignment. It is the steady turning of the soul toward what is eternal.

The music symbolizes harmony with God. When a life is fully attuned to Him, it becomes an expression of something larger—like an instrument carried by a greater song.

The description of them as “angels among men” points to transformation. They lived in the world, but were not bound by its illusions.

This vision is not meant to discourage, but to reveal what is possible when a life becomes rooted in the divine presence.

#### Living This Vision

You do not need to leave the world  
to begin turning toward God.

But you can begin to loosen  
your attachment to what does not last.

Make space—  
even briefly—  
for stillness.

Let your attention rest on something deeper  
than the constant movement of life.

You may not become a contemplative in the full sense.  
But you can become more attentive,  
more present,  
more anchored.

And in those small moments,  
something begins to shift.

The noise quiets.  
The heart softens.  
A different kind of joy appears.

Over time,  
your life begins to resonate  
with something more enduring.

Like a quiet note  
joining a greater song.
