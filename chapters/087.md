## 87 - The Rooted Presence

> *What abides grows deep*  
> *What rests becomes whole*

I saw a single tree  
standing in an open field.

Its branches did not reach wildly.  
They extended with quiet balance,  
held in a still and steady form.

The air moved around it—  
winds passing from many directions,  
carrying distant sounds,  
stirring the grasses below.

But the tree did not follow.

It remained.

Its roots reached downward,  
hidden,  
drawing from a deeper source.

Above,  
its leaves turned gently toward the light,  
not seeking to be seen,  
but simply receiving.

There was no striving in it.  
No need to appear elsewhere.

All that it was  
was fully present  
where it stood.

And in that presence,  
life gathered—  
birds resting in its branches,  
shade forming beneath it,  
a quiet fullness  
that did not announce itself.

Nothing in the tree  
was scattered.

It was whole  
because it remained.

And I understood:

There is a way of being  
that does not need to be everywhere—  
because it has learned  
to dwell  
deeply  
where it is.

#### Living the Vision

Something in you  
wants to move outward.

To be noticed,  
to be known,  
to extend your presence  
beyond where you are.

It can feel like a pull—  
subtle,  
persistent.

A sense that you might miss something  
if you do not reach further.

But you may also notice  
another movement,  
quieter  
and more grounded.

A sense of being here—  
not as a limitation,  
but as a place  
where something real can take root.

You are not asked  
to withdraw from life.

Only to notice  
when you are being drawn away  
from your center.

There is a depth within you  
that cannot be found  
in constant movement.

It reveals itself  
in staying,  
in listening,  
in allowing time  
to shape what is growing.

And as you remain,  
even for a moment,  
you may feel it—  
a quiet settling,  
a gentle coherence,  
a life that does not need to scatter  
to be alive.

> **Inhale:** Root me here
