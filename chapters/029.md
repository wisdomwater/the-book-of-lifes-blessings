## 29 — The Trembling That Reveals

> *What is unsettled is not forsaken*  
> *Even the trembling can open into truth*

I saw the gentle movement  
begin to stir—

what had once drifted  
in quiet rhythm  
now carried a deeper motion.

Not chaotic.  
Not lost.

But purposeful—  
like a wind  
moving through still air.

The calm did not vanish.  
It deepened.

And within that deepening,  
there was a trembling—

soft at first,  
then more fully felt.

The air seemed to awaken.  
The ground responded.

Not in fear,  
but in recognition—

as if something long hidden  
was being touched by light.

There were flashes—  
brief,  
illuminating.

Not to overwhelm,  
but to reveal.

What had been beneath the surface  
came into view—

not all at once,  
but enough to be seen.

And through it all,  
there remained a steady presence—

unchanged,  
unmoved,  
holding everything  
within a quiet care.

The trembling did not undo the world.

It uncovered it.

And in that uncovering,  
there was an invitation—

not to retreat,  
but to return.

#### Living the Vision

There are times when  
your life feels unsettled—

when something shifts,  
and what once felt stable  
no longer holds in the same way.

You may feel this  
in your thoughts,  
your relationships,  
your sense of direction.

It can be disorienting.

And something in you  
may want to resist it—  
to return quickly  
to what felt certain.

But there is another way  
to be here.

You may begin to notice  
what the shaking reveals—

not as something to judge,  
but as something to see.

Patterns.  
Assumptions.  
Quiet attachments  
that have been holding you  
without being noticed.

This is not a moment  
of failure.

It is a moment  
of unveiling.

You are not being cast out  
of what is true.

You are being drawn  
more deeply into it.

And even here—  
in the movement,  
in the uncertainty—  
there is something steady  
beneath you.

You are still held.

And from that holding,  
you can gently turn  
toward what is real.
