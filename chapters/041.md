## 41 - The Waters Made Clear

> *What is released returns to stillness*  
> *The heart clears when it no longer clings*

I saw a wide expanse of water—  
once troubled,  
now gently settling.

The surface moved in soft ripples,  
as though remembering  
a former unrest.

But the motion was easing.

The heaviness beneath  
began to sink,  
drifting downward  
until it no longer clouded  
what could be seen.

Above, the air was warm—  
not harsh,  
but steady—  
like a breath  
that neither rushed nor withdrew.

Light touched the water  
without resistance.

And where it rested,  
the surface grew clear—  
revealing depth  
that had always been there.

Along the edges,  
small streams flowed inward,  
bringing fresh movement—  
quiet, continuous,  
unforced.

Nothing was being driven out.

Nothing resisted.

What had once been stirred  
was simply no longer held.

And in that release,  
the water changed—

becoming calm enough  
to reflect the sky,  
deep enough  
to hold life again.

There was no striving here.

Only a gentle returning—  
as if the waters themselves  
remembered  
how to be at peace.

#### Living the Vision

Something rises in you—  
quick,  
intense,  
alive with feeling.

It may feel justified.  
It may feel strong.

And yet,  
you may notice  
how easily it lingers—  
how it begins to shape  
the way you see,  
the way you speak,  
the way you hold yourself.

There is another way  
of being with it.

You may begin  
by simply noticing—  
not pushing it away,  
not feeding it—  
but allowing it  
to move.

A breath softens.  
A pause opens.

And in that space,  
something loosens  
without effort.

You are not asked  
to deny what you feel.

Only to remain open  
as it passes through.

Over time,  
you may sense  
a quiet clarity returning—  
a steadiness  
that was never lost,  
only hidden.

The heart does not need  
to hold everything.

It can release  
and still remain whole.
