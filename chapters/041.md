## 41 - The Waters Made Clear

> *What is released returns to stillness. The heart clears when it no longer clings.*

I saw a wide expanse of water stretching quietly beneath an open sky. Once it had been troubled, stirred deeply by currents moving beneath the surface, but now the waters were beginning to settle. Gentle ripples still moved across the surface, as though the lake itself remembered its former unrest, yet the motion no longer carried violence within it. Something deeper was softening.

Beneath the surface, the heaviness that had once clouded the water slowly drifted downward. Sediment that had been endlessly stirred began settling into stillness. What had obscured clarity no longer remained suspended within the current. The waters were not forcing anything away. They were simply no longer holding tightly to what had unsettled them.

Above, the air rested warm and steady over the lake.

It felt like a quiet breath—constant, patient, neither rushing nor withdrawing. Light touched the water gently, and the surface received it without resistance. Wherever the light rested, the water became clear enough to reveal its hidden depth. What had always existed beneath the surface slowly came back into view.

Along the edges, small streams continued flowing inward.

Fresh water entered softly and continuously, not disrupting the stillness but renewing it. The movement was natural and unforced, part of a larger rhythm the waters no longer resisted. Nothing was being driven out. Nothing struggled to remain. What had once been tightly stirred simply loosened and settled on its own.

And as the waters released what they no longer needed to carry, they changed.

The lake became calm enough to reflect the sky once more. Deep enough to sustain life again. Spacious enough to hold both movement and stillness together without fear.

There was no striving within this peace.

Only a quiet remembering—as though the waters themselves had finally returned to their natural clarity.

**Living the Vision**

There are moments when strong emotions rise suddenly within us. Anger, frustration, hurt, fear—feelings that move quickly through the body with great intensity. Often these emotions feel justified, and sometimes they are. They reveal that something meaningful has been touched within us.

Yet what begins as feeling can slowly become something we cling to.

The mind replays conversations. The body tightens around old injuries. A moment passes, yet inwardly we continue carrying it. Over time, these unprocessed emotions begin shaping how we see others, how we speak, even how we inhabit ourselves. The waters of the heart grow clouded not because emotions arise, but because we become unable to release them once they have moved through us.

The spiritual life offers another possibility.

We are not asked to deny what we feel or pretend that pain does not exist. Instead, we are invited to become spacious enough to let emotions move without allowing them to define our deepest identity. This often begins very simply—with awareness. A pause. A breath. A willingness to remain present without immediately reacting or feeding the inner turbulence further.

As we soften into this presence, something begins loosening naturally.

Not through force.

Not through suppression.

But through release.

The heart slowly learns that it does not need to hold every wound indefinitely in order to remain safe or whole. Beneath the turbulence there is already a deeper steadiness waiting quietly within us. And as old heaviness settles and clears, clarity begins returning almost on its own.

The soul becomes capable once again of reflecting light rather than only reacting to disturbance.

And in that clarity, peace returns—not as escape from life, but as a deeper way of inhabiting it.
