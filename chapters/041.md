## Day 41 – The Cry of the Lost

> *To turn away from life is to feel its absence—and to know, too late, what was refused.*

I saw, above the darkness, a great multitude.

But they were not like the radiant ones.

They were separated.

Cut off from the harmony I had seen before.

They did not join the song.  
They could not.

They had chosen another path.

Their guide was called the Seducer—  
the one who had been cast down.

And they had followed him.

While they lived, they had turned away from what was good.

They rejected the voices that called them toward life.  
They refused the company of those who walked in righteousness.

They desired what was empty.

And so they became bound to it.

Now, they remained in a state of deep sorrow.

Not imposed—  
but chosen.

They cried out:

“Alas, alas!  
Our deeds have led us here.  
We have chosen death.”

Their voices were filled with regret.

They spoke of loss.  
Of what might have been.

And in their anguish, they blamed Adam—  
for the fall,  
for the darkness.

But I saw the truth.

They had seen the light.

They had heard the call.

And they had turned away.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision portrays the reality of spiritual separation as the result of persistent rejection of truth.

The lost souls are not depicted as victims of arbitrary judgment, but as individuals who chose to align themselves with what is false and destructive.

Their sorrow arises from recognition—the realization of what they refused.

Blaming Adam reflects the human tendency to deflect responsibility. Yet the vision makes clear that each soul had its own opportunity to respond to truth.

The Seducer represents the deceptive pull of evil, which entices but ultimately leads to emptiness.

This vision is not meant to instill fear, but clarity: that choices matter, and that alignment with truth is essential for life.

#### Living This Vision

It is easy to ignore what is true.

To delay.  
To turn away.  
To choose what feels easier in the moment.

But over time, these choices shape something within you.

This vision is not asking you to live in fear.

It is inviting you to live with awareness.

You are always responding to something—  
to truth, or to something less.

And you can choose again.

Even now.

You can turn toward what is life-giving.

You can listen.

You can begin.
