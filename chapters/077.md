## Day 77 — The Burning Hands at the Tower

> *There are powers that seek ruin, yet salvation stands higher and does not yield.*

I saw the eighth image.

It was like a tower.  
At its summit was a fortified place with three open windows.  
Below them appeared two human arms, stretched over the stronghold.  
Those arms were wrapped in darkness, as though darkness itself were their sleeves.

But the hands were bare.

And they burned.

Then the image spoke:

“What reward is there for me but fire?  
It is what I desire, and it is what formed me.  
I turn away from everything that shines.  
I reject every work of light.

I do not want beauty.  
I do not want jewels.  
I am a hunter of souls.

This is my work.  
This is what I was born for.  
I am the curse of the one who made me.”

Its words were terrible.  
It did not merely wander into darkness.  
It chose it.  
It gloried in destruction and named ruin its calling.

Then from the stormy cloud I heard a voice answer:

“You are a dart of the devil,  
flying in the night against the holy ones.  
You desire to bring them down,  
but you cannot overcome them.”

The voice grew stronger.

“The saints rise beneath the banner of faith.  
They rush against you with longing,  
as a deer longs for living water.

You will be overwhelmed  
by baptism  
and by the seven gifts of the Holy Spirit,  
poured forth in the Savior.”

Then another mystery opened.

“I am the house of all good,  
the tower of Jerusalem in the works of the saints.

I uphold the penitent.  
I nourish the simple through faith.  
I strengthen the innocent by the anointing of the Spirit.

And through the pure lily of virginity  
that blossomed in the flesh of Christ,  
the way of salvation is renewed.

Therefore I am also the militia of God.”

And I understood:

The tower of darkness could reach upward with burning hands,  
but it could not become the holy tower.  
Its fire consumed.  
The other fire saved.

#### What This Vision Means

This image reveals a will that has become devoted to destruction. It does not merely stumble into evil; it identifies with it. The burning hands show a power eager to seize, wound, and ruin. The tower shape suggests false strength—something fortified, elevated, and difficult to confront.

Yet the answer from heaven unveils a greater reality. Salvation is not fragile. It is a house, a tower, a defense, a dwelling place. What opposes God may rage, but it cannot finally overcome what is sustained by baptism, grace, faith, and the Holy Spirit.

The contrast is sharp: one tower is built around predation, the other around refuge. One wants to possess souls, the other to heal and restore them.

The mention of penitents, the simple, and the innocent is especially tender. Salvation is not reserved for the impressive. It shelters the returning, the trusting, and the undefended.

This vision reminds us that evil may appear organized and strong, but it is inwardly doomed. The true stronghold is the life of God at work in the saints.

#### Living This Vision

There are times when forces within or around us feel predatory.

A habit that keeps circling back.  
A voice of accusation.  
A pressure that wants to pull the soul downward.

This vision does not ask you to pretend those powers are small.  
It shows them clearly.

But it also asks you to remember where you stand.

You are not left without a tower.  
You are not left without defense.

Faith is not weakness.  
Repentance is not failure.  
Returning to God is not defeat.

The shelter of God is built for people who need mercy.

When you feel hunted by fear, shame, compulsion, or despair,  
do not make your home in the dark fortress.  
Turn again toward the house of all good.

Stand beneath that banner.  
Ask for the Spirit’s help.  
Let salvation be stronger than what stalks you.
