## 77 - The Tower of Quiet Strength

> *What rises in light cannot be overcome*  
> *The soul is held where love abides*

I saw a tower  
standing in the early light.

It rose without strain—  
not built in haste,  
but formed in stillness.

Its walls were open,  
not to expose,  
but to welcome the light  
that moved through it.

Near its summit,  
three windows  
looked outward—  
clear,  
uncovered,  
receiving the morning.

From within,  
a presence extended—  
like arms reaching gently  
over the edges.

Not grasping.

Not claiming.

Only offering.

Light rested upon them—  
soft,  
steady—  
like warmth that does not burn,  
but restores.

Below,  
there was movement in the distance—  
restless,  
searching—  
like something trying  
to rise by its own force.

But it could not reach  
what was held above.

For the tower did not resist.

It remained.

Rooted  
in something deeper  
than effort.

And I saw  
that this strength  
did not come from height alone—  
but from what dwelt within.

A quiet life.  
A living presence.

Holding all things  
in peace.

#### Living the Vision

There are times when  
something within you feels unsettled—

a pull,  
a pressure,  
a sense of being drawn  
away from your center.

It may feel persistent,  
as though it will not let go.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
another place within you—

quieter,  
more steady.

It does not argue.  
It does not chase.

It simply remains.

You may sense it  
when you pause—  
when you return  
to your breath,  
or to a simple awareness  
of being held.

This place  
does not need to defend itself.

It is already rooted  
in something deeper  
than what moves around it.

You are not asked  
to overcome everything  
that rises within you.

Only to remember  
where you stand.

To return,  
again and again,  
to the place  
where light is already present.

And in that returning,  
you may find  
a strength  
that does not strain—

a quiet stability  
that holds you  
without effort.
