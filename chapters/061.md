## 61 - The Unveiling of the Heart

> *What loses its way can be seen again. Light waits beneath every veil.*

I saw a figure standing in a dim and quiet place. At first it appeared familiar and whole, carrying the shape of someone still moving through life as before. Yet something within the figure felt turned inward, as though its attention no longer faced the light surrounding it. There was a subtle distance present—a quiet separation difficult to name, but deeply felt.

A veil rested gently over its head.

It was not harsh or oppressive. It did not wound or imprison. And yet it softened everything beneath it, blurring what had once been clear and dimming what had once carried warmth and brightness. The figure continued speaking, moving, and responding, but the movements felt disconnected somehow, as though the outer actions no longer flowed naturally from the deeper life within.

Around the figure, the air remained still.

Nothing opened. Nothing flowed freely. The whole space carried the quiet heaviness of inward separation. Yet beyond the dimness, a steady brightness remained visible in the distance. The light did not force itself forward or demand to be noticed. It simply waited—unchanging, patient, and undisturbed by the long silence.

Then slowly, something shifted.

The figure paused.

Its restless movement came to stillness for a moment, and within that stillness the veil began to loosen. No hand tore it away. Nothing violent or dramatic occurred. It simply softened its hold, as though it no longer needed to remain in place. A small opening appeared—just enough for light to enter.

And where the light touched, the heart beneath it began to awaken again.

Not suddenly. Not completely. But truly.

It was as though something deep within remembered how to turn toward life once more.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons when we feel strangely distant from ourselves. Outwardly we may continue speaking, working, responding, and moving through daily life, yet inwardly something feels muted. Our words arrive faster than our awareness. Reactions become sharper or more mechanical. We sense a quiet disconnection between the life we are living and the deeper truth of who we are.

Often this veil forms gradually.

Exhaustion, fear, disappointment, distraction, or self-protection can slowly turn our attention inward without light. The soul becomes preoccupied with survival, control, or numbness, and over time we lose contact with the deeper tenderness beneath our defenses. Yet this condition is not something to fear or condemn. It is something to notice gently.

Because the veil is not permanent.

The turning back often begins very simply—with pause. A moment of silence interrupts the momentum of unconscious living. We breathe more deeply. We stop reacting long enough to feel what is actually present within us. And in that stillness, the soul begins opening again to what has patiently remained nearby all along.

Transformation rarely begins through force.

More often, it begins through willingness. A small opening. A softened word. A quiet act of honesty. The light does not demand perfection before it enters. It only asks for enough openness to touch what has grown distant within us.

And slowly, through repeated moments of awareness, the heart remembers how to face the light again.

You are not far from it.

You are already near the turning.
