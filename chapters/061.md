## 61 - The Unveiling of the Heart

> *What loses its way can be seen again*  
> *Light waits beneath every veil*

I saw a figure  
standing in a dim and quiet place.

At first glance,  
it seemed whole—  
familiar,  
recognizable.

But something in it  
felt turned inward—  
as though it no longer faced  
the light.

A veil rested gently  
over its head.

Not harsh.  
Not heavy.

Yet it softened everything—  
blurring what was clear,  
muting what once shone.

Its hands moved,  
its voice formed words—  
but the movement felt distant,  
as if disconnected  
from the life beneath it.

Around the figure,  
the air was still.

Nothing flowed.

Nothing opened.

And yet—  
far off,  
a quiet brightness remained.

It did not approach.

It waited.

Unchanging.

Untroubled.

Then, slowly,  
the figure paused.

Its movement stilled.

And in that stillness,  
the veil began to lift—  
not pulled away,  
but loosening,  
as if it no longer needed  
to remain.

A small opening appeared.

Just enough  
for light to enter.

And where the light touched,  
something softened—  
not all at once,  
but truly.

As though the heart beneath  
remembered  
how to turn again.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes you feel slightly removed—

from yourself,  
from others,  
from what feels true.

Not completely lost—  
but not fully present.

You may notice it  
in your words—  
when they come more quickly  
than your heart.

Or in your reactions—  
when they feel sharp  
or distant.

This is not something  
to fear.

It is something  
to notice.

A gentle veil  
can form  
when attention turns inward  
without light.

But you may also sense  
that it is not fixed.

There is a simple way  
back into clarity.

Pause.

Allow stillness  
to interrupt the movement.

Let your words soften.  
Let your attention return.

You do not need  
to force change.

Only to open  
even slightly.

And in that opening,  
light begins to move again—  
quietly restoring  
what felt distant.

You are not far.

You are already  
near the turning.
