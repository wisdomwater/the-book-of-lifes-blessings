## 61 - The Softening Within

> *A heart that yields becomes a place of rest*  
> *What opens to love is gently restored*

I saw a wide, open field—  
quiet,  
untouched by strain.

The earth was firm,  
yet soft enough  
to receive the falling rain.

There were no sharp edges here,  
no places that resisted the touch of light.

Only ground  
that had been turned over,  
again and again,  
until it could breathe.

A gentle rain began to fall—  
not heavy,  
not urgent—

just enough  
to soak slowly  
into the waiting soil.

Where it touched,  
the ground darkened,  
softened,  
became ready.

And from beneath the surface,  
something unseen  
began to stir.

Small green shoots  
pressed upward—

not forced,  
not rushed—

simply responding  
to what had been given.

I noticed how the earth  
did not hold itself closed.

It yielded.

It allowed.

And in that allowing,  
life emerged.

The light rested over it all—  
steady,  
untroubled—

revealing nothing harsh,  
only the quiet beauty  
of what grows  
when nothing resists.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes your heart feels guarded—

when something in you  
pulls inward,  
choosing distance  
over connection.

It can feel like strength  
to remain unmoved,  
to hold steady  
against what might unsettle you.

But you may also notice  
a certain dryness there—  
a sense of holding  
rather than receiving.

There are other moments, though,  
when something softens.

Perhaps without your effort.

A word reaches you.  
A memory opens.  
A quiet compassion rises  
where there was once resistance.

You may not even know  
where it came from.

Only that something in you  
is no longer closed.

This softening  
does not make you weaker.

It makes you alive.

You do not have to force it.

You can simply notice  
what happens  
when you allow yourself  
to be touched—  
by another,  
by grace,  
by the quiet presence  
that never turns away.

And in that space,  
something begins again.
