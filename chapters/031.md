## 31 - The Quiet Center Within

> *You do not need to chase what is good. It can meet you in stillness.*

I saw an open field washed in gentle light, stretching outward with a quiet simplicity that felt both spacious and alive. Nothing crowded the landscape. Nothing demanded attention. The air itself seemed softened by peace, and across the wide openness moved a small creature full of eager energy and delight.

It darted through the field with quick and restless movements—turning, leaping, circling toward every shimmer that caught its eye. There was joy in its motion, a bright readiness to engage whatever appeared before it. Each new glimmer seemed to promise something worth pursuing, and so it moved constantly from one attraction to the next, never lingering long enough for stillness to settle within it.

At first its movement appeared beautiful in its vitality. Yet as I watched more closely, another feeling slowly emerged beneath the liveliness. The motion scattered outward endlessly, like leaves carried by shifting winds. Though the creature remained active, it never quite arrived anywhere. Each movement gave way to another. Each reaching opened into more reaching.

Then, near the center of the field, I became aware of something else.

There was a still place resting quietly within the openness—not marked by any boundary, not separated from the field itself, yet unmistakable once seen. It carried no force and made no demand. It simply remained there like a hidden well beneath the earth or the calm center beneath moving water.

And slowly, the movement around it began to soften.

Not because anything restrained the creature or commanded it to stop, but because the stillness itself carried a kind of gravity. A gentle invitation. The field remained open. The light did not fade. Yet now there existed within it a place where all the scattered motion could finally rest.

### Living the Vision

Much of human life unfolds through movement of attention. The mind shifts quickly from thought to thought, desire to desire, fear to fear. We pursue what glimmers before us—approval, distraction, achievement, comfort, certainty—often believing that peace waits just beyond the next thing we reach for. And for a time, this movement can feel energizing. It gives the impression of engagement, momentum, even purpose.

Yet beneath constant motion there is often a quieter exhaustion.

Many people live with the subtle ache of never fully arriving within their own lives. Attention becomes scattered across endless concerns and impulses. Even moments of rest are interrupted by inward restlessness. The soul longs for stillness, yet does not always know how to trust it.

But within every person there is a deeper center that has not been lost.

It is not created through force or rigid control. It is already present beneath the surface movements of the mind and nervous system. Sometimes we notice it only briefly—in a slower breath, a silent moment beneath trees, a pause between thoughts when awareness widens and the heart softens. These moments may seem small, yet they reveal something enduring.

The spiritual life is often less about acquiring more and more about returning gently to this quiet center again and again. Not withdrawing from life, but learning to remain rooted while life continues moving around us. Over time, this stillness begins to hold us differently. We no longer need to chase every passing impulse or fear. Something steadier begins to guide us from within.

And in that deeper rhythm, the soul slowly remembers how to rest.
