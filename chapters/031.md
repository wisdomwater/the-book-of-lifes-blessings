## 31 — The Quiet Center Within

> *You do not need to chase what is good*  
> *It can meet you in stillness*

I saw an open field  
washed in gentle light—

wide,  
uncluttered,  
quietly alive.

Across it moved  
a small, eager creature—

quick in its steps,  
bright in its energy.

It turned and leapt,  
circled and paused,  
then darted again  
toward whatever shimmered  
at the edge of its sight.

There was a kind of joy in it—  
a liveliness,  
a readiness to engage  
with all that appeared.

And yet,  
it did not remain  
anywhere for long.

Its movements scattered  
like leaves in a breeze,  
never quite settling,  
never quite arriving.

Then,  
near the center of the field,  
I noticed something else—

a still place.

Not marked,  
not set apart,  
yet unmistakable.

A quiet presence  
resting within everything.

And slowly,  
the movement around it  
began to soften.

Not by force,  
not by command—

but as if  
the stillness itself  
was gently drawing  
all things inward.

The field remained open.  
The light did not fade.

But now there was  
a place to rest  
within it.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes your attention moves quickly—

from one thought  
to another,  
from one desire  
to the next.

You may feel the pull  
to respond,  
to follow,  
to keep moving  
with whatever arises.

And for a time,  
it can feel like life itself—  
active,  
engaged,  
full.

But beneath that motion,  
you may notice  
a quiet fatigue—

a sense of never  
quite settling,  
never quite arriving  
where you are.

There is something in you  
that longs for  
a different rhythm.

Not withdrawal,  
not stillness forced—

but a gentle center  
that does not shift  
with every passing impulse.

You may begin to sense it  
in small pauses—

a breath  
that deepens,  
a moment  
that widens.

It is already here.

You do not have to  
create it.

You can return to it—  
again and again—  
until it begins  
to hold you.
