## Day 31 – True and Righteous

> *The judgments of God do not bend to human desire—they reveal what is real.*

I heard the echo of a voice carried through the vision:

“O Lord God Almighty, your judgments are true and righteous.”

And I understood the weight of these words.

He is called Lord—  
not to inspire terror, but to awaken reverence.

He is called God—  
that love may rise toward Him.

He is called Almighty—  
because nothing escapes His knowing.

In Him, fear is not chaos but clarity.  
Love is not indulgence but truth.  
Power is not domination but order.

But I saw how humanity resists this.

When a person bends the law to serve themselves—  
reshaping what is right to fit their own desire—  
they step away from truth.

And when love is given to what is fleeting—  
to the flesh, to what cannot endure—  
it turns bitter.

It cannot satisfy.

And when the heart bows to idols—  
to anything that takes the place of God—  
it becomes entangled in deception.

Then the world begins to tremble.

Lightning flashes.  
Thunder breaks.  
The land grows barren.  
Sickness spreads.  
War rises.

These are not empty acts.

They are signs—  
reminders that life cannot stand apart from its source.

That nothing can endure without God.

And through all this, one truth remains:

His judgments are true.  
His judgments are righteous.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision confronts the human tendency to redefine truth according to personal desire.

To call God “Lord, God, Almighty” is to acknowledge different dimensions of divine reality: authority, love, and complete knowledge. Together, they form a foundation that cannot be altered by human preference.

The distortion begins when people interpret truth in ways that serve themselves. This creates a disconnection from reality. Similarly, misplaced love—when directed toward what is temporary or self-centered—leads to dissatisfaction and pain.

The imagery of storms, famine, and war reflects the consequences of this disconnection. These are not simply punishments, but manifestations of imbalance. When humanity moves away from truth, the effects ripple outward.

At its core, this vision calls us back to alignment—to recognize that lasting peace and life are found not in reshaping truth, but in returning to it.

#### Living This Vision

It is easy to justify your own path.

To soften what is difficult.  
To redefine what is uncomfortable.

But over time, this creates tension within you.

A quiet unease.

What would it look like to gently return to what is true?

Not harshly.  
Not with self-condemnation.

But honestly.

Where have you been bending things to fit your own comfort?

Where have you placed your love in something that cannot sustain it?

These are not questions meant to shame you.

They are invitations.

Because truth is not your enemy.

It is what frees you.

Even now, you can begin again.
