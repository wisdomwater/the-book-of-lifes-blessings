## Day 187 - The Theft of Trust

> *Greed steals more than possessions. It steals trust, peace, and the soul’s ability to rest in God.*

After this, I saw other evil spirits in the crowd.

They cried out:

“Lucifer will be enriched with many honors, and we will be glorified with him.”

These spirits taught greed. They urged human beings to crave more and more, always reaching for what belonged to others.

Then I saw a mass of hot air, burning intensely.

It seethed with small and terrible worms, carried here and there by the wind. Within it were the souls of those who had become slaves of greed and had always sought to seize the goods of others.

They suffered in the burning air because their desire had been endless. They were bitten by worms because their greed had caused much suffering.

Then I saw a deep well whose bottom I could not see.

A flame rose from it, then fell back into the pit, again and again. Souls rose with the flame and fell with it, crying:

“Why did we sin this way?”

These were those who had stolen through greed.

Then I saw a vast moat filled with horrible worms. Above it burned a terrible flame, and evil spirits ran back and forth, driving souls into torment.

These were those who had stolen secretly, disturbing peace by night and practicing deceit without regard for God.

And I heard the living light say:

“Those who wish to escape this vice must repent, discipline themselves, and repay the poor and those they have defrauded, as much as they are able, in truth and mercy.”

For God looks down from heaven upon the children of men, seeking whether anyone is wise and seeks Him.

He weighs the works of every soul.

The thief imitates the devil.

As the devil steals justice from the heart, so the thief steals what sustains another’s life.

But God tears away injustice and destroys what He did not plant.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision deepens the meaning of greed.

Greed is not only desire. It becomes theft when it takes what belongs to another—property, peace, dignity, livelihood, or trust.

The punishments mirror the sin. Burning air shows restless craving. The deep well shows the rise and fall of stolen gain. The moat and night imagery reveal hidden deceit.

But the remedy is concrete: repentance must become repair.

It is not enough to feel sorry. The soul must restore what it can. Mercy must take form in action.

God’s justice exposes theft, but His mercy opens the way back.

#### Living This Vision

Greed can make us blind to the cost others pay.

We may take time, attention, credit, resources, or emotional energy without noticing. We may justify ourselves because our need feels urgent.

This vision asks:

Where have I taken more than was mine to take?

Where can I restore peace?

Repentance becomes real when it repairs what it has harmed.

Return what you can. Apologize where needed. Practice enoughness. Let trust become wealth again.
