## 26 - The Courage to Stand

> *Peace is not the absence of truth. Love gives the heart a quiet strength.*

Beneath the roots of a great tree there rested a small hollow in the earth, soft with shadow and stillness. It felt like a place shaped for retreat—a hidden place where something weary could curl inward and remain unseen. Within it lay a small form drawn tightly into itself, holding close as though the world beyond asked for more than it could yet give. Above, the branches moved gently in the wind, and the leaves whispered softly to one another while fragments of light filtered through the canopy overhead.

The light touched the ground in scattered patterns, warm in some places and distant in others, yet it did not fully reach the hollow below. There was no danger nearby. No storm gathering in the sky. Only a quiet separation between what remained hidden and what waited patiently in the open air. The earth itself seemed to breathe in silence, holding both shelter and invitation at once.

Then slowly, almost imperceptibly, the light began to change. It did not grow harsher or brighter; it simply drew nearer. It rested at the edge of the hollow without urgency or demand, asking nothing, forcing nothing. Its presence carried the gentleness of something willing to wait as long as needed.

And in that patient nearness, something within the curled form began to stir. Not from fear, but from recognition. Somewhere deep within came the faint memory of standing upright beneath open skies. A memory of wholeness. A remembrance of life beyond hiding.

The body did not rush to rise. It loosened only slightly at first—just enough to feel warmth upon its skin, just enough to sense that the space beyond the hollow was not hostile after all. And within that small opening, strength quietly returned. Not the strength of force or defiance, but the strength of presence. A steady readiness to unfold again into the light.

### Living the Vision

Many people learn early that remaining quiet feels safer than being fully seen. There are moments when speaking honestly may threaten comfort, approval, or belonging, and so the heart instinctively retreats inward. The body tightens. The voice grows smaller. A person begins shaping themselves around caution, hoping that hiding will preserve peace.

And for a time, it often does.

Yet there is a kind of hiddenness that slowly diminishes the soul. Beneath outward calm, something deeper waits quietly beneath the surface—longing not for conflict, but for freedom. The spirit was not made only for protection. It was also made for truth, for openness, for the gentle courage of existing without constantly shrinking itself.

The deeper peace of the soul does not come from avoiding all tension or discomfort. It begins to emerge when a person allows themselves to remain present within what feels true. Sometimes this happens in very small ways: an honest word spoken with tenderness, a boundary held without anger, a refusal to abandon what the heart knows matters. These moments rarely appear dramatic from the outside, yet inwardly they reshape a life.

Courage is not the absence of fear. Often it is simply the willingness to stay awake and open while fear is still present. Love slowly strengthens the soul in this way. Not by hardening it, but by rooting it more deeply in truth. And over time, something steadier than fear begins to grow—a quiet inner stability that no longer depends entirely upon hiding.
