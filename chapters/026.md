## Day 26 - The Threefold Way of Justice

> *God’s justice moves through many paths—burning, refining, and illuminating the soul.*

I heard the trumpet sound again—  
and from it came three winds.

Each moved with purpose,  
yet each was different.

One bore a fiery cloud—  
bright and consuming.

Another carried a storm—  
full of struggle and unrest.

The third shone with gentle radiance—  
clear and pure.

And I understood:

These were the ways of justice.

The first belonged to the angels—  
burning with love,  
desiring only what God desires.

The second was the path of humanity—  
marked by effort,  
trial,  
and hardship.

The third was a mystery—  
the shining purity  
of the Incarnation.

A path of perfect holiness,  
where divinity and humanity meet.

All three were held together  
by the will of God.

I saw how God sustains the angels in love,  
and strengthens humanity in its struggle—  
so that it does not fall completely.

He drives back darkness  
with the light of holiness.

He reveals the way  
through what is pure and true.

Though humanity labors under the weight of flesh,  
a day will come  
when this burden is lifted.

Then the body itself  
will be subject to the spirit.

And all will rejoice—  
in harmony,  
in praise,  
in fullness.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision presents justice not as punishment, but as order expressed through different modes of being. The three paths—angelic, human, and incarnational—show how divine justice adapts to different realities.

The angels embody pure alignment with God’s will. Humanity, by contrast, lives within tension and struggle. Yet this struggle is not without purpose—it is the place where transformation occurs.

The Incarnation represents the union of divine and human, offering a model of perfect justice grounded in holiness.

God’s role is both sustaining and corrective. He preserves humanity even in its weakness, ensuring that it does not fall into total ruin.

The final restoration points toward a future where division within the human person is healed.


#### Living This Vision

Your life may feel like the second path—  
the storm. There are moments of clarity, but also confusion, effort, and resistance. This is not a sign that something is wrong. It is the path you are on.

What matters is not escaping the struggle,  
but remaining within it with intention. Ask yourself:  

*How am I responding to the tension in my life?*

You are not alone in it. There is a strength sustaining you, even when you feel weak. And there is a pattern before you—a life that shows what alignment looks like. You are moving toward that. Slowly, imperfectly, but truly.

And one day, what feels divided within you will be made whole.
