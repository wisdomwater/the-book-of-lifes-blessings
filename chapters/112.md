## Day 112 - The Viper and the Rain

> *I saw Envy burning in darkness and speaking like a destroyer, but Charity answered like living air, like rain, like tears that make the earth bloom.*

Then I saw a second image, monstrous in form.

Its head, shoulders, and arms were like those of a man,  
except that its hands were like a bear’s.  
Its chest, belly, and back were swollen beyond human measure.  
From there downward it was like a man again,  
except that its feet were wooden.

Its head was fire,  
and flames came out from its mouth.

It wore no clothing.  
It was plunged entirely into darkness.  
Its right shoulder leaned down into the gloom.

And it spoke:

“I am a shepherd, guarding so that the proper measure is not exceeded.  
I reject human strength whenever I wish,  
and I cut off capable tongues.

Even if they were as many as the sand of the sea  
and prudent as serpents,  
I would grind them with my tooth  
and they could not resist me.

They call me Gehenna.  
I drag many to myself,  
and I corrupt all that God accomplishes.

If I cannot have what shines,  
I do not care for anything.

In darkness I prepare my tongue like a dart,  
and I wound the upright of heart.

My strength is like the north.  
And all that belongs to me  
I will give to hatred,  
for hatred is born from me  
and is beneath me.”

Then from the storm-cloud I heard Charity answer:

“O dreadful pettiness,  
you are like a viper that destroys itself.

You cannot endure anything founded in honor and steadiness.  
You are like an idol set against God,  
leading people toward ruin through unbelief.

You call yourself Gehenna because you oppose every just form of measure,  
and because you long to destroy what flows from wisdom.  
You can belong neither among what truly shines  
nor among anything of worth.

But I am the air  
that nourishes every living force.

I grow the flowers.  
I ripen the fruits.

I am instructed by every breath of the Spirit of God.  
So I make clear streams flow,  
like tears that come from holy feeling.  
And with those tears I pour out a gentle fragrance for the most blessed works.

I am also the rain that exhales dew,  
through which the herbs smile in the brightness of life.

You, full of malice and poisoned filth,  
bite at all that grows,  
but you cannot destroy it.  
The more fiercely you rage,  
the more it grows.

Day and night I practice fairness and good works.  
By day I am given to noble deeds,  
and by night to the soothing of pain.

I am the faithful friend beside the throne of God.  
Nothing that belongs to God is hidden from me.

And when the Son of God heals the wounds of men,  
I am the soft linen that binds them.”

#### What This Vision Means

This image is Envy, though it first disguises itself as a guardian of measure. That is one of its deceptions. Envy often pretends to care about justice, balance, or proportion, while secretly grieving the good of another and longing to diminish it.

Its swollen torso and fiery head suggest excess inward burning. Its bear-like hands and wooden feet reveal a vice both violent and deadened—capable of grasping, incapable of living tenderness. Its words expose its essence: if it cannot possess what shines, it wants to spoil it.

Charity answers not with accusation alone, but with abundance. She is air, rain, tears, fragrance, blossom, ripening fruit. She belongs to growth, healing, and life shared freely. Envy bites. Charity nourishes. Envy wounds the upright. Charity binds their wounds with softness.

The vision makes something clear: envy cannot create. It can only resent, distort, and attack. Charity, by contrast, participates in God’s own life-giving movement.

#### Living This Vision

Envy often hides beneath comparison.

Why do they have that and not me?  
Why is their goodness seen?  
Why does their life seem to bloom while mine feels delayed?

If we are honest, almost everyone knows this sting.

Envy is painful because it cannot rejoice.  
It turns another person’s light into a personal injury.

But Charity opens a freer way.  
She does not deny the ache.  
She transforms it.

When you feel envy rising, pause before feeding it.  
Bless the good you see, even if your heart resists.  
Thank God that beauty exists, even where it is not yours.

This does not erase longing.  
But it prevents longing from becoming poison.

Then ask:  
How might I become a life-giving presence today?  
How might I water rather than wound?

Charity is not merely being nice.  
It is the soul becoming spacious enough  
to delight in goodness wherever it appears.
