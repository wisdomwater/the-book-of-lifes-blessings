## Day 154 - The Hidden Feet

> *Worldly desire runs everywhere, but cannot see where it is going. It gathers much and becomes poor in the soul.*

Then I saw the seventh image: Worldly Desire.

It came after stubbornness, because when the heart becomes hard and does not seek God, it soon begins to seek the world with restless hunger.

This desire does not lift its eyes toward God. It turns here and there. It runs everywhere like a wolf searching for something to devour. It wounds those around it with anxious grasping and hurries to seize whatever it can.

This figure appeared like a woman down to the legs, but its legs and feet were sunk so deeply in darkness that I could not see them.

And I understood: worldly desire clothes itself in softness. It speaks gently. It says, “I am only being careful. I must gather what I can. I may need this later.”

But beneath this gentle speech is bondage.

The feet are hidden because this desire cannot see its own limits. It does not know where it stands. It does not know where it is going. It sinks into unbelief until even its own path disappears.

Its head was covered like a woman’s, for those ruled by this vice hide their thoughts and intentions. They do not let others know what is in their hearts.

It wore a white garment, as if all its choices were pure and useful. It called its hoarding necessity. It called its grasping wisdom.

But Contempt of the World answered this vice.

It did not despise creation, but it taught the soul to loosen its grip on what passes away.

It urged humanity to flee what cannot last and to desire, with faith, what is eternal.


#### What This Vision Means

Worldly desire is not simply wanting earthly things. It is the restless hunger that makes temporary things ultimate.

This vice is subtle because it often disguises itself as prudence. It says, “I am only preparing. I am only protecting myself.” But over time, preparation becomes possession, and possession becomes fear.

The hidden feet are important. Desire loses its boundaries. It no longer knows when enough is enough.

The white garment shows self-deception. We can dress our cravings in noble language and call them responsibility, wisdom, or necessity.

The virtue that answers is Contempt of the World—not hatred of the world, but freedom from its tyranny.

To despise the world rightly is not to reject beauty, work, or provision. It is to refuse to be ruled by what cannot save us.


#### Living This Vision

Ask gently:

What am I afraid to release?

Sometimes we hold tightly because we do not trust that we will be cared for. We gather, guard, and plan—not from peace, but from fear.

This vision invites you to notice where desire has become anxious.

You do not need to renounce everything.

Begin with one loosening.

Give something away.
Tell the truth about a craving.
Practice enoughness.
Let one possession, plan, or outcome stop being your master.

The soul becomes lighter when it stops trying to own the world.

What is eternal cannot be hoarded.

It can only be received.
