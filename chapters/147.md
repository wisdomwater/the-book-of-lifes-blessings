## Day 147 - The Sun’s Pavilion

> *The Light entered flesh, and the darkness could not keep its hold. The divine fire came near enough to warm what had grown cold.*

The psalm speaks of the sun with a hidden wonder:

God has placed His dwelling in the sun. Like a bridegroom leaving his chamber, like a strong runner beginning his course, he moves from one end of heaven to the other, and nothing escapes his heat.

And I understood this mystery.

The Son of God, radiant in divinity, clothed Himself in the flesh of the Virgin. Her body became a holy tabernacle for the healing of the human race.

God, like the burning sun, illumined what had become dark. With divine heat, He kindled the flesh of the Virgin, and from her came the Man of burning charity and radiant faith.

He came with a love brighter than what humanity had known before the fall.

God had made man strong, and woman tender in weakness. Through weakness came the fall. Yet through holy weakness also came restoration.

Divinity is strong. The flesh of the Son of God is weak. And through that flesh, the world receives again the life it had lost.

The Son came forth from the Virgin’s womb like a bridegroom from his chamber. He rejoiced in His humanity, not with fear or hesitation, but with delight.

Like a giant running his course, He moved swiftly through the world, teaching the path of truth.

He came from the heights of the Father.
He descended to earth.
He worked salvation in His body.
And when His work was fulfilled, He returned to the Father in glory.

No one escapes the heat of His divinity.

For He is the Word through whom all things were made. He entered flesh to free flesh. And He will judge all things—small and great, last and first—because all things began in Him.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision holds together strength and tenderness.

The Son of God is radiant like the sun, yet He enters the world through the fragile flesh of the Virgin. Divine power does not avoid weakness. It chooses it as the place of redemption.

Hildegard’s imagery turns the Incarnation into fire, warmth, movement, and light. Christ is the bridegroom, the runner, the sun, the Word, and the flame that reaches everything.

Nothing escapes His heat—not because He is harsh, but because divine life touches all creation.

The weakness of flesh becomes the doorway of restoration. What seemed breakable becomes holy. What seemed low becomes the place where God enters.

The vision invites us to see salvation not as escape from the body, but as the healing of embodied life.

God comes near enough to be held, born, wounded, and raised.


#### Living This Vision

We often despise our weakness.

We want to be strong, clear, certain, and unshaken. But this vision reminds us that God does not wait for perfect strength before entering.

He comes through tenderness.
Through need.
Through the places we would rather hide.

Your weakness is not proof that God is absent.

It may be the very place where divine warmth is beginning to work.

Let the light come close.
Let it warm what has gone cold.
Let it touch the parts of you that feel fragile or unfinished.

The sun does not ask the earth to shine first.

It simply rises.
