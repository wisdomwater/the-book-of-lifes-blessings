## Day 195 - The Outer Darkness

> *There are depths the embodied soul cannot fully comprehend. Yet even here the vision teaches us to seek the grace of God while life is still before us.*

Then I saw that from the darkness there spread a mist across an immense expanse, filled with many torments.

Yet in one part of that darkness I saw no fire and no severe torture.

There were souls who, apart from other sins, still bore the wound of Adam’s fall and lacked the sign of baptism.

Some endured smoke in the darkness.

Others did not.

Those who had committed lesser sins suffered the smoke. Those who had committed neither lesser nor greater sins, but had not received the sign of faith, endured only the darkness of unbelief.

Then I saw another darkness.

It was black, horrible, and endless.

It burned in a darkness without flame and stretched near the first darkness.

There was Gehenna.

It contained every kind of torment, misery, stench, and suffering.

But I could not see what was within it. I saw only the outside darkness, not its interior. I heard extraordinary groans, indistinct and terrible. I heard grinding and mourning, countless noises of pain, like the roar of the sea and the sound of many waters.

For Gehenna is the dominion of evil spirits, who pour vices into those who consent to them.

Its sufferings are beyond human understanding. A soul still burdened with the body cannot look upon them or comprehend them.

And I saw and understood these things by the living Spirit.

Then the voice of living light spoke:

“These pains are true, and they are as you see them, and more.

The first darkness is the place where, before the triumph of the Son of God, some souls of the righteous were held.

Now there remain souls not burdened by grave sin, yet not marked by the sacred fountain.

Some suffer smoke because they were touched by the pollutions of the world. Others endure only the darkness of ignorance.”

But the other darkness is Gehenna.

It began with the ruin of the fallen angels.

It receives Satan and holds all misery without comfort and without hope.

There remain those who do not seek the grace of God, who refuse to look toward God, and who do not desire life.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision is solemn and difficult.

Hildegard preserves a distinction between darkness and Gehenna. One darkness is marked by deprivation and ignorance. The other is the terrible realm of final opposition to God.

The imagery is intentionally overwhelming. Some realities exceed human sight. The embodied soul cannot fully comprehend them.

At its heart, this vision is not meant to feed curiosity about punishment. It is meant to awaken urgency.

Grace is not something to delay.

Life is not something to refuse.

To seek God is to turn toward light, baptismal cleansing, mercy, and life.

#### Living This Vision

Modern readers may struggle with visions of judgment and darkness.

But even here, we can receive the spiritual warning: choices matter. Consent matters. What we give ourselves to shapes the soul.

The invitation is not fear for fear’s sake.

It is to desire life.

To seek grace now.

To turn toward God while the heart can still turn.

Wherever there is even the smallest desire for mercy, bring it into the light.
