## 46 - The Opening to Light

> *Even in sorrow, a door remains*  
> *The heart remembers light when it reaches*

I saw a quiet valley  
resting beneath a soft, dim sky.

The air was still—  
not heavy,  
but hushed,  
as if holding a long breath.

At its center,  
a single figure sat low to the ground,  
wrapped in the silence.

Nothing moved around it.

No wind.  
No sound.

Only a waiting  
that felt almost forgotten.

Then, at the edge of the horizon,  
a faint light began to appear—

not bright,  
not sudden—

just enough  
to be noticed.

It did not rush forward.

It remained—  
steady,  
patient—  
as if offering itself  
without demand.

For a time,  
nothing changed.

Then slowly,  
the figure lifted its gaze—  
just slightly.

Not fully.  
Not all at once.

But enough  
to see.

And in that small turning,  
the light grew clearer—  
not stronger,  
but nearer.

The valley did not disappear.

The stillness remained.

But something within it  
began to soften—  
as though the space itself  
remembered  
how to receive.

And the light continued to wait—  
not distant,  
but present—  
ready to meet  
even the smallest opening.

#### Living the Vision

There are times when  
sorrow settles deeply—

when it feels  
like there is nowhere to turn,  
nothing to reach for.

In those moments,  
you may feel the pull  
to remain closed—  
to stay within what is known,  
even if it is heavy.

But you may also notice  
a quiet invitation—  
something small,  
almost unnoticed.

A thought.  
A breath.  
A faint sense  
that there could be more  
than what you feel right now.

You do not need  
to force yourself  
into joy.

Only to remain  
slightly open.

You may begin  
with something simple—  
a gentle reaching,  
a quiet asking,  
a willingness to look up  
even a little.

And in that small movement,  
something begins to shift—  
not all at once,  
but enough  
to let light in.

You are not asked  
to leave your sorrow behind.

Only to let it be touched  
by something greater.

And over time,  
that touch  
becomes a path.
