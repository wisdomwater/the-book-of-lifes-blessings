## 28 - The Stillness Beyond the Turning

> *What spins within you can grow quiet. A deeper breath is always near.*

A great wheel turned slowly in the open air. Its motion was not frantic, yet it never seemed to rest. Round and round it moved with steady rhythm, as though it had been turning for far longer than memory could trace. At the center clung a small form—hands gripping tightly, feet braced against the movement, held within a momentum it no longer knew how to leave.

With each rotation came a familiar surge. Heat rose through the turning like sparks searching for dry ground. The movement fed upon itself; one revolution gave strength to the next. Nothing outside the wheel forced it onward. It continued because something within it had not yet learned stillness.

Again and again the turning carried its own energy forward. Old reactions awakened old fears. Memory called to memory. Tension tightened around itself until motion felt inevitable. The form at the center held on desperately, believing perhaps that survival depended upon never letting go.

Then, almost beyond notice, a breeze moved quietly through the air.

It did not arrive with force. It did not strike against the wheel or demand its surrender. Gentle and patient, it simply surrounded the turning. The breeze touched the outer edges of the motion with a steadiness that asked nothing and resisted nothing. It remained near without urgency, like a presence willing to wait beside exhaustion itself.

And slowly, where the breeze lingered, something within the form began to soften.

The grip loosened slightly. Breath, once shallow and strained, deepened almost on its own. The heat that had once tightened inward began opening into something more spacious and alive. The wheel did not stop immediately, but its hold no longer felt absolute. Between one turn and the next, a small stillness appeared—quiet, spacious, almost hidden beneath the movement.

And in that stillness came the realization that peace had never been entirely absent. It had been waiting patiently beneath the turning all along.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when something inside us begins to spin before we fully realize what is happening. A memory surfaces. A painful conversation replays itself. A fear awakens quietly in the body, and suddenly the mind begins circling familiar patterns once again. Thoughts tighten around themselves. Emotions gather momentum. Reactions feel inevitable.

Often these inner cycles seem automatic, as though once they begin they must run to completion. Many people carry invisible wheels within them—old anxieties, recurring shame, anger, longing, self-protection. The nervous system learns these movements deeply, and over time the soul may begin to mistake constant turning for normal life.

Yet hidden within our experience there is often a small space that goes unnoticed at first.

A pause between thoughts.

A single deeper breath.

A moment when awareness quietly returns before the next reaction fully takes hold.

This pause cannot usually be forced into existence through harsh effort. It appears more like an opening—something gently discovered rather than conquered. In that space, another presence becomes possible. A steadiness untouched by the spinning. A deeper self beneath the anxious momentum of the mind.

The invitation is not to fight the wheel with greater violence. It is to become gradually rooted in the stillness surrounding it. Through gentleness, breath, prayer, awareness, and compassionate attention, the soul slowly learns that it does not have to cling so tightly to every passing motion within itself.

And over time, what once felt endless begins to lose its power—not because life becomes perfectly calm, but because something deeper within you has remembered how to rest.
