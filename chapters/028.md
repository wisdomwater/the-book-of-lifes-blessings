## Day 28 – One Flame, One Will

> *The countless are made one, not by force, but by love that binds without dividing.*

I looked again into the fiery cloud, and what I saw could not be counted.

A multitude—glittering, alive, radiant beyond measure.

They were not scattered.  
They were not competing.  
They were not separate in spirit.

Though many, they moved as one.

Each burned with life, yet no flame overshadowed another.  
Each shone with glory, yet no light diminished its neighbor.

They were bound together—not by constraint, but by shared desire.

They wanted what God wanted.

And in that wanting, they became unified.

It was as if I were looking upon a single body, though it had many parts.  
Each distinct.  
Each alive.  
Yet all belonging to one life.

Their harmony was not silence—it was fullness.  
A concord so complete that difference did not fracture it.

I could not measure their number.  
It stretched beyond sight, beyond thought.

Only God knew them fully.

And they were content in that knowing.

Before them, I saw something like a great board—wide and filled with feathers that moved and shimmered.

The feathers were not still.

They stirred, lifted, and shifted according to the will of God, as if carried by invisible currents.

I understood that this board held the mysteries of divine judgment.

Not judgment as accusation—but as unveiling.

Written upon it were secrets—deep things that the blessed spirits beheld with careful attention.

They watched.  
They listened.

For there were mysteries still hidden, even from them, resting within the secret plan of God.

And when the time came, these mysteries would move.

The feathers would stir.

And what was hidden would be revealed.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the unity of the divine order—a unity not based on sameness, but on shared alignment with the will of God.

The countless spirits represent a state of being where individuality is not erased but harmonized. Each being remains distinct, yet all are united in purpose. This is the opposite of the fragmentation we often experience, where competing desires pull us in different directions.

To “want what God wants” is not about losing oneself. It is about becoming whole.

The image of the board filled with feathers introduces the mystery of divine judgment. This is not a crude system of reward and punishment, but a deeper unfolding of truth. The feathers, moving at God’s command, suggest that revelation happens in its proper time—not forced, not premature.

Even the blessed spirits do not see everything at once. There is a humility in this—an acknowledgment that some things remain hidden, held within the wisdom of God.

This vision invites us into trust: that there is an order beyond our understanding, and that unity comes not from control, but from surrender to a greater love.

#### Living This Vision

You may feel divided inside.

Part of you wants one thing.  
Another part pulls in the opposite direction.

This inner conflict can feel like failure.

But it may simply be a sign that your desires have not yet found their center.

What would it mean—not to suppress parts of yourself—but to gently bring them into alignment with something deeper?

Not by force.  
Not by shame.

But by asking:  
What is truly life-giving here?

What reflects love?

Unity begins there.

And as for the hidden things—the unanswered questions, the unresolved tensions—  
you do not have to force clarity.

Some things are meant to unfold slowly.

Like feathers moved by a wind you cannot see.

Your role is not to control the timing, but to remain attentive.

To watch.  
To listen.  
To trust.
