## Day 189 - The Fire That Searches

> *Worldly sorrow scatters the soul until it hides from life. Yet God’s searching fire comes not only to judge, but to awaken trust again.*

Again I heard the voice from the living light.

It said:

“The punishments you see are true. Those who are stained by sadness over life in the world, and who wish to defeat the spirits that urge it, must turn toward the life of the spirit.

Let them meditate earnestly on the Scriptures, for there heavenly joy is born.”

Then I understood the nature of this sadness.

It has no joy in heavenly things.

It is like a useless wind—not strong enough to give life, not dry enough to cleanse, but only able to scatter whatever it touches.

It has no straight stability.

It says, “I do not know what God is, or what God claims to be.”

So its vitality dries up, because it is not enlivened by the breath of the Spirit.

This sadness divides the soul into many fragments.

It piles sorrow upon sorrow.

It finds no joy.

It does not call a friend back with gladness.

It does not even make peace with an enemy.

When it has gathered enough grief, it hides like a serpent in the den of sorrow, afraid of everything that passes by.

This way is like death.

It does not long for heaven, and it does not trust the world.

Therefore the zeal of God rises against it, as the Scripture says:

“Fire has been kindled in my wrath, and it burns to the depths.”

For God examines all things with righteous judgment. His fire reaches whatever opposes Him. No creature can escape it or extinguish it.

When God shows the way of salvation and a person turns away in unbelief, His zeal searches that refusal.

And yet even this searching is meant to awaken the soul.

For the spirits of evil fight human beings with many vices, trying to wound them in every element and every work.

But against them stand the ranks of holy spirits.

They attend humanity. They restrain the forces of the earth. They bring the works of the saints before the throne of God, where nothing good is forgotten.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision continues the struggle with worldly sadness, showing it as a force that scatters and dries the soul.

It is not holy grief. It is not honest lament. It is despair turned into a dwelling place. It loses confidence in heaven and trust in life.

The image of the useless wind is especially vivid. This sorrow does not refresh or purify. It only disperses. It breaks the heart into fragments.

Yet God’s zeal is not absent. His fire searches what refuses life. His judgment exposes the sadness that has become unbelief.

At the same time, the holy spirits stand near. The soul is not abandoned in its struggle. Its good works, prayers, and efforts toward healing are carried before God.

#### Living This Vision

Sadness can become familiar.

We may begin to live inside it, like a serpent hidden in a den. We may fear joy because joy feels unsafe. We may distrust consolation because disappointment has taught us to brace for loss.

This vision invites one brave movement toward heavenly joy.

Read something sacred.

Speak one honest prayer.

Let one trusted person draw near.

The soul does not heal by pretending sadness is gone. It heals by allowing the Spirit to breathe life where sorrow has grown dry.
