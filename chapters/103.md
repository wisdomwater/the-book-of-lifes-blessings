## Day 103 – The Weight of Ungodliness

> *I saw a boiling fire, heavy and suffocating, where souls bore the weight of a life without mercy.*

Then I saw another terrible place.

A great fire burned, bubbling like molten metal.  
Within it flowed boiling lead mixed with sulfur,  
and through it crawled countless fiery worms.

In this place were the souls of those who had lived in ungodliness.  
They had chosen ruthlessness.  
They had turned away from compassion.

The fire scorched them for their wickedness.  
The boiling lead pressed upon them with unbearable weight—  
the heaviness of their own actions.

The sulfur burned them for the indignity  
with which they had treated others.

And the worms tormented them—  
for they had refused to recognize the humanity of those before them.  
Their fury had consumed them,  
and now it returned upon them.

Again, I heard the voice from the living light:  
“What you see is true.  
Those who live without mercy,  
if they wish to escape such suffering,  
must humble their bodies through discipline and restraint,  
according to wise counsel.  
For they have acted without compassion.”

I understood that ungodliness is not only disbelief—  
it is the absence of mercy.

It is the hardening of the heart  
against the suffering of others.

It is to see another human being  
and refuse to recognize them.

#### What This Vision Means

Ungodliness in this vision is not simply a rejection of God in thought, but in action. It is lived out through a lack of mercy—a failure to respond to others with dignity, compassion, and care.

The boiling lead represents the weight of such a life. Actions done without love accumulate. They press down on the soul, making it heavy and bound.

Sulfur signifies corruption—how contempt for others distorts the inner life. And the worms reveal how unchecked cruelty consumes from within.

To “not recognize others” is one of the deepest wounds a person can inflict. It is to strip away dignity, to treat another as less than human.

This vision reminds us that mercy is not optional in the spiritual life. It is central. To live without it is to disconnect from the very nature of divine love.

#### Living This Vision

There are moments when it is easy to harden.

When someone irritates us.  
When we feel wronged.  
When we see people as obstacles instead of souls.

Ungodliness begins in these small shifts.

A lack of patience.  
A refusal to care.  
A quiet indifference.

But mercy can also begin in small ways.

A pause before reacting.  
A choice to see the person behind the behavior.  
A willingness to soften judgment.

We do not need grand gestures.

Only a turning of the heart.

To look again.  
To recognize.  
To remember: this person, too, carries a story, a struggle, a longing.

Mercy restores what harshness erases.

And each act of compassion  
lightens the soul.
