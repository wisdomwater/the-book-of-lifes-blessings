## Day 113 - The Twig and the Flowers

> *I saw a figure dressed in black, holding green things with great care, and I heard how conceit dresses itself in beauty while quietly enthroning the self.*

Then I saw the third image.

It had the appearance of a man,  
but its hands were covered with hair,  
and its legs and feet were like those of a crane.

Upon its head it wore a cap woven of grass.  
Its garment was black.

In its right hand it held a green twig.  
In its left it carried flowers,  
which it studied with great attention.

And it said:

“I examine everything carefully.  
I am my own witness.  
I judge all things by my own understanding.

How could I deny the good I see?  
I know what is worthy,  
and I trust that by my own power  
I can move freely through towns and streets  
as birds move through the woods.

I want to learn their song  
and add it to the skill of man.  
I want to gather every charm,  
every grace,  
so that all who see me may rejoice,  
and all who hear me may honor me.

I know how to please birds,  
to handle beasts,  
to show myself wise among men.  
I join myself pleasantly to the joyful.

And when I do this,  
who is like me?

If I do not investigate,  
I will find nothing.  
If I do not ask,  
nothing will be given.

I have no prosperity  
unless I gain it by my own wisdom  
and my own integrity.

Why should it matter to God  
if I claim the glory that belongs to me?  
Was I not created this way?”

Then from the storm-cloud I heard the answer of holy fear:

“What can man do without the grace of God?  
Nothing.

When a person turns the wheel of knowledge toward vanity,  
God brings him low.  
When he turns toward the good,  
God helps him.

You want to grasp whatever occurs to you,  
but in the judgment of God  
your head is brought down  
and your feet are turned upward.

You do not seek the healing of God.  
You rush into vanity  
and do not desire what is truly alive.

But I, in the fear of God,  
consider what sin is  
so that I may flee it.

I sigh for the love of God.  
I fear His judgment.  
I rejoice in His rewards.

I do not examine every creature  
for the taste of sin,  
but seek only what nourishes life.

So God gives me food  
from the tree of life.  
He establishes the good man  
as a dwelling place for His tabernacle.”

#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows conceit as self-admiration refined into a way of life. It is not crude boasting alone. It is the inward habit of making oneself the measure of all things—one’s own witness, one’s own judge, one’s own source of glory.

The image is telling. It handles living things delicately: twig, flowers, song, beauty, skill. Conceit often surrounds itself with what is fine and admirable. It may even appear cultivated, intelligent, and graceful. But underneath is a black garment: the hidden darkness of self-exaltation.

The hairy hands and crane-like legs suggest distortion. The soul reaches and stands awkwardly when it tries to rise by its own brilliance. It wants appreciation, wonder, and honor, yet it does not know how to receive life as gift.

God’s Fear answers by restoring proportion. Holy fear is not terror. It is the truthful awareness that all wisdom, beauty, and capacity are sustained by grace. Without that reverence, knowledge turns vain. With it, the soul learns what truly nourishes.

#### Living This Vision

Conceit can wear very polished clothing.

It may sound like this:  
I know what I’m doing.  
I see more clearly than others.  
I deserve to be recognized.

Sometimes it hides beneath excellence.  
Sometimes beneath sensitivity.  
Sometimes beneath spiritual insight itself.

The question is not whether you have gifts.  
You do.

The question is whether those gifts lead you into gratitude  
or into self-occupation.

When you notice yourself wanting admiration,  
do not panic.  
Just become honest.

Offer the gift back to God.  
Let beauty remain beautiful  
without making it a mirror for your ego.

Holy fear is gentle medicine.  
It reminds us:  
I am not my own source.

The freer soul is not the one who shines the most,  
but the one who can shine  
without needing to possess the light.
