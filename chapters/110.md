## Day 110 - Trust Beyond the Veil

> *I saw the pit prepared for destruction, and I heard that faith does not demand possession of every secret, but a humble trust in the God whose light already shines through all things.*

After this I saw spirits of destruction  
mocking God as though He were nothing.  
They persuaded people not to trust Him,  
but to despise Him  
and so rush headlong toward ruin.

For such destruction I saw the pit of hell prepared with the devil.  
Those who refuse trust in God  
make themselves fit for that darkness.

Then the living light spoke:

“If human beings would turn away from the spirits  
that urge them toward destruction,  
let them trust in God.  
Let them fulfill His commandments in righteousness and holiness  
as far as they are able.  
Let them strive through watchfulness, fasting, and almsgiving  
to be separated from the devil who leads toward ruin.”

And I understood why distrust runs so deep.

Whoever has no faith in God  
does not understand the soul.  
Such a person does not know how to seek God  
through the wonders He has made among creatures.

God stands upon a summit higher than man can climb in this life.  
He cannot be seized by human sight.

Yet He shines toward us  
through the brightness of His works.

He gives to creatures what they need.  
And who could do this, if not God?

Then another understanding was given to me:

If a master were to hand all his secrets  
to a servant,  
the servant would begin to imagine  
that he should rule the household.

So it is with the soul.

If man insists on knowing all the mysteries of God,  
he forgets his place.  
He ceases to trust  
and begins to grasp.

But the purity of faith sees differently.

It does not say in despair,  
“I do not know who God is,  
and so I am lost.”

It does not turn God into an empty statue.

Rather, it trusts.  
It rejects the devil’s deception.  
It repents truly.  
And by that repentance  
the soul is made lighter for the journey beyond death.

The one who would be saved  
does not demand mastery over divine secrets.

He entrusts himself to the God  
who remains greater than all understanding  
and yet nearer than breath.

#### What This Vision Means

This final vision of the second part centers on trust. Destruction begins when the soul no longer believes that God is truly God—living, wise, active, and worthy of confidence.

The pit of destruction is not only future punishment. It also symbolizes what happens inwardly when contempt replaces trust. The soul loses orientation and begins to fall.

A particularly beautiful insight appears in the image of the servant and the master’s secrets. Human beings are not meant to possess God as an object of total knowledge. Faith is not control. It is reverent participation in a mystery greater than us.

This is not anti-understanding. Hildegard is not rejecting thought. She is rejecting the proud demand to know everything before one can trust. God’s hiddenness is not abandonment. His works already shine with His presence.

So the soul is invited into humility: to know what can be known, to honor what remains beyond us, and to let trust become a form of sight.

#### Living This Vision

There are times when faith feels hardest  
not because God seems cruel,  
but because He seems hidden.

We want answers.  
We want certainty.  
We want to know why.

And when those answers do not come,  
the temptation is either despair  
or contempt.

But this vision invites a holier path.

You do not need every secret  
in order to live in trust.

Look for the brightness of God’s works.  
Look for what is still given:  
breath, mercy, beauty, conscience, love, warning, wonder.

Faith is not blindness.  
It is a humble seeing.

It says: I do not understand everything,  
but I will not make mystery into meaninglessness.

In daily life this may mean  
choosing prayer without full clarity,  
obedience without full explanation,  
hope without immediate relief.

Trust grows when it is practiced.  
And each small act of reliance on God  
weakens the voice that says ruin is all that remains.
