## 74 - The Stillness of Peace

> *What does not strike back remains whole*  
> *Peace holds what anger cannot*

I saw a wide, open space  
after a great wind had passed.

The air was quiet now—  
no longer rushing,  
no longer filled with force.

At the center stood a single tree.

Its branches were steady,  
its leaves unmoved—  
not because nothing had touched it,  
but because it remained rooted  
beneath all movement.

Around it,  
the ground bore signs of disturbance—  
places where the soil had shifted,  
where something had been torn.

Yet the tree remained.

A soft fragrance moved through the air—  
almost unseen,  
yet unmistakable.

It did not push outward.

It simply filled the space  
with a gentle presence  
that changed everything it touched.

The ground began to settle.

The air softened.

And what had felt unsettled  
slowly came to rest.

The tree did not fight the wind.

It did not resist what had passed.

It remained grounded—  
drawing from something deeper  
than the storm itself.

And in that rooted stillness,  
peace spread quietly—  
restoring what had been shaken,  
without force,  
without demand.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you feel the impulse  
to respond immediately—

to return what was given,  
to correct what feels unjust,  
to protect yourself  
from being diminished.

It can feel right.

Necessary.

But you may notice  
how quickly it grows—  
how it begins to take hold  
within you.

There is another way  
that does not deny  
what has happened.

You may begin  
by not reacting  
in the first moment.

A breath.  
A pause.

And in that space,  
you remain rooted—  
not in the offense,  
but in something deeper.

You do not lose strength here.

You preserve it.

Peace is not weakness.

It is the quiet decision  
not to let harm  
shape your response.

And as you remain there,  
you may find  
that something steadier  
begins to guide you—

a way of responding  
that does not wound you  
in the process.
