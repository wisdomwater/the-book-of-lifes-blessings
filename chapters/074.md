## Day 74 — The Axe of Strife

> *The one who lives by offense wounds themselves again and again.*

I saw a man with a face burning like fire.

His garments were bright with many colors, yet torn at the shoulders.  
In his hand he held an axe.

Again and again, he struck himself with it.  
Blood soaked his clothing.

And still he spoke with fury:

“No one will burden me.  
No one will press me down.

If I am struck, I will strike back harder.  
If I am wronged, I will return it in greater measure.

I will not grow weary of this.”

His anger was endless.  
It fed on itself.

Then the voice from the cloud answered:

“You are a storm of violence.

You crush and wound.  
You spread injury wherever you go.

You do not seek peace.  
You do not love it.”

And another voice rose, gentle yet strong:

“I am Peace.

I am healing for wounds.  
I am balm for those you harm.

I do not delight in conflict.  
I do not feed on strife.

I move like fragrance—  
quiet, unseen, yet transforming all it touches.”

And I saw:

The one who clung to conflict was never at rest.  
But peace stood firm—  
like a pillar, untouched by the storm.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the cycle of retaliation.

The desire to “even the score” feels justified. It promises protection and control. But in reality, it deepens suffering.

The man’s self-inflicted wounds show the hidden truth:  
when we live in constant offense, we harm ourselves as much as others.

Conflict becomes identity.  
Peace becomes foreign.

Peace, in this vision, is not passive. It is active healing. It refuses to participate in the cycle of harm.

It does not mean allowing injustice without response.  
But it means not becoming shaped by the same violence we resist.

Peace restores what conflict destroys.

#### Living This Vision

Notice how you respond when you feel wronged.

Do you replay the offense?  
Do you imagine returning it?

This is where the cycle begins.

Pause.

You are not required to escalate.

You can respond without mirroring the harm.

Peace may feel vulnerable at first.  
But it is deeply powerful.

It protects your inner life.

Try choosing one moment today  
where you do not retaliate—  
in words, in tone, or in thought.

Let that be enough.
