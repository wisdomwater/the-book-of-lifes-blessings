## 74 - The Living Field

> *What is planted within you is alive. It responds to every gentle tending.*

I saw a wide field resting quietly beneath the soft light of morning. The earth stretched outward in stillness, deep and fertile beneath the open sky. It did not feel empty or barren. Beneath the surface, something already lived there—hidden seeds resting patiently within the soil, carrying within themselves the quiet promise of becoming.

At first the field appeared unmoving.

But as I watched more carefully, small signs of life began to emerge. Tiny openings formed in the earth. Subtle movements stirred beneath the surface where unseen roots were beginning to awaken. The whole field seemed alive with a hidden unfolding too gentle for hurried eyes to notice.

Then a presence began moving slowly through the field.

It carried no force or urgency. It did not command the earth to produce growth before its time. Instead, it moved with deep attentiveness and care—loosening hardened places, clearing crowded spaces, turning the soil gently where it had become closed. Nothing was neglected. Nothing was rushed.

Each place was tended according to what it needed.

And wherever the ground opened, light entered.

Where light entered, life began rising naturally toward it.

The field responded not through strain or effort, but through becoming more fully what it had always been created to hold. Growth emerged not because the field forced itself into transformation, but because it remained open to what quietly nurtured it from within.

And I saw then that everything necessary for life had already been planted there.

It was simply waiting to be tended with patience and love.

### Living the Vision

There are times when we wonder what is truly forming within us. We long for visible clarity about who we are becoming, whether our lives are changing, whether anything meaningful is taking root beneath the surface of ordinary days. Often we look for dramatic signs of growth while overlooking the quieter movements already unfolding within the soul.

Yet the inner life responds continually to what we nourish.

Every thought we return to, every act of kindness we practice, every moment of honesty, silence, prayer, or compassion becomes part of the soil from which our lives grow. Even small patterns of attention shape the inner landscape over time. The soul slowly becomes what it repeatedly opens itself toward.

This can feel overwhelming at times, as though we must somehow manage the entire field perfectly.

But spiritual growth rarely unfolds through control.

It grows through gentle tending.

We soften what has become hardened through fear or exhaustion. We clear space where distractions have crowded out deeper life. We remain present to the small and ordinary acts that nourish goodness rather than constantly demanding immediate transformation.

And slowly, hidden things begin rising toward the light.

Not all at once.

Not in ways that always appear impressive from the outside.

But truly.

The beautiful thing is that we do not do this work alone. A deeper presence continues moving quietly through the field of our lives—guiding, loosening, nourishing, sustaining. Grace works patiently beneath the surface long before we fully understand what it is creating.

And over time, what once felt hidden begins revealing itself little by little.

Enough to keep walking.

Enough to trust that life is growing even now.

### Breath Prayer

**Inhale:** Tend what is within me

**Exhale:** Let goodness grow
