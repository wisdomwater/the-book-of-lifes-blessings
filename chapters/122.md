## Day 122 - The Bear-Hands of Envy

> *I saw envy swollen with bitterness, fiery in its gaze and empty of any true order. It could not rejoice in another’s good, so it labored instead to spoil what it could not possess.*

Then I was shown more clearly  
the meaning of the second image.

It is Envy.

It walks beside pride,  
for envy is born from pride.  
When a person lifts himself up,  
he soon begins to grieve  
at the good that appears in another.

And so envy becomes  
a burning of evil within the soul.

The image was monstrous,  
for by envy the devil performs many works,  
drawing the human being away  
from the holiness of heaven.

Its head, shoulders, and arms  
were almost like those of a man,  
except that its hands were like a bear’s.

For the envious may still know something of God  
in the mind,  
yet they do not judge others  
according to God’s wisdom.

Instead they take injustice  
and set it in the seat of justice.  
They trust their own power.  
They grow hard in the strength of their arms.

Its chest and belly and back  
were swollen beyond the measure of man,  
because everything envy knows, believes, and supports  
is enlarged by distortion.

It has no sound teaching,  
no right proportion,  
no ordered method.  
Only gnashing,  
swelling,  
and violent excess.

Below the loins  
it resembled a man,  
but its feet were wood.

For the envious person,  
driven by fleshly desire,  
does much damage to others,  
yet all his works leave behind  
the dryness and sterility of death.

Its head was of fire,  
and flames came out of its mouth.

So it is with envy.  
Its gaze burns like a serpent’s,  
and its words are like fire  
that dries and wounds.

It wore no clothing,  
for it has no protection of goodness.  
It rejects justice,  
casts off all right order,  
and investigates nothing honorable,  
whether great or small.

It was plunged in darkness,  
because it roots itself  
in the bitterness of unbelief  
and longs only to hinder the flourishing of others.

Then I understood this also:

By the envy of the devil,  
death entered the world.

He could not bear the honor God gave.  
He saw the creature loved by God  
and could not rejoice.  
So he sought to tear man away  
from the work of God.

All who imitate him  
wound others by envy,  
eating away the gifts God has given.

But those who refuse envy  
and embrace God in faith  
become heirs of heaven’s reward.

#### What This Vision Means

Envy is not merely wanting what another has. It is the inability to rejoice in another’s good. It is sorrow at someone else’s flourishing, coupled with a secret desire to diminish it.

That is why envy is linked so closely to pride. Pride says, *I must be elevated.* Envy says, *Then no one else must shine above me.* Together they poison the soul.

The bear-like hands are important. Envy becomes grasping, rough, and predatory. The swollen body suggests distortion without proportion. Envy cannot hold another person’s blessing in a balanced way. It magnifies, resents, exaggerates, and broods. Its wooden feet show its barrenness: it cannot walk in life-giving paths.

The fiery head and flaming mouth reveal how envy thinks and speaks. It burns inwardly, then wounds outwardly. It destroys by suspicion, criticism, resentment, and subtle sabotage.

Charity is the true antidote, because charity can delight in goodness wherever it appears.

#### Living This Vision

Envy often speaks quietly.

Why them and not me?  
Why is their gift noticed?  
Why does their life seem to bloom?

It may show up in small ways:  
a sharp comment,  
a refusal to celebrate someone,  
a secret pleasure when they stumble.

The cure is not pretending the pain isn’t there.  
The cure begins with honesty.

Yes, I feel diminished by another’s good.  
Yes, I am tempted to resent their light.

Once you name it,  
you can place it before God.

Then practice the opposite of envy.  
Bless the person.  
Thank God for the gift you see in them.  
Pray for their flourishing, even if your heart resists.

This is not hypocrisy.  
It is medicine.

Envy withers the soul.  
But gratitude loosens its grip.
