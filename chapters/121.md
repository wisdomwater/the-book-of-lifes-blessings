## Day 121 - The Thin Gold Thread

> *I saw that pride, first among the vices, rises high in foolish nakedness, yet even there a thin golden limit remains, for God does not let its boasting become eternal.*

Then I understood the meaning  
of the seven images in the fog of unbelief.

They are vices that rise up  
against the gifts of the Holy Spirit.  
They contend against those gifts  
in every way they can,  
yet they do not prevail,  
for the power of God surpasses  
all the malice of the devil.

And I was shown again  
the first image—  
the one that is haughtiness,  
the first of vices,  
the mother and matter of many evils.

It was pride  
that cast the angel from heaven.  
It was pride  
that drove man from paradise.

And now, even when souls  
begin to return toward life by good works,  
pride lies in wait near the end of their labor,  
hoping to steal the reward of heaven  
by persuading them  
to admire themselves.

So often a person may do what is good,  
and then, by pride,  
the holiness of the work withers.

This image had a woman’s face,  
for pride changes the will into folly  
and seduces with softness  
before it devours.

Its eyes were fiery,  
because its gaze burns toward evil.

Its nose was smeared with mud,  
for it lacks discernment  
and becomes ugly in its stupidity.

Its mouth was closed,  
for it has no love of honest speech,  
but inwardly denies God.

It had no arms or hands,  
because its power does not give life,  
only death.

The bat-like wings on its shoulders  
stretched east and west,  
for pride opposes God in heavenly things  
and runs with the devil in earthly ones.

Its chest was like a man’s,  
swollen with vain grandeur.

Its lobster-like legs and feet  
showed a life stubborn, proud,  
empty, and unstable.

It had neither belly nor back,  
for it offers no fertile ground  
and gives no support for perseverance in good.

Its head had no hair,  
and its body no garment,  
for pride is foolish and naked—  
without prudence,  
without salvation.

Yet I saw one more thing:

Though it was surrounded by darkness,  
there remained a very thin thread  
like a circle of gold  
running from head to chin.

For even pride is not beyond God’s limit.

Pride shows contempt for those who know God,  
and grinds its teeth against Him.  
But as it once fell in shame,  
so it still falls.  
The higher it rises in the minds of fools,  
the lower it drags them down.

And against it stands humility,  
calling the soul back to truth.

#### What This Vision Means

This passage makes pride the root vice, not because every sin looks proud on the surface, but because pride places the self in rivalry with God. It is the old refusal: I will be my own height, my own measure, my own glory.

The symbolism is rich. Pride is all distortion. It has fire without light, wings without honor, swelling without substance, motion without stability. It is striking that it lacks both belly and back: it can neither nourish nor support. Pride is spiritually barren.

The thin golden thread is a profound detail. Even in the midst of darkness, God’s ordering power is still present. Pride is never sovereign. It cannot become ultimate. Its boundary remains held by divine wisdom.

This vision also reveals something subtle: pride can corrupt even good works. A person may truly do what is right, then secretly turn inward and begin feeding on self-praise. In that moment, the work loses some of its radiance. Humility preserves what pride tries to consume.

#### Living This Vision

Pride is not only boasting.

Sometimes it is the quiet pleasure  
of being better than someone else.  
Sometimes it is needing to be seen as good.  
Sometimes it is spiritual vanity—  
wanting even holiness to become a mirror.

This is why pride is so hard to notice.  
It can grow in the very field of good works.

A helpful question is this:  
When I do something good,  
where does my heart go next?

Toward gratitude?  
Or toward self-congratulation?

Humility does not deny the good.  
It simply returns it.

It says:  
This was grace.  
This was gift.  
Let the fruit remain with God.

When you notice pride rising,  
do not hate yourself.  
Just come back down.  
Come back into truth.

The golden thread remains.  
God’s mercy has not vanished.  
Even here, there is still a way home.
