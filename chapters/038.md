## Day 38 – The Sign of Victory

> *The Church bears a quiet victory—the defeat of the ancient serpent already written into its life.*

I saw the moon again, and within it there stood a ram.

It was not weak.  
It stood with quiet strength, as one who had endured and overcome.

And I understood—this was the Church.

Marked not by earthly power,  
but by victory.

For the ancient serpent, who once held sway, had been struck down.  
Its power broken.  
Its hold undone.

This victory was not loud.

It spread.

Across the earth.  
Through hidden lives.  
In faithful hearts.

And above all, I saw the sun blazing—  
its light reaching heaven, earth, and even the depths below.

This was the mystery of the Incarnation.

The greatest of all mysteries.

From it, all others unfold.

It touched all things.

It illuminated what was hidden.  
It stirred what had been still.

When it rose, new virtues appeared—  
ways of being not seen before.

And when it declined, I saw judgment at work—  
not as destruction,  
but as a clearing.

The distortions of vice fell away.  
What was empty was revealed as empty.

And truth remained.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the victory of Christ as already accomplished, yet still unfolding through the life of the Church.

The ram symbolizes strength and triumph—not through domination, but through endurance and faithfulness. The Church carries this victory into the world, not by force, but by embodying the life of Christ.

The sun represents the Incarnation—the central mystery through which divine light enters all levels of existence. Its rising brings new virtues into being; its setting reveals and removes what is false.

This is not a cycle of gain and loss, but of revelation. What is true is strengthened. What is empty is exposed.

The defeat of the serpent is not merely a past event—it is a present reality being lived out wherever truth and love take root.

#### Living This Vision

You may not feel victorious.

Your life may feel ordinary.  
Even fragile.

But this vision invites you to see differently.

Victory does not always look like triumph.

Sometimes it looks like quiet faithfulness.  
Like choosing what is good when it would be easier not to.

Each time you do this, you participate in something already won.

You are not fighting alone.

You are walking within a victory that has already begun.
