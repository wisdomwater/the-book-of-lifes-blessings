## 38 - The Fire Becoming Light

> *The fire within you is not against you. It can become a warmth that gives life.*

I saw a great wheel turning in quiet and steady rhythm, carrying within its movement a hidden fire. At times the fire flared suddenly—bright, fierce, reaching outward with startling intensity. It carried a living force within it, awake and urgent, like something longing to be seen, heard, and fully known.

Its heat filled the air around it.

And I understood that this fire was not merely destructive. It was power. Energy. The capacity to move, to respond, to speak when silence could no longer remain. There was something holy hidden within its intensity, something deeply alive that refused numbness and false peace.

Yet when the flame rushed outward without grounding, it scattered itself.

The fire stretched too far beyond its center, touching everything yet truly holding nothing. The wheel continued turning, but the movement led nowhere new. It circled endlessly within itself, exhausting its own force without bringing life. The heat became restless. The brightness lost its clarity.

Then I saw another way unfolding within the same fire.

The flame was not extinguished or denied. It was gathered inward and held gently within a deeper stillness. No shame surrounded it. No rejection. It was simply received—contained within something spacious enough to carry its intensity without being consumed by it.

And there, within that stillness, the fire began to change.

Its sharpness softened.

Its brightness steadied.

What had once surged outward chaotically became a quiet and enduring warmth. It no longer burned through everything it touched. Instead, it sustained. It illuminated. It gave life without destroying what stood near it.

From this transformed warmth emerged a different kind of strength—clarity without violence, truth without cruelty, presence without domination. The fire remained fully alive, yet now it moved in harmony with wisdom and compassion.

And I understood that the deepest transformation is not the loss of fire, but its healing.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when something rises quickly within us. A surge of anger. A tightening in the chest. A fierce awareness that something feels wrong or unjust. Often this inner heat arrives suddenly, carrying tremendous energy behind it. It can feel powerful, urgent, even necessary.

And sometimes it is.

For the fire within us is not always the enemy. Anger itself often reveals that something precious matters deeply to the soul. It may point toward wounded boundaries, unmet needs, grief, compassion, or a longing for truth and dignity. The problem is not always the fire itself, but what happens when we become fully carried away by it.

When strong emotion rushes outward without grounding, it can easily move beyond our deeper intentions. Words sharpen. Reactions escalate. What began as a desire for truth or protection becomes entangled with harm. The nervous system takes over, and the soul loses contact with its deeper center.

Yet within the very same fire there remains another possibility.

We can learn to stay present with what we feel without becoming consumed by it. We can allow the heat to settle into awareness rather than immediately forcing it outward into action. This is not suppression. It is transformation. The energy itself is being invited into a larger and steadier presence.

Over time, the fire begins to change shape.

What once erupted impulsively becomes clarity. What once wounded becomes courage guided by compassion. The soul discovers a strength capable of speaking honestly without cruelty and acting firmly without violence. This kind of gentleness is not weakness. It is disciplined love. It is fire that has learned how to illuminate rather than destroy.

You do not need to lose your passion in order to become whole.

The fire only needs to be held deeply enough that it can become light.
