## Day 12 — The Hidden Number

> *I saw that the works of God cannot be counted,
> and that the destiny of souls rests in a mystery beyond human knowing.*

Then I understood something deeper.

Though a person may possess great knowledge, no one can count the works of God.

No one can number those who will dwell in the heavenly kingdom.

Just as the wonders of God are beyond measure, so too are those who, through their good works, are drawn into the dwellings of heaven.

And likewise, those who are led astray by temptation and turn away into darkness cannot be counted.

Their number is hidden.

Only God knows.

For He sees all things at once.

Nothing escapes His knowing.

Then I understood further:

There are many secrets in God.

These secrets are not fully revealed to anyone.

He reveals only what He wills, and only in part.

Yet He contains all things.

He holds all things.

He preserves all things according to His purpose and grace.

Even this vision is given only as a glimpse.

#### What This Vision Means

This passage reveals the limits of human understanding.

Even the most learned person cannot fully grasp the scope of God’s work. The number of souls, the unfolding of history, and the final destiny of creation all remain within divine mystery.

This is not meant to create fear, but humility. Human beings often seek certainty—clear answers about who is saved, who is lost, and how everything will unfold. But this vision reminds us that such knowledge belongs to God alone.

At the same time, it affirms something hopeful:

God is not distant or unaware.

He knows every soul.

He holds every life within His care.

The mystery is not chaos. It is a hidden order guided by divine wisdom.

#### Living This Vision

There is a deep human desire to understand everything.

We want clear answers about life, about others, about ourselves. But the spiritual path invites us to live with a different posture.

Instead of control, we are invited into trust. Instead of certainty, we are invited into humility.

There are questions you may carry that do not yet have answers. There are situations you cannot fully understand. There are people whose journeys remain hidden from you.

This does not mean that meaning is absent. It means that meaning is held in a place deeper than your current sight.

Faith grows in that space. Not by knowing everything, but by trusting the One who does.
