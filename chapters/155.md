## 155 - Gathered into One

> *What has been scattered can be gently gathered*  
> *Love remembers what belongs together*

I saw a wide circle of light  
resting upon the earth.

It did not force itself outward,  
but held its shape—  
quiet,  
whole,  
unbroken.

Within it,  
figures stood at a distance  
from one another—  
each turned slightly away,  
each holding something close  
to themselves.

The air between them  
felt thin,  
stretched by unseen lines  
pulling in different directions.

Yet at the center,  
there was a flame.

Not large,  
not consuming—  
but steady.

It gave no command.  
It only burned  
with a soft, unwavering warmth.

And as the figures  
became aware of it,  
something in them  
began to turn.

Not all at once.

A glance.  
A softening.  
A loosening of what was held.

One step inward.

Then another.

The space between them  
shifted—  
no longer strained,  
but opening.

And as they drew nearer  
to the center,  
they found themselves  
nearer to one another.

Nothing was taken.

Nothing was forced.

But what had felt separate  
began to belong again.

And the circle of light  
held them—  
not as many divided,  
but as one  
resting in the same flame.

#### Living the Vision

There are times  
when distance forms within you.

A tightening toward someone.  
A quiet pulling away.  
A sense of standing apart,  
even when nothing is spoken.

You may notice  
how easily this happens—  
how the heart can close  
around a thought,  
a memory,  
a small hurt.

And in that closing,  
something begins to separate.

There is a gentle turning  
that can begin here.

Not by forcing closeness.  
Not by ignoring what is real.

But by allowing your attention  
to rest  
on something deeper  
than the division.

You may feel it  
as a quiet warmth—  
a willingness  
to soften  
even slightly.

To listen  
without preparing your reply.

To release  
one small claim  
to being right.

In that softening,  
space begins to open.

And within that space,  
something gathers—  
not by effort,  
but by presence.

You are not asked  
to resolve everything.

Only to remain  
near the center  
where love holds  
what you cannot.
