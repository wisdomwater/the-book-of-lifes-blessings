## Day 155 - One Heart, One Soul

> *Discord hangs upside down in darkness, but love gathers what division scatters.*

Then I saw another figure: Discord.

It came after Worldly Desire, because when people long for what they cannot have, they often fall into anger and division. Their minds grow wild. They strike at others like a raging dog.

Discord stirs disagreements. It scatters harshness and bitterness. It breaks apart what God has joined. It does not want peace. It rejoices when others are wounded by words or actions.

This figure was suspended in darkness, hanging by its feet.

Those ruled by discord are ready to walk into unbelief. They move with arrogance and stubbornness. They spare no one. They disturb whatever they can. They forget the beauty of unity.

But Scripture showed another way:

The multitude of believers had one heart and one soul. No one claimed anything as private possession, but all things were held in common.

I understood that the faithful are sealed by the fire of the Holy Spirit. In that fire, they see God in the mirror of unity.

They are called to have one heart in the mystery of the Trinity. They are called to burn with one love, looking first to God.

They do not cling to riches consumed by greed. They receive what they have as gift. They know that nothing belongs to them by their own merit.

Truth and justice are the true goods that cover all goods.

Those who choose God let go of their own will. They do not follow every path the world offers. They remember that God created all things, and whatever humanity has today may be taken tomorrow by His righteous judgment.

Everything comes from God.

Everything rests in God.


#### What This Vision Means

Discord is the opposite of holy communion.

It does not merely disagree. It delights in fracture. It takes the natural differences between people and turns them into weapons.

The figure hanging upside down shows disorder. Discord reverses what should be upright. Instead of walking toward peace, it moves toward darkness.

The Acts passage offers a luminous contrast. The believers are not uniform, but united. They share one heart and one soul because they are oriented toward the same divine center.

Their common life is not merely economic. It is spiritual. They no longer treat possession, status, or personal will as ultimate.

Discord says, “Mine.”

Communion says, “God’s.”

Unity begins when the soul stops clutching its own small kingdom.


#### Living This Vision

Discord often begins inside before it appears outside.

A resentment grows.
A comparison hardens.
A desire becomes entitlement.
A wound becomes accusation.

Soon the heart hangs upside down.

This vision invites you to practice communion in one small way.

Listen before defending.
Bless someone you envy.
Share what you are tempted to hoard.
Choose peace without pretending harm does not matter.

Unity is not denial.

It is the deeper labor of refusing to let bitterness become your home.

You do not need to agree with everyone to resist discord.

You need only remember that every person stands before God as more than an obstacle to your will.
