## Day 192 - Works Remembered in Light

> *No holy act is lost. Prayer, mercy, and goodwill rise before God and remain alive in the memory of eternal joy.*

The holy actions of the living help the souls who are not forgotten.

Prayers, alms, and other works of mercy live in the memory of supreme blessedness. They bring healing and relief to souls still undergoing purification.

By the strong power of divine mercy, sins are forgiven and souls are delivered from hell.

Those being purified are placed among certain elements. When they are cleansed and released from punishment, they are soon brought into the earthly paradise.

And for the glorious and holy souls, there is the celestial light—a light no human eye can fully behold or understand. This light is prepared for those whose virtues come from the strength of God.

When earthly matter has completed its course, the grime that gathered through Adam’s fall will be scattered. Then the earth will shine as it did in the beginning, when it was first created.

When the Holy Spirit gives a person the grace to pray holy prayers in the heart, those prayers are not hidden. They rise pure before God.

And God is praised by both angels and human beings.

When people sing the laments and words of the prophets in praise of God—words that reveal His justice and His miracles—they may bring help to those suffering in body, and peace to departed souls, according to what God sees as fitting.

When a person gives alms from what God has given, God remembers Abraham’s sacrifice. As God spared Abraham’s child, so He may show mercy to those for whom alms are offered, according to His wisdom.

Goodwill is the sweetest fragrance before God.

For even in ancient sacrifices, God did not delight in blood itself, but in the willing heart.

When, by the gift of the Holy Spirit, a person offers worthy sacrifice for the living or for the dead, God receives that holy sorrow.

But whoever refuses to serve God—in prayer, mercy, sacrifice, or good works—is struck by the emptiness of the heart.

So let no faithful person cease from laboring for God, for others, or for themselves.

The One who searches the heart rewards every honest effort and every act of goodwill.

#### What This Vision Means

This final movement of the vision is filled with hope.

Nothing good is wasted.

Prayer rises. Alms are remembered. Goodwill becomes fragrance. Holy sorrow is received. Even the dead are held within the mercy of God.

Hildegard sees the living and the departed as connected through love and prayer. The holy actions of one life can become relief and blessing for another.

The vision also looks toward restoration. Even the earth, burdened by the fall, will one day shine again as it did in the beginning.

At the center is goodwill—a heart turned sincerely toward God. This is the fragrance God receives.

#### Living This Vision

Sometimes our small acts of goodness feel invisible.

A prayer whispered in fatigue.

A gift given quietly.

A sacrifice no one notices.

A tear shed for someone else.

This vision says: none of it is lost.

The heart’s sincere offering rises before God.

Today, do one good thing with goodwill. Pray for someone. Give what you can. Offer your work. Remember the suffering.

Let your life become a fragrance of mercy.
