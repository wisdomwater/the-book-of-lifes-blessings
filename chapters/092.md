## Day 92 – The Shapeless Darkness

> *When truth is abandoned, the soul loses its form and wanders without light.*
> *What is false cannot hold shape—it dissolves into shadow and confusion.*

I saw a darkness so dense that nothing within it could be clearly seen. No limb, no face, no feature could be distinguished. It was thick, suffocating, and without clarity.

This was Untruth.

Within that darkness, there appeared something like a human figure—but misshapen, distorted, lacking all beauty. It moved uncertainly, as though it did not know its own direction. It wavered between certainty and doubt, never grounded, never whole.

There was no integrity in it. No light. No righteousness.

It did not dwell in love, nor did it seek God. Instead, it lived in deception—creating lies, spreading them, and multiplying them through others. It did not merely speak falsehood; it became a source of it, feeding a cycle that grew wider and darker.

Beneath it was a strange foundation—dry, hardened, black, like foam that had lost all life. From time to time, flames burst forth from it. These were not flames of light, but of agitation and anger—restless, consuming, without peace.

I understood that this dryness was a life without truth.  
Hard without kindness.  
Dark without virtue.  
Burning without warmth.

Then a voice spoke through the words of David:

“The wicked go astray from the womb;  
they are liars from their beginning.”

And I saw how the first turning away from God had opened the door to this. When humanity listened to the serpent and reached for what was not given, truth was fractured. From that moment, the taste of food was joined to the taste of deception.

And so, when the soul gives itself over to excess—when it fills itself without restraint—it begins to lose its clarity. It drifts. It forgets. It begins to call darkness light and destruction life.

I saw people praising what leads to ruin.  
I saw them naming falsehood as truth.  
I saw them building empty things and calling them holy.

And yet, all of it was hollow.

For what is not rooted in God cannot stand.  
It fades. It collapses. It returns to nothing.

#### What This Vision Means

Untruth is not just the act of lying—it is a state of being disconnected from reality as God holds it.

The shapelessness of the figure reveals this clearly. Truth gives structure, coherence, and beauty to the soul. When truth is rejected, the soul loses its form. It becomes unstable, uncertain, and divided.

The dense darkness represents confusion. A person living in untruth may feel certain at times, but that certainty is fragile and shifting. It cannot endure.

The dry, black foundation shows a life without nourishment. Truth feeds the soul. Without it, everything becomes brittle and lifeless. The occasional flames of anger or intensity do not bring warmth—they only reveal unrest.

The connection to the first deception reminds us that untruth is deeply rooted in the human condition. It is not merely personal—it is inherited, learned, and reinforced.

But it is not final.

Truth remains.  
And it calls us back.

#### Living This Vision

Pay attention to where your life feels unclear, unstable, or divided.

Where do you sense confusion instead of peace?  
Where do your words or thoughts drift away from what is honest?

Untruth often begins subtly:
- small justifications  
- quiet exaggerations  
- avoiding what is uncomfortable  

Over time, these create distance from reality.

The invitation is not to condemn yourself, but to return.

Speak truth, even when it is simple.  
Choose honesty, even when it is costly.  
Sit with what is real, even when it is difficult.

Truth does not demand perfection.  
It asks for alignment.

As you return to truth, the soul begins to take shape again.  
Clarity returns.  
Light begins to filter in.

And what once felt heavy and dark begins to loosen its hold.
