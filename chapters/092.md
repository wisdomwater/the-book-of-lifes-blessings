## 92 - The Wings of Trust

> *You are not sustained by striving alone. Life is given as well as gathered.*

I saw a figure standing in dimness, holding itself with quiet tension. Its posture carried the weight of constant vigilance, as though everything depended upon its ability to remain prepared. The figure’s gaze stayed fixed upon the ground—upon what could be gathered, secured, protected, and held close against uncertainty.

Its hands never fully rested.

Its thoughts moved endlessly through plans, calculations, fears, and responsibilities. Everything within it leaned toward survival. The body itself seemed shaped by effort, tightened around the belief that safety could only come through unceasing control.

Then something shifted above it.

Not a command. Not an interruption. A widening.

The atmosphere opened like a great sky unfolding beyond the narrowness of anxious thought. And from within this spaciousness came a gentle lifting—as though some forgotten part of the soul remembered there was another way to move through life besides carrying everything alone.

Wings formed.

Not visible in any outward sense, but felt inwardly in the loosening of strain. The figure grew still. Its hands softened. Its breath deepened. And when it looked again at the same ground beneath its feet, the world no longer appeared solely as something to grasp in fear.

It became something that could also be received.

Nothing essential had been taken away from the figure. Work still remained. Needs still existed. The earth itself had not changed.

Only the unbearable weight of believing it must sustain everything alone had begun to lift.

### Living the Vision

Much of human life becomes organized around survival. We think constantly about provision, security, responsibilities, health, money, relationships, and the uncertain future waiting beyond our control. These concerns are not imaginary. They belong to the reality of being human in a fragile world.

Yet beneath these necessary concerns, many people quietly carry another burden: the belief that everything depends entirely upon them.

The nervous system tightens around this fear. Rest becomes difficult. Even moments of stillness feel unsafe because the mind remains convinced that vigilance itself is what keeps life from collapsing. Over time, the soul grows exhausted from trying to hold together what was never meant to be carried alone.

The contemplative life does not ask us to abandon responsibility.

We still work. We still plan. We still care for what has been entrusted to us. But slowly we begin learning another posture beneath our effort—a deeper trust that life is also being given to us moment by moment.

Breath continues without our commanding it. The earth continues bearing life. Grace arrives in ordinary forms we often overlook: nourishment, friendship, beauty, kindness, rest, unexpected help, resilience, love. Much of what sustains us has always come as gift alongside effort.

And when the soul begins remembering this, something softens within us.

We stop gripping life quite so tightly.

A small release becomes possible.

Not passivity, but participation without panic.

Like wings unfolding within the heart, trust allows us to move through life with less fear and more spaciousness. We discover that we are not abandoned to hold existence together through willpower alone.

Even now, beneath all striving, something larger is carrying us.

And in remembering this, the soul begins to rest.
