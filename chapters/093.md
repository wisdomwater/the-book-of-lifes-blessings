## 93 - The Freedom of Open Hands

> *You are not made of what you hold. You are held by what does not pass away.*

I saw a figure clothed in brightness, adorned with many things that caught and reflected the light. Every movement shimmered with beauty and significance. The figure carried itself carefully, as though each object it possessed added another layer of meaning, another measure of worth to sustain its place in the world.

And yet beneath the brightness there was heaviness.

Its footing seemed uncertain, hidden beneath shadow. Though surrounded by beautiful things, the figure moved with quiet tension, gathering more and holding each new possession close—as though something essential might disappear if its hands were ever emptied.

Then a quiet fire drew near.

It did not arrive with violence or destruction. Its presence was steady, clear, and deeply calm—like truth revealed without accusation. The fire illuminated rather than consumed. And within its warmth, something in the figure began to soften.

One by one, its hands loosened.

Not through force. Not through shame. It was as though the soul remembered something older and more enduring than what it carried. As its grip relaxed, the brightness around it changed. It no longer depended upon what was being held. The light became gentler, truer, arising not from possession but from presence itself.

And beneath the figure, the ground grew firm.

No longer did it need to stand upon borrowed reflections. It rested instead upon something deeper—something that remained even when the hands were empty.

And I saw then that freedom begins when the soul no longer confuses what it possesses with what it is.

**Living the Vision**

It is easy to build an identity around what can be seen. Possessions, achievements, status, appearance, knowledge, success, influence, even spiritual accomplishments—these things often become quiet ways of proving our worth to ourselves and others. We hold them tightly because they seem to offer security, meaning, or belonging.

Yet what we cling to eventually begins clinging to us.

The soul grows heavy when it believes its value depends upon maintaining certain appearances or keeping hold of what can always change. Fear quietly enters the heart: fear of loss, failure, aging, insignificance, being forgotten, or no longer appearing worthy in the eyes of others.

The contemplative life invites another kind of freedom.

Not rejection of beauty, work, or the gifts of life, but a loosening of the grip. We learn how to receive what is given with gratitude without turning it into the foundation of our identity. We begin asking more honest questions: If this disappeared tomorrow, would I still know myself as loved? As whole? As held?

These questions are not meant to shame us.

They are meant to open space within us.

For there is a deeper ground beneath all passing things—a place where the soul rests not upon possession or performance, but upon the quiet truth of its belonging. The more we touch this deeper ground, the less fear controls us. We become freer to enjoy what is beautiful without needing to possess it completely. Freer to give. Freer to share. Freer to let go when necessary.

Open hands discover something clenched hands cannot.

That what is most essential cannot be lost through release.

And what remains when everything else shifts is often the truest thing we have ever known.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Kindle freedom within me

**Exhale:** I rest in what remains
