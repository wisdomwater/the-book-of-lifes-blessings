## 93 - The Freedom of Open Hands

> *You are not made of what you hold*  
> *You are held by what does not pass away*

I saw a figure  
clothed in brightness—  
adorned with many things  
that caught the light.

Each movement shimmered.  
Each gesture revealed  
something to be noticed.

And yet,  
beneath it,  
there was a heaviness—  
as though its footing  
was uncertain,  
hidden in shadow.

It gathered what it could,  
holding each new thing  
close—  
as though each one  
added something essential.

Then,  
a quiet fire  
drew near.

Not consuming in haste,  
but steady,  
clear—  
like a presence  
that reveals  
without taking.

One by one,  
the figure loosened its grasp.

Not forced—  
but as though it remembered  
something deeper.

As its hands opened,  
the light did not fade.

It changed.

It became softer,  
truer—  
no longer reflecting  
what was held,  
but what remained.

And I saw  
that the ground beneath it  
grew firm  
when nothing was clung to.

The brightness  
was no longer borrowed.

It was received.

#### Living the Vision

It's easy to measure yourself  
by what can be seen.

What you have.  
What you’ve built.  
What others recognize.

It can feel natural—  
as though these things  
give shape to your life.

And yet,  
you may notice  
a subtle weight—  
a need to hold onto them,  
to protect them,  
to prove something through them.

There is another way  
that begins quietly.

Not by rejecting what you have,  
but by loosening your grip.

You can still use,  
still enjoy,  
still share.

But you do not need  
to be defined by it.

You may try it gently—  
a moment of release,  
a small inward turning  
that says:

This is not my source.

And in that release,  
something steadies.

Your worth becomes less fragile.  
Your life feels less divided.

You are free  
to receive what is given  
without needing to hold it  
as proof of who you are.

And in that freedom,  
your heart turns again  
toward what cannot be taken.
