## Day 93 – Fire of Strife

> *When falsehood takes root, conflict follows close behind.*
> *A heart turned inward upon itself begins to burn against others.*

Then I saw another figure rise from falsehood, and it was filled with restless fire.

Its face was like a man’s, but its hair was black and tightly curled, as though coiled with tension. It carried within it the sharp awareness of reason—but used it not for peace, but for domination.

This was the Desire for Strife.

It moved with agitation, always seeking conflict. It twisted its thoughts and sharpened its will, turning even small matters into causes for division. Its anger burned like fire, flaring suddenly, consuming whatever lay near.

It wore a garment of many colors, layered and shifting, with openings at the shoulders where its arms thrust through. I saw that it wrapped its desires in many disguises, hiding its unrest beneath different forms.

Its arms reached outward constantly, acting without restraint. It did not consider the needs or good of others. It followed only its own will.

In its left hand, it gripped an axe tightly. And with it, it struck wildly—cutting, wounding, and drawing blood. These were not only physical acts, but the wounds of words, of accusations, of harsh judgments spoken without mercy.

Its speech was filled with darts—sharp, piercing, and destructive.

And I saw how it revealed itself through its own words, exposing its inner chaos. In attacking others, it wounded itself. In stirring conflict, it deepened its own unrest.

It could not be still.  
It could not be at peace.

Yet a quiet presence stood against it—the voice of a higher peace, calling gently, urging restraint, inviting stillness.

But this figure resisted, driven by its own fire.

#### What This Vision Means

The Desire for Strife is what grows when untruth is allowed to take root in the soul.

When truth is lost, the mind becomes unstable. And from that instability comes conflict—both within and without. The person begins to project their inner unrest onto others.

The fire imagery reveals the nature of this state. Anger becomes the driving force. It feels powerful, even justified, but it is ultimately destructive.

The many-colored garment shows how this desire can hide behind different masks:
- righteousness  
- humor  
- intelligence  
- even concern  

But beneath it is a restless need to assert, to win, to dominate.

The axe represents harmful action—especially through words. Words become tools of division rather than instruments of truth.

And yet, the presence of peace remains. It does not force itself, but it calls—inviting the soul back to stillness and clarity.

#### Living This Vision

Notice where you feel the urge to argue, correct, or prove yourself.

Pause.

Ask:  
Is this coming from truth… or from unrest?

Strife often feels urgent. It demands immediate expression. But wisdom moves more slowly.

When you feel that inner heat:
- take a breath  
- soften your posture  
- delay your response  

Not every thought needs to be spoken.  
Not every disagreement needs to become conflict.

Choose peace where you can.  
Choose understanding over victory.

And when you do speak, let your words carry clarity—not fire.

In this way, the cycle of strife begins to loosen.
