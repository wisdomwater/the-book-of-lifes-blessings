## Day 18 - From Shadow into Full Light

> *What is now partial will one day be whole; what is dim will be made clear in fullness.*

I was shown again the unfolding of divine truth.

The old law appeared as something veiled—  
real, yet shadowed,  
like a form seen through dim light.

But the new law shone openly,  
clear and radiant,  
revealing what had long been hidden.

Then I saw the souls of the blessed.

They were filled with peace,  
yet something in them remained incomplete.

For though they were free from the weight of the body,  
they did not yet behold the fullness of God.

It was as if something had been divided—  
and what is divided cannot see the whole.

They waited.

And I understood:  
when they are restored to wholeness—  
when body and soul are joined again—  
then their vision will be complete.

They will see the face of the Father  
not in part, but in fullness.

Just as the new law fulfills and clarifies  
what the old law could only hint at,  
so too will their joy be perfected.

Nothing will be lacking.  
Nothing will be obscured.

All will be whole.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision speaks of incompleteness—not as failure, but as a stage in a greater unfolding. The old law, though true, was partial. It pointed toward something greater that had not yet fully appeared.

In the same way, even the souls of the blessed await completion. Without the fullness of their being, their vision of God remains limited. This reflects a profound truth: wholeness matters. God’s intention is not to save fragments, but to restore the entire person.

The comparison between the laws and the soul reveals a pattern—God brings things from partial clarity into full revelation. What is hidden becomes visible. What is divided becomes united.

Perfection, in this sense, is not about flawlessness—it is about fullness.


#### Living This Vision

There are moments when your life feels incomplete—when understanding is partial, joy is mixed, and clarity seems just out of reach.

This vision invites you to see that differently.

You are not failing.  
You are unfolding.

What you experience now is not the final form of your life.  
It is a stage within it.

Instead of resisting that incompleteness, you can hold it with patience.

Ask yourself gently:  
*What might still be forming in me?*

There is a quiet hope here:  
what feels fragmented will not remain so.

One day, what you now see dimly  
will become clear.

Until then, you are invited to live faithfully  
within the partial light—  
trusting that it is leading somewhere real.
