## 85 - The Light That Remains

> *Even when you forget, something in you still remembers*  
> *A quiet light waits beneath the noise*

I saw a long valley  
resting in shadow—  
wide,  
silent,  
as if it had forgotten  
how to hold the day.

The air was dim,  
softened by distance,  
as though everything  
had grown faint.

And within that quiet,  
a figure moved slowly—  
not lost,  
but uncertain,  
as if searching  
for something once known.

Nothing called out.

No path was marked.

Only a stillness  
that seemed to stretch  
in every direction.

Then,  
very gently,  
a small light appeared.

Not above—  
but within.

A soft glow,  
like a memory  
returning without words.

It did not illuminate  
the whole valley.

It did not remove the shadow.

But it was enough  
to be seen.

And as the figure  
turned toward it,  
something shifted—  
not outwardly,  
but inwardly.

A quiet recognition.

A sense  
of having been here before,  
of belonging  
to something beyond the dimness.

The light remained small.

But it did not fade.

And as it was followed,  
step by step,  
the valley no longer felt empty—  
but held.

As if even here,  
nothing had been forgotten  
completely.

#### Living the Vision

Your awareness  
feels far away.

Not gone—  
just quiet,  
as though covered  
by many other things.

You may move through your day  
without noticing  
what once felt close.

And yet,  
something in you  
still carries a trace.

A small awareness.  
A subtle knowing.  
A quiet pull  
toward what is real.

You may notice it  
in a simple moment—  
a pause,  
a breath,  
a glimpse of something  
that gently draws your attention back.

You do not need  
to recover everything at once.

You may begin  
by turning  
toward that small light.

Let it be enough.

In following it,  
even faintly,  
you begin to remember.

Not through effort,  
but through presence.

And in that remembering,  
you may sense  
that you have not been  
as far away  
as you once thought.
