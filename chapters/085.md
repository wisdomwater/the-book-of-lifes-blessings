## Day 85 — Drawing from the Deep Well

> *Those who hunger for truth are never left empty.*

And I saw that the book was filled  
with many writings in white letters—  
drawn from deep places.

And I understood:

Those who seek wisdom  
draw from the depths of what has been given.

They gather the mysteries  
of the old and the new together.

They do not separate them.  
They hold them as one.

With care and devotion,  
they prepare paths of righteousness—  
against darkness,  
against unbelief,  
against the subtle work of the enemy.

They thirst for Scripture.

They draw from it again and again,  
like water from a well.

And they do not stop  
until their hearts are filled.

For just as God did not cease  
until His work was complete,  
so too the wise continue seeking  
until understanding takes root.

And I heard the words:

“All nations will come  
and worship before God.”

For all things come from Him.  
All life returns to Him.

Even those who shape and build  
cannot give life itself.

Only God breathes life.

Yet some are deceived.

They look upon the work of their own hands  
and call it divine.

They seek God—  
but do not recognize Him.

Still, God has given both the old law  
and the new,  
so that humanity might know Him.

And those who seek with sincerity  
are filled.

And I saw:

The well was deep.  
And it did not run dry.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision emphasizes the importance of sustained seeking.

Truth is not exhausted in a single reading, a single insight, or a single moment of understanding. It requires return, reflection, and desire.

The well represents the depth of divine revelation. Scripture is not shallow—it invites continual drawing.

The unity of the Old and New Testaments shows that truth unfolds but does not contradict itself. Those who are wise learn to hold this continuity.

The warning against idolatry is subtle but important. It is possible to become so focused on human effort—our own creations, systems, or ideas—that we lose sight of the source of life itself.

This vision calls for both humility and persistence.

Seek deeply.  
But remember where the depth comes from.

#### Living This Vision

Let your spiritual life have rhythm.

Return to what nourishes you—  
again and again.

Do not assume that because you have read something once,  
you have exhausted it.

There is always more beneath the surface.

Approach truth with hunger,  
but also with patience.

You do not need to rush to mastery.

Instead, let understanding accumulate—  
like water drawn daily.

And be mindful of subtle distractions.

Where are you tempted to rely  
on your own constructions  
instead of seeking what is given?

Gently return.

The well is still there.  
It has not run dry.
