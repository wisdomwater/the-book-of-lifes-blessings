## 85 - The Light That Remains

> *Even when you forget, something in you still remembers. A quiet light waits beneath the noise.*

I saw a long valley resting beneath shadow. It stretched wide and silent beneath a dim sky, as though it had forgotten how to hold the warmth of day. The air carried a distant softness, and everything within the valley seemed muted—as though memory itself had faded into quietness. Nothing moved quickly there. Nothing called for attention. The stillness felt ancient and deep.

And within that quiet landscape, a figure walked slowly.

It did not seem entirely lost, yet neither did it know clearly where it was going. There was uncertainty in its steps, like someone searching for a place once known but long forgotten. No clear path appeared before it. No voice broke the silence. Only the wide stillness remained, stretching in every direction.

Then, very gently, a small light appeared.

It did not descend from above or blaze suddenly across the valley. It emerged from within the dimness itself—a soft glow, delicate as memory returning without words. The light was small, almost fragile, yet unmistakably alive. It did not illuminate the whole valley at once, nor did it erase the surrounding shadow.

But it was enough to be seen.

And as the figure turned toward it, something shifted inwardly. Not certainty. Not complete understanding. Something quieter than that—a recognition. A feeling of having belonged all along to something deeper than the shadow surrounding it.

The light remained small.

Yet it did not fade.

And as the figure followed it step by step, the valley no longer felt abandoned. Even here, within uncertainty and dimness, there was a hidden sense of being held. As though nothing essential had ever been entirely forgotten.

### Living the Vision

There are seasons when spiritual awareness feels distant. Life becomes crowded with responsibilities, distractions, grief, fatigue, noise, or survival. What once felt alive within us may grow faint beneath the weight of ordinary days. We continue moving forward, but something inward feels quieter than before—as though the deeper parts of the soul have gone dormant.

Yet forgetting is not the same as losing.

The contemplative life reminds us that beneath all distraction there often remains a small, persistent awareness quietly waiting to be noticed again. Sometimes it appears unexpectedly—in a moment of stillness, in beauty, in sorrow, in prayer, in silence, or simply in the feeling that something more real is gently calling us home.

We do not need to recover everything all at once.

The soul rarely awakens through force. More often, it returns gradually through small acts of attention. A breath consciously taken. A few quiet moments without distraction. A willingness to turn toward what feels true, even faintly. These small movements matter more than we realize.

For the inner light does not demand perfection before it shines.

It waits patiently beneath the noise of life, remaining present even when we feel far from ourselves. And every time we turn toward it, however uncertainly, something within begins remembering again—not through effort alone, but through presence.

The path back is often much gentler than we feared.

Because the light has remained with us the entire time.
