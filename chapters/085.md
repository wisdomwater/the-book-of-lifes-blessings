## 85 - The Well That Remains

> *What you return to continues to give*  
> *Depth opens to those who linger*

I saw a quiet well  
set within a wide and open place.

Its stone was smooth,  
worn by many hands—  
not hurried,  
but faithful.

Light rested at its edge,  
softly illuminating  
what could be seen.

But the water—  
it lay deep.

Hidden beneath stillness,  
untroubled,  
clear.

A vessel was lowered slowly.

No strain.  
No urgency.

Only a steady descent  
into what could not be grasped  
from the surface.

And when it rose again,  
it carried water—  
cool,  
bright,  
enough.

Around the well,  
there was no crowding.

Only a quiet returning.

Again and again,  
the same movement—  
lowering,  
waiting,  
receiving.

And I saw  
that the well did not diminish.

It welcomed each approach  
with the same quiet abundance.

Nothing was withheld.

Nothing was forced.

Only a depth  
that remained—  
ready  
for those who came  
with open hands.

#### Living the Vision

You feel the desire  
to go deeper—

to understand more,  
to be nourished  
in a way that lasts.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
the pull to move quickly—  
to gather insight  
without truly receiving it.

But there is another rhythm  
that feels quieter.

You may sense it  
when you return  
to something familiar—  
a word,  
a truth,  
a practice—  
and find that it meets you  
differently each time.

Not because it has changed,  
but because you have come  
again.

There is a depth  
that does not reveal itself  
all at once.

It opens  
through presence,  
through patience,  
through returning  
without hurry.

You are not asked  
to take everything in.

Only to receive  
what is given now.

And gently,  
you may find  
that what once felt small  
begins to fill you—  
not by force,  
but by faithfulness.

The well remains.

And you are welcome there.
