## Day 168 - The One Who Holds All

> *Even the abyss is held within a greater strength. What appears chaotic is still sustained, shaped, and carried by the One who restores all things.*

And I saw the Man.

He stood and looked intently upon the whole world. His gaze did not pass lightly over it—He beheld it fully, as one who knows the depths of what He sees.

From His calves down to His feet, He stood upon the abyss.

Yet the abyss did not consume Him.

Instead, it revealed His power.

For He rose above it, and through Him all things were restored, purified, and sanctified. He contained everything and carried everything within Himself.

His essence moved through all creatures.

It held them together, as the soul gives strength and coherence to the body.

And I understood that even the abyss belonged within His dominion. It was like a workshop of the supreme craftsman, where hidden tools shape what is not yet complete.

Then I saw from His calves something like breath—like air mingled with sweat—rising and stirring the waters of the abyss.

And with His feet, He pressed down the forces of the elements: those upon the earth, within the earth, and beneath it.

Nothing escaped His ordering.

Then the Man spoke:

“You who hear and understand—do penance, for God shows you what is true. If you do not turn, correction will come, so that repentance may awaken within you.”

And I trembled, and I understood.

#### What This Vision Means

This Man is a living image of divine presence—both transcendent and intimately woven into creation.

Nothing exists outside of Him. Even the abyss, which seems like chaos or danger, is not abandoned. It is held, worked, and transformed.

The image of the craftsman is key. Creation is not static—it is being shaped. The movement of breath over the waters recalls the beginning of life, suggesting that even now, renewal is taking place.

Repentance is not merely correction—it is participation. To turn toward God is to come into alignment with the One who sustains all things.

#### Living This Vision

There are places in our lives that feel like an abyss.

Uncertainty. Fear. Inner turmoil.

This vision does not remove those realities—but it reframes them.

You are not lost within the abyss. You are held above it, within a greater presence that is still shaping, still restoring.

Repentance becomes less about fear and more about returning—returning to the One who already holds you.

Where can you turn again today?
