## Day 183 - The Bronze Zeal

> *God’s justice moves with terrible clarity. It searches what must be searched and brings falsehood to nothing.*

Before the Man who stood above the abyss, I saw a bronze mace.

It was fixed there like a weapon of judgment, ready in the presence of God. It moved from side to side as if preparing to strike.

This was the Zeal of God.

It did not strike wildly. It examined. It weighed. It searched all things that must be judged by the righteous judgment of God.

And as it moved, it made a sound.

The sound spoke severely against the empty images of perdition—against every mocking spirit that rises against God, trying to climb upward while falling into the deep.

The mace stripped them of honor.

It cast them into confusion.

It rejected them like a decaying corpse.

For they came from the throat of the ancient serpent. They bubbled out of his mouth to deceive humanity, wounding souls with fiery darts and drawing them away from the justice of God.

But the Zeal of God stood against them.

“I will wage war against you,” it said. “I will pursue you. I will show you the strength of God, and I will reduce you to nothing.”

Then I understood the words spoken in Egypt, when God passed through the land at midnight and struck down the firstborn.

When evil becomes strong and believes no one can overcome it, God acts.

He struck down the pride of the devil.

He judged the transgression of Adam.

He weakened the roots of every vice.

And through His Son, He struck death with death and brought the faithful back to life through water and the Spirit.

The blood of Christ and the blood of the martyrs were joined to holy purity, and the ancient serpent was put to shame.

Yet the mercy of God never dries up.

He raises repentant sinners.

Those who begin again, who cleanse their sins with tears, begin to live.

Let whoever longs for life take these words and place them deep within the heart.

#### What This Vision Means

The Zeal of God is not petty anger. It is holy judgment against what destroys life.

The bronze mace represents divine strength that examines and exposes. It strikes not from cruelty, but from truth. It opposes deception because deception wounds the soul.

This vision holds judgment and mercy together. God destroys the roots of evil, but raises repentant sinners. He defeats the serpent, but does not abandon those who have been bitten.

The deepest movement is restoration. Death is struck. Sin is exposed. Mercy remains.

#### Living This Vision

We often fear judgment because we imagine it as rejection.

But divine judgment also protects. It names what is false. It breaks what enslaves. It refuses to let deception have the final word.

This vision invites us to let God’s zeal search us.

Not to crush us, but to free us.

What false thing needs to be brought to nothing in you?

What old serpent-whisper no longer deserves your obedience?

Let mercy and truth work together.
