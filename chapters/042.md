## Day 42 – The Cloud Without Light

> *Where the light is refused, joy cannot enter, and the soul becomes a place of dryness.*

At the far edge of the vision, I saw another cloud.

It came from the north—slow, heavy, and without warmth.

As it spread, it merged with a deep darkness already present.  
But this darkness was not like the others.

It was empty.

Dry.

No light touched it.  
No warmth stirred within it.

The sun did not reach there.  
Its rays did not penetrate.

And because of this, there was no joy.

No spark.  
No movement of life.

Only a stillness that carried suffering.

I understood that this was a different kind of torment.

Not one of noise or struggle—  
but of absence.

The absence of light.  
The absence of delight.  
The absence of hope.

This was a place where nothing blossomed.

Where nothing lifted.

And I saw that this darkness was bound to the devil—  
joined to him in a state that could not receive the light it had rejected.

Here, there was no expectation of change.  
No anticipation of joy.

Only the echo of what had been refused.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the nature of spiritual emptiness—not as active punishment, but as the consequence of separation from divine light.

The dryness of the cloud symbolizes a state where life cannot flourish. Without connection to the source of life, joy cannot arise.

This is not merely external suffering, but an inner condition—a soul that has closed itself to what gives life.

The absence of light is central. It is not that light is unwilling to shine, but that it is not received.

This vision invites us to see that joy is not self-generated. It flows from connection—to truth, to love, to God.

When that connection is severed, what remains is not fullness, but emptiness.

#### Living This Vision

There are times when you may feel dry.

Disconnected.  
Without energy.  
Without joy.

Not all dryness is the same.

Some seasons are part of growth.

But this vision gently asks:  
is there anything in you that has turned away from what gives life?

Not in shame.  
But in honesty.

Where have you closed yourself off?

Where have you stopped receiving?

Even a small turning back—  
a small opening—  
can begin to let light in again.
