## 42 — The Place That Opens Again

> *Even what feels empty can receive*  
> *A small opening is enough for light*

At the edge of what I saw,  
there was a quiet place—

still,  
unadorned,  
without the shimmer  
of the fields before it.

It felt closed—  
not sealed,  
but gently turned inward.

No movement stirred there.  
No warmth yet rose.

And yet,  
it was not abandoned.

It waited.

Like earth  
before the rain,  
like air  
before the first light of morning.

There was a simplicity to it—  
nothing distracting,  
nothing covering over  
what it was.

Just space.

Just stillness.

And within that stillness,  
something almost imperceptible—

a softness,  
a place  
where light  
could enter.

Not forced.  
Not rushed.

Only welcomed  
when it was received.

And I sensed  
that even here,  
nothing had been lost beyond return.

Only unopened.

Only waiting  
for the slightest turning  
toward what gives life.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes there is a kind of dryness—

a quiet distance,  
a lack of movement,  
a sense that something  
has grown still within you.

You may wonder  
what has changed.

Or feel the urge  
to push past it,  
to fix it quickly.

But something in you  
already knows  
that not all stillness  
is empty.

You may notice  
a gentle invitation—  
not to force feeling,  
but to become open again.

A small willingness.

A simple pause.

A breath  
that does not rush away.

You do not need  
to fill the space.

You only need  
to allow it  
to be touched.

Even the slightest opening  
is enough.

Light does not demand.

It enters  
where it is received.

And what once felt dry  
can slowly soften—  
not all at once,  
but truly.
