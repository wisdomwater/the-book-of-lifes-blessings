## Day 100 – The Field of Knowing

> *Within every soul is the knowledge of good and evil, waiting to be cultivated.*
> *What we grow depends on what we choose to tend.*

Then I saw the human soul as a field.

It was not empty. It carried within it the knowledge of both good and evil—planted deep from the beginning. This knowledge stirred both love and fear, drawing the soul toward God, yet also revealing its capacity to turn away.

And I understood: no one is without this knowing.

Each person is given the tools to cultivate their inner ground.

I saw a figure working the soil—plowing, turning, breaking the hardness of the earth. It labored steadily, removing weeds, uprooting what did not belong. It did not grow weary of the work.

This was the soul responding to truth.

For the field does not tend itself. It must be shaped, guided, and cared for. Without attention, it becomes overgrown. Without effort, it yields little.

Yet when it is cultivated, it becomes fertile. It produces life.

And I saw something else: this order could not be undone. Neither heaven nor earth could overturn this truth. It was established from the beginning.

The soul has within it both the possibility of life and the possibility of ruin.

What grows depends on what is nurtured.

And a voice spoke clearly:

“Whoever desires life, take these words and store them deep within the heart.”

For this is not knowledge meant to remain outside us.  
It must be received inwardly.

It must take root.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision brings the focus inward, away from external forces and back to the responsibility of the soul.

Every person carries within them an awareness of good and evil. This is not merely intellectual—it is experiential. It shapes how we love, how we choose, and how we respond to God.

The image of the field emphasizes cultivation. The soul is not static. It is dynamic, capable of growth or decline depending on how it is tended.

The act of plowing and removing weeds represents intentional effort:
- self-examination  
- repentance  
- discipline  

These are not burdens, but necessary acts of care.

The permanence of this order—that neither heaven nor earth can change it—shows its importance. This is a foundational truth of human existence.

We are participants in our own formation.

#### Living This Vision

Think of your inner life as a field.

What is growing there right now?

Are there areas that feel overgrown, neglected, or hardened?

Begin gently.

You do not need to transform everything at once.  
Start with small acts of tending:
- noticing your thoughts  
- choosing kindness  
- removing one harmful pattern  

Be patient with the process.

Growth takes time.  
Change requires consistency.

And remember: you are not working alone. The same God who planted the field walks with you as you tend it.

Store what is true within your heart.  
Return to it often.

For the life you seek is already being planted within you.
