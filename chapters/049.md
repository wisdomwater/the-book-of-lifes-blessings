## 49 — The Softening Into Life

> *Nothing in you is meant to remain closed*  
> *Even now, something within you can soften*

I saw a quiet expanse  
like deep water at rest—

still,  
heavy,  
unmoving.

There was a thickness to it,  
as though something  
had settled over time,  
layer upon layer,  
until no light  
seemed to pass through.

And within this stillness,  
I sensed a kind of watching—

not active,  
not searching,  
but present.

Aware.

Yet untouched  
by what it beheld.

Nothing reached outward.  
Nothing received.

It simply remained—  
held within itself,  
unmoved.

But then,  
very gently,  
I noticed a change—

not from above,  
not from outside,  
but from within.

A warmth,  
almost imperceptible,  
began to gather.

Like the first hint of dawn  
beneath the horizon.

It did not force its way in.

It waited.

And where it touched,  
something long held  
began to loosen—

not all at once,  
not completely—

but enough  
for a small opening  
to form.

And through that opening,  
light entered.

Soft.  
Steady.  
Alive.

The stillness remained—  
but it was no longer closed.

It was becoming  
receptive again.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
something in you  
feels distant—

not in turmoil,  
not in motion,  
but quiet  
in a way that feels  
unreachable.

You may notice  
a reluctance to feel,  
a hesitancy to open,  
a sense of holding  
something in place.

It often comes  
so gradually  
you hardly see it forming.

And yet,  
beneath even that,  
there is still  
a living awareness—

something that has not left,  
something that still sees,  
still waits.

You do not have to  
break through it.

You do not have to  
force yourself open.

But you may notice  
a small willingness  
beginning to return—

a softening,  
a curiosity,  
a readiness  
to feel just a little more.

That is enough.

Life does not rush you.

It meets you  
where you are  
and gently invites you  
back into movement,  
back into connection,  
back into light.
