## Day 198 - The Joys Beyond Speech

> *No human tongue can fully describe the joy prepared for those who choose God. Yet even a veiled glimpse can strengthen the heart toward faithfulness.*

Whoever wishes to avoid the torments of darkness must flee from the devil and reject his suggestions.

Let the soul receive faith from the fountain brought by the One who came without sin. Let faith be guarded by just works, so that the soul may reach the joys prepared for those who choose God.

Yet as no human language can fully explain these joys, no human knowledge can fully describe the miseries of separation from God.

Then I saw an immense radiance.

Its brilliance was so intense that I could not look upon it directly, nor could I clearly behold what was within it. I saw only as through a mirror.

Yet I knew that within this brightness there was every kind of flourishing beauty.

There was the soft perfume of many aromas.

There were many delights.

And there were the souls of the blessed—those who, while living, moved God with just sighs and worshiped Him with righteous works.

These souls rejoiced in sweetness.

Some I saw as through a mirror. They all wore dazzling garments.

Some had crowns bright as the dawn, and shoes whiter than snow.

Others wore crowns like gold, and their shoes gleamed like emeralds.

Many other adornments were there, but they were hidden from my sight and understanding.

All these blessed souls had rejected the devil through faith and brought faith to perfection.

Some did this through worthy repentance.

Others did it through good works.

Now they rested in the radiance and rejoiced in its delight.

Because they had turned from sin and desired the commandments of God, they were clothed again in the white garment that Adam lost.

Those who returned by repentance, even late in life, wore the crown like dawn and shoes white as snow.

Those who lived in the world without forgetting God, keeping His commandments in their hearts, wore crowns like gold and shoes shining like emeralds.

And I understood that God remembers every faithful turning.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision gives a veiled glimpse of blessedness.

Hildegard cannot fully see or explain it. The joy of God is too bright for human comprehension. Yet even through the mirror, she perceives beauty, fragrance, delight, rest, and radiance.

The garments matter. The white robe is the innocence humanity lost and God restores. The crown of dawn belongs to those who returned through repentance. The golden crown belongs to those who remained faithful in ordinary life. The shoes suggest the path each soul walked toward God.

There are different stories, but one joy.

Some return late. Some remain steady. Both are received into light.

#### Living This Vision

This reading is full of hope.

It says that repentance is not too late.

It also says that ordinary faithfulness matters.

A life hidden in the world, quietly keeping God’s commandments, is seen. A soul that stumbles and returns with tears is also seen.

The joys of God are beyond speech, but not beyond desire.

Let this vision awaken longing.

Walk toward the light you cannot yet fully see.
