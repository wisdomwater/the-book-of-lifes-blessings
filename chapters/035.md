## 35 - From Shadow into Full Light

> *What feels unfinished is still becoming. The light you see is already leading you home.*

I saw a soft horizon where light was slowly rising across the earth. It did not burst forth suddenly or overwhelm the landscape with brilliance. Instead, it unfolded with steady gentleness, like morning gradually awakening the world from sleep. In the distance were forms half-veiled in mist—shapes not hidden in darkness, but waiting patiently to be revealed in their proper time.

As the light continued to grow, what had once seemed distant and separate began quietly drawing together. Edges softened. Spaces narrowed. The world did not become something new; rather, what had always been there slowly emerged into clarity. The radiance revealed connection where the eye had once perceived division.

And I saw souls resting within this widening light.

There was peace within them—a deep unburdening, free from fear or striving. Yet even within their peace remained a gentle longing, like eyes not yet fully opened to the fullness of day. They lacked nothing essential, and still something within them leaned toward a deeper union, a more complete seeing.

The longing did not feel painful.

It felt holy.

Like rivers moving naturally toward the sea or flowers turning instinctively toward the sun.

And through all of it I sensed a quiet promise woven into the fabric of creation itself—that nothing true would remain divided forever. What had once been known only in fragments would one day be known in fullness. The light already present within all things would eventually be seen without veil, without distance, without confusion.

And in that seeing, everything would belong.

Nothing abandoned.

Nothing outside.

Only wholeness—clear, alive, and complete within the fullness of divine love.

**Living the Vision**

There are many moments in life when we experience only partial understanding. We sense something true moving beneath the surface of our experience, yet cannot fully name it. Certain parts of ourselves remain difficult to see clearly. Relationships feel unfinished. Longings remain unresolved. The heart often lives at the edge of a horizon it cannot yet fully reach.

And this incompleteness can feel unsettling.

Many people quietly believe they must force themselves into certainty before they can rest. Yet the spiritual life rarely unfolds through total clarity all at once. More often it grows gradually, like dawn slowly revealing a landscape hidden moments before in shadow and mist.

Even within uncertainty, a deeper light remains present.

Grace often guides the soul gently rather than dramatically. A small intuition. A quiet moment of peace. A subtle sense of direction that emerges without full explanation. These are not signs that you are outside of wholeness. They are signs that you are already within its unfolding.

Something within you is already deeply held by life and by God, even while other parts are still awakening into visibility. The soul does not become worthy only after reaching perfect understanding. It belongs even now, in the middle of becoming.

This allows us to live more gently with ourselves.

We do not need to rush ahead of our own unfolding. We can remain present within the in-between spaces of life, trusting that clarity ripens in its own time. And often when greater understanding finally arrives, it feels less like discovering something entirely new and more like remembering something ancient and familiar—as though the truth had quietly accompanied us all along.

The light that calls you forward is already surrounding you.

And slowly, patiently, it is leading you home.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Lead me into fullness

**Exhale:** I trust what is becoming
