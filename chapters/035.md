## Day 35 – The Multitude of the Living

> *Those who have lived in holiness are not lost—they dwell in a life that cannot be counted.*

Within the vast cloud, I saw a great multitude.

They were not dim or distant.

They were alive.

Their presence filled the space with a quiet radiance, as though life itself moved through them without obstruction.

They could not be counted.

Their number stretched beyond all reckoning.

Only God knew them fully.

And I understood—these were the souls of the holy.

They had walked the path of goodness.  
They had lived in faith.  
And now they dwelt in a place prepared for them.

Not a place of emptiness,  
but of fullness.

Their lives had not ended.

They had opened.

They moved in joy.

A joy not fragile, not fading—  
but steady.

Unbroken.

And yet, I heard their voices.

They rose together like the sound of many waters—  
deep, full, and unified.

Their praise was not scattered.  
It was one sound.

One will.

One breath.

They remembered.

They spoke of the life they had once lived in the body—  
a life given by God.

Though that body had returned to dust,  
they did not see it as lost.

They longed for it.

Not out of lack—  
but out of hope.

For they knew that one day,  
they would be made whole again.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the continuity of life beyond death.

The souls of the saints are not inactive or diminished. They are fully alive, dwelling in a state of joy and unity. Their countless number reflects the abundance of those who have participated in divine life.

Their unified voice symbolizes harmony—a shared alignment with truth and love.

Their longing to be reunited with their bodies points to a deeper completeness. Human existence is not meant to be divided. The body is not discarded, but destined for restoration.

This vision affirms that death is not an end, but a transition into a fuller reality, with the promise of eventual wholeness.

#### Living This Vision

It is easy to think of life as fragile.

As something that slips away.

But this vision invites you to see differently.

What is lived in truth is not lost.

What is rooted in love continues.

Even beyond what you can see.

And your life now matters in that continuity.

The choices you make.  
The way you love.  
The faith you carry.

All of it is shaping something that endures.

You are not moving toward disappearance.

You are moving toward fullness.
