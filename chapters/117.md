## Day 117 - The Bed of Burning Hay

> *I saw a figure reclining as though at ease, yet everything near it dried and burned. It spoke of pleasure as freedom, but Chastity answered with cool dew and living rest.*

Then I saw the seventh image.

It had an almost feminine shape.  
It lay stretched out on its right side,  
drawing up its legs  
like one resting in bed.

Its hair flamed like fire.  
Its eyes were white as chalk.  
On its feet were white sandals,  
so smooth and slippery  
that it could neither stand nor walk in them.

From its mouth came poisonous breath and drool.

At its right breast  
it suckled a small creature like a dog.  
At its left,  
a thing like a viper.

With its hands  
it tore flowers and grass from trees and meadows  
and lifted them to its nose.

It wore no garment.  
Everything beside it was scorched and dried  
like hay in burning heat.

And it said:

“I will wrap the image of God in filth.  
This offends God,  
and so I will ruin everything.

I am glorious,  
and I dwell in heights.  
I take what is permitted,  
for this is my nature.

Why should I abstain?  
Why should I deny myself  
the pleasures of a cheerful life  
and a bright spirit?

If I only fulfill a small part  
of what I desire,  
am I guilty?

And if I fulfill what my flesh requires,  
then what?  
Heaven has its justice,  
the earth has its concerns.

If the flesh were displeasing to God,  
would He have made it capable of these things?”

Then Chastity answered from the storm-cloud,  
as if from a royal diadem:

“I am not idle like you,  
you filth who forever play with lust.

I do not lie in that bed of dishonor  
where you spread yourself out.

From my pure mouth  
do not come poisonous words  
teaching shamelessness.

I drink instead  
from the soft dew in the well of blessing,  
and all my works rest fresh with God.

I stand in the sun  
and look upon the King of kings,  
for I gladly do what is good.

I do not desire the scorpion’s tail  
that wounds with filth.

I have the joy of honesty  
and the harmony of a pleasant life.

You are the ravenous womb of the serpent,  
grown from what was whispered  
into the ear of Adam and Eve  
when obedience faded.

But I have my origin  
in the supreme Word of the Father.

Heaven and earth  
will be shaken  
when they see you stripped bare  
in your shame.”

#### What This Vision Means

This vision portrays lust as a false ease that consumes what it touches. The reclining posture suggests indulgence, self-abandonment, and a life bent toward sensation. Yet nothing near it thrives. The flowers it handles are plucked and used, not cherished. Everything at its side becomes dry like hay.

Its white eyes and slippery sandals are haunting images. Lust may seem bright, but it cannot see clearly or walk steadily. It promises freedom while making the soul unstable. Its poisoned breath shows how desire, when severed from reverence, corrupts what it speaks and what it loves.

Chastity answers not with coldness but with freshness. Dew, blessing, rest, sunlight, harmony—her life is not deadened. She shows that purity is not the rejection of delight, but its healing. Chastity preserves what lust consumes. It keeps desire ordered toward love, honesty, and union with God.

#### Living This Vision

This vision does not condemn the body.  
It warns against desire that forgets its purpose.

Lust takes what is living  
and turns it into appetite alone.  
It uses.  
It rushes.  
It burns through tenderness.

The question is not whether desire exists.  
It does.

The question is whether desire remains joined  
to honesty, reverence, and love.

Chastity is often misunderstood.  
It is not sterility.  
It is not fear of beauty.  
It is the quiet strength  
that protects beauty from becoming consumption.

In daily life, chastity may mean restraint of the eyes,  
cleaner imagination,  
truer speech,  
or refusing to turn another person  
into material for fantasy.

What lust dries out,  
chastity keeps fresh.

Ask for dew.  
Ask for a cooled heart.  
Ask for the kind of purity  
that makes love more real, not less.
