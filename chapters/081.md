## Day 81 — The Books Within the Wings

> *Reason, when held in God, becomes a light that discerns and reveals.*

And I saw that within each wing there was a book.

And I understood:

Reason itself lives within the shelter of God’s power.

It is not separate from Him.  
It is given by Him—  
to discern, to order, to understand.

There is nothing that reason cannot examine  
when it is aligned with His will.

In the left wing, I saw two pages.

One was green—  
alive, growing, full of promise.

The other was silver—  
clear, reflective, pure.

And I understood:

In the ancient law, reason revealed the seeds of what was to come.

Like the earth bringing forth new life,  
the commands of God planted future righteousness.

The green page spoke of Noah—  
obedient, entering the ark,  
carried through water that destroys and renews.

So too, the waters of baptism  
bring life where there was death.

The silver page spoke of the law written on stone—  
given to hearts that were still hard.

Yet later, those same words  
would be written upon living hearts,  
softened and made receptive.

Then I looked to the right wing.

Again, two pages.

One sapphire,  
one gold.

And I understood:

Here reason reveals the fullness of the Son of God.

The sapphire shone with purity—  
the mystery of the virgin birth,  
the call to a life set apart.

The gold radiated divine glory—  
the eternal Word,  
existing before all things,  
creating all things,  
unchanged even in becoming flesh.

He was not diminished.  
He was revealed.

And those who look toward Him with a pure heart  
begin to see.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the role of reason in the spiritual life—not as an independent force, but as something held within divine protection.

Reason is not opposed to faith. When rightly ordered, it serves faith. It helps us perceive patterns, understand revelation, and recognize truth.

The two wings represent two movements of understanding.

The left shows preparation—symbols, laws, and events that point forward.  
The right shows fulfillment—truth revealed clearly in Christ.

The colors deepen the meaning:

Green speaks of growth and promise.  
Silver of clarity and reflection.  
Sapphire of purity and mystery.  
Gold of divine reality and fullness.

Together, they show that understanding unfolds over time. What begins as symbol becomes substance.

Reason helps us see this unfolding—but only when it remains anchored in God.

#### Living This Vision

You are invited to use your mind—  
but not to rely on it alone.

Let your thinking be guided by humility.  
Let it remain open to mystery.

When you encounter something you do not understand,  
do not rush to resolve it.

Sit with it.  
Let it deepen.

Ask for insight—not just information.

At the same time, do not neglect what has already been shown.

Live what you know.

Reason becomes clearer  
when it is joined with practice.

As you live truth,  
your understanding of it grows.

And slowly, what once seemed distant or symbolic  
becomes real—  
like gold revealed beneath the surface.
