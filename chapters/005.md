## Day 5 — The Mask of Empty Joy

> *Not all happiness leads the soul toward life.
> Some laughter hides a heart wandering in vanity.*

In the vision I saw a third image.

This figure also resembled a man, yet something about him was distorted. His nose was twisted. His hands were shaped like the claws of a bear. His feet were like those of a goat. His hair was black, and he wore pale garments that seemed strangely lifeless upon him.

Then the figure spoke.

“Better to rejoice than to be sad. Joy is no crime.

All who know God rejoice and sing. Heaven itself delights in creation. Why should I not be joyful as well?

If I only brought sorrow to others, they would flee from me. So I will not live that way. Instead, I will devote myself to many kinds of pleasures so that everyone may rejoice with me.

God created the air that carries pleasant sounds to my ears. He made flowers that delight my sight. Why should I not enjoy them?

Even humans find pleasure in animals, and animals in humans. Such enjoyment is natural. And so it should be.”

The words sounded cheerful and reasonable.

But again I heard a voice answering from the stormy cloud.

“You are an idolater who follows only your own will.

You are like a hollow sound struck by human hands—noise without life. Your desires are both human and animal, and your habits follow the same path.

All your actions belong to mortal things that fade away. They are not the works of living light but of death. You wander through shifting paths of vanity, chasing whatever pleases you in the moment.

But I am ashamed of such emptiness.

I shelter myself beneath the wings of the Cherubim. I learn the mysteries of God through divine wisdom and sacred order. Heavenly life fills me.

My eyes look with innocence, and my ways seek honesty according to the will of God.

But you flee from that will.

You wander in blindness, not knowing the path you have left behind.”

#### What This Vision Means

The third figure represents **vain amusement**—a joy that has lost its center in truth.

Unlike the previous image of restless joking, this figure appears almost philosophical. It defends pleasure with appealing arguments. It points to the beauty of creation, the delight of music, and the enjoyment of companionship.

Yet the creature’s body reveals a deeper distortion. Its mixed human and animal features symbolize a divided soul—partly rational, partly driven by instinct and impulse.

The voice of Modesty exposes the hidden problem: this form of joy worships the self.

It places personal pleasure at the center of life. Even the beauty of creation becomes merely a tool for entertainment. Instead of receiving creation as a gift that points toward God, the soul turns it into an idol that serves its own desires.

Modesty represents a different posture. It does not reject joy, but it receives joy with humility and reverence. It lives under the “wings of the Cherubim,” meaning it remains close to divine wisdom and order.

In this way joy becomes something sacred rather than self-centered.

#### Living This Vision

There is a subtle difference between **joy** and **distraction**.

Joy connects us more deeply to life. It awakens gratitude. It expands the heart.

Distraction does the opposite. It keeps us moving from one pleasure to another without ever letting us rest in meaning.

The figure in the vision mistakes one for the other.

This temptation is familiar. When life becomes difficult or uncertain, it is easy to fill our time with endless stimulation—music, noise, entertainment, or activity. None of these are wrong by themselves.

But when pleasure becomes our guide, the soul slowly loses its direction.

Modesty offers another path.

It invites us to enjoy the beauty of life while remaining anchored in something deeper. Gratitude replaces entitlement. Wonder replaces restless consumption.

In this way, joy becomes more than entertainment.

It becomes communion.
