## 5 — The Joy That Listens

> *Not every brightness is alive*  
> *True joy listens before it speaks*

A gentle brightness shimmered across the open field.

Soft sounds drifted through the air—  
laughter, music,  
the echo of delight  
touching everything lightly.

A figure moved within it,  
graceful in motion,  
drawn from one pleasure  
to the next.

Color caught its attention.  
Sound held it for a moment.  
Then it turned again,  
following whatever spark appeared.

Nothing harsh.  
Nothing heavy.

Only a constant reaching  
for what felt good,  
what felt easy,  
what felt alive.

And yet—

something beneath it all  
remained untouched.

Like a cup never filled,  
only brushed at the edges.

Then, almost unnoticed,  
the light shifted.

Not fading—  
but deepening.

The movement slowed.

The sounds softened.

And in the quiet that followed,  
another presence became known—

not outside,  
but within.

Still.  
Clear.  
Unhurried.

It did not call attention to itself.  
It did not compete.

It simply remained.

And as the figure grew still,  
the brightness it once chased  
began to gather  
from within—

no longer scattered,  
no longer fleeting.

A joy that did not need to be found.

A joy that could be received.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when joy feels like something to pursue.

One more experience.  
One more distraction.  
One more movement  
toward what feels good.

You may notice how natural this is—  
how easily the day fills  
with small pleasures  
stacked one upon another.

And still,  
something in you  
remains unsatisfied.

Not empty—  
but quietly searching.

There is another kind of joy  
that does not rush to meet you.

It waits.

You may sense it  
in the spaces you usually pass by—  
a pause,  
a breath,  
a moment without noise.

At first,  
it may feel like less.

Less stimulation.  
Less excitement.

But if you remain,  
even gently,  
you may begin to feel  
a different fullness rising.

Not dependent on what you take in—  
but flowing from what is already here.

This joy does not ask you to gather more.

It invites you  
to receive more deeply.
