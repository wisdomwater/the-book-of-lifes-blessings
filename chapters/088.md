## Day 88 — The Longing for Full Sight

> *Even the holy still wait for the day when nothing will veil the face of God.*

And I saw that these souls,  
though filled with joy,  
still longed.

They looked toward God and asked:

“How long must we wait?

When will we be restored—  
body and soul together—  
so that we may see fully,  
without veil?”

Their works shone before God—  
clear, radiant, complete.

And yet, something remained unfinished.

For while they rested in Him,  
they had not yet received the fullness.

And I understood:

The soul alone,  
though alive and at peace,  
still awaits its union with the body.

For together they had labored.  
Together they had endured.  
Together they had done what was good.

And so together  
they will receive the fullness of joy.

Then came the answer:

The time is not yet.

The world must be shaken.  
The old serpent must be broken.  
All things must be gathered.

Then—  
the body and soul will be restored.

Then—  
they will see the face of God  
without any veil.

And their longing was like that of a child  
crying out for bread—  
certain that the father will give it.

They waited with desire,  
but also with trust.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals that even in blessedness, there is still a future fulfillment.

The saints are not lacking peace, but they are awaiting completion. Their longing is not born of absence, but of anticipation.

The union of body and soul is essential. Human life is not meant to be purely spiritual or purely physical. It is a unity—and its fulfillment must also be a unity.

The image of seeing God “without veil” points to direct, unmediated encounter. What is now glimpsed in part will then be fully known.

The child imagery is powerful. Their longing is not anxious—it is trusting. They know the promise will be fulfilled.

This vision invites patience. Even what is already good is not yet complete.

#### Living This Vision

There are places in your life  
where something feels unfinished.

A longing not yet fulfilled.  
A clarity not yet reached.

This vision invites you  
to hold that longing differently.

Not as frustration,  
but as expectation.

You are allowed to desire more—  
more clarity,  
more wholeness,  
more direct experience of what is true.

But you are also invited to trust the timing.

Like a child waiting for bread,  
you can hold both hunger and confidence.

You are not forgotten.

What is incomplete now  
is not lost.

It is moving toward fulfillment.

Stay open.  
Stay faithful.  
Stay patient.
