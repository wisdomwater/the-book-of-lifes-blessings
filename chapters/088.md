## 88 - The Deep Held in Peace

> *Even the depths are gently ordered. What feels vast is still within care.*

I saw a great expanse hidden beneath the earth—a vast depth stretching farther than sight could follow. It was unseen by those walking above it, yet it carried immense quiet strength, like a deep basin holding everything resting upon the land. There was nothing chaotic within it. The waters moved slowly through hidden channels, rising and settling in steady rhythms that did not break their bounds.

A soft breath moved across the deep.

It could not be seen directly, yet its presence was unmistakable—like warmth passing gently through stillness. And wherever this breath touched the waters, they stirred with life. Streams began flowing outward across the land, nourishing dry places and bringing freshness to what had begun to fade beneath the heat of the day.

Then, just as gently, the waters returned again.

Nothing was wasted. Nothing overflowed into ruin. The deep received what flowed outward and gathered it back into itself without strain. All things moved within a quiet measure, held by a wisdom larger than the waters themselves.

And above it all remained a steady presence—calm, unshaken, effortlessly holding both the surface and the hidden depths beneath it. The deep did not resist this holding. It rested within it.

And I understood then that what lies beneath the surface of life is not forgotten.

Even the hidden places are carried within a greater peace.

### Living the Vision

There are depths within every person that cannot easily be explained. Emotions rise unexpectedly. Old memories surface without warning. Longings, fears, griefs, and questions move through the soul like unseen currents beneath water. Sometimes these inner movements feel overwhelming simply because they are difficult to name or fully understand.

And when the depths become active, the mind often reacts with fear.

We may try to control what we feel, suppress what unsettles us, or distance ourselves from anything that seems too large to manage. Yet much suffering comes not from having deep emotions, but from believing those emotions exist outside the reach of care.

The contemplative life offers another possibility.

Instead of treating the depths as enemies, we begin learning how to remain gently present with them. Not every feeling requires immediate resolution. Not every inner current must be mastered before peace can exist. Often the deeper work is simply learning to trust that something within us—and beyond us—is already capable of holding what we fear may overwhelm us.

There is a quiet steadiness beneath the turbulence of the mind.

We glimpse it in moments when breath returns after anxiety tightens the body. We notice it when sorrow softens enough to let tenderness appear beside it. We sense it whenever we discover that we are still being carried, even in seasons we thought might undo us.

Over time, the soul learns that the depths themselves are not signs of failure.

They are part of being fully human.

And when held with patience, honesty, and compassion, even the deeper places within us can begin serving life rather than frightening us. Like hidden waters nourishing the earth, they flow outward and return again within a rhythm far wiser than control alone.

Nothing within you is beyond care.

Not even the depths.

### Breath Prayer

**Inhale:** Breathe through my depths
