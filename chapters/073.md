## 73 - The Steady Voice

> *What is true does not need to shift*  
> *The soul stands firm where words are clear*

I saw an open space  
held in quiet stillness.

The ground was firm—  
not hardened,  
but steady,  
able to carry what stood upon it.

The air moved gently,  
a soft wind passing through—  
touching everything  
without disturbing it.

In the center,  
a single tone began to rise.

Clear.  
Unbroken.

It did not change  
to match the wind.  
It did not scatter  
into many sounds.

It remained.

And as it continued,  
the space around it  
seemed to settle—  
as if everything  
recognized its place  
in relation to it.

There was no strain  
in this sound.

No effort to persuade.

Only a quiet clarity  
that held its ground  
without resistance.

The wind still moved,  
but it no longer shaped the voice.

Instead,  
the voice gave shape  
to the space.

And I saw  
that what was steady  
could be trusted—

not because it was loud,  
but because it did not waver.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you feel the pull  
to adjust your words—

to soften,  
to shape,  
to guide how you are seen.

It can happen quickly—  
almost without noticing.

A phrase changes.  
Something is left unsaid.

And while it may seem small,  
you may feel  
a subtle shift within—  
a loss of grounding,  
a quiet unease.

There is another way  
that does not require force.

You may begin  
by speaking simply—  
just enough  
to be true.

Not everything needs  
to be explained.

Not everything needs  
to be protected.

You can allow your words  
to rest  
in what is real.

And in doing so,  
something steadies within you.

You may feel  
less divided,  
less pulled  
in different directions.

Truth does not demand  
perfection.

Only presence.

And as you remain with it,  
your life begins to take on  
a quiet clarity—  
like a voice  
that holds its tone  
no matter how the winds move.
