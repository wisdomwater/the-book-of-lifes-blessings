## Day 167 - From Fire to Darkness

> *Discord scatters what was meant to be united. It burns, divides, and finally leaves the soul in confusion and shame.*

After this, I saw other evil spirits shouting in a great uproar:

“Lucifer is our lord! As long as we are with him, we will not fall!”

And they taught human beings to embrace discord, to turn away from harmony, and to reject the concord of virtue.

Then I saw a great fire.

Near it lay thick darkness.

Within both were dreadful worms, and many evil spirits moved among them.

There the souls were punished—those who, while in the body, had abandoned unity and joined themselves to discord.

They were driven from fire to darkness, and from darkness back to fire, without rest.

They burned in the fire because they had stirred up conflict and harm. They were tormented in the darkness because they had led others into error. They were pierced by worms because of their cruelty. And evil spirits forced them to wander back and forth, as their discord had caused many to wander and fall.

And I saw and understood these things.

Then the living light spoke again:

“These punishments are true. Those who wish to avoid them must turn away from discord and discipline themselves, rejecting what feeds division.”

And I saw how discord works.

It whispers like a serpent.

It mocks wisdom but refuses to follow it.

It criticizes, distorts, and tears down what is good.

It spreads anger and delights in ruin.

It takes what is sacred and exposes it to scorn, as though casting holy vessels into the streets.

It deceives, confuses, and leaves everything in disorder.

And those who cling to it without repentance fall into destruction.

But those who turn back, even if they have been caught in its snare, are not rejected by God. They are raised again through repentance.

#### What This Vision Means

Discord is more than disagreement. It is the breaking of relationship.

It feeds on division, suspicion, and pride. It distorts truth and delights in tearing down what is good. In Hildegard’s vision, it moves between fire and darkness—between visible conflict and hidden confusion.

Fire represents the outward harm: arguments, cruelty, destruction.

Darkness represents the inward harm: blindness, confusion, and loss of truth.

The constant movement between the two shows how discord traps the soul in cycles it cannot escape on its own.

Yet even here, grace remains. Repentance is still possible.

#### Living This Vision

Discord is easy to enter and difficult to leave.

It begins in small ways—harsh words, silent resentment, the desire to be right at the cost of love.

Left unchecked, it grows.

This vision invites us to become people of peace—not by avoiding truth, but by holding it with humility and care.

Where am I feeding division?

Where can I choose reconciliation instead?

Peace is not passive. It is a deliberate turning toward unity, again and again.
