## 167 - The Returning Harmony

> *What has been scattered can be gathered*  
> *What listens deeply begins to come home*

I saw a wide valley  
held in a quiet light.

At first, the air was unsettled—  
currents moving in different directions,  
crossing,  
pulling,  
never resting.

The grasses bent one way, then another,  
as if unsure  
which voice to follow.

And the sound there  
was thin and strained—  
not loud,  
but restless.

Then,  
a deeper stillness began to form.

Not imposed,  
but rising gently  
from beneath all movement.

Like a low note  
sounding through the valley.

The winds did not vanish.

They began to align.

One by one,  
their motion softened—  
not forced into silence,  
but drawn into a shared rhythm.

The grasses lifted.

The air cleared.

And what had felt scattered  
became whole again—  
not by losing difference,  
but by resting  
in a deeper unity.

The valley did not strive to hold itself together.

It simply yielded  
to the harmony already present.

And I understood:

There is a peace  
that does not erase what is within us—  
but gathers it  
into a quiet belonging.

#### Living the Vision

Something within you  
pulls in different directions.

A thought here.  
A reaction there.  
A tension that rises  
before you have named it.

You may feel the subtle strain—  
the sense of being divided,  
even within yourself.

And often, the impulse  
is to push one part away,  
to quiet it quickly,  
to restore control.

But there is another way  
that does not begin with force.

You may notice  
a quieter invitation—  
to listen  
before choosing.

To remain present  
as the movement unfolds,  
without immediately taking sides.

In that gentle attention,  
something begins to shift.

Not everything resolves at once.

But the urgency softens.

A deeper steadiness  
starts to gather what was scattered.

You may feel it  
as a kind of inner alignment—  
not perfect,  
but real.

And from there,  
your words, your responses,  
your presence  
begin to carry a different tone.

Less sharp.  
More whole.

Not because you have made yourself peaceful,  
but because you have allowed  
peace  
to find you.
