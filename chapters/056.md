## Day 56 – Remembered in Light

> *I saw that some souls were held in remembrance, while others faded into forgetfulness.*

Then I understood something subtle and weighty.

Those who were held in the memory of holy eternity were drawn upward through their purification. Their cleansing led to relief, to light, to rest.

But others…

Those who were forgotten remained bound.

They lingered in their suffering, not because mercy was absent, but because they were not turned toward it. They were not remembered in the same way—neither by themselves, nor by the living, nor in the orientation of their own hearts.

And I saw that remembrance is not merely recall.

It is belonging.

To be remembered in eternity is to be aligned with it—to be oriented toward God, even if imperfectly.

But to live in forgetfulness is to drift, to remain turned away, to lose the thread that leads back to life.

#### What This Vision Means

Memory here is spiritual alignment.

To be “remembered” is not about being known intellectually—it is about being held within the life of God. It reflects a soul that, however imperfectly, has turned toward truth, toward love, toward eternity.

Forgetfulness, by contrast, is a state of disconnection.

It is the gradual loss of awareness of God, of purpose, of the deeper reality that gives life meaning. It is not imposed—it is chosen, often subtly, through neglect.

This vision emphasizes the importance of orientation.

Where the soul turns determines what it experiences.

Remembrance leads to restoration.

Forgetfulness prolongs separation.

#### Living This Vision

There is a quiet question beneath this vision:

What am I remembering?

Not in your mind—but in your life.

What are you returning to, again and again?  
What shapes your attention?  
What holds your loyalty?

These are the things that form your remembrance.

You are always becoming oriented toward something.

The invitation is gentle:

Return to what is true.  
Return to what gives life.  
Return to God—not just once, but daily.

Because remembrance is not a single act.

It is a way of living.
