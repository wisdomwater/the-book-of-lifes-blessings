## 56 — Held in Living Remembrance

> *You are not lost to what is eternal*  
> *Something in you is always being held*

I saw a quiet expanse  
filled with a soft, steady light—

not distant,  
not unreachable,  
but near  
in a way that felt like home.

Within it,  
there was a gentle gathering—

like threads being drawn together,  
like scattered things  
remembering where they belong.

Some moved easily,  
as though already turned  
toward this light.

They were lifted  
not by force,  
but by a natural nearness—  
as if they had always  
been leaning this way.

Others seemed to drift—  
not cast away,  
not forgotten—  
but turned slightly aside,  
their movement slower,  
their path less clear.

And yet,  
even here,  
the light remained.

It did not withdraw.  
It did not close.

It simply waited—  
present,  
faithful,  
holding all things  
within its quiet reach.

And I felt a deep truth  
beneath it all:

that to be remembered  
is not to be recalled,  
but to be held  
within what is living.

To belong  
to the light  
that does not forget.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you may feel  
slightly turned away—

not lost,  
but distant,  
as though your attention  
has drifted  
from what matters most.

You may notice  
how easily this happens—  
through small distractions,  
through the weight of daily life,  
through a quiet forgetting  
of what gives you life.

And yet,  
something in you  
still knows the way back.

You can feel it  
in a pause,  
in a breath,  
in a moment  
when you remember  
what is true.

This remembering  
is not effort.

It is a return.

A soft turning  
of your awareness  
toward what has never  
let go of you.

You are not required  
to hold yourself there perfectly.

You can come back  
again and again—  
each time  
being gathered,  
each time  
being held  
a little more deeply.
