## Day 102 – The Bitter Flame of Harshness

> *I saw a fire of many colors, and within it souls who had hardened their hearts against both God and neighbor.*

After this, I beheld another vision.

A vast fire appeared—  
black, red, and white flames twisting together in terrible motion.

From within it rose serpents of fire,  
their mouths pouring out burning breath.

In this place were the souls of those who, in their earthly life, had given themselves to harshness.  
Their words had wounded.  
Their hearts had become rigid and unyielding.

The black flame burned them for the unbelief hidden deep within.  
The red flame consumed them for the hardness of their hearts.  
The white flame struck them for their deception and scorn.

And the serpents tormented them—  
for in their speech and actions, they had placed bitterness like a barrier  
against both divine order and human affection.

I heard again the voice from the living light:  
“What you see is true.  
Those who oppose God and others through harshness must turn from it.  
Let them humble themselves through fasting and prayer,  
according to the weight of their fault,  
for in their severity they have despised both love and truth.”

I saw that harshness rejects God.  
It refuses mercy.  
It grinds its teeth against goodness.

It does not act from love or reverence,  
but from its own will.

It abandons wisdom,  
rejects joy,  
and humiliates charity.

It calls falsehood truth,  
and truth falsehood.

Such a soul becomes bitter.  
It drinks from the cup of death,  
seeking control, dividing what is whole,  
even betraying those it once promised to defend.

It sows nothing that can grow.  
It gathers nothing that nourishes.

And yet, in its pain,  
it still cries out to God.

#### What This Vision Means

Harshness, in this vision, is more than anger. It is a settled disposition of the heart—a resistance to softness, to mercy, to being moved.

The three flames reveal its layers:

- Black: the inner unbelief that distrusts goodness  
- Red: the outward hardness that wounds others  
- White: the subtle deception that justifies cruelty  

The serpents symbolize the poisonous nature of words and actions that create distance, division, and injury.

At its root, harshness is a refusal of relationship. It rejects both divine compassion and human connection. It prefers control over love, certainty over humility.

Yet there is something deeply human here: even the harsh soul still cries out. Beneath the bitterness is pain. Beneath the rigidity is a wound that has not been softened.

The vision invites not condemnation, but transformation—a return to humility, where the heart can be softened again.

#### Living This Vision

Harshness can appear quietly.

In a sharp tone.  
A dismissive comment.  
An unwillingness to listen.

It often feels justified.  
Even necessary.

But over time, it builds walls.

To live this vision is to notice when the heart begins to harden.

When we feel the urge to withdraw compassion.  
To speak quickly instead of gently.  
To prove a point rather than preserve connection.

In those moments, there is a choice:

To press forward in harshness—  
or to pause.

Softness does not mean weakness.  
It means remaining open when it would be easier to close.

A gentle word.  
A moment of restraint.  
A willingness to see the other as human.

These are small acts—  
but they loosen the grip of bitterness.

And even when we fail,  
we can return.

The heart that once hardened  
can learn again to bend.
