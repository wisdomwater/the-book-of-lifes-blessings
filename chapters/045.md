## Day 45 – The Living God

> *What is made by human hands cannot give life—the soul is sustained only by the One who is life.*

Then I saw a turning.

A contrast.

Those who desired true happiness did not follow the fog.

They lifted their gaze.

They worshiped the living God.

The One who gives life—  
not only begins it,  
but sustains it.

He dwells beyond what can be seen,  
yet is closer than breath.

I heard the words:

“Our God is in heaven.  
He does whatever pleases Him.”

And I understood—this is not distance.

It is transcendence.

A life beyond limitation.

Not bound by human form or human making.

But then I saw the idols.

Formed by human hands.  
Shaped in silver and gold.

Polished.  
Adorned.

Yet empty.

They could not see.  
They could not hear.  
They could not speak.

They promised life—  
but had none to give.

And I saw the deeper truth:

When a person places their trust in what they themselves have made—  
their own ideas,  
their own creations—  
they become like those idols.

Disconnected from life.

Yet the living God remains.

Unfading.  
Uncreated.  
The source of all that lives.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision contrasts the living God with idols—both physical and internal.

God is described as life itself—unlimited, self-sustaining, and the source of all existence. Idols, by contrast, are lifeless, though they may appear valuable or beautiful.

The deeper meaning extends beyond physical idols. Anything we place ultimate trust in—our own understanding, achievements, or desires—can become an idol.

Such trust leads to disconnection from true life.

The vision reminds us that life flows from God alone. Everything else is derivative and cannot sustain the soul.

#### Living This Vision

What are you relying on?

Where do you look for stability?  
For meaning?  
For identity?

Some of these things are good.

But they are not ultimate.

When they become ultimate, they cannot hold the weight you place on them.

And they begin to fail.

This is not a failure of life.

It is an invitation.

To return.

To place your trust in what is truly alive.
