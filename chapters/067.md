## Day 67 - Under the Wings of Wisdom

> *Where sin has torn creation, divine wisdom does not abandon it. God enters the wound and makes a way back through love.*

Then the vision widened.

I saw that all things were made by God and ordered in wisdom before the ages. Nothing came forth outside his knowing. He made living creatures, ordered the elements, and brought his works into being with purpose and beauty.

Yet God did not will to enjoy glory alone.

He shared it with his creatures, gathering them as a mother bird gathers her young beneath her wings, so they might rejoice with him.

But the first angel fell.

And the first man fell.

And with the fall of man, the harmony of the elements was shaken. I saw creation itself wounded by sin. The death of Abel stained the earth. Blood entered the ground. Disorder touched what had been made for peace.

And the devil said within himself that he would work his will within the work of God, that through man he would achieve what he could not achieve by himself.

But the wisdom of God stood deeper than all rebellion.

God looked upon lost humanity and found a way to purify what had fallen. No creature could have imagined it. No creature could resist it.

I saw that his Son would be born of the Virgin.

And then I understood the mystery more deeply.

Against human sin, the Son of God entered human life. Against our excess, he accepted hunger. Against our temptation, he accepted weakness and weariness. Against our sorrowful disobedience, he took sadness upon himself. Against the insults and tyranny of the wicked, he bore mockery. Against murder and grave evil, he stretched himself upon the cross and entered death so that death might not keep us.

He took upon himself the sins of penitents who did not refuse him.

Therefore he is called the Angel of Good Counsel, just and merciful in every circumstance.

And though all sins are dangerous, murder was shown to me as evil among evils, because it strikes what bears the image of God. Yet even here, the pierced wounds of Christ were shown as a refuge for the truly repentant.

He alone suffered the Passion.

He alone redeemed fallen man.

And I understood that the final word over human ruin is not wrath, but wisdom joined to mercy.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision turns from judgment to redemption. It places all earlier warnings inside a larger story: creation, fall, disorder, and divine rescue.

The image of God sharing glory with creatures is tender and central. Creation was made for participation, not exclusion. Sin disrupts that shared joy, not only morally but cosmically. Hildegard sees the fall as something that touches the elements themselves. Evil is never merely private.

Yet the answer is not abandonment. Divine wisdom descends into the damage. The incarnation is presented as God’s deliberate answer to every human distortion. Christ does not save from a distance. He enters hunger, grief, insult, bodily suffering, and death itself.

The title “Angel of Good Counsel” emphasizes that Christ is not only sacrifice, but living wisdom—justice and mercy joined. His wounds become the place where even grave sin can meet repentance and not be finally lost.

The vision insists that no darkness has the final authority. God’s wisdom is deeper than the fall.


#### Living This Vision

There are times when your failures feel larger than repair. You may see what you have done, or what humanity has done, and feel only sorrow.

This vision asks you to look again.

Not away from sin, but beyond it.

The same God who sees the ruin also sees the remedy. The same God who witnesses blood on the earth does not withdraw his love. He moves toward the wound.

That means your repentance does not happen alone. It happens beneath the wings of divine mercy. Christ has already entered the places you fear most: shame, grief, exhaustion, even deathlike despair. There is no part of the human condition he refused to touch.

So bring him what is broken.

Bring him what you regret.

Bring him what still feels beyond forgiveness.

Wisdom has already made a way where you could not make one.

Your task is not to save yourself.

Your task is to turn and receive.
