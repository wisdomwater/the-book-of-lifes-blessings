## 67 - The Dew That Softens

> *You do not need to stay hardened. A small asking is enough to begin.*

I saw a wide stretch of earth lying beneath the pale light of dawn. The ground was dry and firm, holding itself tightly closed against everything around it. Nothing seemed able to enter it. No water moved through it. No seed stirred beneath its surface. It remained strong in its stillness—untouched, unaffected, and sealed within itself.

And yet it could not receive.

There was a quiet heaviness in that kind of strength, as though the earth had learned to protect itself so completely that it no longer knew how to welcome life. It endured, but it did not flourish. Beneath the hardened surface, something waited in silence.

Then, in the stillness of early morning, a softness began to appear.

Not rain falling in torrents. Not force breaking the ground apart. Only dew.

It settled gently across the earth without demand or urgency. Small drops rested lightly upon the surface, asking nothing of the soil except openness enough to receive them. At first, nothing seemed to change. The ground remained quiet and unmoving.

But slowly, almost imperceptibly, the earth began to soften.

Not collapsing. Not breaking apart in violence. Simply loosening enough for life to enter again. And where the ground opened even slightly, something hidden beneath the surface began to stir. Tiny movements awakened quietly within the waiting soil, as though life itself had been patiently waiting for this moment of tenderness.

And I saw then that the dew had always been near.

It had only been waiting for the earth to welcome it.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons when the heart quietly closes itself in order to survive. Not always through anger or bitterness, but through exhaustion, disappointment, fear, or the long habit of carrying everything alone. We become guarded without fully realizing it. Vulnerability feels dangerous. Need feels uncomfortable. And so we harden ourselves gently over time, believing this is what strength requires.

Yet beneath that hardness, something often remains thirsty.

The soul longs to be met, comforted, understood, and held. But when we remain tightly closed, even love and grace struggle to reach the places within us that most need healing. We may continue functioning outwardly while inwardly feeling dry and untouched by deeper life.

The beginning of softening is often very small.

Not dramatic emotion. Not perfect faith. Sometimes it begins with a single honest prayer whispered quietly within the heart: Help me. Acknowledging need does not weaken the soul. It opens space for nourishment to enter where self-sufficiency has grown too rigid.

This opening does not need to happen all at once.

Like dew upon dry ground, healing often arrives gently. A conversation. A moment of stillness. A tear finally allowed. A willingness to stop pretending we are unaffected by pain or loneliness. These small softenings create openings through which life begins moving again beneath the surface.

And slowly, what once felt closed becomes capable of receiving once more.

The soul remembers that tenderness is not weakness.

It is the beginning of restoration.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Soften what is closed

**Exhale:** I ask for Your help
