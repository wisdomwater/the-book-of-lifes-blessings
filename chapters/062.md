## 62 - The Turning Toward Life

> *What is gently tended begins to flourish*  
> *Attention is the doorway to light*

I saw a quiet landscape  
just after the passing of rain—

the air washed clean,  
the sky opening  
in soft layers of light.

Drops still rested  
on leaf and branch,  
catching the brightness  
without effort.

There was no urgency here.

Only a sense  
that something had shifted.

The ground, once unsettled,  
now held steady—  
ready to receive  
what would come next.

A path appeared,  
not carved sharply,  
but formed  
by small, repeated steps.

It moved gently forward,  
winding through open space,  
inviting rather than directing.

Along its edges,  
new growth had begun—

tender,  
almost unnoticed—

yet unmistakably alive.

I saw that nothing here  
had been forced.

Nothing rushed  
into fullness.

Each movement  
had been small,  
attended to,  
allowed.

And the light  
did not overwhelm—

it revealed.

It rested on each thing  
as it was,  
drawing it forward  
without demand.

In this place,  
there was no storm—

only the quiet  
after turning,  
the stillness  
where life begins again.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
your attention drifts—

when the day fills  
with small distractions,  
and what matters most  
fades into the background.

It can feel harmless,  
even restful.

But you may notice  
a quiet distance forming—  
a sense of being  
slightly removed  
from your own life.

And then,  
there are moments  
when something in you  
turns back.

Not all at once.

Just a small remembering.

You may pause.  
Take a breath.  
Return to what is here.

And in that simple turning,  
something begins to steady.

You do not need  
to recover everything.

You do not need  
to rush forward.

Just attend  
to what is near.

A single act of care.  
A moment of presence.  
A quiet yes  
to what is good.

This is how the soul  
comes back to life—

not through force,  
but through noticing,  
again and again,  
where the light rests.
