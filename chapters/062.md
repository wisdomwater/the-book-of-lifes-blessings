## Day 62 — Storms of Neglect

> *What is left unattended in the soul becomes a storm that overtakes it.*

I saw a great black cloud, heavy and full of motion. Fierce winds tore through it. Thunder roared. Rain fell in torrents.

But this was no ordinary storm.

Within the rain, fire fell like drops, burning as it struck.

And in the midst of this chaos were souls—overwhelmed, unsteady, unable to find rest.

They were driven about by the winds.

They were struck by the fire.

They had no shelter.

I understood that these were the souls of those who had lived in laziness—not merely of body, but of spirit.

They had not served with zeal.

They had not attended to what was good.

They had not listened to the quiet calls of justice, nor feared the loss of what matters most.

And so now, they were caught in the storm.

The winds that drove them reflected the aimlessness of their lives. The fire that struck them was the consequence of what they had ignored. The darkness surrounded them, for their inner light had grown dim.

Then the Living Light spoke:

“These things are true. But those who turn from this while they live—who discipline themselves, who care for others, who awaken their souls—these may be spared such suffering.”

And I saw:

Laziness darkens the soul.

It dulls awareness.

It quiets the voice that calls us toward life.

It is not rest—but neglect.


#### What This Vision Means

Laziness, in this vision, is not simply inactivity. It is a failure to engage with what is good, true, and life-giving.

It is the quiet drifting away from purpose.

The storm represents the inner chaos that forms when the soul is left unattended. Without intention, without care, without effort toward growth, the soul becomes vulnerable to forces it cannot control.

The fire in the rain is especially striking. What should nourish instead burns. What should sustain instead harms. This is what happens when life is lived without awareness—good things are misused, and their purpose is lost.

The darkness reflects a dimming of spiritual perception.

Yet again, the vision offers hope.

Through discipline, through care, through small acts of faithfulness, the soul can awaken.

Light can return.


#### Living This Vision

There is a kind of tiredness that needs rest.

And there is a kind that avoids life.

The second is more subtle.

It shows up as delay. As distraction. As putting off what you know matters.

This vision invites you to gently wake up.

Not with harshness—but with intention.

What small thing can you attend to today?

A moment of prayer.

An act of kindness.

A task you’ve been avoiding.

These are not insignificant.

They are the beginnings of movement.

You do not need to fix everything at once.

Just turn, even slightly, toward what is good.

The storm does not have to define you.

You can learn to stand within it—and even step out of it.
