## 62 - The Softening Flame

> *What has hardened can be warmed again. Mercy rises where the heart opens.*

I saw a fire moving in many tones across a quiet darkness. Some parts burned deep and dim, while others flashed bright and sharp, restless in their constant shifting. The flame did not know how to settle. It turned inward and outward at once, as though something within it longed for peace yet could not release its tension enough to receive it.

There was a tightening within the fire.

A holding.

The movement carried heat, but not rest. It seemed driven by an uneasiness hidden beneath its brightness, as though the flame feared what might happen if it stopped striving and simply remained still. Its sharp edges reached outward defensively, consuming more than they illuminated.

Then from beyond the fire, another warmth began to approach.

It was not stronger in force or intensity. It did not seek to overpower the flame or extinguish it. Instead, it remained steady and gentle, like a presence rooted in deep peace. Quietly and patiently it drew near, holding its warmth without aggression or withdrawal.

For a long while, nothing appeared to change.

And then, slowly, the edges of the fire began to soften.

The sharpness eased. The restless movement slowed. What had once burned with strain and tension gradually began glowing with a quieter warmth. The flame did not disappear. It became gentler, steadier, more capable of giving life rather than wounding what came near.

And within that softening, something opened.

Not all at once, but enough.

Enough for light to rest there.

Enough for the heat to become nourishing again.

And I saw then that the fire itself had never been meant for harm. Its deepest purpose was always warmth—to illuminate, protect, and give life when held within gentleness.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when the heart begins to tighten before we fully notice it. A reaction rises quickly. Judgment forms almost automatically. Words sharpen inside us before they are spoken aloud. Sometimes this hardness feels justified, especially when we have been hurt, exhausted, or afraid. The impulse to protect ourselves can easily disguise itself as strength.

Yet afterward, we often sense the cost.

Something closes within us. Warmth fades. Connection becomes more difficult to hold. Even when our reactions feel understandable, the soul quietly recognizes when it has drifted away from tenderness. This awareness is not meant to shame us. It is an invitation to return.

And return often begins very simply.

Not by forcing ourselves into artificial gentleness, but by pausing long enough to notice what is happening within us. A breath. A moment of silence. A willingness to delay the sharp response that first rises to the surface. These small acts create space where mercy can begin moving again.

Over time, we discover that softness is not weakness.

There is a deeper strength found in remaining open when it would be easier to harden. To listen longer. To speak more carefully. To resist the urge to wound simply because we ourselves feel wounded. Mercy grows through these quiet choices, slowly transforming the inner fire from something reactive into something life-giving.

And little by little, what once felt sharp becomes steady.

The heart learns how to carry warmth without burning others in the process.

### Breath Prayer

**Inhale:** Soften what is tight

**Exhale:** Let mercy flow
