## Day 159 - Thorns of Injustice

> *Injustice wounds the world, and the wound returns to the soul. What refuses fairness becomes a field of burning thorns.*

I heard evil spirits crying out in a loud voice:

“Lucifer will do whatever he desires, and we will do the same. He will do nothing else, and neither will we.”

These spirits whispered injustice into human hearts. They urged people to take from one another what rightly belonged to them.

Then I saw a ghastly place filled with burning thorns, thistles, and horrible worms. Evil spirits moved through that place with fiery whips. There they pursued the souls of those who, while living in the body, had persisted in injustice.

The souls were pierced by thorns and thistles because they had upheld injustice in their words and deeds. They were tormented by worms because they had carried a harsh and bitter spirit. They were lashed with fiery whips because they had shown respect to no one.

And I saw and understood.

Then I heard a voice from the Living Light:

“These punishments are true. Therefore, those who wish to overcome these spirits must reject injustice. Let them fast, discipline themselves, pray with fervor, and seek counsel for the healing of their souls.”

Those who love injustice do not want law. They flatter others when it serves them, but they do not listen to instruction. They make their own desires into law and stretch it as far as they please.

Injustice is like a night when the moon is darkened and the stars give no light. Nothing can be rightly distinguished.

It is like food cooked without flavor.

It lacks the sound of reason—the holy music by which the faithful soul praises its Creator.

For reason is like a trumpet. It calls creation back into praise.

The soul itself carries harmony like a hidden symphony. And when it remembers its homeland while living in exile, it often breaks open into tears.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows injustice as spiritual disorder.

It is not only unfair action. It is the refusal to recognize right order, right relationship, and the dignity of others. Injustice makes the self into its own law.

The burning thorns reflect the pain injustice causes. What pierces others eventually pierces the soul. The worms reveal corruption beneath harshness. The fiery whips show the violence returned to those who lived violently.

But the vision does not end with punishment. It turns toward purification.

The Living Light calls the soul to repentance through fasting, prayer, discipline, and counsel. These practices are not punishment for punishment’s sake. They are ways of retraining desire, humbling pride, and restoring justice within.

The final image is beautiful: reason as music.

A just soul becomes tuned again. It learns to praise. It hears the symphony of creation and longs for home.


#### Living This Vision

Injustice often begins when we stop listening.

We stop listening to conscience.
We stop listening to those we affect.
We stop listening to wisdom that corrects us.

Then our desires become law.

This vision invites you to ask:

Where have I taken more than I should?
Where have I dismissed another person’s dignity?
Where has my self-protection become unfairness?

Do not ask with despair.

Ask with willingness.

Justice begins when the soul becomes teachable again.

Let your life be tuned back toward praise. Let reason become a trumpet. Let humility restore the sound that injustice silenced.

Even tears can be music when they lead the soul home.
