## Day 180 - The Circle Without Center

> *Knowledge without reverence circles creation but never reaches its heart. True worship does not grasp at hidden powers; it returns all wonder to God.*

The third image represented the dark art of enchantment.

It followed Frivolity, for when the soul wanders without reverence, it becomes curious about forbidden powers. It begins to search creation not to praise God, but to use hidden things for its own desire.

This image had the head of a wolf and the tail of a lion. The rest of its body was like a dog.

Those who follow this vice give their soul to what devours. Like a wolf seizing a lamb, it consumes what should have been guarded. Like the rough tail of a lion, its end is harsh and violent. Like a dog, it casts what should have been wisdom into the dirt.

It played with Frivolity and said, “We are one in all things.”

For these vices cling together. The wandering soul is easily drawn toward false mysteries.

Great winds roared in its ears. The image listened closely, studying where they came from. It exalted the winds as if they were gods.

So the heart that welcomes dark suggestions begins to reverence them secretly. It mistakes noise for revelation and power for truth.

Then the image lifted one foreleg toward the fierce wind from the North. With the other it gathered the elements and gusts of wind to itself.

So those ruled by this vice turn from the straight path. They call upon forces that do not belong to them. They use creation against its Creator.

But True Worship of God answered:

“What is more pleasing to God—to worship Him, or His works?”

Creation cannot give life by itself.

The human being has reason and speech, but these gifts must lead back to God. When knowledge moves only around the circle of creation and forgets the center, it becomes empty.

True wisdom worships the Creator.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision warns against knowledge severed from reverence.

The problem is not learning, study, or wonder. Creation is full of mystery, and wisdom may rightly seek understanding. But when the soul seeks power without humility, knowledge becomes dangerous.

The image circles creation but misses the center. It studies winds, elements, planets, and hidden forces, but forgets the One who gives them being.

The animal form shows instinct without holy reason—hunger, domination, and restless searching.

True Worship restores the proper order: creation is not God. It is a sign pointing toward God.

#### Living This Vision

We may not think of ourselves as practicing dark arts, but we can still seek control through knowledge.

We want techniques, systems, secrets, explanations, and certainty. We want to master life so we do not have to trust.

This vision asks:

Does my search for knowledge lead me into humility?

Does wonder become worship?

Or am I trying to control what I should receive with reverence?

Let every created thing become a doorway back to God.
