## 180 - The Quiet Center

> *What returns to the center finds rest*  
> *What wonders in love becomes whole*

I saw a wide circle  
traced gently across the ground.

It was beautiful—  
complete,  
unbroken,  
stretching evenly in every direction.

Along its edge,  
there was movement.

Currents of air,  
shifting patterns,  
subtle motions that drew the eye  
from one place to another.

Everything seemed to happen there—  
at the edge,  
where motion never ceased.

But at the center,  
there was stillness.

A quiet presence  
resting without movement,  
holding the circle  
without effort.

The winds did not reach it.  
The shifting patterns did not disturb it.

It simply remained—  
clear,  
undivided,  
full.

And as I watched,  
something within the circle  
began to turn inward.

Not pulled,  
not forced—  
but drawn  
as if remembering its place.

The movement softened.  
The edge grew quiet.

And what had been scattered  
found its way  
to the center.

There,  
nothing needed to be grasped.  
Nothing needed to be understood.

All was already held.

And I understood:

There is a knowing  
that circles endlessly—  
and there is a knowing  
that comes to rest  
in what gives all things life.

#### Living the Vision

You find yourself moving  
from one thought to another.

Seeking understanding,  
connecting ideas,  
trying to make sense of what you see.

It can feel like progress—  
like you are drawing closer  
to something important.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
a quiet distance.

A sense that even with more knowing,  
something essential  
remains untouched.

But there may be  
a different movement  
gently available.

You may feel it  
when you pause—  
when you let the search  
soften for a moment.

In that stillness,  
something deeper becomes present.

Not another idea.  
Not another answer.

But a quiet awareness  
that does not need to grasp  
to be full.

You are not asked  
to stop wondering.

Only to let your wondering  
lead you inward—  
toward what does not move,  
what does not change,  
what quietly holds you.

And there,  
knowledge becomes something else.

Not control.

But a doorway  
into reverence.
