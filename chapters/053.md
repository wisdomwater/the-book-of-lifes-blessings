## 53 - The Opening of the Tower

> *What has been closed can open again. Light waits at every window.*

I saw a tall tower rising beneath a quiet and spacious sky. Its walls were smooth and strong, carefully formed through long years of standing firm against wind and weather. Nothing about it appeared weak or unstable. It carried the dignity of something built to endure, reaching upward with quiet certainty into the stillness above.

Near the highest part of the tower, three windows opened outward across the land. Through them, one could see far into the distance—the fields, the hills, the changing light upon the earth. The windows were clear and watchful, yet no warmth or brightness moved from within the tower itself. It stood enclosed within its own strength, self-contained and silent, as though nothing beyond its walls was truly needed.

Then light began to gather near it.

It did not descend suddenly from heaven, nor strike the tower with force. It approached gently from every direction at once, soft and patient, like a presence that had always remained nearby. The light did not demand entrance or press against the walls. Instead, it waited quietly beside the windows, offering itself without urgency.

For a long while, nothing changed.

Yet within the stillness of the tower, something slowly began to soften. There was a pause—a quiet recognition that strength alone could not create fullness. And then, almost imperceptibly, the windows opened wider. Not broken apart by pressure, but opened willingly from within.

And as they opened, light entered.

It moved softly through the empty spaces, filling the tower without overwhelming it. The structure remained strong, yet it no longer felt closed. What had once stood apart now seemed alive with connection. The tower had not lost itself by opening; it had become capable of receiving what it could never generate alone.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons when we rely almost entirely upon our own understanding. We build inward towers made of certainty, self-protection, achievement, or control, believing that safety comes through holding ourselves together carefully and remaining untouched by vulnerability. Often these structures are not created from pride alone, but from fear—fear of disappointment, uncertainty, or dependence upon what cannot be controlled.

And for a time, such strength can feel necessary.

Yet eventually the soul begins to sense a quiet separation within itself. Life may remain functional and outwardly stable, but something deeper feels distant, as though the heart has forgotten how to receive. We may continue looking outward through our carefully guarded windows while remaining inwardly closed to wonder, tenderness, or grace.

The opening often begins very gently.

A moment of humility. A willingness to admit we do not have every answer. A small surrender of the need to manage everything alone. These moments can feel fragile at first, yet they become openings through which light quietly enters. The spiritual life rarely unfolds through forceful collapse; more often, it deepens through gradual softening.

And as the windows widen little by little, the soul discovers that openness is not weakness. What is truly strong does not fear light. It welcomes it.

Over time, what once felt isolated begins to feel connected again—to others, to the sacred, and to the deeper life moving patiently beneath all things. The tower still stands, but now it breathes with warmth and presence, illuminated from within.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Open what is within

**Exhale:** Let Your light enter
