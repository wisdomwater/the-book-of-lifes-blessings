## Day 178 - The Senses Guarded

> *The vices press against the senses like mist against a window. But the clear eye and steady heart remain guarded by God.*

I saw five vices appear within the mist.

The mist itself was a terrible sign of falling away from faith. Within it were many kinds of corruption, many hidden movements of evil. And from that fog these five came forward, each with its own appearance, each with its own voice.

They raged against the five senses of human beings.

They sought to bend sight, hearing, speech, touch, and desire away from God. They worked to twist what was given for truth into instruments of confusion.

But those with clear eyes and steady commitment, helped by God, rejected them. They did not allow the vices to rule.

The first image was Fatuity.

It stood first among these vices because of its lying words. It did not desire truth. With flattery and insult, it stirred evil and refused peace.

From head to waist it looked like a youth, for those who choose this vice become uncontrolled in words and deeds. Their vanity scatters them. Their hearts run here and there, restless and foolish.

From the waist down it had the form of a crab. For those ruled by this vice begin badly and end badly. First they praise, then they slander. They move from incontinence into instability.

Its hair was black, and it was naked.

So this vice makes the mind dirty and shameless. It strips away modesty and persuades the tongue to flatter one moment and tear down the next.

But Respect answered it.

Respect treated this vice like mud beneath the shoes. For speech that wounds and manipulates has no true dignity.

#### What This Vision Means

Fatuity is foolish speech wearing the mask of confidence.

It uses words without reverence. It flatters when flattery is useful and insults when insult gives power. It does not seek truth, peace, or love. It seeks effect.

The crab-like body is important. This vice does not move straight. It moves sideways—indirect, unstable, and evasive. Its speech leads others into confusion.

The five senses are also at stake. Our senses are gateways. Through them we receive the world, but through them we can also be distorted. Fatuity especially corrupts speech and hearing, making the tongue a net and the ear a doorway to vanity.

Respect restores order. It knows that not every word deserves honor.

#### Living This Vision

Words can quickly become careless.

We may speak to impress, to gain approval, to appear clever, or to wound before we are wounded. We may flatter people we do not love and criticize people we do not understand.

This vision asks us to guard the senses, especially the tongue.

Before speaking, pause.

Is this true?

Is this necessary?

Does this lead toward peace?

Respect begins when speech becomes humble.
