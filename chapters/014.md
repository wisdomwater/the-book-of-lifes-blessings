## Day 14 - The Fire Beyond All Knowing

> *God is not distant or silent—He is a living fire, sustaining all things while remaining beyond all grasp.*

I saw a vast and immeasurable presence—  
a form whose reach extended from the beginning of creation to its end.

Yet I understood this:  
the human creature can only speak of a small span—  
the brief path between birth and death.

What came before?  
What lies beyond?

These remain hidden.

For only God knows what existed before the world was formed.  
He stands above all things, beyond the clouds, in a clarity untouched by shadow.

No angel can fully comprehend Him.  
No soul can contain Him.

All things come from Him—  
yet He Himself has no beginning.

He lives in Himself.  
He has power in Himself.  
He knows by Himself.

And in this I saw a mystery:

God is life.  
God is power.  
God is knowing.

From these flow all things.

Then I beheld eternity—not as stillness, but as fire.

Not a quiet flame, hidden and contained,  
but a living, active fire—  
radiant, working, full of purpose.

This fire orders all things.  
It moves through creation as the head directs the body.  
From it come sight, sound, breath, and speech.  
All life is sustained by its unseen presence.

Yet this fire does not reveal all its depths.

Within it are hidden mysteries—  
secrets of life that no creature can fully grasp.

Still, I saw that God purifies.

Before the law, He tested humanity through fire and water.  
Afterward, through discipline and trial.  
And in the fullness of time, He Himself entered creation—  
to break the power of darkness  
and cleanse humanity with mercy.

Thus the fire that judges  
is also the fire that restores.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision confronts the limits of human understanding. We live within a narrow slice of time, yet often speak as though we grasp the whole. Hildegard reminds us: there is a vastness before and beyond us that only God knows.

God is described not as an abstract idea, but as a living reality—self-sustaining, self-knowing, and complete. Everything else derives from Him, yet He depends on nothing.

The image of God as fire is essential. This is not destructive fire alone, but active, ordering, life-giving fire. It sustains creation, governs it, and purifies it.

Importantly, this fire is both revealing and hidden. God allows us to know what we need, but not everything. Mystery is not absence—it is depth.

The purification of humanity unfolds across time: before the law, through the law, and ultimately through divine incarnation. The same fire that tests also heals.


#### Living This Vision

It is natural to want certainty—to understand where we came from and where we are going. But this vision invites a different posture: humility within mystery.

You do not need to hold all knowledge to live faithfully.

Instead, you are invited to trust the fire that sustains you.

There are seasons when life feels like testing—like being passed through fire or deep waters. These moments are not always punishment. Often, they are refinement.

Ask yourself gently:  
*What is being purified in me right now?*

Rather than resisting the unknown, you can rest within it.  
God is not absent in mystery—He is present within it.

The same fire that feels intense  
is also the fire that gives life.

And it is already at work within you.
