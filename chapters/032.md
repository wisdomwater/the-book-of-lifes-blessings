## Day 32 – Praised in Heaven

> *The good done in quiet places is not unseen—it is lifted into song.*

I saw again the radiant multitude of angels.

Their praise did not cease.  
Not for a moment.

It flowed continuously—like a river that does not run dry.

But I saw something more.

They were not only praising God.

They were also praising the works of humanity.

Every act done in goodness—  
every movement of love,  
every step toward what is right—  
they saw it.

And they spoke of it.

They lifted it up in praise before God.

Not because humans are perfect,  
but because their works reflect something greater.

They bear the image of God.

And this image, even in its fragility, is worthy of being honored.

God delights in this.

He desires to be praised by angels—  
for it magnifies His glory.

But He also desires the works of humanity to be known,  
to be spoken,  
to be brought into the light.

And more—  
He desires that humanity itself would join in this praise.

That the same voice that rises in heaven  
would begin to awaken on earth.

For just as angels glorify God,  
so too are humans invited to do the same.

And in this shared praise,  
something is completed.

Heaven and earth draw closer.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the dignity of human action within the divine order.

The angels’ praise of human works emphasizes that what we do matters—not only in the visible world, but in the unseen. Acts of goodness participate in the nature of God and are therefore worthy of recognition.

To say that humans are made in the image of God is to affirm that their actions, when aligned with love and truth, reflect divine reality.

God’s desire for praise is not rooted in need, but in relationship. Praise is a way of participating in divine life—a response to recognizing goodness.

The shared praise between angels and humans suggests a unity between heaven and earth. Humanity is not separate from the divine story, but deeply woven into it.

This vision invites us to see our lives as meaningful, even in small and hidden ways.

#### Living This Vision

You may wonder if what you do really matters.

The small choices.  
The quiet kindnesses.  
The unseen efforts.

This vision says: they do.

Even when no one notices, something is being witnessed.

Something is being lifted up.

You are participating in something larger than you can see.

What if you lived today with that awareness?

Not to perform.  
Not to earn approval.

But to simply reflect what is good.

And perhaps also to begin noticing the good in others.

To speak it.  
To name it.

In doing so, you join the song already being sung.
