## 32 — The Song That Sees

> *Nothing given in love is lost*  
> *What is true is quietly lifted*

I saw again  
a field of light—

alive,  
yet at rest.

Within it,  
countless beings  
stood in gentle radiance.

Their voices moved  
like a flowing river—

unceasing,  
clear,  
full of joy  
that did not strain.

And as I listened,  
I began to hear  
something within the song—

not only praise  
rising upward,  
but recognition  
moving outward.

They were seeing.

Small things—  
barely noticeable  
to the human eye—

a kindness offered  
without being named,  
a quiet turning  
toward what is right,  
a moment of love  
that asked for nothing in return.

Each one  
was received.

Each one  
was gathered  
into the song.

Nothing was rushed past.  
Nothing was overlooked.

It was as if  
every fragment of goodness  
was being gently lifted  
into light—

not made greater,  
but revealed  
for what it already was.

And the song widened—

until it felt  
like heaven itself  
was leaning toward the earth,  
drawing near  
through this quiet honoring.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
what you do feels small—

easily missed,  
quickly forgotten,  
hidden within the ordinary rhythm  
of your day.

You may wonder  
if it carries any weight.

But something in you  
still leans toward goodness—

not loudly,  
not perfectly,  
but sincerely.

You may notice it  
in simple choices—

a pause before reacting,  
a gentle word,  
a willingness to care  
even when it goes unseen.

These moments  
do not disappear.

They settle  
into something deeper.

And sometimes,  
if you become still,  
you may feel  
a quiet warmth—

not as recognition from others,  
but as a soft sense  
that what you offered  
has found its place.

You are not being measured.

You are being witnessed  
in love.

And as you begin  
to notice this—

both in yourself  
and in others—

something within you  
naturally responds.

A quiet appreciation.  
A gentle honoring.

A way of seeing  
that begins to sound  
like song.
