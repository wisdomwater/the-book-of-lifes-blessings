## Day 145 - Fire That Overcomes

> *What is born of God does not argue with darkness—it burns through it.*

To the left of the man, I saw another form—almost human, yet not entirely.

Around its head was a circle of fire. From it rose tongues of flame, alive and moving. Its face flashed like lightning—bright, sudden, impossible to grasp. The rest of its form was veiled, wrapped in something like stone, hidden in mystery.

And from this being came a voice—strong, unwavering.

“O you twisted forces of evil,” it declared, “by the power of God I will cast you down.”

“As the devil was overthrown when the first light broke forth, so will you fall.”

“As Goliath fell, as Nebuchadnezzar was humbled, so will all who rise against righteousness be brought low.”

“The fire of the Holy Spirit will reduce them to dust.”

“I stand in strength and constancy against all that gathers in darkness. You cannot endure what I carry.”

Then another voice came from heaven—gentle, yet filled with authority.

“God formed the earth and filled it with life. He sustains it so it does not fall into dust.”

“And man, formed from this earth and sent into the world, is given a path—not of wandering, but of return.”

“Through true obedience, he may come again into grace.”

“Reject the voices that pursue you with darkness. Attend instead to the virtues sent by God.”

“Do not separate yourself from your Creator.”

The vision deepened.

I saw that God calls the human soul toward the light—not by force, but with longing. He invites it to turn away from blindness and from the subtle pull of darkness.

To love what is holy.
To choose it fully.
To walk toward it with desire.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision contrasts two forces: destructive unrest and divine constancy.

The fiery figure represents the strength of divine virtue—especially perseverance and spiritual clarity. The circle of fire suggests completeness and protection. The flames signify the living presence of the Spirit, which does not merely oppose evil but transforms what it touches.

Unlike discord, which spins and scatters, this force stands firm.

Its power does not come from argument, but from alignment with God.

The heavenly voice shifts the focus to humanity. It reminds us that we are not abandoned in the struggle. We are formed with purpose, sustained by grace, and invited into restoration.

“Obedience” here is not harsh submission—it is alignment. It is the turning of the soul toward what is life-giving.

The call is simple but profound:
Do not separate yourself from the Source of life.

The invitation is always open.


#### Living This Vision

You do not overcome darkness by obsessing over it.

You overcome it by turning toward the light.

There are moments when the pull of old patterns feels strong—habits, thoughts, impulses that seem to rise from nowhere. They gather quietly, like clouds.

This vision reminds you: you are not powerless.

There is a strength available to you that does not come from force of will alone.

It comes from alignment.

From choosing, again and again:
What leads to life?
What leads to peace?
What leads to truth?

You do not need to burn brightly all at once.

Even a small turning matters.

Even a quiet “yes” to what is good carries the fire of transformation.

Stay close to that flame.

It knows the way.
