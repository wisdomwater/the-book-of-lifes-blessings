## 196 - The Borrowed Light

> *What is separated from love grows empty*  
> *What receives light begins to shine*

I saw a still pool  
resting beneath the open sky.

The water held no light of its own.

And yet,  
when morning came,  
it shimmered with brightness—  
clear,  
alive,  
filled with the radiance above it.

The pool did not strive to become the sun.

It simply received.

And because it remained open,  
the light rested easily within it.

Nearby,  
I saw another vessel—  
closed,  
sealed tightly against the light.

It appeared strong at first,  
self-contained,  
untouched by what surrounded it.

But within,  
there was only shadow.

No warmth moved there.  
No reflection appeared.

The more it turned inward upon itself,  
the emptier it became.

And slowly,  
what had seemed solid  
began to hollow from within.

But the open water remained full—  
not because it possessed light,  
but because it welcomed it.

And I understood:

There is no shame  
in being a reflection.

The soul becomes radiant  
not by creating its own light,  
but by remaining open  
to the One  
who gives it freely.

#### Living the Vision

You feel the pressure  
to sustain yourself alone.

To hold everything together,  
to become strong enough,  
clear enough,  
self-sufficient enough  
to never need help.

It can seem wise.  
Even admirable.

And yet,  
you may also notice  
how exhausting it becomes.

A quiet emptiness  
begins to grow  
when everything depends  
on your own strength.

But there is another way  
gently waiting.

You may notice it  
in moments of openness—  
when you allow yourself  
to receive rather than strive.

A kindness accepted.  
A breath taken slowly.  
A moment of stillness  
where you stop trying  
to generate light from yourself.

And in that openness,  
something softens.

You begin to remember  
that life was never meant  
to come only from within you.

You were made  
to reflect,  
to receive,  
to remain connected  
to the fullness  
that sustains all things.

And as you rest there,  
even quietly,  
you may feel  
a deeper light begin to appear—  
not forced,  
not possessed,  
but given.
