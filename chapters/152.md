## Day 152 - Naked in the Dark

> *Earthly concern can feel like responsibility, yet still leave the soul unclothed before God.*

Then I saw the fifth image: Concern for Earthly Things.

It came with Inconstancy, because when the soul is unstable, it becomes easily tangled in worldly anxieties. It turns away from heavenly nourishment and searches only for what is temporary.

This figure had a human form.

Its hair was pale, showing a mind that wandered here and there with noise and foolishness. It carried the weight of many concerns—concerns of the body, concerns of the soul, concerns of the world that changes and passes away.

It stood naked in the darkness, shaped like a barrel.

And I understood: those captured by this vice are covered not with holiness, but with worry. Their hearts and senses are wrapped in the blackness of worldly anxiety. Yet they take strange pleasure in it, as though bathing in their own unrest.

They do not seek the garment of salvation.

They do not ask God to clothe them.

Their eyes and efforts are fixed on earthly goods, on temporary work, on what must be managed, gained, guarded, and held.

They become busy with what perishes.

And all the while, the soul remains bare.

Then Celestial Desire answered this vice.

It called humanity to lift its gaze. It urged the soul not to despise earthly life, but to refuse being mastered by it. Temporary things must serve eternal things, not replace them.

For earthly concern becomes dangerous when it consumes the heart.

The soul was made for more than managing dust.

It was made to desire heaven.


#### What This Vision Means

This vision does not condemn ordinary responsibilities. Food, shelter, work, family, and bodily needs matter.

But Concern for Earthly Things becomes a vice when these concerns become the soul’s center.

The figure is human because this struggle is deeply human. We worry because life is fragile. We plan because things matter. We labor because bodies need care.

Yet the nakedness reveals the spiritual danger: a person can be surrounded by activity and still be unclothed inwardly.

The barrel-like form suggests containment. The soul becomes enclosed in its own concerns, sealed within the anxieties of daily life.

Celestial Desire restores proportion. It does not ask us to abandon the earth. It asks us to remember heaven while living on earth.

The problem is not having responsibilities.

The problem is forgetting what they are for.


#### Living This Vision

Worry often disguises itself as devotion.

We tell ourselves:
“I am only being responsible.”
“I just need to think this through.”
“I cannot rest until everything is handled.”

But sometimes our concern becomes a dark garment that does not warm us.

This vision invites you to notice what has captured your gaze.

What are you carrying that has become heavier than love?
What temporary thing has begun to feel ultimate?
Where are you trying to clothe yourself with control?

Place one concern before God today.

Not all of them.
Just one.

Let it be held by a larger mercy.

You may still need to act. You may still need to plan. But you do not need to be consumed.

Your soul was made for desire, not bondage.
