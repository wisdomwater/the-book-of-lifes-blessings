## 59 - The Well That Remains

> *What you return to continues to give. Depth opens to those who linger.*

I saw a quiet well resting within a wide and open place. Its stones were smooth from the touch of many hands, worn not by haste or striving, but by long faithfulness. The space around it carried a sense of stillness, as though countless souls had come there over many seasons seeking something deeper than passing thirst.

Soft light rested along the edge of the well, illuminating what could easily be seen. Yet the water itself remained hidden far below the surface—clear, undisturbed, and deep beyond immediate sight. It did not rush upward to meet those who approached. It waited quietly in its depth, steady and full.

A vessel was lowered slowly into the darkness below.

There was no urgency in the movement. No frantic grasping. Only a patient descent into what could not be reached from the surface alone. The rope unwound gently, deeper and deeper, until at last the vessel disappeared into the hidden water beneath.

And when it rose again, it carried enough.

The water shimmered cool and bright in the light, simple and life-giving. Around the well there was no crowding or striving to possess more than was needed. Only a quiet rhythm of return. Again and again, people came—lowering, waiting, receiving. And I saw that the well did not diminish through giving. Its abundance remained untouched by the many who drew from it.

Nothing was withheld.

Nothing was forced.

The depth remained patiently available to all who approached with openness and willingness to linger.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when the soul longs for something deeper than quick answers or passing inspiration. We hunger for nourishment that truly sustains—for wisdom that remains present long after emotion fades or circumstances change. Yet often we also carry the impulse to move quickly, gathering insights without allowing them time to settle deeply within us.

The deeper life unfolds through another rhythm.

It asks for returning.

Again and again, we come back to the same truths, the same prayers, the same quiet practices, and discover that they meet us differently each time. A passage once overlooked suddenly speaks directly into grief. A silence once uncomfortable becomes a place of rest. What seemed ordinary begins revealing hidden depth simply because we have remained present long enough to receive it.

This is how spiritual understanding often grows.

Not through consuming more and more, but through dwelling more deeply within what is already life-giving. The soul slowly learns that wisdom cannot always be rushed to the surface. Some things reveal themselves only through patience, trust, and repeated return.

And in time, even small practices begin carrying surprising nourishment.

A morning prayer. A moment of stillness. A familiar sacred text. A quiet walk. These become wells we return to—not because they constantly overwhelm us with novelty, but because something living continues flowing beneath them. The surface may appear unchanged, yet the depth remains inexhaustible.

The well remains.

And no matter how many times you return, you are still welcome there.
