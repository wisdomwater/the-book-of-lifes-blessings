## 54 — Held in Living Remembrance

> *You are not lost to what is eternal. Something in you is always being held.*

I saw a vast and quiet expanse filled with a soft, unwavering light. It did not feel distant or unreachable, but near in a way that awakened a deep sense of homecoming. The light carried no harshness within it. It rested gently over everything, spacious and alive, like a presence that had never ceased surrounding the world.

Within this radiance, there was a subtle gathering taking place. It was as though scattered threads were slowly being drawn back together, each one remembering the pattern to which it belonged. Some moved naturally toward the light, almost effortlessly, as though their whole being had long been quietly leaning in that direction. They were not pulled forward by force or fear, but lifted by a deep inner nearness already alive within them.

Others drifted more slowly.

They did not appear rejected or abandoned, only turned slightly aside—as though distracted by distance, uncertainty, or forgetfulness. Their movement was less clear, their path more wandering. And yet even here, the light remained present. It did not withdraw itself from them. It did not close its warmth or turn away in disappointment. Patiently and faithfully, it continued holding all things within its quiet reach.

And beneath the vision, I sensed a deeper truth unfolding.

To be remembered by the Eternal is not merely to be recalled like a distant memory. It is to be continuously held within a living presence that does not cease loving what it has created. The soul belongs to a light that does not forget. Even when awareness fades, even when attention wanders, something deeper continues carrying us within its quiet compassion.

**Living the Vision**

There are times when we feel inwardly distant from what gives us life. Not necessarily lost, but turned aside—caught within distraction, exhaustion, fear, or the endless movement of ordinary concerns. The soul becomes scattered across responsibilities, anxieties, and inner noise, and slowly we begin to forget the deeper center from which peace once flowed.

This forgetting happens gently and often without notice.

We may continue functioning outwardly while inwardly feeling disconnected from ourselves, from others, or from the sacred presence quietly sustaining us beneath the surface of things. Yet even in these moments, something within us still remembers. A small longing remains alive. A breath, a moment of stillness, a sudden tenderness, or an unexpected silence can awaken the awareness that we are not as alone or abandoned as we feared.

The spiritual life is often less about achieving closeness to the Divine and more about returning to the awareness of a closeness that has never disappeared.

This return does not require perfection. We are not asked to hold ourselves in constant spiritual certainty or unbroken attention. Instead, we are invited to come back gently, again and again, trusting that every small turning of the heart is already met by a presence waiting patiently within us.

And over time, this quiet remembrance begins to heal the fear of separation itself.

The soul learns that it lives not outside of love, but within it.

**Inhale:** You in me
