## Day 36 – The Waiting Fire

> *Even in glory, there is a waiting—for the fullness of what is yet to come.*

I listened as the voices of the blessed rose like waters.

Their longing was clear.

They desired to be whole again—  
to live not only in spirit,  
but in the fullness of body restored.

And a response came.

Not harsh.  
Not distant.

But firm.

They would not yet receive what they longed for.

Not until the final shaking.

Not until the elements themselves were stirred—  
until fire moved through all things,  
purifying, revealing, completing.

Then, I saw a great call.

The voice of God would go forth.

No one would remain hidden.

The dead would rise—  
all of them.

Those who had lived in truth.  
Those who had turned away.

Each would stand again in a body—  
no longer as before,  
but changed.

Transformed.

Some would rise into life that cannot be broken.  
Others into a state marked by loss.

And I understood—this was not arbitrary.

It was the unveiling of what had been formed within each life.

The fire would reveal what had always been there.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision speaks to the future fulfillment of human existence—the restoration of body and soul.

The longing of the saints reflects an incomplete state. Even in joy, there remains a movement toward something greater.

The final judgment is portrayed as a purification—a revealing fire that brings all things into clarity.

The resurrection is not merely a return to physical life, but a transformation. The body becomes something new, aligned with the reality of the soul.

The distinction between life and loss is not imposed from outside, but flows from the inner condition of each person.

This vision emphasizes that what is formed within us now has lasting significance.

#### Living This Vision

You are becoming something.

Even now.

Through your choices.  
Through your loves.  
Through what you hold onto.

This formation is quiet.

But it is real.

And one day, it will be revealed.

Not to condemn,  
but to make clear.

So the question is not one of fear.

It is one of direction.

What are you becoming?

And is it leading toward life?

There is still time.

Still space.

Still invitation.
