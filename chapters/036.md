## 36 — Strength That Does Not Collapse

> *You are allowed to bend*  
> *What holds you will not give way*

I saw the living presence  
resting upon the earth—

not distant,  
but grounded,  
like a quiet strength  
woven into the soil itself.

There was a firmness here,  
but not rigidity.

A yielding  
that did not weaken.

The ground held everything—  
water moving in its course,  
air settling in gentle balance,  
life rising and returning  
without strain.

And within this holding,  
I noticed a subtle trembling—

like a moment  
when weight becomes known.

A bending.  
A soft giving way.

Yet nothing broke.

The movement itself  
was held.

As if the strength beneath  
was not threatened  
by the strain above.

Then I saw lives  
passing through pressure—

seasons that pressed inward,  
testing what could remain.

There was no force pushing back.

Only a deeper resting—  
a returning  
to what could not be shaken.

And in that return,  
something steadied.

Not by resistance,  
but by being held  
within a strength  
that did not collapse.

The bending became part of the standing.

And what endured  
was not what resisted—

but what remained rooted  
in something deeper than itself.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes you feel the weight of your life more fully—

when things press in,  
and your usual strength  
feels less certain.

You may notice  
a bending within you—

a weariness,  
a quiet trembling,  
a sense that you cannot hold everything as before.

And something in you  
may want to resist that.

To stay firm.  
To push through.

But there is another way  
already present.

You may begin to sense  
that even in the bending,  
you are not falling.

That something beneath you  
remains steady.

You are not required  
to be unyielding.

You are invited  
to remain connected  
to what holds you.

And as you do,  
even gently,  
you may feel a different kind of strength—

one that does not come  
from holding yourself together,  
but from being held.

And in that,  
you endure.

Not perfectly.  
But truly.

> **Inhale:** You hold me steady  
>
> **Exhale:** I rest and remain
