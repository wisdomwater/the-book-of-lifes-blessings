## Day 172 - The Devouring Hand

> *What grasps without trust is never filled. The more it takes, the more it becomes hollow within.*

Then I saw the fourth image.

It appeared as a man, yet something in him was deeply distorted. His face was bare of hair, yet he bore the beard of a goat. His eyes were strange—small pupils surrounded by wide, pale whites. He breathed heavily through his nose, as if driven by constant hunger.

His hands were made of iron.

His legs were stained with blood.

His feet were like those of a lion.

He wore a robe woven of black and white threads, knotted at the top and spreading wide below, as if tangled within itself.

Upon his chest clung a black vulture, its claws dug deep, feeding upon him even as it turned away.

Near him stood a tree.

Its roots reached into Gehenna.

Its fruit was foul—apples of pitch and sulfur.

And the man watched the tree intently. With his mouth, he plucked the fruit and devoured it eagerly, again and again.

Around him moved many worms, stirring the darkness with restless motion.

Then he spoke:

“I am no fool.

I gather everything. I take what I can. The more I collect, the more I have.

Why should I depend on others when I can secure all I need for myself?

There is no fault in taking from those who have more.

Once I possess what I want, I fear no one. I live as I please.

No one can deceive me.

I take what is mine with my own cunning.”

And his voice was hard.

Then from the storm-filled cloud came a reply:

“You are swift to devour like a wolf and greedy like a vulture.

You are burdened by what you hoard, yet nothing satisfies you.

You have forgotten God and trust only yourself.

You are hard, without mercy, and nothing is ever enough for you.

But I rejoice in what God gives.

I am satisfied in His abundance.

Why should I desire more than I need?”

And I understood the emptiness hidden beneath grasping.

#### What This Vision Means

This image reveals avarice—not simply greed for possessions, but a deeper posture of the heart.

It is the refusal to trust.

The iron hands show rigidity. The bloodied legs reveal harm done in pursuit of gain. The vulture feeding on the chest shows how greed consumes the one who carries it.

The tree rooted in darkness reveals the source of what is gathered—what is taken without trust cannot nourish.

In contrast, the response speaks of sufficiency. Of joy that comes not from accumulation, but from receiving.

#### Living This Vision

Avarice often hides beneath practical concerns.

Security. Provision. Planning.

But beneath these can be a quiet fear: *Will there be enough?*

This fear drives grasping.

This vision invites a different posture—trust.

To receive what is given.

To release what is excess.

To believe that enough is truly enough.

Where are you holding too tightly?
