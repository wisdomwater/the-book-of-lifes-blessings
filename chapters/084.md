## 84 - The Sky Within the Mind

> *Even in unknowing, light remains*  
> *Small and steady, guiding from within*

I saw a wide expanse  
like a night sky  
held close and near.

It was deep,  
dark—  
not empty,  
but full.

And within it,  
countless lights  
shimmered.

Not bright enough  
to remove the darkness—  
but present,  
clear,  
unchanging.

Each one held its place.

Each one quietly alive.

The darkness did not resist them.

It seemed to carry them—  
like a vast space  
that made their light visible.

I drew closer  
and saw  
that this sky was not distant.

It was within—  
like a hidden horizon  
stretching through the soul.

The lights did not speak.

They did not demand attention.

They simply were.

Steady  
amid all that could not be seen.

And I understood—  
the darkness was not absence.

It was depth.

A place where light  
could be known  
without needing to fill everything at once.

And the stars remained—  
faithful,  
guiding,  
quietly enough.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
your thoughts feel unclear—

when questions rise,  
when understanding feels distant,  
when you are aware  
of how much you do not see.

It can feel like darkness.

And you may be tempted  
to resolve it quickly—  
to reach for certainty  
before it has time to form.

But you may also notice  
something else within you.

Small lights—  
simple knowings,  
quiet trust,  
a sense of what is true  
even without full clarity.

They do not remove every question.

They do not explain everything.

But they remain.

You may begin  
to rest with them.

To let them guide you  
one step at a time.

There is a humility here—  
a willingness  
to live without seeing all things clearly,  
while still moving forward  
in what has been given.

And gently,  
you may find  
that what once felt like confusion  
begins to open—  
not by force,  
but by staying present  
to the light you already carry.
