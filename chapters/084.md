## 84 - The Quiet Song Within

> *You do not need to feel to begin*  
> *A small turning becomes a song*

I saw a figure  
standing at the edge of a shifting cloud—  
dark in one place,  
bright in another,  
never fully still.

Its gaze moved outward,  
searching only what could be touched,  
held,  
understood.

And yet,  
something in the air  
felt incomplete—  
as though a deeper music  
had gone unheard.

Then,  
very softly,  
a sound arose.

Not from outside,  
but from within—  
like the gentle strings of a zither  
being touched  
with quiet care.

It did not force attention.  
It did not compete  
with the movement of the world.

It simply played—  
steady,  
clear,  
almost hidden.

And as the sound continued,  
the air began to settle.

The cloud thinned.  
The shifting slowed.

The figure paused.

For a moment,  
it listened.

And in that listening,  
something awakened—  
not a sudden clarity,  
but a remembering  
of what had always been present.

The music did not explain.  
It aligned.

And I saw  
that this quiet song  
was always there—  
waiting  
to be heard again.

#### Living the Vision

Sometimes your attention is pulled outward—  
toward what you can manage,  
control,  
understand.

It can feel like  
that is all there is.

And when something deeper  
is not felt,  
it is easy to turn away  
from it altogether.

But there is another movement  
available to you.

A small pause.  
A gentle listening.

You may not feel anything at first.

That is okay.

Prayer does not begin  
with certainty.

It begins  
with turning.

A quiet word.  
A breath offered.  
A moment of stillness  
in the middle of everything else.

You may notice  
how something within you  
begins to soften—  
not all at once,  
but enough  
to sense a different kind of presence.

You are not creating it.

You are remembering it.

And as you return  
again and again,  
what once felt distant  
becomes a quiet song  
that steadies you  
from within.
