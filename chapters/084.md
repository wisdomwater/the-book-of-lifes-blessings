## Day 84 — The Star-Filled Darkness

> *Even when the mind turns toward the flesh, light still shines within it.*

And I saw the book upon the wing of the chest.

It was dark—entirely black.  
Yet within it, countless stars shone.

And I understood:

Human reason rests within the wisdom of God.

It does not exist apart from Him,  
even when it wanders.

For though people live in many different ways,  
truth remains one.

All that is spoken—  
in the old covenant and the new—  
finds its center in Christ.

Yet reason is darkened.

It turns often toward the flesh,  
toward what is immediate and visible.

It questions.  
It doubts.  
It struggles to understand what it speaks.

And still—  
the stars remain.

For within humanity there is faith  
and there is understanding.

Through faith,  
we trust what we cannot see.

Through understanding,  
we begin to grasp what is hidden.

And even when we do not fully comprehend,  
we recognize this:

We are creatures,  
and God is beyond us.

The darkness does not erase the stars.  
It holds them.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision holds together two truths that often feel opposed.

Human reason is limited and easily drawn toward what is immediate and tangible. It questions, struggles, and sometimes resists what it cannot fully grasp.

Yet it is never abandoned.

The stars within the darkness symbolize faith and insight—small but real lights within the human mind. Even when understanding is incomplete, these lights guide.

The unity of the Old and New Testaments in Christ shows that truth is not fragmented. What appears divided is actually converging.

This vision encourages humility. It acknowledges the limits of reason without dismissing its value.

Reason is not the enemy of faith.  
It is a field in which faith shines.

#### Living This Vision

There will be times when you feel uncertain.

When questions rise.  
When clarity feels distant.

Do not be afraid of this.

The presence of questions  
does not mean the absence of faith.

Look for the stars.

What small truths do you already know?  
What quiet convictions remain?

Hold onto those.

You do not need full understanding  
to move forward.

Faith often begins as a small light—  
steady, quiet, enough.

And over time,  
those lights begin to gather.

What once felt like darkness  
becomes a sky.
