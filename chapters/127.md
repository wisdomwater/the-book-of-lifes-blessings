## 127 - The Coolness of Living Desire
What is restless can become whole again

> *Desire is not lost when it is healed*  
> *It becomes gentle enough to carry life*

I saw a figure  
resting low to the ground—  
curled inward,  
as though seeking comfort  
in its own nearness.

Around it,  
the earth felt dry—  
not barren,  
but weary,  
as if something had been taken  
too often,  
too quickly.

Its movements were soft,  
but heavy—  
reaching,  
drawing in,  
never quite satisfied.

Then,  
without disturbance,  
a coolness entered.

Like early morning  
before the heat of the day.

Moisture gathered  
on the edges of things—  
on leaves,  
on stems,  
on the quiet surfaces  
that had grown tired.

The air changed.

What had been tight  
began to loosen.

The figure grew still—  
not forced,  
but held  
in a gentler presence.

And in that stillness,  
something new appeared—  
not a loss of desire,  
but a different kind.

Not grasping,  
but open.

Not consuming,  
but receiving.

Like the earth  
drinking what is given  
without needing to take more.

And I saw  
that this was rest—  
not escape,  
but a return  
to what can truly sustain life.

#### Living the Vision

Desire can feel overwhelming—  
pressing in,  
asking to be satisfied  
immediately.

You may notice  
how it narrows your focus—  
drawing everything inward  
toward one need,  
one relief.

And beneath it,  
there may be something deeper—  
a quiet exhaustion,  
a longing not just for pleasure,  
but for rest.

You do not need  
to fight desire.

Only to slow down  
long enough  
to feel what is underneath it.

What are you truly seeking?

Comfort?  
Connection?  
Relief from something unspoken?

When you allow that question  
to remain gently open,  
desire begins to soften.

It no longer needs  
to rush.

You can meet it  
with patience—  
with breath,  
with presence,  
with a willingness  
to stay.

And in that staying,  
something begins to change.

What once felt consuming  
becomes clearer—  
less urgent,  
more honest.

You are not losing desire.

You are learning  
how to let it live  
without taking more than it gives.
