## 90 - The Clearing of the Waters

> *Even when the heart feels heavy, there is still a way back to light.*

I saw a wide expanse covered in shadow. It was not empty, nor abandoned, but dimmed—as though something heavy had settled quietly over its surface. Beneath the stillness lay waters that no longer moved freely. They had become thick with silence, clouded in a way that obscured both depth and reflection. Nothing shimmered there. Nothing seemed alive in the way it once had.

And yet above the waters, the light remained.

Soft. Patient. Unhurried.

It did not force itself downward or break violently through the darkness. It simply rested there, steady and present, waiting without accusation. The light seemed to understand that what was hidden beneath the heaviness had not disappeared entirely. It was only covered.

Then, almost imperceptibly, the surface began to stir.

Not through striving. Not through force. Something within the waters slowly opened to what had always been near. A small yielding occurred—a quiet willingness to let the stillness breathe again. And as this openness deepened, the heaviness began to loosen. The clouded waters slowly cleared.

The movement was gentle enough to be missed at first.

But little by little, life returned.

A reflection emerged where none had been visible before. Depth reappeared beneath the surface. What once felt dull and unreachable began to shimmer faintly with recognition, as though the waters themselves remembered how to receive the light again.

And I saw that nothing essential had been destroyed.

The soul had not lost its capacity for life.

It had only become covered for a time.

**Living the Vision**

There are seasons when the heart feels heavy in ways that are difficult to explain. Meaning grows dim. Prayer feels distant. Beauty no longer reaches us with the same immediacy it once did. We move through life carrying a quiet numbness, wondering whether something vital within us has faded beyond recovery.

In these moments, many people become harsh with themselves. They try to force clarity, force feeling, force certainty—as though spiritual life depends upon constant emotional brightness. Yet the contemplative path teaches something gentler.

The soul often heals the way water clears.

Slowly.

Patiently.

Through openness rather than force.

We are not always asked to solve our heaviness immediately. Sometimes we are only invited to remain available to small moments of light: a breath consciously taken, a brief prayer whispered without certainty, a conversation that softens loneliness, a glimpse of beauty that reaches us unexpectedly. These small openings matter more than we realize.

For light does not abandon the soul simply because the waters become clouded.

Grace remains near even when we cannot clearly feel it.

And often faith begins not with strong conviction, but with a quiet turning—a willingness to look again toward what still carries life, even faintly. Over time, this turning allows movement to return within us. The heaviness loosens. Reflection becomes possible again.

Not all at once.

But enough to remind us that hope was never fully gone.

The light remained the entire time.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Turn me toward Your light
