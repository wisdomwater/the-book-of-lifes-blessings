# FIRST PART

## MAN WATCHES EAST AND SOUTH

I saw a Man of immense size. His height reached up to the clouds of heaven and stretched down to the depths of the abyss.

His shoulders rose above the clouds into the clear and peaceful sky.
From his shoulders down to his thighs, he stood within a bright white cloud.
From his thighs to his knees, he was in the air above the earth.
From his knees to his calves, he stood upon the earth.
And from his calves down to the soles of his feet, he stood in the waters of the abyss itself.

In this way he stood over all things, even the abyss beneath him.

He faced toward the east, looking over the east and the south.

His face shone with such brilliance that I could not look at it closely. In his mouth there was a white cloud shaped like a trumpet, filled with powerful sounds. When the Man blew this trumpet, three winds rushed out.

One wind carried a fiery cloud.
Another carried a stormy cloud.
The third carried a radiant cloud.

Each wind bore its own cloud.

The wind with the fiery cloud remained before the Man’s face. The other two descended toward his chest, spreading out their winds there.

The wind before his face stretched its cloud from the east toward the south. Within this fiery cloud was a great multitude of living and burning beings. They were united in one will and shared one life together.

Before them stood a small table covered with feathers that moved and fluttered everywhere according to God’s command. Upon this table the wisdom of God had written hidden mysteries. The shining beings fixed their eyes upon it.

Whenever they looked at the writings, they received strength from God. Then together they sounded the trumpet in perfect harmony, producing music of every kind.

The wind that carried the stormy cloud moved it from the south toward the west. The cloud spread out in a vast square so immense that the human mind could not grasp its size.

Inside it was a countless multitude of saints. All were alive in spirit, and no one could number them.

Their voices roared like rushing waters, saying:

“We dwell here according to the gentle will of the One who makes the wind blow. But when will we receive our bodies again? Only when we have them will our joy be greater than it is now.”

Then the multitude within the fiery cloud answered them with praise, saying:

“When the Divine One lifts the trumpet, lightning and thunder and burning fire will be sent upon the earth. The fire within the sun will be stirred so that the whole earth trembles. This will happen when God reveals His great signs.

“Then the trumpet will call all the peoples of the world in every language. And all whose names are written will receive their bodies again.”

The wind that carried the radiant cloud spread it from the east toward the north.

From the west, terrible darkness approached—thick and frightening. It pressed against the radiant cloud, but it could not pass through it.

Within the radiant cloud appeared the sun and the moon. In the sun was a lion, and in the moon a ram.

The sun shone everywhere—into heaven, across the earth, and even beneath the earth—moving from its rising to its setting.

Whenever the sun moved forward, the lion advanced with it, seizing and tearing apart much prey.

When the sun declined, the lion withdrew with it, roaring loudly in triumph.

The moon followed the sun in its rising and its setting, and the ram moved with it. Then the wind spoke, saying:

“The woman who is with child will give birth, and the ram will fight against the north.”

In the darkness I saw an uncountable multitude of lost souls. They kept far away from those in the south who sang hymns of praise, because they did not want anything to do with them.

Their leader was called **Seducer**, because they followed the one who overthrew Christ and now has no power.

They cried out loudly:

“Alas! Alas! Terrible and destructive deeds have taken life from us and led us into death!”

Then I saw a cloud coming from the north that stretched out into this darkness. It held no joy or happiness, for it had never been touched by the light of the sun.

It was filled with evil spirits wandering from place to place. They laid traps for human beings. Yet whenever they thought about the great Man I had seen, they became ashamed.

Then I heard the ancient serpent speak to himself:

“I will gather all my strength and prepare for battle. I will fight my enemies with all the power I have.”

He spat out foul drool filled with corruption and vice upon humanity. Mocking them, he said:

“They call themselves suns because of the light of their deeds. But I will make them dark, harmful, and dreadful in the darkness.”

Then he breathed out a horrible mist that spread across the earth like thick black smoke.

From within the fog came powerful voices shouting:

“No one should worship a god unless they can see and know him! Why should anyone honor what they do not see?”

Within this fog I saw many forms of vice.
