## 2 - The One Who Holds All

> *Nothing is outside the Presence. All things are gently held.*

A vast stillness opened before me—not empty, but living, like a great breath without edge or boundary. Within it stood a Presence both intimate and immeasurable, not distant from the world but quietly permeating it. Its radiance rested above the clouds in a softness beyond striving, where light simply existed because it could do nothing else. And from that hidden brightness its form descended gently through the open heavens, through the air and earth, into the deep unseen places beneath all things, even into the silent waters where human words can no longer reach.

Yet nowhere was there division. Heaven did not end where earth began, nor were the depths abandoned beneath the light. All things belonged within one continuous wholeness—held together inside a Presence vast enough to contain both the visible and the hidden, the joyful and the unfinished, the known and the mysterious. What appeared separate to human eyes was gathered together there in quiet belonging.

Its face shone with a tenderness too whole to fully comprehend. Not harsh brightness, not overwhelming power, but the kind of beauty that softens resistance simply by being near. And from its breath came a low and gentle sound, like wind passing through a hollow reed in the evening fields. It carried no urgency, no demand, no violence of command. Yet everything seemed to awaken beneath it, as flowers slowly turn toward morning light without being forced.

From this living harmony three movements flowed outward into creation. One moved with warmth and quiet fire, alive with beings who rested in deep communion, their awareness joined together like steady flames that neither consumed nor flickered with fear. Another moved like distant rain across open land—a longing current filled with souls still listening for something greater than themselves, sensing already the promise stirring beneath the surface of things. The third shimmered with a clear brightness that carried neither strain nor pride, only a gentle clarity that illuminated whatever it touched.

Nothing clashed within these movements. Nothing struggled for dominance. Each belonged to the greater music holding all things together. And the sound beneath them was not the sound of control, but of harmony already unfolding—like a song creation has been learning since the beginning.

**Living the Vision**

The human heart often experiences life as fragmented. We move between fears, responsibilities, memories, disappointments, and hopes, trying to hold together what feels scattered within us. There is a deep temptation to believe that peace will come only when every uncertainty is resolved, when every wound is healed, or when we finally understand the meaning of all we carry. Yet the soul slowly discovers that wholeness does not begin with complete understanding. It begins with belonging.

There are moments—often quiet and easily overlooked—when something deeper becomes perceptible beneath the noise of ordinary life. A stillness arrives while watching rain gather at a window, while sitting beside someone we love, while breathing slowly after a long season of exhaustion. In such moments we sense that even our unfinishedness is being gently held. The longing within us is not evidence of abandonment, but a sign that we are already participating in something larger than ourselves.

Spiritual maturity does not always arrive through certainty. Often it grows through learning to rest inside mystery without panic. To trust that what appears fragmented to us may still belong to a greater harmony we cannot yet fully hear. The soul need not force itself into peace. It only needs to become quiet enough to notice that it has never been outside the Presence that holds all things together.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** You hold all things

**Exhale:** I rest within You
