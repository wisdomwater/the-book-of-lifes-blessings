## Day 2 — The Man Who Spans Creation

> *I saw a living mystery that stretched across the whole world.
> Heaven, earth, and the deep were held within a single presence.*

In my vision I saw a man of immense stature.

He was so great that his height reached the summit of the clouds of the sky, and yet his form descended all the way to the depths of the abyss.

His shoulders rose above the clouds into the most serene and luminous ether.

From his shoulders to his thighs he stood within a white cloud.

From his thighs to his knees he was suspended in the open air of the earth.

His knees rested upon the ground.

And from his calves downward to the soles of his feet he stood within the waters of the abyss, so that even the deep waters were beneath him.

He faced toward the East and the South.

His face shone with such brilliance that I could not gaze upon it closely. The light was too radiant for my sight.

From his mouth came a white cloud shaped like a trumpet, filled with swift and powerful sounds. When he breathed into it, three winds came forth into the air.

Each wind carried a cloud.

One carried a fiery cloud.

Another carried a stormy cloud.

And the third carried a cloud filled with radiant brightness.

The wind bearing the fiery cloud remained before the face of the Man, stretching from East toward South. Within that cloud I saw a great multitude of living beings. They burned with ardent life and were perfectly united in their will.

Before them stood a small table covered with feathers that moved and lifted themselves according to the commands of God. Upon this table were written hidden mysteries of divine wisdom. As the living beings looked upon these writings, the power of God filled them.

Then they sounded the trumpet together, producing a harmony of many kinds of music.

The wind carrying the stormy cloud moved from the South toward the West. Within it I saw a vast multitude of saints. Their number could not be counted. Their voices sounded like rushing waters as they cried out:

“We dwell here according to the gentle will of the One who sends the winds. But when shall we receive our bodies again? Only then will our joy be greater than it is now.”

Then the beings within the fiery cloud answered them with praise:

“When the Divine takes up the trumpet, lightning, thunder, and burning fire will descend upon the earth. The fire within the sun will be stirred so that the whole world trembles. Then the trumpet will call every people of the earth in every tongue. And those whose names are written will receive their bodies again.”

And still the vision continued.

#### What This Vision Means

The great Man in this vision reveals a profound truth about the structure of creation.

His body spans the entire universe—from the highest heavens to the hidden depths of the abyss. Nothing lies outside his presence. Heaven, air, earth, and sea are all gathered within him.

This figure represents the divine order that holds all things together.

The brightness of his face shows that the fullness of divine reality cannot be grasped directly by human sight. We glimpse its radiance, but its true nature remains beyond our comprehension.

From his mouth comes the trumpet of divine command. Creation responds through the winds that carry the clouds across the world.

Each cloud reveals a different movement of God’s work. The fiery cloud contains beings united in divine wisdom. The stormy cloud contains the souls of the saints who wait in hope. Their longing expresses the deep human hope for resurrection—for the restoration of body and spirit together.

The trumpet will one day call all people. The scattered story of humanity will be gathered into a single moment of revelation.

#### Living This Vision

When we look at the world, we often see confusion.

Events appear scattered. Suffering seems random. The future feels uncertain.

But this vision invites us to see with deeper eyes.

Behind the movements of history there is a greater presence holding everything together. The Man who spans heaven and earth reminds us that existence is not drifting without purpose.

The winds still move.

Hidden wisdom is still being written.

And like the saints in the stormy cloud, we too live in a space of longing. We sense that life is not yet complete. Something greater waits beyond what we can presently see.

Faith does not require that we understand the whole vision.

It only asks that we trust the One who stands above the clouds and yet places his feet even in the depths.
