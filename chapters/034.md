## 34 - Restored by Holy Fire

> *Nothing in you is beyond renewal. What feels dim is being filled with light.*

I saw a gentle turning toward the rising light, as though all creation were slowly orienting itself toward warmth and quiet fullness. The light did not arrive suddenly or demand attention. It unfolded gradually, like dawn spreading across the earth after a long night. Everywhere it touched, something began to awaken.

What had once seemed still softened beneath its presence. What had grown dim slowly began to glow again from within. The movement carried no violence, no urgency—only the patient rhythm of life returning gently to itself. It felt like winter soil warming beneath the first breath of spring, or closed flowers opening almost imperceptibly toward the morning sun.

Yet there were places where the radiance met a kind of clouding.

Not broken places.

Not abandoned places.

Only hidden ones.

It was as though a veil had quietly settled over certain parts of the soul, dimming what had once shone clearly. And still, the light did not withdraw from them. It moved toward them with tenderness. It entered slowly, not with harshness or exposure, but like warmth moving through frozen ground long waiting to soften again.

Then I saw something like fire.

Not destructive.

Not consuming.

Alive.

The fire carried a deep and holy warmth that did not erase what it touched. Instead, it filled it. Places that had once held only shadow began carrying a quiet radiance of their own. What emerged was not merely restored to what it had been before. It became deeper somehow—more open, more spacious, more fully alive.

And I understood then that nothing true is ever lost within the light of God.

What has dimmed is not forsaken.

What feels broken is not beyond renewal.

Everything is being drawn patiently toward a fuller becoming.

### Living the Vision

There are seasons when parts of us begin to feel distant from life. A sense of clarity fades. Joy becomes difficult to reach. We may remember moments when the heart felt more open, more aligned, more awake, and quietly wonder where that version of ourselves has gone. Sometimes this loss feels subtle. Other times it feels profound.

In these moments, many people begin searching desperately for the person they once were.

But the soul’s healing rarely happens through returning backward.

The deeper movement of transformation carries us forward into something we have not fully become yet.

Often renewal begins quietly. A softening in the body. A small willingness to hope again. A tenderness returning where numbness once lived. These moments may seem fragile or incomplete, yet they are often signs that life is already moving beneath the surface. Grace does not always arrive dramatically. Sometimes it comes like warmth slowly entering places long held in coldness.

Even the parts of ourselves we regret are not excluded from this healing movement.

Failures, wounds, fears, and seasons of confusion are not outside the reach of divine restoration. The holy fire of love does not seek to destroy the soul, but to illuminate and transform it from within. What once felt like shame can become compassion. What once felt like emptiness can deepen into wisdom and humility.

You are not being asked to erase your past or recover some idealized former self.

You are being invited into a fuller life—one that gently gathers every part of your story into deeper wholeness. And as you allow yourself to remain open to this quiet work of renewal, you may begin to discover that nothing essential within you has been abandoned.

Only slowly, patiently transformed by light.

**Inhale:** Restore me in Your light

**Exhale:** I receive what You give
