## 34 — The Quiet Expansion of Good

> *What is held with care begins to widen*  
> *What is steady grows beyond your seeing*

I saw a gentle wind  
moving through a field of light—

not rushing,  
not restless,  
but steady  
and sure.

It did not scatter.  
It gathered.

Where it passed,  
something deepened—

like roots  
finding their place  
in the earth.

There was a quiet strength  
in its movement—

a shaping  
that did not strain,  
yet did not turn away.

And I sensed  
this was not against anything.

It was for something—

drawing what was fragile  
into firmness,  
what was uncertain  
into rest.

Then the space opened.

Before me  
stretched a wide expanse—

formed,  
yet without edge.

It held the feeling  
of something complete—

balanced,  
unshaken,  
whole.

And within it,  
small beginnings  
had become something vast—

gestures of care,  
moments of choosing,  
threads of goodness—

now woven  
into something  
far beyond their origin.

Nothing was lost.

Everything had grown.

Not through force,  
but through remaining.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
you continue in something good—

quietly,  
without recognition,  
without knowing  
what it will become.

You may feel  
how small it seems—

a choice,  
a kindness,  
a steady returning  
to what matters.

And at times,  
it may feel  
as though nothing is changing.

But something in you  
keeps showing up—

not loudly,  
not perfectly,  
but faithfully.

You may notice  
a quiet strengthening—

a deeper steadiness,  
a softness  
that does not give way.

This is not effort alone.

It is something  
taking root.

You are not asked  
to see the full expanse.

Only to remain  
with what is true  
in this moment.

And as you do,  
something begins to widen—

beyond your awareness,  
beyond your measure—

a goodness  
that no longer belongs  
only to you.
