## Day 27 – The Winds of Justice

> *The winds that move before me are not empty air, but the living breath of divine justice, steady and watchful.*

I saw a fiery cloud, alive with motion, and within it a wind that moved with great purpose.  
It stood before the face of a human figure, unwavering, as if guarding something sacred.

This wind burned with a quiet strength.  
It did not wander.  
It did not hesitate.

I understood that this was the justice of God—radiant and unyielding—holding within it the glory of the angels.  
These angels were not distant beings drifting in abstraction.  
They burned with love.  
They lived in the will of God as flame lives in fire.

Their gaze was fixed—not on the world, not on themselves—but on the face of God.  
And in that gaze, they were ready.

Always ready.

They did not turn away.  
They did not grow weary.  
They did not question their place.

They moved only as God willed, like breath that follows the body.  
Like light that cannot separate itself from its source.

Around them, other winds descended, carrying clouds that unfolded over the chest of the human form.  
These winds spoke of the long path of justice—stretching through time.

I saw the ancient law, firm and foundational.  
And I saw the Incarnation, where divine love entered flesh.

Both moved together, like two currents in one stream.

And through them, I saw that God knows the works of the faithful.  
Nothing is lost.  
Nothing fades into silence.

Even what is hidden is held.

The wind before the face stretched from east to south, steady and luminous.  
It leaned toward the righteous works of humanity, as if drawing them upward—  
toward a joy that had always been intended.

For God has given both angels and humans the gift of reason.  
And more—He has given each person a guardian, a presence that walks beside them.

In the beginning, this presence spoke like a distant voice.  
But now, it is closer.

Now, it is as a word joined to breath.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision reveals the nearness and constancy of divine justice—not as punishment, but as alignment with truth.

The angels represent a state of being that is fully turned toward God. Their gaze does not wander because they are not divided within themselves. They are unified in love, and therefore always ready.

The winds symbolize the movement of divine justice through history. The Old Law and the Incarnation are not opposed but connected—two expressions of the same divine intention to bring humanity into alignment with truth and love.

The image of God remembering the works of the saints speaks to a deep spiritual assurance: nothing done in love is ever lost. Even unseen acts are held within the eternal awareness of God.

The shift from angel as “voice” to angel as “word joined to breath” suggests increasing intimacy. Divine guidance is no longer distant or external, but inward, near, and woven into the very life of the soul.

This vision invites us to see justice not as cold judgment, but as a living force that gently draws us toward the fullness of who we are meant to be.

#### Living This Vision

There are moments when it feels like what we do does not matter. Quiet acts. Hidden efforts. Small choices toward goodness.

They seem to disappear. But this vision whispers otherwise. Nothing aligned with love is forgotten.

Even when you cannot see the fruit, something is being held—kept safe within a deeper order. You are not moving alone, either.

There is a guidance that does not shout.  
A presence that does not force. It is closer than you think. Perhaps the invitation is simple:  
to turn your gaze, even briefly, toward what is true.

To choose one small act that reflects that truth. And to trust that this turning—however small—is already part of something eternal.
