## 27 - The Cry of Truth

> *Truth does not strive to rise. It stands, and all else settles.*

A vast brightness stirred through the silence—not suddenly, but with the quiet familiarity of something ancient and enduring. It felt less like an arrival and more like a veil gently lifting from what had always been present beneath the surface of things. Everywhere, light moved in harmony. Nothing strained toward greatness. Nothing grasped beyond its place. Each presence rested fully within what had been given to it, and in that resting there was peace—an order shaped not by control, but by love.

The radiance carried no anxiety within it. It did not compete or compare. Like rivers flowing naturally toward the sea, everything belonged to the larger movement of wholeness. The beauty of the light was not in dominance, but in its deep and effortless coherence. All things seemed most alive precisely because they were no longer trying to become more than they were.

Then, within the stillness, a subtle tension appeared.

It was not violent or dramatic. It emerged quietly, almost imperceptibly at first—a reaching upward without root, a movement disconnected from the deeper harmony surrounding it. Like a shadow stretching itself toward a height it could not sustain, it sought elevation without grounding. For a brief moment it appeared to rise, and yet something within it had already begun to thin. What was built apart from truth could not endure the weight of its own illusion.

Into this silence came a single cry.

Not harsh. Not condemning. Clear as clean water moving over stone.

Who is like God?

The words moved gently through everything—not as a weapon, but as revelation. They did not force anything downward. They simply illuminated what was real. And in that illumination, what could not live within truth quietly fell away. Not rejected, not violently cast aside, but unable to remain where illusion could no longer hide itself from the light.

The radiance itself did not change. It remained what it had always been—steady, patient, whole. And within that constancy, all things slowly found their rightful place again.

### Living the Vision

There are moments when the soul becomes tempted to rise ahead of its own becoming. A person may long to appear wiser, more certain, more spiritually complete than they truly feel within themselves. Often this longing comes from a deeply human ache—the desire to be seen, valued, secure, or enough. And so the heart sometimes reaches upward before its roots have fully deepened into the soil of truth.

At first, this striving can feel convincing. The mind imagines that peace will come through achievement, recognition, certainty, or spiritual perfection. Yet beneath the effort there is often exhaustion, because the soul was never meant to sustain itself through performance alone. What is forced eventually becomes fragile.

But there is another movement available to us—slower, quieter, and far more trustworthy. It begins whenever we allow ourselves to stand honestly within the life we are actually living. In small moments of sincerity, something settles. The nervous striving softens. The need to appear greater than we are begins to loosen its grip.

Nothing precious is lost in this surrender to truth.

In fact, this is where genuine growth begins.

The soul does not need to manufacture fullness through endless effort. What is real unfolds naturally over time, like roots deepening beneath the earth or dawn slowly filling the horizon with light. And as we remain gently grounded in what is true, a quiet alignment begins to emerge within us—one that no longer needs to prove itself to the world. It simply becomes.
