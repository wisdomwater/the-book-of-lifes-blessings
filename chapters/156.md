## Day 156 - The Circle of Fire

> *The zeal of God burns with judgment, but also with mercy. What repentance purifies, wrath need not consume.*

Then, to the left of the man, I saw a figure almost human.

It stood near the forgotten places, near the iniquities done by those who disobey the law of God. This was the Zeal of God—the righteous judgment that sees all things clearly.

On its head was a circle of fire. From the circle came tongues of flame.

This fire has burned since the beginning of the world. It burned when the first angel fell. It burns against every offense kindled by the desire of rational creatures. Nothing escapes its examination.

Its face flashed with dazzling lightning.

And I understood: divine judgment reveals what must be purified. It comes to each soul according to what has been earned. It is not blind rage. It is radiant truth.

The rest of the figure was hidden beneath a marble covering, so I could not fully distinguish its form.

For the deep judgments of God cannot be measured or fully understood. They are wrapped in invincible strength. No creature has power to soften them, alter them, or examine them completely.

They pierce everything that has not been removed and cleansed by repentance.

The zeal of God does not consume what repentance has already purified.

Repentance is itself a fire.
Repentance is the whip that turns the soul back.

But what repentance does not burn away, the zeal of God will burn.

And this zeal cried out against the vices.

It overthrew the suggestions of evil spirits. It brought down the old invader and all who rejected justice.

For light overcomes darkness.

Good destroys evil.

And all things are subject to God.


#### What This Vision Means

The Zeal of God can be difficult imagery because it burns with judgment. Yet in Hildegard’s vision, judgment is not arbitrary cruelty. It is the fierce clarity of divine holiness.

The circle of fire suggests completeness. The tongues of flame suggest active purification. The lightning face reveals sudden truth—the kind that exposes what we have hidden even from ourselves.

The marble covering reminds us that God’s judgments are beyond full human understanding. We cannot reduce them to simple formulas or control them with our explanations.

But the passage gives hope: repentance changes how judgment meets us.

What is confessed and surrendered is already being purified. What is brought into the healing fire of repentance does not need to remain for the consuming fire of judgment.

God’s zeal is not opposed to mercy.

It is mercy refusing to let evil last forever.


#### Living This Vision

There are things in us that love darkness because darkness lets them remain unexamined.

A hidden resentment.
A private indulgence.
A pattern we excuse.
A harm we minimize.

The zeal of God is frightening only because it tells the truth.

But repentance lets us tell the truth first.

Today, bring one hidden thing into prayer. Not with panic. Not with self-hatred. With honesty.

Say:
This is here.
This is harming me.
This is not who I want to become.

Let repentance become a smaller fire now, so the soul does not need a greater burning later.

God’s fire is not sent to destroy what is true in you.

It burns to free it.
