## Day 135 - The Fall from Sacred Promise

> *What is offered to the Divine carries weight, and forgetting that offering leads the soul into disorientation and cold darkness.*

I lifted my gaze and saw a great height suspended in the air.

From it fell a strange storm—hail that burned with fire and froze with bitter cold. It struck with force, relentless and unyielding. Within this storm were souls who had once made a sacred vow of chastity, offering themselves wholly to God—and yet had turned away.

They fell from that height again and again.

As if driven by unseen winds, they were cast downward, only to be forced back up into the storm. They could not escape. Darkness surrounded them, binding them in place so that they could not move freely. The hail struck them without pause—fire and ice together, a contradiction that brought no relief.

Around them, harsh voices rose.

“Why did you abandon what you vowed?” the spirits cried out. “Why did you turn from what you freely gave?”

The souls had no answer.

They had fallen because they had forgotten. Not by accident, but by choosing not to remember. What they once held as sacred had become distant in their hearts. And so they wandered from it, step by step, until they could no longer feel its weight.

Now they endured both fire and cold.

The fire reflected the desires they chose over love. The cold revealed the emptiness left behind when that love was abandoned. Between these two, they found no rest.

And I saw another place—a vast swamp, thick with foul mud.

Within it struggled souls who had distorted the natural order of their being. They moved through the mire, pushed and driven by unseen forces. Evil spirits poured scalding water over them and forced them back into the mud with burning implements.

They had rejected the dignity of their nature, and now found themselves unable to stand upon it.

They sank and rose, again and again, without peace.

And I understood: when the soul turns away from its created harmony, it loses its footing.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision centers on faithfulness—especially to what is freely and consciously offered to God.

A vow is not merely a promise; it is a shaping of the soul around a sacred intention. To abandon it is not just to break a rule, but to unravel something within oneself. The fall from height symbolizes this loss of alignment. The soul that once stood in clarity now finds itself disoriented.

The mixture of fire and cold is deeply symbolic. Fire represents unrestrained passion, while cold reflects spiritual emptiness. Together, they show the inner contradiction of a life divided—seeking fulfillment in what ultimately cannot satisfy.

The swamp imagery speaks to the loss of grounding when one rejects the inherent dignity and order of one’s nature. To distort what was created with intention leads to confusion and instability.

At its core, this vision is about remembrance. Forgetfulness—especially willful forgetfulness—leads the soul away from life.

#### Living This Vision

We may not all take formal vows, but we all make inner commitments.

Moments where we say: this is who I want to be.  
This is what matters most.  
This is the path I will walk.

Over time, it is easy to drift.

Not in one dramatic moment, but in small choices. Quiet compromises. A slow forgetting.

This vision invites you to remember.

What have you once held as sacred?  
What have you quietly set aside?

There is no condemnation here—only an invitation to return.

Even if you have fallen many times, the act of remembering begins the restoration. You do not need to climb back all at once. You simply need to turn your attention again toward what is true.

Faithfulness is not perfection. It is returning, again and again.
