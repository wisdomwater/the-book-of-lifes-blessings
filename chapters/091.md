## Day 91 – The Veiled Beast

> *When the heart hardens fully, it no longer only resists God—it begins to destroy what is good.*
> *It wears a human face, but its spirit has turned away from life.*

Then I saw another figure arise from this roughness. It was called Ungodliness.

It appeared like a man—but its head was wrong. It sat low between its shoulders, more like the head of a wild beast than of a human being. Its form deceived, for it looked human at a glance, yet its nature was something else entirely.

Its eyes burned with a fierce and unyielding light. Its mouth was like that of a leopard—ready to tear and devour. It did not delight in what belonged to God. Instead, it sought to destroy whatever it could.

It had no mercy.  
It knew no grace.  
It did not recognize wisdom or restraint.

It trampled the just and mocked the holy.

From its mouth hung the heads of serpents, writhing and watchful. And flames poured out continually, like arrows of fire. These were its words—sharp, angry, and wounding—striking others again and again.

Its lower face was marked like a fish, signifying a strange and restless instability. Its will was stubborn and terrifying, yet also foolish—caught in illusions that it believed to be truth.

It knelt, but not in reverence. Its posture bent toward idols, drawing others into false worship. And those it deceived believed themselves to be righteous, even as they walked further into darkness.

A black veil covered its head, like a shadow that clung and would not lift. Its entire body was wrapped in a dark garment, stripped of all brightness.

Its sleeves hung empty.

Its works produced nothing.

Though it moved and spoke, there was no true life in it—only a hollow force that consumed and deceived.

And I understood: this is what becomes of the soul that has fully turned away from God. It not only grows hard—it loses its true form.

#### What This Vision Means

Ungodliness is the full flowering of a hardened heart.

What begins as excess becomes roughness.  
What continues as roughness becomes rejection.  
And rejection, if left unchecked, becomes destruction.

The distorted head reveals a loss of true perception. The person may still appear human outwardly, but inwardly their understanding is no longer aligned with truth. They cannot see clearly.

The flames from the mouth show how words become weapons. Speech, once meant to bless, now wounds and divides.

The serpents represent cunning and endless aggression—always seeking new ways to harm or deceive.

The black veil is especially important. It is not just darkness—it is a covering that feels almost like light. A false clarity. A person may feel certain, even righteous, while being deeply misled.

And the empty sleeves reveal the final truth:  
a life disconnected from God produces no lasting fruit.

There may be activity. There may be intensity.  
But there is no life-giving substance.

#### Living This Vision

This vision is not meant to condemn—but to awaken awareness.

Ungodliness rarely begins as something obvious. It often starts quietly:
- dismissing what is good
- justifying harshness
- losing tenderness toward others

Pay attention to your words.

Do they bring life… or do they carry heat, irritation, or subtle harm?

Notice where certainty has replaced humility.  
Where reaction has replaced discernment.  
Where you feel justified in being unkind.

These are early signs.

The path back is not through fear, but through returning to softness, truth, and reverence.

Lift the veil by choosing honesty.  
Lay down the fire by choosing gentleness.  
Turn from destruction by choosing life.

Even now, the soul can remember what it was made for.
