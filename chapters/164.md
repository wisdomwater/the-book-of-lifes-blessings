## Day 164 - The Black Flame

> *Earthly care becomes dangerous when it closes the eyes to heaven. The faithful hand may hold the plow, but the heart must still look toward God.*

After this, among the crowd I had seen before, I saw other evil spirits.

They cried out with loud voices:

“We will dwell in heaven where Lucifer will stand in honor.”

These spirits led human beings into anxious concern for earthly things. They persuaded them to give themselves completely to the cares of this world.

Then I saw a great fire.

Its flame was black, and many worms moved within it.

There the souls were punished who, while living in the world, had neglected heavenly things and poured all their strength into earthly concerns.

They shook in the fire from one side to another, as if driven by a violent wind.

They suffered the black flame because, in the darkness of unbelief, they neglected God while craving the things of earth. They were tormented by worms because worldly desire had made them insensitive. And they were shaken with great sorrow because they had persevered in this restless way.

And I saw and understood these things.

Then I heard a voice from the living light:

“The punishments you see are real. Those who wish to overcome the spirits that urge earthly anxiety must train their bodies and restrain their hearts. Let them turn their thoughts again toward heaven.”

Then those who are consumed by earthly concerns spoke foolishly within themselves:

“We will look to creation. We will attend to what is ready for our use. It gives us food and clothing. Let God do what pleases Him. If we place our care anywhere but earthly things, we will quickly fall.

What would we be then?

Like birds gazing at their faces in the water, doing nothing until death comes.”

They said, “The creatures were made for our use. If we did not use them, we would sin. We ask only what God has given. A horse without reins runs dangerously. If we do not attend to the earth, it will bring forth thorns and thistles.”

So they spoke, directing all their care toward the present life and not the life to come.

But the believer grips the plow behind the oxen and looks to God, who gives fertility to the ground and fruit to the earth.

The faithful one works with earthly things, but does not abandon divine things.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision does not condemn the earth. It does not reject work, food, clothing, farming, roads, animals, or the needs of the body. Hildegard’s image is more careful than that.

The danger is not caring for earthly life. The danger is being consumed by it.

The black flame shows what happens when concern loses its light. Earthly responsibility becomes anxiety. Use becomes craving. Stewardship becomes forgetfulness. The soul shakes from side to side, driven by every urgent need, every fear, every demand of survival.

Yet the final image is beautifully balanced. The believer still holds the plow. The oxen still move. The earth is still cultivated. But the eyes are lifted toward God.

This is the holy pattern: hands in the soil, heart in heaven.

#### Living This Vision

We live among many necessary concerns.

Bills must be paid. Meals must be prepared. Bodies must be cared for. Work must be done. The earth needs our attention.

But earthly care becomes a vice when it convinces us that God is secondary, distant, or unnecessary.

This vision asks us to notice where concern has become captivity. Where are we being shaken by the black flame of anxiety? Where have we mistaken control for faithfulness?

The invitation is not to abandon the plow. It is to hold it differently.

Work faithfully. Care for what has been given. But let the heart look toward the One who gives the fruit.
