## 52 — Joy That Lifts the Heart

> *Joy does not need another’s fall*  
> *It rises from what is whole within you*

I saw a light  
spreading softly across an open space—

warm,  
steady,  
without urgency.

Within it,  
there was movement—

small gestures,  
flickers of expression,  
a kind of laughter  
passing through the air.

At first,  
it rose quickly—  
bright,  
sharp,  
almost weightless.

It touched the surface of things  
and moved on,  
never settling,  
never resting.

But beneath it,  
I sensed a difference—

a quieter joy  
waiting.

Not rising from comparison,  
not shaped by what was lost  
or gained—

but present,  
like a still pool  
holding light.

And slowly,  
the lighter laughter  
began to soften—

losing its edge,  
its urgency,  
its need to move on.

It settled  
into something deeper.

The movement slowed.  
The space widened.

And what remained  
was not less—

it was fuller.

A joy that did not depend  
on anything outside itself.

A joy that did not fall  
when the moment passed.

It stayed—  
gentle,  
rooted,  
alive.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when  
something in you  
feels a quick lift—

a reaction,  
a sense of release,  
a passing satisfaction  
that comes and goes.

You may notice  
how easily it forms—  
how it can rise  
from comparison,  
from relief,  
from something shifting  
outside of you.

And yet,  
it rarely stays.

There is another kind of joy  
you may begin to sense—

quieter,  
less reactive,  
not dependent  
on what happens around you.

It does not need  
to be defended  
or repeated.

It simply is.

You can feel it  
in moments of presence—  
when you are not measuring,  
not comparing,  
not looking outward  
for confirmation.

It gathers you inward.

It steadies you.

And in that steadiness,  
something in you  
remembers what is true.

You do not have to  
chase this joy.

You can turn toward it—  
and let it rise  
from within.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Lift my joy  
>
> **Exhale:** Toward what is true
