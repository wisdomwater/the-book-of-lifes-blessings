## 52 - The Quieting Flame

> *What burns can become gentle warmth. Peace waits beneath the heat.*

I saw a fire moving across an open space, flickering restlessly beneath the darkening sky. It did not burn with steadiness, but leapt and turned in every direction, reaching outward toward whatever came near. The air around it trembled with tension, as though the flame itself could not find a place to settle. Sparks rose briefly into the night—bright for a moment, then vanishing into shadow.

There was energy within the fire, but also unrest. A searching that seemed unable to rest in its own center. It stretched outward constantly, consuming its own strength through ceaseless motion, as though it feared what silence might reveal if it became still.

Then from the distance, rain began to fall.

It came softly at first—light enough to barely touch the ground. The rain did not arrive to extinguish the fire or shame its intensity. Instead, it rested gently upon the edges of the flame, quieting what had become scattered and sharp. Slowly the movement changed. The reaching softened. The frantic turning eased.

And little by little, the fire gathered inward.

What had once flared wildly became smaller, steadier, and clear. At its center remained a living glow—warm and luminous, held peacefully within itself. No longer driven to consume, the fire began simply to give light. And I saw then that the flame itself had never been the danger. Only its restlessness had caused harm.

For when fire is gathered in peace, it becomes warmth. When it rests within stillness, it illuminates rather than destroys. And the gentle rain continued falling—faithful and patient—until the whole space grew quiet beneath its tender mercy.

**Living the Vision**

There are moments when something rises quickly within us—a sharp reaction, a surge of anger, defensiveness, urgency, or fear. In those moments, the inner fire can feel entirely justified, almost impossible to question. The body tightens. The mind races ahead. Words press against the lips before silence has time to form around them.

Yet beneath that immediate heat, there is often something deeper asking to be heard.

Sometimes the soul does not need suppression so much as spaciousness. A single breath. A pause long enough for awareness to return. The quiet wisdom traditions remind us that peace is not the absence of passion or emotion; it is the gentle grounding of those energies within love and clarity. Fire itself is not wrong. It can illuminate, protect, energize, and awaken. But when it moves without rest, it easily becomes destructive—to ourselves and to others.

This is why stillness matters so deeply.

In moments of pause, the sharpness begins to soften. What first felt urgent often reveals hidden sorrow, exhaustion, fear, or longing beneath it. The heart slowly learns that it does not need to react to every inner spark. Some flames are meant not to spread outward, but to become a quiet warmth carried within.

And over time, as we return again and again to this inner quieting, our presence changes. Our words become gentler without losing truth. Our strength becomes steadier without becoming cold. What once reached outward in restless intensity begins instead to offer warmth that others can safely draw near to.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Quiet what rises within

**Exhale:** I rest in Your peace
