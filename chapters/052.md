## 52 - The Quieting Flame

> *What burns can become gentle warmth*  
> *Peace waits beneath the heat*

I saw a flickering fire  
moving across an open space.

It was not steady.

It leapt and turned,  
reaching outward—  
touching whatever came near.

The air around it trembled,  
as though it could not rest.

Sparks rose and fell,  
brief and bright,  
then gone.

And beneath the movement,  
there was a tension—  
a searching  
that did not know where to settle.

Then,  
from a distance,  
a soft rain began to fall.

Not heavy.  
Not forceful.

Just enough.

It touched the edges of the flame—  
not to extinguish,  
but to quiet.

The fire shifted.

Its reaching slowed.  
Its sharpness softened.

What once leapt outward  
began to draw inward—  
becoming smaller,  
steadier.

At its center,  
a glow remained—  
warm,  
clear,  
held in place.

No longer scattered.

No longer consuming.

And I saw  
that the fire itself  
was not the problem—

only its unrest.

When it was gathered,  
it gave light.

When it was still,  
it gave warmth.

And the rain continued—  
gentle,  
faithful—  
until the whole space  
rested in quiet.

#### Living the Vision

You feel a rising heat within—

a quick reaction,  
a need to respond,  
a pull to speak  
before stillness has time to form.

It can feel strong.  
Certain.

As if it must be expressed  
right away.

But you may also notice  
another presence—  
quieter,  
slower.

It does not argue.

It simply invites you  
to pause.

You may feel it  
in a breath,  
in a moment of waiting,  
in the choice  
not to move immediately.

This pause  
is not weakness.

It is space.

And within that space,  
something begins to settle.

The sharpness softens.  
The urgency eases.

What remains  
is clearer—  
more grounded,  
more true.

You are not asked  
to remove your fire.

Only to let it be held  
in peace.

And as you return  
again and again  
to this quieting,  
your words begin to change—  
less driven,  
more alive.

Like warmth  
offered gently,  
instead of flame  
that reaches to consume.
