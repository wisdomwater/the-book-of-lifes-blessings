## Day 89 — The Serpent and the Open Mouth

> *Temptation begins by dulling the hunger for what truly satisfies.*

And I saw again the serpent  
lying in darkness.

Its eyes burned with deception.  
Its tongue stretched outward, ready to strike.

Its body was black—  
filled with forgetfulness of God.

And I understood:

This was the ancient enemy,  
drawing the desires of the righteous downward—  
from heaven toward the earth.

Its ways were poisoned,  
spreading disorder through the soul.

Its tail was cut,  
for it could not complete its purpose.

For though it seeks to devour all,  
it is restrained.

And I saw its open belly—  
ready to consume.

Within it lay the image of a man,  
resting in indulgence.

And I understood:

This was how the enemy works.

Not always through great evils,  
but through small indulgences  
that slowly turn the heart.

Through excess.  
Through distraction.  
Through forgetfulness.

The first fall came through food—  
through desire turned away from trust.

And still it works the same way.

Gluttony opens the door.  
Other vices follow.

And the soul, lulled into comfort,  
forgets its true hunger.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision returns to the root of many distortions—misdirected desire.

The serpent does not always force. It persuades. It shifts attention from what is higher to what is immediate.

Gluttony here is symbolic as well as literal. It represents excess—taking more than is needed, seeking satisfaction in what cannot truly satisfy.

The open belly shows how desire becomes consuming. The soul rests within what it feeds, gradually shaped by it.

The restraint of the serpent’s tail is important. Evil is not ultimate. It is limited.

This vision is both a warning and a reassurance.

Temptation is real.  
But it is not all-powerful.

#### Living This Vision

Pay attention to what fills your life.

Not only food—  
but attention,  
entertainment,  
comfort,  
distraction.

What you take in  
shapes what you become.

You do not need to reject everything.  
But you are invited to choose carefully.

Ask yourself:

Does this lead me upward  
or downward?

Does it bring clarity  
or dullness?

Even small adjustments matter.

A little more awareness.  
A little more restraint.

These are not losses.  
They are recoveries.

They restore your ability  
to hunger for what is real.
