## 89 - The Dew That Makes You Bloom

> *You do not rise by lifting yourself. You grow by receiving what is given.*

I saw a figure reaching upward with all its strength, straining toward a height it could not sustain. There was intensity in its movement, a restless determination that seemed convinced it must remain elevated in order to be secure. It stretched higher and higher, fearful perhaps that to lower itself would mean losing something essential.

And yet beneath this striving, the ground remained dry.

Nothing rooted there. Nothing truly flourished. The earth beneath the figure felt closed and hardened, unable to receive what might nourish it. There was movement, but no growth. Effort, but no deepening life.

Then the air began to soften.

A coolness gathered quietly across the land, light as breath moving before dawn. Dew descended gently upon the earth—not with force, not as something seized or conquered, but as a gift resting where the ground allowed itself to open. Tiny drops settled upon the waiting soil, and wherever they were received, life began stirring again.

Small green shoots emerged from the earth.

They did not rise through striving. They rose because they had been nourished.

The figure paused.

And for a brief moment, it lowered itself just enough to feel the coolness resting upon the ground. In that small yielding, something within it softened. The dryness loosened. The hardened places became open once more. What could not grow through effort alone slowly began to live through receiving.

And I saw then that nothing essential had been withheld.

Grace had always been near.

It was simply waiting for a heart willing to receive it.

**Living the Vision**

Many people spend much of their lives trying to rise above their own vulnerability. We strive to appear strong, self-sufficient, certain, capable, unaffected by weakness or need. Often this striving is not rooted in arrogance alone, but in fear—the fear that if we lower ourselves enough to admit our need, we may discover we are unsupported or unloved.

So the soul keeps reaching upward.

Yet endless self-reliance can quietly exhaust the heart. It becomes difficult to receive help, comfort, tenderness, or grace because receiving requires openness, and openness feels vulnerable. We begin holding ourselves together through effort alone, even while something deeper within us longs simply to rest.

The contemplative life offers another way.

Not humiliation. Not self-rejection. But honesty. A gentle recognition that we were never meant to sustain ourselves entirely alone. There is profound healing in admitting: I, too, need nourishment. I, too, need mercy. I, too, am being carried by gifts I did not create.

And when we allow ourselves to soften enough to receive, something living begins awakening within us.

Growth rarely happens through striving alone. Much of what transforms the soul arrives quietly—through grace, relationship, beauty, stillness, forgiveness, love, and the patient nourishment of ordinary moments. Like dew resting upon dry ground, these gifts cannot be forced into existence. They can only be welcomed.

The soul blooms not because it lifted itself high enough to deserve life, but because it finally became open enough to receive it.

And that openness is already enough for grace to begin its work.

**Breath Prayer**

**Inhale:** Open me to receive

**Exhale:** Let Your life grow in me
