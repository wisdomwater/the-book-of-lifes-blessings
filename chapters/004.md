41. SAINTS HAVE A FEELING UNANIMOUS SPIRITS.

Within the fiery cloud I saw a shining multitude. They lived in perfect harmony—one mind, one union—because the burning hosts of the holy spirits dwell in blazing glory and honor. Their splendor cannot be described, and their number cannot be counted except by the knowledge of God.

They desire all that God desires, and they are joined together as though they were one. They are united in a single principle, like one body made of many members. Though there are many, they share one life and one agreement of spirit.

42. GOD LAUNCHES TO EARTH BY THUNDER AND LIGHTNING TRIALS OF
    CITIZENS OF HEAVEN, AND TERRORIZES THE TRANSGRESSORS OF HIS
    LAW IN TIMES OF FAMINE, PESTILENCE AND WAR.

Before them stood a board covered everywhere with feathers, moving and flying according to God’s command, just as the precepts of God sustain them. This board represents the mysteries of divine judgment, which are revealed only when God chooses to reveal them.

The wisdom of God has written hidden things upon this board. The heavenly host looks upon it with careful attention, for God holds many mysteries within His secret plan, and the blessed spirits watch these mysteries with reverence.

When God sees people turning to idols, or breaking His law, the fiery trials of heaven—burning with His love—are stirred. Then the burning feathers of the winds are set in motion. Lightning and thunder are sent to the earth. People are shaken by famine, disease, and war. In this way the whole world is struck with fear and awakened to the power of divine judgment.

43. EACH JUDGMENT OF GOD LOS ANGELES REPRISE THEIR PRAISES.

As the angels gaze upon the board, the power of God within them resounds like a mighty trumpet, filled with every kind of harmonious music.

This means that when they behold the will of God, they also receive His virtues. They are filled with strength, with overflowing joy, and with one united praise of God.

They sing only the praise of God and proclaim that all His judgments are true and righteous, just as John heard and wrote in the Revelation.

44. JOHN IN REVELATION ON THIS ISSUE.

“O Lord God Almighty, your judgments are true and righteous!” (Revelation 16:7).

Its meaning is this:

O Lord! You are called “Lord” in reverent fear, “God” in love, and “Almighty” because you know all things. Your judgments are true and righteous. By true fear you overcome every false fear. By true love you gather and unite all love. By your true omnipotence you rule over every power.

When a person twists the law to serve their own interests instead of following what God has spoken, then your just judgment appears, showing that nothing can stand against you.

When a person places their love in the desires of the flesh rather than in your righteous love, you allow that love to become bitter with suffering, so that it finds no comfort unless it returns to you.

When someone breaks your law through sin and worships idols instead of honoring your name, you rise in righteous judgment against your enemy, who persuaded humanity through the first deception.

Then lightning and thunder fall upon the earth. Floodwaters are poured out. The land becomes barren. Disease and war are sent among people. Through these things humanity learns that it can do nothing without God, and that His judgments are indeed true and righteous.

45. THE ANGELS PRAISE THE GOOD WORKS OF MEN, AND GOD WANTS TO
    BE PRAISED BY ANGELS AND MEN.

The multitude of angels also praises human beings for the good works they perform. Because of this, they never cease their praise—not for an hour, nor for a single moment. Their praise continues without end.

God desires to be praised by the angels, for this increases His glory and gives Him delight. In the same way, He desires that the works of holy people be proclaimed through the praise of the angels. For the works of humanity are made in the image and likeness of God.

Thus God wills that the works of human beings be openly praised before Him by the angels. And since humanity is sustained by the help of God, He also desires to be praised by human beings, just as He is praised by the angels, so that His glory may be magnified.

46. NO MAN CAN KNOW ALL THE MYSTERIES OF GOD, SO, SOME OF WHICH
    ARE CELESTIAL SPIRITS IN THE PRESENCE OF GOD HAVE BEEN
    APPOINTED BY THE PROPHETS, SOME NOT.

God has ordered all things wisely. His secrets shine forth, yet they remain beyond the reach of human understanding. These mysteries belong to the angels and ministering spirits who rejoice in His presence.

Human beings cannot fully know these mysteries. They cannot understand what they are, or how they exist. Even though human knowledge may seem like a mountain rising high, seeking to imitate the wisdom of God, no one can see the summit of that mountain. No one can reach the height of God’s wisdom.

No person has seen it, no one can climb to it, and no one can fully learn the secrets of God—not even the secrets of those spirits who always stand in His presence.

Some of these heavenly spirits were revealed by the prophets and saints through the Holy Spirit. Others were not named, because the prophets spoke only a little, moved by divine inspiration, as the apostle Paul wrote.

47. PAUL ON THIS ISSUE

“I heard arcane words that no man can say.” (II Cor 12:4), which means:

The heart of Paul, strengthened and comforted by many great wonders, was filled with divine power. From this strength he planted seeds of virtue in others. In this state he heard hidden words—mysteries filled with divine knowledge—that had been concealed from humanity. People do not know where these mysteries come from, why they are hidden, or how they are revealed.

Since the divine nature cannot be fully examined by human beings, it is not lawful for a person to explain how these words were spoken, or how the praises of God’s mysteries sound. These mysteries are hidden deep within the secret places of the spirits and remain beyond human understanding while we live in the flesh.

48. WHEN MEN PERSEVERE GOOD AND LIVING IN HOLINESS, THE SPREAD
    OF GOOD WORKS HIS GOES BEYOND THE HUMAN HEART.

I saw the wind move the thundercloud mentioned above, driving it from the southwest. This signifies that divine justice tests human actions through many trials. From the south it leads people toward perfect faith through perseverance.

In this way those who struggle against the devil endure in goodness and live in holiness with God.

The cloud appeared square in its length and width, yet its size could not be grasped by the human mind. This shows that the reach of good works, the greatness of virtue, and the love that flows from them are so vast that their fullness surpasses the capacity of the human heart to understand.

49. IN THE HALLS OF HEAVEN, THEY SOUGHT THEM YOUR HOLY WORKS,
    ARE THE SOULS OF SAINTS.

Within this cloud there was also a vast multitude of saints who possessed the spirit of life. Their number could not be counted.

They are the blessed dwellings prepared for the souls of the saints because of their holy and faithful works. There they live in joy and blessedness. Their number is so great that no one can count them except God alone.
