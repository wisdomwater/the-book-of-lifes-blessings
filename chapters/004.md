## 4 — The Joy That Rooted

> *Not all laughter nourishes*  
> *Some joy waits for stillness*

A soft meadow opened in the early light.

The air was gentle—  
alive with small sounds,  
like quiet laughter  
carried on the breeze.

A creature moved there,  
light on its feet,  
playful and bright.

It leapt and turned,  
pausing only long enough  
to look for something new to follow.

Its energy was warm,  
inviting—

like a familiar rhythm  
that asks nothing  
but to be enjoyed.

And for a time,  
everything felt easy.

The movement,  
the lightness,  
the way each moment slipped  
into the next.

But beneath the surface,  
the ground remained untouched.

No roots pressed downward.  
No stillness gathered.

Only motion.

Only sound.

Then, slowly,  
the wind softened.

The meadow grew quiet.

And in that quiet,  
something deeper emerged—

not replacing the joy,  
but holding it.

A presence  
steady as the earth itself.

Unmoving,  
yet full of life.

The creature stilled.

Its breath slowed.

And the laughter that once scattered outward  
began to settle inward—

becoming something quieter,  
truer.

Not lost—  
but rooted.

#### Living the Vision

There are moments when joy comes quickly.

A joke,  
a distraction,  
a burst of lightness  
that lifts the weight of the day.

You may notice how easily the mind follows—  
moving from one moment to the next,  
grateful for the relief.

There is nothing wrong here.

And yet,  
there are other moments  
when something in you longs  
for a different kind of joy.

Not louder,  
but deeper.

You may feel it  
in the spaces between things—  
when the noise fades,  
and you are left  
with yourself.

At first,  
it can feel unfamiliar.

Even uncomfortable.

But if you remain,  
just for a breath longer than usual,  
you may begin to sense  
a quiet steadiness beneath it all.

A place in you  
that does not need to chase  
or perform  
or stay in motion.

From there,  
joy does not disappear.

It softens.

It deepens.

It becomes something  
you can rest in—  
not something you must keep alive.

#### Breath Prayer

> **Inhale:** Ground me in stillness  
>  
> **Exhale:** Let my joy be true
