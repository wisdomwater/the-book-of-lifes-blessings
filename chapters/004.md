## Day 4 — The Laughing Hound

> *Not every laughter brings life.
> Some laughter hides the slow erosion of the soul.*

In the vision I saw another image.

It appeared like a dog trained for the hunt. The creature stood upright upon its hind legs. Its front paws rested against a straight cane, as though leaning upon it for balance.

Its tail wagged with eager excitement.

The creature seemed cheerful and lively, and it spoke with playful delight:

“What harm is there in joy? Why should a person not laugh and enjoy themselves, even with the simplest things?

The breath of the soul is beautiful and lively. It sings with melody and brightness. What kind of man would feel only the heaviness of death?

No one!

So let us laugh and enjoy ourselves while we can.”

The creature spoke as though its words were harmless and wise.

But then I heard a voice answer from the stormy cloud.

“You wicked and restless spirit, always eager to mock and jest.

You are like the wind that blows in every direction, never steady and never rooted. With your foolishness you resemble worms that dig endlessly through the earth.

When people see you, they welcome you gladly. You run toward them with cheerful gestures like a friendly dog, and by this charm you persuade them to follow their own desires.

But your words are empty.

They wound the heart even while they pretend to bring delight.

You turn your whims into laws and capture the hearts of men with your careless laughter.

But I wear the belt of holiness and the cloak of honesty.

I am the noble companion of the royal marriage.

Through me shines the lineage of discipline, adorned with the jewels of justice.”

#### What This Vision Means

The hunting dog represents **petulance**—a restless spirit that seeks amusement without wisdom.

Its laughter is not the deep joy that arises from love or truth. Instead, it is the shallow laughter that mocks seriousness, avoids reflection, and turns everything into entertainment.

The image of the dog standing upright is important. It imitates human posture, suggesting a distortion of the human spirit. The creature appears lively and appealing, wagging its tail and approaching others with cheerful energy.

This is how petulance works.

It disguises itself as harmless fun.

But the voice of Discipline exposes the deeper danger. When laughter becomes constant distraction, the soul loses its center. Life becomes a series of impulses, jokes, and passing pleasures.

Such a spirit blows “like the wind in all directions,” never rooted in wisdom.

Discipline is not the enemy of joy. Instead, it guards the deeper forms of joy that endure. Hildegard calls it the companion of “royal marriage”—a symbol of harmony between soul and divine order.

Without discipline, laughter becomes noise.

With discipline, joy becomes music.

#### Living This Vision

Modern life is full of noise.

Entertainment is constant. Humor fills our screens. Everything is designed to keep our attention moving quickly from one amusement to another.

None of this is wrong in itself.

But the soul can quietly lose its depth when laughter becomes our only refuge.

Sometimes we laugh because we are avoiding something deeper—grief, reflection, responsibility, or the quiet call of the spirit.

Petulance lives in that constant motion.

Discipline, however, invites us into a steadier rhythm.

It does not remove joy from life. Instead, it makes room for a deeper joy to grow. When we slow down, when we listen, when we allow silence to exist beside laughter, the soul begins to breathe again.

The goal is not to eliminate delight.

It is to let our delight grow roots.

> **Inhale:**
> Ground my heart in wisdom

> **Exhale:**
