## Day 162 - The Returning Spirit

> *Remember the One who formed you before the body weakens and the spirit returns to its source. The soul was made for light, and even now it is being shaped for joy.*

I heard the words of wisdom:

“Remember your Creator in the days of your youth, before the days of sorrow come, before the dust returns to the earth, and the spirit returns to God who gave it.”

And I understood them in this way.

You who long for the glorious life and the peace that does not end, remember the One who created you. Remember Him not only with your thoughts, but with good and holy works.

Do this while strength is still within you.

Do this while the soul can still grow in holiness.

For a time comes when flesh and blood grow thin, when the bones seem uncovered, when the body returns to the earth from which it was formed. The body becomes dust again, and the spirit that gave it life leaves it behind.

Then the spirit returns to the Lord of all, the One who first breathed life into it according to His grace.

God is like a blacksmith.

He tends the fire with bellows. He turns the iron. He strikes and shapes the work until it is brought to completion.

So too the soul is shaped in the furnace of life.

When the human spirit is rightly guided by good works, it may return to eternal happiness. It may enter the joy that has no ending. It may see the pure light and hear the song of the angels, the light and song Adam knew before he fell into death.

And the soul longs for the body also to share in this joy.

For what was once stripped and weakened will one day be clothed again in glory.

These things were shown concerning the cleansing and salvation of souls who turn back to God. Whoever has faith should consider them carefully and remember to do what is good.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision begins with remembrance. To remember God is not merely to think of Him from time to time. It is to live as one who knows where life comes from and where life returns.

The image of God as a blacksmith is strong and tender at once. Fire does not only burn; it also shapes. The soul is not abandoned in the heat of life. It is being formed. The blows, the waiting, the turning, the pressure—all may become part of a hidden work of grace.

The body weakens, but the spirit is not lost. Hildegard sees death not as disappearance, but as return. The soul returns to the One who gave it breath. And beyond the shrinking of flesh, there is still light. There is still song. There is still the promise that what was wounded may be restored.

#### Living This Vision

This reading invites us to remember before we are forced to remember.

We often wait until grief, sickness, age, or loss awakens us. But wisdom calls earlier. It calls while we still have strength to choose mercy, truth, humility, and love.

To live this vision is to ask: What is being shaped in me today? Am I resisting the fire, or allowing grace to form me through it?

Every ordinary act of goodness becomes part of the soul’s return. Every small turning toward God prepares us for the light.
