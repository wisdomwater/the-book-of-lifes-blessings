## Day 129 - The Fire of Arrogance

> *I saw pride rise as the first lie, wanting to stand above God, yet it could not receive the dew of blessing or bear the fruit of virtue.*

After this I saw other evil spirits in the crowd.  
They cried out with a loud voice:

“Who is God?  
Lucifer is God, and there is no other.”

These spirits teach arrogance to human hearts.  
They persuade people to think themselves better than others.

Then I saw a great fire burning with terrible force.  
Within it was a mass of large and dreadful worms.

There the souls were punished  
who, while living in the body,  
had lifted themselves up in pride  
through words and deeds.

They were tormented by fire  
because of their arrogance.

They were tortured by worms  
because of their presumption.

And I heard the voice from the living light say:

“What you see is true.  
Those who wish to overcome the spirits  
that draw them into pride  
must kneel.  
They must submit themselves.  
They must humble the body  
with sighs and tears.”

For these are the instruments of battle  
against pride.

The rough garment holds back proud thoughts  
from rising too easily in the mind.

Kneeling bends the body  
so the soul may learn to sigh.

The scourge restrains pride.

Sighs wound it.

Tears drown it.

God sees the sighs and tears  
that confess before Him.

And so pride is pressed down.

Then I understood:

Arrogance is the first lie,  
the lie that denies God.  
It is the mother of many vices.

As the human body is directed through the senses,  
so pride gathers many sins to itself—  
hatred, disobedience, vainglory, falsehood—  
and drags them along its crooked path.

Arrogance wants to rise above God.

Hatred refuses to acknowledge Him.

Disobedience rejects His commands.

Vainglory seeks a name it cannot possess.

Falsehood tries to limit the living God  
and chooses another god in His place.

As Eve became mother of the human race,  
so pride becomes mother of vices.

The one stained by pride  
lacks the love of God  
and does not bloom  
beneath the dew of blessed virtues.

Therefore whoever desires to serve God  
must flee pride  
and move far from it  
into humility.

#### What This Vision Means

This vision shows arrogance as the root distortion of the soul. It does not merely mean confidence or strength. It is the lie that the self can stand above God, above others, and above truth.

The fire reveals the burning nature of pride. Pride inflames the soul with self-importance. The worms reveal its hidden corruption. What looks grand outwardly is inwardly being eaten away.

The remedies are bodily and humble: kneeling, sighing, tears, submission. These are not punishments for their own sake. They are ways of teaching the proud self that it is not sovereign. The body bows so the soul can remember its place.

The phrase “dew of blessing” is tender and severe. Virtue grows like a living plant under grace. Pride cannot receive that dew because it refuses dependence. It wants to be self-made, self-exalting, self-justifying.

Humility is not self-hatred. It is the return to reality: God is God, and all goodness grows from Him.

#### Living This Vision

Pride often feels like protection.

It keeps us from feeling small.  
It helps us avoid shame.  
It gives us a story where we are right, superior, and secure.

But pride also isolates.

It makes correction unbearable.  
It makes gratitude thin.  
It makes other people feel like threats.

This vision invites a simple practice: bow.

Not only with the body,  
but with the heart.

When you feel yourself rising above another person,  
pause and return to humility.

Say quietly:  
I am not above them.  
I am not my own source.  
I am held by mercy.

Let tears come if they come.  
Let sighs pray what words cannot.

The soul does not lose dignity by kneeling.  
It recovers it.
