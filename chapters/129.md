## 129 - The Dew That Makes You Bloom

> *You do not rise by lifting yourself*  
> *You grow by receiving what is given*

I saw a figure  
reaching upward—  
straining toward a height  
it could not sustain.

Its movement was strong,  
but restless—  
as though it needed  
to remain above  
in order to be secure.

And yet,  
the ground beneath it  
was dry.

Nothing rooted.  
Nothing grew.

Then,  
very quietly,  
the air softened.

A coolness gathered—  
light as breath.

Dew began to fall.

Not in force,  
but in gentleness—  
settling upon the earth,  
resting where it was welcomed.

Where the ground opened,  
life stirred.

Small green shoots  
pressed upward—  
not by striving,  
but by receiving.

The figure paused.

For a moment,  
it lowered itself—  
just enough  
to feel the coolness.

And in that small yielding,  
something changed.

The dryness loosened.  
The ground softened.

What could not grow before  
began to live.

And I saw  
that nothing had been withheld—  
only waiting  
for a place  
willing to receive.

#### Living the Vision

Something in you  
wants to rise above—  
to feel certain,  
secure,  
untouched by weakness.

It can feel like strength.

But you may also notice  
how it closes you—  
how it makes it harder  
to receive help,  
to admit need,  
to remain open.

There is another movement  
that may feel unfamiliar at first.

A softening.

A willingness  
to come down—  
not into shame,  
but into truth.

You may feel it  
as a quiet honesty:  
I do not hold myself together.  
I am being held.

This is not loss.

It is the beginning  
of something living.

When you allow yourself  
to receive—  
to be met,  
to be given to—  
something in you  
begins to open again.

Growth does not come  
from holding yourself high.

It comes  
from allowing grace  
to reach you  
where you truly are.

And that is enough  
for life to begin.

#### Breath Prayer

>
