## Part I — The Man Who Faces the Rising Light

A great and luminous Man appears, towering so high that his head touches the clouds of heaven while his feet reach toward the depths below. His shoulders rise above the clouds into the clear light of heaven. His body stretches through the air of the world, and every part of him stands within a realm of creation that holds its own meaning.

He faces the East, the place of dawn, and he looks toward the South, where warmth and life flourish.

His face shines with a radiant brightness. From his mouth emerges a white cloud, a place where truth is spoken.

But from the North comes darkness. There the devil dwells, empty of joy and filled with spirits that weave traps for human hearts.

From this dark mist appear images of human vices. Each one takes a form that reveals its character. Yet each time a vice appears, a voice rises from the bright cloud—a virtue answering it with truth.

In this way the soul learns that every deception has its healing opposite.

---

## Part II — The Man Turns Toward the West

The Man turns and looks toward the West and the North.

Great wings rest upon his shoulders and across his chest and back, lifted as if ready to rise into flight.

Between these wings appear books filled with wisdom, each revealing something about the path of life.

The white cloud that surrounds his body is filled with the souls of the righteous, resting in the peace of God.

Yet in the mist above them, new images appear—more vices that trouble the human heart. Again, virtues arise to answer them, revealing that every distortion of the soul can be healed by its opposite grace.

---

## Part III — The Man Turns Toward the North

The Man now turns to face the North and the East.

The winds, the air, and the fertile forces of the earth clothe his lower body like a living garment. Fire and light adorn his form.

The elements of creation cry out to him. They lament that human wrongdoing has disturbed the harmony of the world. Because of this, the earth groans and longs for justice.

The Man answers that he will purify creation. Though humanity often forgets its Creator, every other creature remembers the One who made it.

Again the mist reveals new vices, and again virtues rise to answer them, guiding the soul back toward harmony.

---

## Part IV — The Man Looks Toward the South

The Man now turns toward the South and the West.

The earth surrounds his legs, alive with *viriditas*—the greening power of life. It holds the seeds of all living things and reflects the beauty and strength that God has placed within creation.

For the earth itself was formed to serve the work of God in humanity.

Once more the mist reveals vices that distort the human heart. And once more virtues appear, reminding us that every disorder in the soul can be restored.

---

## Part V — The Man Beholds the Whole World

Now the Man looks upon the entire globe.

His feet rest within the deep waters of the abyss. Yet this abyss does not threaten him—it reveals his power to sustain and purify all things.

The life within him flows through creation like a soul animating a body.

From his presence the waters move and the forces of the world are held in balance.

Even here the mist brings forth more images of vice, and again virtues answer them, calling the human heart back to wisdom and stability.

---

## Part VI — The Renewal of All Things

At last the Man stretches his power to the four corners of the earth.

Upon his thigh appears a unicorn, symbol of the Son of God.

The unicorn speaks:

The things that were made will pass away.
And what has not yet been created will come to life.

At the end of time God will shake the foundations of the world. All things stained by human wrongdoing will be purified.

The forces of darkness will be stripped of their power. The devil will be defeated.

Then a new creation will appear—a radiant heaven and a purified earth.

In this final vision there are no more vices, because the time of temptation has ended.

Human works will remain forever. Those shaped by love will shine in the light of heaven, while those bound to darkness will remain with it.

And the faithful—those who served their Creator with love—will enter a joy so great that no earthly language can fully describe it.

For what they now taste only in the soul, they will then experience in both body and soul together, in a life that will never fade.
